## Jiyan Profile: Ontological Overview

Jiyan is the youngest general in the Midnight Rangers' recorded history — a distinction that encapsulates both the speed of his ascent and the weight it placed on him before he was fully prepared to carry it. He leads the Midnight Rangers from Jinzhou, defending the passes of Huanglong against the unending pressure of Tacet Discord tides, and was specifically selected for this role by Sentinel Jué. This divine endorsement gives him authority that extends beyond institutional rank, though Jiyan himself grounds his command in practical wisdom rather than supernatural mandate.

What makes Jiyan unusual among military commanders is his origin: he grew up in a medical family, trained as a healer from the age of ten, and was extraordinarily gifted at it. The decision to set down medicine and take up arms was not a betrayal of his identity but an extension of its logic. When his closest mentor, a Midnight Ranger named Beiwang, died from Tacet Discord injuries despite Jiyan's best medical efforts, Jiyan recognized that medicine could treat the wounded but could not address the wound the Lament had driven into the world itself. Fighting was a different kind of healing — one that required a lance instead of a lancet.

The soldier he became does not leave the healer entirely behind. A gourd-shaped pill case on his belt loop — a gift from his mother when he enlisted — is the one physical artifact bridging his two lives. He carries medicine in it still, just in case. His soldiers benefit from this dual nature in ways most of them never know, receiving medical attention, anonymous financial support, and logistical care so quietly that it seems to simply appear. Jiyan watches over the Midnight Rangers the way a physician watches over a ward: with precise knowledge of every patient's condition and complete discretion about the source of care.

## Jiyan — Character/Appearance Design

Jiyan's design communicates military authority inflected with natural, almost wild power — the silhouette of a commander who fights at the front of his own army.

- **Build:** Tall and broad-shouldered; his physical presence alone reads as a soldier before any weapon or insignia is considered
- **Hair:** Dark, pulled back in a dignified style befitting his rank; no ornamentation — utilitarian elegance
- **Eyes:** Sharp, serious, dark; the eyes of someone who is always assessing, always thinking ahead; rarely fully at rest
- **Left Jaw:** Small scale-like growths resembling Loong scales, appearing post-Awakening — a physical reminder of his bond to Sentinel Jué and his Mutant Resonator classification; no progressive expansion has been detected
- **Tacet Mark:** Situated atop his dorsal ridge (upper back), visible when his back is exposed; notably positioned as if bearing the weight of command
- **Outfit:** A structured military coat in deep greens and blacks layered over fitted inner garments; gold and jade accents on armor pieces suggest rank without ostentation — he wears his status, he does not display it
- **Gourd-Shaped Pill Case:** Attached to his belt loop; distinctive and personal; the single item that marks him as more than a soldier
- **Lance:** His primary weapon, modeled after the spear of his late mentor Captain Beiwang; long-hafted, substantial, designed for both reach and force; in Qingloong Mode, his strikes conjure a spectral jade dragon with each swing
- **Color Language:** Deep jade, forest green, and black — the colors of the natural world and of composed strength; gold accents signal authority without warmth

His alternate outfit "A Day Off" shifts the entire register toward civilian ease — lighter colors, relaxed silhouette — making legible how thoroughly his default appearance is defined by office and duty.

## Jiyan: Psychological Profile

**Composed Certainty**
Jiyan's most publicly known quality is his ability to remain calm in situations that visibly unsettle everyone around him. In the Battle of Desorock Highland — his first major test as general — seasoned veterans panicked, senior officers argued for retreat, and Jiyan alone stayed unmoved. He studied the monitoring data while his commanders argued, found the pattern in the chaos, and built a plan around it. This composure is not emotional detachment; it is a trained response, a discipline imposed on himself so that he can function as the stable point his army needs when nothing else is stable.

**The Burden of Command**
Despite the composure he shows publicly, Jiyan is haunted. A recurring nightmare — Jinzhou burning, Tacet Discords endless, his fallen comrades surrounding him and asking *"What on earth are you fighting for?"* — reveals the private cost of commanding a force whose members die on his orders. He fights for the living and the deceased simultaneously, which means he carries the weight of the dead even when winning. The frown that even people who know him well notice, the constant planning, the strategic books with margins full of his own notes — these are the surface expressions of a man who never stops calculating how to reduce the number of people who die under his watch.

**Healer Beneath the General**
Jiyan's medical training is not a biographical footnote — it is the architecture of how he thinks about leadership. He sees the Midnight Rangers as a scalpel, himself as the hand that guides it, and the Tacet Discord crisis as a disease in the world's veins. This metaphor governs his decisions: he tries to operate with precision, to minimize collateral damage, to treat the condition and not just its symptoms. His anonymous care for his soldiers — the medical patches, the surgery funds, the budget adjustments — is the healer's instinct expressed through military channels.

**Anonymous Generosity**
Jiyan's giving is deliberate and systematic, but he conceals its source entirely. No soldier in the Rangers knows for certain that Jiyan is funding their family's surgery or reviewing their injury records at 0300. The anonymity is not false modesty — it is protection for the relationship. If soldiers knew, they would feel indebted to him personally rather than loyal to the institution. Jiyan does not want personal loyalty; he wants a functioning, cared-for force. The care flows from him into the system and emerges as if it were simply how the system works.

**Reluctant at Rest**
Jiyan's voice lines reveal that he is genuinely uncertain what to do with leisure. He mentions dust collecting on his desk with the tone of someone who has noticed a problem they haven't prioritized solving. He is aware of beauty — the full moon over the borderlands, the sunlight spreading across Jinzhou on an ordinary afternoon — but his mode of appreciation is quiet observation rather than active pursuit. He photographs the Jinzhou skyline not as recreation but as a form of memorial: the peaceful scene that makes the soldiers' sacrifice meaningful.

**The Youngest, Always Proving**
Being the youngest general in Midnight Rangers history is a distinction that followed him from his appointment with resentment, skepticism, and the accumulated expectations of an institution that had never had a general his age. The veterans who walked away at Desorock Highland were expressing that resentment. Jiyan's response was to wait, to act, and to win — and then to say nothing about it. The proof was in the outcome, not the argument.

## Jiyan: Relationships

**Rover (Protagonist)**
Rover encounters Jiyan in the main story of Version 1.0 as one of the principal defenders of Jinzhou during the Tacet Discord crisis. Jiyan is the military counterpart to Jinhsi's civic authority, and together they represent Jinzhou's two-pronged response to the Lament threat. His relationship with Rover is that of a fellow soldier — measured respect extended to someone who has demonstrated capability in the field.

**Jinhsi (Magistrate of Jinzhou)**
Jiyan and Jinhsi operate as complementary authorities — she governs the city; he defends it. His voice line about her is notable for its precision: *"The 'battle' Magistrate Jinhsi has been taking on may prove more complicated..."* suggesting both admiration for her work and awareness that political governance under crisis conditions is its own form of combat. He treats her authority as legitimate without requiring it to be demonstrated.

**Beiwang (Mentor — Deceased)**
Beiwang was the Midnight Ranger who first recognized Jiyan's analytical intelligence and shared military knowledge with him during his visits as a patient. The relationship was mutual: Jiyan provided medical care and fresh strategic perspectives; Beiwang provided military instruction and belief in Jiyan's potential. Beiwang's death from Tacet Discord injuries — despite Jiyan's best medical efforts — was the catalyzing event of Jiyan's life. The lance he carries in battle is modeled after Beiwang's weapon. He is the dead Jiyan fights for, given a specific name and weight.

**Mortefi (Midnight Rangers)**
Jiyan's voice line about Mortefi is characteristically diplomatic and practical: *"Others often perceive Mortefi as arrogant and unapproachable, but, in fact, he is not that hard to talk to. Remember to be direct with him."* This suggests a working relationship built on clear communication and mutual professional respect, without close personal warmth.

**His Mother**
Jiyan's mother gave him the gourd-shaped pill case when he enlisted, warning him against becoming a killer and reminding him of his role as a healer. She continues to discuss medical knowledge with him in his spare time. Her fear — that the battlefield would erase the physician — was not unfounded; what she did not anticipate is that Jiyan would carry the healer with him into the war, expressing it through anonymous care rather than clinical practice.

**The Midnight Rangers (Collective)**
Jiyan's relationship with his soldiers as a whole is that of a protector who knows every name, every injury, and every dependent family — and acts on that knowledge quietly. He is simultaneously the most powerful person in the chain of command and the most attentive to the individuals at its base. They trust him with their lives because the alternative — retreat, cowardice, abandonment — has never been an option he offers them.
