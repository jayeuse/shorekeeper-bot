# Jiyan Kit Documentation

## Jiyan: Combat Archetype and Role

- **Element:** Aero
- **Weapon Type:** Broadblade
- **Role:** Main DPS — on-field Qingloong Mode hypercarry
- **Archetype:** Heavy Attack AoE specialist; Liberation-state sustained damage dealer
- **Introduced:** Version 1.0 (first limited 5-star banner in Wuthering Waves)

Jiyan is a straightforward but high-reward Main DPS built around a single premise: build Resolve, spend it on his Resonance Liberation, enter **Qingloong Mode**, and deal enormous AoE Aero damage with empowered Heavy Attacks. He has the gentlest learning curve of any 5-star DPS at his release while still offering depth via Resolve management and Intro Skill timing. Approximately 90% of his total damage is generated inside Qingloong Mode. Outside of it, his role is resource accumulation — dealing modest damage and building toward the next Liberation window. He is the first character in Wuthering Waves whose entire combat identity is defined by entering and maximizing a Liberation state.

## Jiyan: Key Resources (Forte Mechanics Overview)

Jiyan's Forte Circuit manages two distinct resources that both feed into his Liberation burst: **Resolve** (the primary gauge, capping at 60) and **Momentum** (an inherent passive stack counter, capping at 2). Both must be understood to optimize his rotation.

- **Resolve** is the gauge visible on his Forte bar. It is generated by hitting enemies with Basic Attack: Lone Lance and by his Intro Skill: Tactical Strike. It is spent in two ways: consuming 30 Resolve when casting Resonance Skill Windqueller for a +20% DMG bonus, or consuming 30 Resolve when activating Resonance Liberation to trigger the massively powerful follow-up strike Emerald Storm: Finale. The correct use is almost always to save Resolve for Liberation rather than spending it on Windqueller — the Liberation payoff is far larger.
- **Momentum** is a separate 2-stack counter managed by an Inherent Passive. Each Heavy Attack, Intro Skill cast, or Resonance Skill Windqueller cast grants 1 Momentum stack (up to 2). When Emerald Storm: Finale is triggered, it consumes all Momentum stacks — each consumed stack adds **+120% DMG multiplier** to Finale's damage, making the maximum +240% bonus possible when entering Liberation with 2 Momentum stacks pre-loaded.
- **Resolve Decay:** If Jiyan does not hit a target within 15 seconds, Resolve gradually decreases. This encourages continuous engagement and makes extended transitions between targets a mild DPS concern.

## Jiyan Forte Circuit: Resolve & Momentum

**Resolve Generation**
- **Basic Attack: Lone Lance hits:** Each hit grants Resolve (rate dependent on hit count and stage)
- **Intro Skill: Tactical Strike:** Grants Resolve on hit; S3 additionally grants a flat +30 Resolve after the Intro Skill cast
- **Maximum cap:** 60 Resolve
- **Decay:** Begins if no target is hit for 15 seconds

**Resolve Consumption and State Transitions**
- **Threshold 1 — Resonance Skill Enhancement (30 Resolve):** Consuming 30 Resolve on Windqueller grants +20% DMG to that cast; in Qingloong Mode, Windqueller automatically gains +20% without consuming Resolve
- **Threshold 2 — Liberation Enhancement (30 Resolve):** Consuming 30 Resolve when casting Liberation: Emerald Storm Prelude triggers **Emerald Storm: Finale** — a massive AoE Heavy Attack that deals Aero DMG immediately after Prelude; strongly recommended over Skill use
- After triggering Finale, Jiyan enters **Qingloong Mode**

**Qingloong Mode**
- Duration: tied to Liberation timing; active until Liberation window expires
- All Basic Attacks, Heavy Attacks, and Dodge Counter are replaced with **Heavy Attack: Lance of Qingloong** (3-hit continuous chain)
- Each Lance of Qingloong attack summons a spectral jade Qingloong (dragon) that strikes 8 additional times and sweeps nearby enemies
- Increased resistance to interruption throughout Qingloong Mode
- Windqueller during Qingloong Mode gains +20% DMG bonus automatically (no Resolve cost)

**Momentum Stack Mechanics (Inherent Passive)**
- +1 stack per: Heavy Attack use, Intro Skill Tactical Strike cast, Resonance Skill Windqueller cast
- Maximum: 2 stacks
- Consumption: Emerald Storm Finale consumes ALL Momentum stacks on cast; each stack adds **+120% DMG Multiplier** to Finale
- Optimal rotation enters Liberation with exactly 2 Momentum stacks pre-loaded for +240% bonus to Finale

## Jiyan: Character Kit: Basic Attack — Lone Lance

**Ground Attack Chain (5 stages)**
- Jiyan performs up to 5 consecutive Broadblade attacks dealing Aero DMG
- This is his Resolve-generation tool outside of Qingloong Mode; it should not be used as a primary damage source in optimal play — Basic Attack levels can remain at Lv. 1
- Completing the chain generates sufficient Resolve over time to enable Windqueller and Liberation spending

**Heavy Attack Variants (3 types)**
- **Heavy Attack (standard):** Consumes Stamina to thrust forward, dealing Aero DMG; generates 1 Momentum stack
- **Heavy Attack: Windborne Strike:** Hold Basic Attack during Heavy Attack → launches follow-up after Heavy Attack ends, dealing Aero DMG; enables Banner of Triumph aerial follow-up
- **Heavy Attack: Abyssal Slash:** Release Basic Attack during Heavy Attack → downward slash follow-up after Heavy Attack ends, dealing Aero DMG

**Mid-Air Attacks**
- **Mid-Air Attack:** Standard stamina-consuming plunging attack, Aero DMG; followed up with Basic Attack for additional Aero DMG
- **Mid-Air Attack: Banner of Triumph:** After casting Windborne Strike or Resonance Skill Windqueller in mid-air, Jiyan performs an enhanced aerial attack dealing Aero DMG

**Dodge Counter:** Aero DMG counterattack after successful Dodge

**Qingloong Mode Replacement (all above replaced by):**
- **Heavy Attack: Lance of Qingloong:** Up to 3 consecutive attacks dealing Aero DMG, counted as Heavy Attack damage; each attack summons the Qingloong for multi-hit follow-up strikes; this is the source of ~90% of Jiyan's total damage

## Jiyan: Character Kit: Resonance Skill — Windqueller

- Jiyan dashes forward a set distance and strikes, dealing Aero DMG
- Can be cast in mid-air
- **Resolve-Empowered variant:** If Jiyan has 30+ Resolve, consumes 30 Resolve to deal +20% increased DMG on this cast
- **Qingloong Mode variant:** In Qingloong Mode, Windqueller automatically gains +20% DMG without consuming Resolve (free buff)
- Generates Concerto Energy and Resonance Energy on hit
- Grants 1 Momentum stack per cast
- Useful in rotation for Momentum pre-loading and Concerto generation; not recommended as primary Resolve sink

## Jiyan: Character Kit: Resonance Liberation — Emerald Storm: Prelude / Finale

**Emerald Storm: Prelude (Core Activation)**
- Jiyan unleashes a wide AoE Aero strike across nearby enemies — the opening stage of the Liberation
- Enters **Qingloong Mode** immediately after cast
- On its own (without Resolve), this is the basic Liberation with solid but unenhanced damage

**Emerald Storm: Finale (Resolve-Empowered — Primary Damage Ability)**
- Triggered automatically when Prelude is cast with 30+ Resolve
- Consumes 30 Resolve to immediately follow Prelude with an enormous second AoE Heavy Attack dealing Aero DMG
- Consumes all Momentum stacks on cast; each stack adds +120% DMG Multiplier (up to +240% at 2 stacks)
- Can be cast in mid-air at low altitude
- This is the highest-damage single event in Jiyan's kit; the entire rotation is designed to maximize its multiplier
- **Forte Passive interaction:** Entering with 2 Momentum stacks + 30+ Resolve makes Finale deal +240% bonus damage from Momentum plus its base multiplier — an enormous total hit

**Skill Priority:** Resonance Liberation = Forte Circuit should be leveled first; both directly scale Qingloong Mode and Finale. Resonance Skill secondary. Basic Attack can remain at Lv. 1.

## Jiyan: Inherent Passives

**Inherent Skill 1 — Fortitude (Momentum System)**
- Every time Jiyan uses a Heavy Attack, casts Intro Skill Tactical Strike, or casts Resonance Skill Windqueller: gains 1 Momentum stack (max 2)
- Emerald Storm: Finale consumes ALL Momentum stacks on cast; each consumed stack increases Finale's DMG Multiplier by **+120%**
- Net effect at 2 stacks: +240% bonus multiplier on Finale — the primary reason the rotation must pre-load 2 Momentum stacks before activating Liberation

**Inherent Skill 2 — Precision Strike**
- Provides an increase to Aero DMG or ATK under specific conditions (confirmed via wuthering.gg data; exact trigger and value should be verified with the Fandom Wiki)
- Functions as a sustained combat buff that rewards consistent Resonance Skill and Liberation usage

## Jiyan: Intro/Outro Skills

**Intro Skill — Tactical Strike**
- Triggered when Jiyan enters the field via swap
- Deals Aero DMG on a forward strike targeting the enemy
- Generates Resolve on hit (important — entering via Intro gives an immediate Resolve boost rather than starting from 0)
- Grants 1 Momentum stack on cast
- **S3 Enhancement:** Grants an additional flat **+30 Resolve** after cast and increases ATK by +28% for 15 seconds
- The Intro Skill is the reason Jiyan's rotations strongly prefer entering from a swap — both the Resolve generation and Momentum pre-loading from Tactical Strike make Liberation activation faster and stronger

**Outro Skill — Windborne**
- Triggered when Jiyan swaps off-field (Concerto Energy full)
- Effect: For 8 seconds after the swap, whenever the incoming character's Heavy Attack hits a target, Jiyan materializes a Qingloong **Coordinated Attack** dealing **313.40% Aero DMG** (ATK-based)
- Trigger rate: Once per second, up to **2 times** total (potential maximum: 2 × 313.40% = 626.80% Aero DMG)
- This Outro makes Jiyan an exceptional transition character in Heavy Attack compositions — swapping to any melee character with accessible Heavy Attacks (such as Mortefi) triggers the full Coordinated Attack value

## Jiyan: Resonance Chains (Sequences)

**S1 — Unyielding Lance**
- Resonance Skill Windqueller can be used **1 additional time** (charge added)
- When casting Windqueller, the Resolve cost is reduced by **15** (from 30 to 15 per cast)
- *Impact:* Additional Windqueller charge improves rotation flexibility and Concerto Energy generation. The Resolve cost reduction allows more frequent Windqueller usage without sacrificing Liberation Resolve. Moderate personal DPS gain (~6–8%); good quality-of-life improvement.

**S2 — Calm Before Battle**
- When Jiyan casts Resonance Skill Windqueller, Resonance Liberation Emerald Storm: Prelude, Emerald Storm: Finale, or Intro Skill Tactical Strike: Jiyan's **CRIT Rate is increased by +16%** and **CRIT DMG is increased by +32%** for 8 seconds
- *Impact:* Activates on almost every major damage event. +16% CRIT Rate is significant for stat-budget optimization — it reduces pressure on Echo and sub-stat CRIT Rate investment. +32% CRIT DMG directly amplifies all Qingloong Mode damage. Approximately +15–20% overall DPS increase. High priority sequence.

**S3 — Tactical Advantage**
- After casting Intro Skill Tactical Strike: Jiyan gains an immediate **+30 Resolve** AND his **ATK is increased by +28%** for 15 seconds
- Trigger: Once every 15 seconds
- *Impact:* The flat +30 Resolve on entry nearly guarantees Jiyan can immediately spend Resolve on Liberation after a single Intro Skill + a small Lone Lance chain, dramatically reducing the ramp-up time per rotation. +28% ATK affects all of his Qingloong Mode damage. Approximately +18–22% overall DPS increase. One of the best quality-of-life and damage sequences simultaneously.

**S4 — Stormcaller**
- When Jiyan casts Resonance Liberation Emerald Storm: Prelude or Emerald Storm: Finale: all team members' **Heavy Attack DMG Bonus is increased by +10%** for 15 seconds
- *Impact:* A party-wide buff that benefits Jiyan's own Qingloong Mode (which is all Heavy Attack) and any team members with Heavy Attack damage. Stacks well in Heavy Attack compositions. Approximately +6–10% team-wide benefit; personal DPS gain is ~5–7%. Best value in dedicated Heavy Attack compositions.

**S5 — Dragon's Ascent**
- Increases Jiyan's Resonance Liberation Emerald Storm: Finale DMG Multiplier substantially
- *Impact:* Directly amplifies his highest single-damage event. Approximately +15–18% personal DPS increase. A strong stopping point for invested players.

**S6 — Eternal Qingloong**
- A significant amplification to Jiyan's core Qingloong Mode damage output and/or Liberation multipliers, applying across the entire Qingloong Mode window
- *Impact:* Approximately +20–25% overall DPS gain at S6. Full S6 Jiyan is one of the strongest Aero damage profiles in Version 1.x, particularly in AoE scenarios.

**Sequence Pull Priority:** S0R1 baseline is strong and clearable. S2 is the standout individual upgrade (CRIT stats on every major move). S3 is exceptional for rotation speed and ATK. For most players: S0 with signature weapon > S2 without signature. Budget recommendation: skip sequences, invest in weapon instead.

## Jiyan: Recommended Echo Sets

**Best-in-Slot: Sierra Gale (5-piece)**
- Full 5-piece Sierra Gale provides Aero DMG Bonus that multiplies directly with Jiyan's Heavy Attack multipliers in Qingloong Mode
- The most reliable set for all Aero DPS characters; straightforward activation (no complex conditions)
- **Main Echo:** Nightmare: Feilian Beringal (recommended as of updated meta) — performs a powerful kick and follow-up that deals Aero DMG; on hit, grants the current character +12% Aero DMG Bonus and +12% Heavy Attack DMG Bonus for 15 seconds; both bonuses directly amplify Jiyan's core damage type and element
- **Alternative Main Echo:** Standard Feilian Beringal (non-Nightmare version) is usable if Nightmare variant is unavailable; marginally lower value

**Transitional / Early Set: Sierra Gale + Lingering Tunes (2+3 hybrid)**
- A mixed set used while farming the full Sierra Gale set; Lingering Tunes 2-piece provides ATK% bonus that partially offsets the missing Sierra Gale 5-piece bonus
- The Mech Abomination Echo (Lingering Tunes set) has fast animation and provides ATK buff; Jiyan cannot take advantage of its Electro scaling, but the ATK bonus remains
- Recommended only as a placeholder until 5-piece Sierra Gale is complete

**Echo Main Stats Priority**
- 4-Cost Echo: CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG (whichever balances to 1:2 ratio); CRIT DMG preferred if using signature Verdant Summit (which provides CRIT DMG as weapon stat)
- 3-Cost Echoes (×2): Aero DMG Bonus (primary) × both slots if possible; ATK% is a close secondary
- 1-Cost Echoes: ATK% (sub-stats matter more than mains at this tier)

**Sub-Stat Priority:** Energy Regen (to ~130% breakpoint) ≥ CRIT Rate = CRIT DMG > ATK% > Heavy ATK DMG Bonus > Flat ATK

## Jiyan: Best Weapon

**Signature — Verdant Summit (5-Star Broadblade)**
- Best-in-slot by a meaningful margin; purpose-designed for Jiyan's kit
- **Stat:** CRIT DMG (complementary to S2's CRIT Rate buff — together they reduce the need for CRIT Rate on Echoes and substats)
- **Effect:** +24% Attribute DMG Bonus baseline; every Intro Skill or Resonance Liberation cast increases Heavy Attack DMG Bonus by **+48%**, stacking up to 2 times (max +96%), lasting 14 seconds — perfectly tuned for Qingloong Mode uptime
- Also a top-tier weapon for any other Broadblade Main DPS that uses Liberation and Heavy Attacks

**Top 5-Star Standard Alternative — Lustrous Razor**
- High base ATK + ATK% passive + Energy Regen component
- Does not provide CRIT DMG or Heavy Attack specific buffs, but the ATK and Energy Regen combination ensures Liberation uptime — Jiyan's most important stat efficiency target outside of CRIT
- Beats all 4-star options outright

**Top 4-Star Alternatives**
- **Helios Cleaver:** Aero DMG Bonus or ATK% stat; good baseline for a 4-star weapon
- **Broadblade#41:** Energy Regen main stat (reduces the ER investment needed on Echoes); trades some personal damage for rotation consistency — valuable for players struggling with Liberation uptime

**F2P Best — Guardian Broadblade**
- 3-star craftable broadblade that provides Heavy Attack DMG Bonus — directly relevant to Jiyan's damage type
- Low base ATK is the only significant drawback; viable for early game players without premium weapon options

**Weapon Priority Note:** Jiyan's signature is one of the most impactful investments for him specifically. Players choosing between weapon pulls and sequence pulls should strongly favor the weapon.

## Jiyan: Best Teams

**Premium Meta — Jiyan / Iuno / Shorekeeper**
- **Jiyan** (Main DPS): On-field Qingloong Mode; Liberation AoE
- **Iuno** (Sub DPS / Buffer): Premier Heavy Attack DMG amplification; provides up to +50% Heavy Attack DMG Amplification that directly scales with all of Jiyan's Qingloong Mode strikes; also deals solid Sub-DPS damage and heals, making her the single best support for Jiyan as of Version 2.x+
- **Shorekeeper** (Support): Stellarealm state provides CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG to all team members; universal best-in-slot support for the entire team composition
- *Rotation:* Shorekeeper Liberation → Shorekeeper Outro → Iuno rotation → Iuno Outro → Jiyan Intro (Tactical Strike — Resolve + ATK buff via S3) → Lone Lance chain → Liberation Prelude + Resolve spend → Finale → Qingloong Mode → Lance of Qingloong chains → Outro swap

**Aero Full-Stack — Jiyan / Ciaccona / Aero Rover**
- **Jiyan** (Main DPS): Liberation burst + Qingloong Mode
- **Ciaccona** (Sub DPS / Buffer / Debuffer): Builds Aero Erosion stacks on enemies; grants multiple Aero DMG Bonus buffs; her Outro provides Aero DMG Amplification benefiting Jiyan's entire element
- **Aero Rover** (Flexible Sub-DPS / Healer / Buffer): Provides Aero synergy, healing access, and buffs from the same element stack
- *Advantage:* Full-element Aero team maximizes Aero Resonance bonuses and Ciaccona's erosion debuffs; strong option post-Ciaccona availability

**Classic Version 1.0 — Jiyan / Mortefi / Verina**
- **Jiyan** (Main DPS): Standard Liberation + Qingloong setup
- **Mortefi** (Sub DPS / Buffer): Aero support character; his kit increases Aero DMG for the team; Outro passes buffs to Jiyan on re-entry; also benefits from Jiyan's Outro Qingloong Coordinated Attacks when Jiyan swaps to Mortefi
- **Verina** (Support/Healer): Universal healer providing team ATK buff, Vitality restoration, and consistent sustain; best-in-slot healer available at Version 1.0
- *Note:* This team was the dominant Jiyan composition at launch and through much of Version 1.x; still viable with full investment

**Budget F2P — Jiyan / Yangyang / Baizhi**
- **Yangyang:** 4-star Aero support; her Resonance Liberation provides team mobility and Concerto Energy assist; Aero element synergy
- **Baizhi:** Best F2P healer; provides healing and ATK buff via her Resonance Liberation
- *Limitation:* Significantly lower damage ceiling than premium teams, but fully functional for Story Mode and early endgame content

## Jiyan: DPS Benchmarks

- Jiyan is rated as a reliable A-tier to S-tier Main DPS throughout Version 1.x depending on team composition and investment level
- His primary strength is **AoE damage** — Lance of Qingloong's Aero dragon sweeps multiple enemies simultaneously, making him one of the most effective characters for mob-clearing and multi-target Tower of Adversity floors
- His primary weakness is **Intro Skill dependency** — entering via Intro grants Resolve generation that makes the rotation function smoothly; being forced onto Jiyan without a swap-entry costs both Resolve and the S3 ATK/Resolve burst
- At S0 with Verdant Summit, premium team (Iuno + Shorekeeper), and proper rotation execution, Jiyan delivers competitive damage output for all current content
- At S6, the amplification to Finale and Qingloong Mode makes him substantially stronger in AoE scenarios; few characters match his AoE clearing speed at full investment
- His damage floor (casual, suboptimal play) is meaningfully higher than many high-skill-ceiling characters because his kit is legible — even imperfect rotations still produce significant Qingloong Mode uptime
