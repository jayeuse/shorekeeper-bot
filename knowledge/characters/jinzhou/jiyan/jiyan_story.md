## Jiyan Official Introduction

*"Jiyan is the General of Midnight Rangers stationed in Jinzhou, embracing strong sense of justice and resolution. Born in a family devoted to medicine, Jiyan is both skilled and attentive. Later, in a bid to fend off the encroaching Tacet Discords, he relinquished his role in medicine and took up arms as a soldier."*

His signature motivational statement, used in official promotional materials and voice lines, is: *"I have never regretted to brave the long night."*

The official website describes him as: *"Jiyan, leader of the Midnight Rangers, acts with swift and resolute righteousness. He possesses the formidable ability to conjure a powerful Qingloong from the winds, making him invincible on the battlefield."* His Forte archive designation is **Windborne Rider**. His banner title at Version 1.0 was *"Prevail the Lasting Night."*

## Jiyan Personality

Jiyan is a person defined by the gap between what he shows and what he carries. What he shows is composure, authority, and decisiveness — the deliberate profile of a general who knows that panic is contagious and that his soldiers read their survival odds in his face. What he carries is a recurring nightmare, a private ledger of the dead, and an unresolved question about whether any amount of fighting can justify the cost in lives that makes it possible.

He is not a warrior by nature. He is a healer who concluded that medicine was insufficient to the scale of the problem — a diagnostician who determined that the disease was too systemic for clinical tools and took up a lance as the only instrument large enough. This origin is not incidental; it is the source of his entire governing philosophy. He thinks in terms of precision, minimized collateral damage, and care for the individual within the institution. The Midnight Rangers are his ward. He knows every patient by name.

The most distinctive thing about Jiyan is that his care is structural and anonymous. He does not build personal loyalties by being seen to be generous; he routes his generosity through the institution so that it presents as normal function. Soldiers receive medical care, financial assistance, and logistical support as if these things simply exist — which is exactly what Jiyan intends. He is not managing impressions; he is managing outcomes. The outcome he wants is a functioning, cared-for force that fights well because it is well.

Beneath the composure and the strategy is a man who photographs ordinary afternoons in Jinzhou because the peace of them is what makes the soldiers' deaths meaningful — and who mentions, quietly, that there is a flower in Knell Square he would like planted there for him if he fails to keep his promise of witnessing humanity's victory.

## Jiyan Character Story I: "The Unyielding Will"

Shortly after Jiyan's appointment as Midnight Rangers' General, an unexpected Tacet Discord Outbreak occurred — the battle in which he first suspected the Threnodian's resurrection. The location was the pass between the Norfall Barrens and Desorock Highland: a strategic chokepoint whose loss, the outnumbered army understood, could spell the end for Huanglong.

As weary soldiers recovered from a prior engagement on the frontline, shadow ravens circled overhead and Tacet Discord swarms charged toward their position. Even veterans with decades of experience had never faced a crisis of this scale or urgency.

Jiyan alone remained composed.

*"All of you. Prepare to fight."*

A senior veteran protested, arguing for retreat toward the city. Jiyan answered without anger:

*"Choose now: stand with courage, seize our chance. Or run and face defeat. For centuries, the Midnight Rangers have held this pass without retreat. Or do you prefer to go down in history as cowards?"*

The veterans left. Their emotions ranged from disappointment to rage.

Jiyan did not follow them. He studied real-time images from monitoring devices with the attention of someone reading an X-ray, and noticed something the panicked veterans had not: a pattern connecting the Tacet Discords' movement to the shadow ravens circling above. He summoned the remaining commanders, devised a plan around this observation, and acted.

The plan: Sound Emulators placed in a narrow valley, configured to emit the shadow raven's frequency. As he predicted, the Tacet Discords followed the sound — drawn into a kill zone. The Midnight Rangers at Norfall Barrens coordinated with those at Desorock Highland for a pincer attack. Jiyan fought through the center of a sandstorm, opening the path for his soldiers through sheer force.

His Tacet Mark flickered — a sign of Forte overexertion — but he did not stop. His Qingloong flew above the battlefield like a banner that refused to fall.

They held the pass. The story of his leadership in that battle spread through the Rangers and through Jinzhou's collective memory. The veterans who had left were not celebrated. Jiyan said nothing about them.

## Jiyan Character Story II: "The Reason to Fight"

Jiyan is the youngest general in the Midnight Rangers' history. He is the Qingloong that everyone looks up to, and the invincible warrior no enemy stands any chance against.

At some point, he became haunted by a recurring nightmare.

In the dream, Jinzhou is engulfed in flames and consumed by Tacet Discords. The monsters feed on the frequencies of the dead. Chaotic sounds echo across the city in a symphony of destruction. On the flame-lit battlefield, a lone figure fights relentlessly with a lance — one swing, two, three — hundreds of thousands of Tacet Discords falling, endlessly replaced by more.

Day after day, year after year, the figure fights alone until he is the sole survivor on a desolate field. The Tacet Discords transform into his fallen comrades. They encircle him and close in.

*"That's a clueless kid! How can we trust him with our lives?"*

*"You said you would protect us... but why?"*

*"Jiyan, what on earth are you fighting for?"*

Few people know that Jiyan — known for his calm and decisive nature — is sometimes jolted awake by these voices. The frown that others notice. The constant thinking. The books on warfare with margins full of his own notes. These are the surface of something deeper: a general who carries the weight of every death under his command and has not yet found a way to put it down.

*"He fights for the living, and for the deceased."* — this is the official summary of his motivation, and it is precise. The dead ask him questions in dreams, and one day he intends to answer them — not by stopping, but by winning.

## Jiyan Character Story III: "Silent Caretaker"

Strange things happen in the Midnight Rangers' camps.

A soldier with a knee injury begins receiving specialized pain relief patches from the medics each time the seasons change — despite never having told anyone about it. Another soldier, previously inattentive during training, learns a few days later that the General Affairs Department has quietly paid for his mother's surgery. When the logistics department runs short on funds, a budget increase notice arrives for next quarter before any formal request was made.

No individual problem, regardless of its size or how long it has existed, goes unaddressed.

The voice behind these solutions, never publicly identified:

*"Team 3's captain obviously had difficulties using his left leg during the drill. I went through his operation records, and it seems to stem from his prolonged exposure to the swamp during one of the previous missions. Pass along this prescription to the medics. They'll know what to do."*

*"Transfer some money to this account in the name of the General Affairs Department. Debit my account for the funding."*

*"I remember there has been a 20% increase in the number of recruits this year. Verify this with the Logistics. If confirmed, adjust next quarter's budget accordingly."*

This story does not name Jiyan in the narration until its last line. It does not need to. The character it describes — someone who reads injury records at night, funds soldiers' family surgeries from personal funds routed through institutional channels, and adjusts budgets before requests are filed — could only be the general who was trained from childhood to treat suffering as an obligation, not an inconvenience.

*"This is Jiyan, whose ruthlessness is reserved for enemies, while his tenderness is shown to comrades."*

## Jiyan Character Story IV: "What's Inside His Gourd?"

When asked about his childhood, Jiyan recalls a plethora of memories: the smell of medicine, the bright lights of the operating room, the sound of medical equipment, and the hallway where many goodbyes were exchanged.

Born into a medical family, Jiyan became his mother's assistant at age 10. He was exceptionally gifted — not only technically skilled, but unusually able to soothe patients' distress and anticipate their needs. Despite this, some lives were beyond saving.

The last patient he could not save was **Beiwang** — his friend and mentor, heavily injured by Tacet Discords.

Beiwang had initially come to Jiyan as a patient, one of many Midnight Rangers who required medical care after fighting the Tacet Discord tides. He was impressed by Jiyan's intellect and analytical intuition, and began sharing details of the Rangers' marches and providing military instruction. Jiyan, in turn, contributed strategic perspectives to their discussions despite having never experienced battle himself. His fresh analytical frameworks invigorated Beiwang's thinking in ways that the veteran valued deeply.

Beiwang's death ignited a transformation. The wistfulness, the frustration, the regret — these became Jiyan's strength to pick up a lance. He had recognized that the Lament is a disease so deep in the world's veins that no medical instrument or surgeon could remove it. He would cure it another way. He took up arms with the same intention he had brought to medicine: to save as many as possible.

Now the only external marker of his medical past is the **gourd-shaped pill case** on his belt loop — a gift from his mother the year he enlisted. She feared he would become a killer and gave it to him as a reminder of who he was. He carries medicines in it still, *just in case*. Speculation among his soldiers runs from sugar pearls for frightened children to something he drinks after battles. If asked directly, Jiyan answers frankly:

*"Just some common medicines, in case someone needs them."*

## Jiyan Character Story V: "The Temporary Team"

During the Jinzhou Riverside Games, a group of young men approached a bright-eyed young man in the crowd. Their relay team was one person short due to their captain's urgent absence — they needed an anchor runner or they would forfeit the Gulpuff Relay race they had trained months for.

The young man agreed without ceremony.

He asked about each teammate's condition and the current relay order, then reorganized the strategy and took the anchor leg himself. The Gulpuff Relay was the signature event of the Riverside Games — originally a celebration of Jinzhou's first triumph over a Tacet Discord Outbreak, now one of the largest public events in the city.

The race started, and a baton exchange mistake in the early legs put the temporary team far behind. The commentators moved on to the leading contenders.

No one noticed the anchor runner closing the gap.

*"Oh man! Look at the temporary team! There was a hiccup earlier, but now the last runner is overtaking! There's a key curve ahead... Wha — what's happening? He's overtaking everyone on the curve! That's absolutely amazing!"*

The temporary team won. They broke the race record. His teammates swarmed him in excitement and raised the trophy, vowing to compete together at the next Games.

The story does not name Jiyan either — but the character who reorganizes a stranger's relay strategy in fifteen minutes, identifies himself as the anchor, blocks out the crowd noise, and breaks a record while closing a multi-position gap is unmistakably the general who reads battlefield data in sandstorms. His composure scales to any situation, no matter how different its stakes from the ones it was trained for.

## Jiyan: Cherished Items

**The Art of War (Book)**
*"A general is a man of wisdom, faith, benevolence, courage, and discipline."*
Jiyan's desk is always organized with military strategy books covered in his own handwritten notes. He reads them not as historical documents but as active reference material — his annotations reflect current strategic thinking rather than academic study. Leadership, for Jiyan, is not an instinct but a discipline that requires continuous refinement. He does not believe in invincibility; he believes in preparation.

**Medicine Box (Gourd-Shaped Pill Case)**
His mother's gift when he enlisted. She feared he would become a killer on the battlefield and reminded him — in the form of this object — that he was fundamentally a healer. He accepted the reminder and the responsibility. He has carried the case through every battle since, maintaining the medicines inside with the same quiet diligence he applies to everything else. It is the most personal object he carries and the one most legible to anyone who knows his history.

**A Corner of Jinzhou (Photograph)**
*"On an ordinary, everyday afternoon in Jinzhou, the sun was shining through the clear sky and spreading to thousands of homes, and Jiyan couldn't help but take pictures of the scene. The soldiers' sacrifices hold even greater significance against this tranquil backdrop."*
A photograph of ordinary Jinzhou — not a battle, not a monument, not a ceremony. An afternoon. Sunlight through clear sky. Thousands of homes. Jiyan photographs this scene as a way of seeing what he is fighting for when it cannot be named in a briefing. The peace of the city gives meaning to the cost of defending it; having the image allows him to hold both truths simultaneously.

## Jiyan: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite — Jinzhou Stew**
*"Jinzhou Stew is renowned for its wide variety of ingredients and easy preparation, making it a beloved homemade dish in Jinzhou. Having been stationed in the borderlands for most of my time, I sometimes miss that taste."*
The longing for Jinzhou Stew is a longing for the city itself — for the domestic reality of thousands of homes cooking the same meal. It is the food of ordinary life, and Jiyan spends most of his time far from ordinary life.

**Disliked (Childhood) — Bittberry**
*"In Jinzhou, there is a vegetable known as 'Bittberry'. It possesses a bitter and slightly astringent flavor, and is renowned for its detoxifying properties. When I was young, my mother was somehow convinced 'those who can endure bitterness will rise above others.' She frequently incorporated this vegetable into our meals. I must confess that its taste was far from pleasant. However, now serving in the army, I have grown accustomed to its flavor. After all, resources are scarce, and it is crucial not to squander them."*
The Bittberry detail is one of the most humanizing fragments in Jiyan's voice lines — a childhood resentment folded into adult pragmatism, with the characteristic Jiyan qualifier that the reason for acceptance is rational (scarcity, efficiency) rather than emotional (his mother was right). He does not say she was right. He says he adapted.

## Jiyan: Ideals

**Passion — Precision Over Power**
Jiyan's passion is not battle — it is the problem of how to win while minimizing harm. His framing of the Midnight Rangers as a scalpel and himself as the guiding hand is revealing: a scalpel is the smallest tool that can accomplish a surgical goal. He brings a healer's instinct for minimal intervention to the problem of military force. The enemy is never the soldiers in his care.

**Resonance — The Bond With the Dead**
Jiyan is acutely aware of the dead — the ones who died under his command, the ones who died before he could save them medically, the ones who appear in his nightmares asking unanswerable questions. He fights for them as much as for the living. His resonance with Beiwang's memory, expressed through the lance modeled on his mentor's weapon, makes every battle a continuation of a conversation that ended abruptly. One day he intends to answer the questions the dead are asking.

**Gratitude — For the Ordinary**
Jiyan's gratitude is visible in a photograph of an afternoon. He does not take ordinary days for granted; he documents them and names them as the reason the fighting matters. This capacity to find meaning in the peaceful and mundane — alongside rather than instead of the weight of command — is what keeps him functional under the cumulative pressure of the Midnight Rangers' mission.

**Perseverance — The Lasting Night**
His banner title and core statement — *"I have never regretted to brave the long night"* — is a declaration of unconditional commitment. Not conditional on victory, not conditional on recognition, not conditional on the dream ever becoming the ordinary afternoon instead of the battlefield. He will continue until the borderlands no longer need a general. That is his wish. Until then, he persists.

## Jiyan: Narrative Chronicle

- **[Childhood — Medical Training]** Jiyan grows up in a medical family; becomes his mother's assistant at age 10; demonstrates exceptional clinical skill and unusual ability to soothe patients; comes to know the Midnight Rangers as frequent patients due to the ongoing Tacet Discord tides

- **[Youth — Beiwang's Death]** Beiwang is fatally injured by Tacet Discords; Jiyan cannot save him; this is the pivotal event of his life — the moment medicine's limits become undeniable and the decision to take up arms becomes unavoidable
- **[Enlistment]** Jiyan enlists in the Midnight Rangers; his mother gives him the gourd-shaped pill case; he is reminded of his healer's identity even as he becomes a soldier; he carries the case ever after
- **[Rise Through the Ranks]** Jiyan advances through the Rangers with exceptional speed, driven by the same analytical precision and strategic intelligence that distinguished him as a medical assistant; he becomes the youngest person ever appointed to the rank of General in the organization's history
- **[Desorock Highland — First Test as General]** Shortly after appointment, a Tacet Discord Outbreak strikes the Norfall Barrens / Desorock Highland pass; senior veterans panic and argue for retreat; Jiyan alone stays composed, analyzes monitoring data, identifies the shadow raven / Tacet Discord connection, devises the Sound Emulator plan, and leads the coordinated pincer attack; the pass holds; his Forte Mark flickers from overexertion but he does not stop; this battle establishes his reputation and his personal brand of leadership

- **[Riverside Games — Temporary Team]** At some point during peacetime, Jiyan joins a stranger's relay team at the Jinzhou Riverside Games on short notice, reorganizes their strategy, takes the anchor leg, closes a large gap, and helps the team break the record; he does not identify himself as a general
- **[Version 1.0 — Main Story]** Rover arrives in Jinzhou; Jiyan functions as the military counterpart to Jinhsi's civic governance during the Lament crisis in Chapter 1; he fights alongside Rover in the defense of Huanglong; his Sentinel-selected status as General is made explicit in the story context
