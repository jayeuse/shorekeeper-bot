## Qiuyuan Official Introduction

*"Former senior agent of Mingting's Internal Security Agency, he is a swordsman of cool resilience and absolute integrity, wandering Huanglong alone and seeks not fame nor high office, but mastery of swordsmanship and a life guided by high morals."*

His Forte archive designation is **Bambooscape**. His self-introduction: *"Qiuyuan, a blade drawn at your word. Wield me as you will."* His standard greeting: *"Name the target. I'll take care of the rest."*

His ideals statement: *"Ideals, wishes... those things mean little to someone who lies down each night unsure if tomorrow comes. So I see no point in dwelling on them. All I know is I can't die yet. Not before I've had my revenge, not before I've fulfilled my duty. What happens after that... I'll leave that to fate to decide."*

## Qiuyuan Personality

Qiuyuan is the kind of person whose composure makes other people nervous — not because it is threatening but because it is too complete. The Forte Examination annotator asks whether he is *"a little too devoid of emotion,"* and the question is reasonable from the outside. What the character stories reveal is that the composure is not emptiness but discipline over depth: a flame that could not be quenched by five years of rain; hatred described as something that couldn't be cleansed; grief that is present and acknowledged and simply not shown. His Ascension V voice line gives it away: *"my blade seems to grow heavier with each swing."* The skill remains; the weight accumulates.

He is a person who has been placed in a position of obligation multiple times and has chosen to honor every one of those obligations, even when honoring them cost him more than he could afford. His master taught him and was also the source of his greatest loss. Doctor Zhang saved him and returned him to the world he was trying to escape. Grandmaster Dongyuan gave his service meaning and died before Qiuyuan could repay a day of it. He is still paying. He expects to be doing so for the remainder of whatever time he has, which he assesses as not very long.

He is also a man who, at some point in a doctor's hut in Chongzhou, tended a fire and heard patients and realized that ordinary life had a texture he recognized as his own. He was not made only for killing. Doctor Zhang saw this clearly enough to send him away before the hut became a retreat instead of a recovery. Qiuyuan went.

## Qiuyuan Forte Examination Report

*Miscellaneous Records of Mingting's Minister of Sentinel Affairs: Correspondence Collection*
*To my dear friend Dongyuan,*

A few days prior, the Internal Security Agency forwarded documents from the Justice Commission and the Huaxu Academy to the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs. The Ministry instructed the Chongzhou official to assist and thoroughly examine the local Resonator register. It has been confirmed that the individual in question has **no connection with the Sentinel or the Threnodian of Chongzhou**.

According to Ministry records, this individual has been a Resonator for over **fifteen years**. Described as one who *"sees the world through bamboos and hides his blade within them,"* he is **blind yet wields a remarkable ability known as Mindsight**. Through this power, he perceives his surroundings through a bamboo forest in his mind, enabling him to discern opponents' intentions and vulnerabilities. He can even manifest the Mindsight into tangible illusions. *"This is not a person to be underestimated."*

Moreover, his swordsmanship appears to stem chiefly from **personal skill rather than his Resonance Ability**. Observations indicate that he consistently restrains the use of his Resonance power. He claims that the bamboo flask he carries contains a concoction — prepared by a wandering healer in Chongzhou — that stimulates his Mindsight and Forte, used only when necessary.

*Please await the Academy's formal report for detailed test data...*

**Dongyuan's annotation:** Simply ensure this is recorded clearly. We follow the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs's judgment… My my, Lin still writes with his characteristic formality.

**Overclock Diagnostic (appended correspondence):**

Test subject waveform analysis presents an elliptical oscillation. Time-domain readings are stable with no anomalous patterns. Resonant Criticality: **High. Stability is high with no risk of Overclocking. No prior history of Overclocking.** Psychological intervention is deemed unnecessary.

*Note: The subject admits to repeated offenses involving violent breaches of the law, though his demeanor was notably composed. It is recommended that these claims should be verified by the Ministry of Justice and local authorities in Chongzhou.*

The evaluating correspondent's personal assessment: *"this man harbors no murderous intent. His composure is unwavering, his demeanor steady. His swordsmanship is among the finest, and few in all of Mingting could rival him. He could have been a remarkable asset to the Internal Security Agency."*

**Dongyuan's annotation:** Naturally, the steadier the better. But judging from this report... *isn't this lad a little too devoid of emotion?*

The Forte report's most structurally interesting feature is that Qiuyuan's Overclock history is empty — no prior incidents. For a person who has been a Resonator for over fifteen years and has documented *"repeated offenses involving violent breaches of the law,"* this absence suggests that whatever drives his actions does not manifest as emotional overflow into his Forte. The restraint of his Mindsight is voluntary; the composure is genuinely disciplined. The annotator who asks about the absence of emotion is reading the same data.

## Qiuyuan Character Story I: "Tempered"

*"Every mountain stands as a trial. Pass this one, and you are free to take your own path. Fail… then this mountain shall keep what you have."*

Those were the first words Qiuyuan heard from the old man five years ago, spoken in *a hell of fire and blood*. Words impossible to forget.

He called him Master, though he never knew his face. A white-bearded elder in rags, perhaps. The old man trained Qiuyuan relentlessly, taught him martial arts sharp enough to let him *"see"* the bamboo forest swaying in his mind.

Rumors about the master spread like mist. Some said he was the **Sword Specter of Chongzhou**, a butcher who left trails of corpses in his wake. Others whispered that he had once been Mingting's celebrated general, a conqueror of vast lands whose power nearly rivaled the Huanglong ruler, only to fade into solitude after a fall from grace. Some claimed he was no more than a myth. After all, who could believe a mortal could slay a mountain of Tacet Discords with nothing but a bamboo stick?

But Qiuyuan believed. For five years, he enjoyed countless victories — but never once bested his master.

*"You said the same words five years ago, Master."*
*"Five years… gone in a blink."*

Qiuyuan had been waiting for this day. Defeat this old man, and he could leave the bamboo forest, cross this mountain, and finally — surpass the one who had always won.

*"I've taught you almost everything. But the last move, the most vital one, I'm still keeping for myself. Do you know what it is?"*
*"I beg for your teachings, Master."*
*"Then tell me, Qiuyuan, what does the bamboo forest show you?"*
*"Leaves like spears. Stalks hiding dangers. Shadows upon shadows, like ghosts surrounding me."*
*"So, the flame from five years ago is still burning in your heart."*
*"Isn't it also burning your heart... Sword Specter?"*
*"It has been... many years since the last living soul called me by that name. Very well, I see you don't need more explanation. Now, come take the final move from me."*

It was he who wrought the hell of fire and blood five years ago. It was also he who brought Qiuyuan out of that hell of fire and blood.

*"With this blade, I will honor your teachings!"*

The flame has never died.

The story is not resolved — the duel itself is not described, only framed. What remains: Qiuyuan's five-year training; his master as simultaneously destroyer and teacher; the mutual acknowledgment of a flame neither of them has been able to extinguish; and the final move given. The master's name is never confirmed in available sources as anything other than the Sword Specter. Whether Qiuyuan defeated him, and what became of the master afterward, is not documented in the character stories.

## Qiuyuan Character Story II: "Concealed"

*"Lie still. Don't even think of moving."*

A harsh voice reached Qiuyuan as he awoke, body aching, wounds bound. Yet within the harshness lingered a gentleness unlike the killing intent he had grown used to. The memory of how he came to be broken returned.

*"I…"*
*"You killed dozens with your bare hands. Not a creative way to beg for death, I'd say."*
*"…I should be dead."*
*"Many who live deserve to die, and many who died should have lived. Such is fate."*

Regret tangled in vengeance had taught Qiuyuan when to sheathe the blade. But it couldn't cleanse the hatred he had carried for five long years. The flame that couldn't be quenched by the pouring rain from five years ago is still burning vividly in his mind, just like the old man who once stood before him among the fires, with a sword in his hand.

But there had been no rain when he left the mountain. Only liquor dripping down his face and a whispering sigh.

A hatred born of blood may only be washed away by blood in return. But what comes after? No one ever knows.

He was left with no reason to raise his sword. When opponents' weapons struck, he simply stood and let them come.

*"Heh… whether you deserve life or death, I cannot say,"* the voice murmured. *"The choice lies with you alone."*

And so, Qiuyuan laid aside his blade. With only a bamboo stick in hand, he idled outside the hut as decoctions simmered on the stove, spending his days picturing the patients who came, and the stubborn middle-aged man they called **Doctor Zhang**.

For the first time in years, he enjoyed peace again. Since the day Doctor Zhang saved him, he could not *"see"* the bamboo forest of blades anymore — only **pure, silent darkness, free of hatred and rage**. There were sighs and tears, words of gratitude and joy, the scent of medicine, and the crackle of stove fire.

It was moments like these that made him realize he was no different from the ordinary.

*"Here, take this flask. And my recipe too. You need three bowls of water for one bowl of decoction. Keep that in mind."*
*"…What's this?"*
*"My potent regimen pulled you back from death that day, but your Forte has been crippled. This medicine restores your Mindsight, at least for a little while. It will be of some help on the day you raise your sword again."*
*"I won't. You saved me, Doctor. The least I can do is stay and take care of sundry matters for you."*
*"I don't think so. Bloodshed clings to your face. Your palm's lifeline is cut off. Followed by fears, haunted by distress... You are bound to die in battle, not of old age here in my hut."*
*"Followed by fears, haunted by distress... My master once told me the same."*
*"Master, you say? To have a disciple like you… He must be a tough nut."*
*"…Even so, Doctor, I will not take up the blade again. But I'll accept your recipe. Let me stay here. If trouble comes to this hut because of me, I'll leave."*
*"Well… medicine is like a blade. It all comes down to the usage and dosage. It can kill, but also save."*
*"Now, go open the door, Qiuyuan. Light the stove. I won't claim I'm better than you, but no one dares stir trouble under my roof. That I can guarantee."*

This story establishes the bamboo flask's origin: the decoction that temporarily restores Qiuyuan's Mindsight was invented by Doctor Zhang specifically to compensate for the Forte damage caused by the regimen that saved Qiuyuan's life. The trade is permanent: his life for his full Forte access, with the decoction as a partial bridge. He carries both the debt and the means to partially repay it every day.

The comparison Doctor Zhang draws — *"medicine is like a blade"* — is the philosophical center of their relationship. The same tool can kill or save; what matters is usage and dosage. Qiuyuan has been using his blade only to kill. Doctor Zhang opens the door in both directions.

## Qiuyuan Character Story III: "Unsheathed"

*"Hah… all that strength wasted in the wilderness. How will you answer the lives that fell beneath your blade?"*
*"You've got the wrong man. Doctor, another patient is here."*

Qiuyuan ignored the older man and kept tending firewood under the stove. At first, the man had merely come by to exchange idle words with Doctor Zhang. Later, he began appearing more often, spouting riddles and nonsense.

This time, he suddenly hurled a **fist-sized iron weight** at Qiuyuan. Instinct took over. Qiuyuan raised his hand and caught it against his brow without flinching.

*"You should recognize what you're holding."*

The beast's head, forged from the finest steel, radiating a chill. There was no mistaking it.

*"The Internal Security Agency… are you here to arrest me?"*
*"If you are a wanted man, I wouldn't be the one to catch you. You've left behind a trail of death. Normally, the Justice Commission would prosecute your crimes."*
*"Then let them do it."*
*"...Do you truly wish to die on the execution ground?"*
*"Death comes either way. What difference does it make?"*
*"It would be a waste of your strength."*

The man invoked the power of Mingting's ISA: the **iron head of the Qiongqi beast**, bestowed by Huanglong's highest authority, embodies the Emperor's very presence. Wherever it appears, officials must bow low. Its envoys traverse the realm unfettered by any court, answering only to the throne, entrusted with only the gravest matters. Should they seek a man's life, not even death itself dares to intervene.

*"You may wield a sword for yourself, sure, but you may also raise it for the greater good. Serve Mingting, punish the wicked, shield the innocent."*
*"I am but a blind man. The weight of the greater good is far heavier than I can bear."*

The man picked up a piece of wood and stared at the stove.

*"Do you know why Doctor Zhang left Mingting?"*
*"…No. He never said, and I never asked."*
*"To heal all, rather than to heal one."*

The man chucked his firewood under the stove and spoke no more.

Three days later, Qiuyuan stood with the man at the hut's door. The mountain dew of Chongzhou's deep valleys had yet to soak their straw hats. No smoke rose from the stove's chimney. Only the faint dawn light touched them.

Doctor Zhang's parting:

*"That man from the Internal Security Agency is... like me, stubborn to a fault. But perhaps that is why he could persuade you to follow him. The world is vast. All I can do is try my best to save more lives. But that man, Liang Dongyuan… he's the one in court who can 'save' people. The time has arrived. Go now. Linger here too long, and you'll only scare my patients away."*

The doctor patted Qiuyuan's shoulder and slipped into the hut without a backward glance, leaving him no chance to reply.

Years later, Qiuyuan would still recall that morning, prostrating on the dirt path worn smooth by countless patients.

*"Thank you, Doctor. For everything."*

This story is the most deliberate in the set. The ISA agent does not appeal to duty or law — he appeals to Doctor Zhang's life philosophy. The argument is not *"serve Mingting"* but *"Doctor Zhang left Mingting to heal all rather than one; the man who recruited me can 'save' people at the institutional level; you could be part of that."* Qiuyuan was unmoved by the greater good. He was moved by the extension of Doctor Zhang's logic into a domain Qiuyuan could serve.

The iron weight that the agent throws is the ISA token that Qiuyuan carries throughout all subsequent stories — the one the Cherished Items entry says *"should have been confiscated along with his uniform after he left the service, yet someone within Mingting ensured he kept it."* That someone was Grandmaster Liang Dongyuan himself, who gave it back with: *"I hereby entrust this to you. Let all officials of every state know: whoever bears this token carries my presence."*

## Qiuyuan Character Story IV: "Shattered"

Shackles clattered in the prison cell.

Qiuyuan sat within — no different from the corrupt officials and rogues he had once cast into jail.

*"Open the gate."*
*"B-but, Minister Lin, the execution is about to begin—"*
*"Open it."*

The bars groaned. A man stepped inside, bringing with him the faint aroma of food and wine.

*"What now? You here to supervise the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs?"*
*"Forgive me, Minister… I'm only carrying out my duty."*
*"Very well. Then you must know that the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs is required to question every felon before their execution. Now, I must also fulfill my duty. Leave. All of you."*
*"As you command…"*
*"And lock the gate. I will summon you when I am done."*

Hurried footsteps faded. Two remained in the cell.

*"The execution is supposed to begin at noon, the forty-fifth minute. You're an hour and a half early."*
*"…Are you here to see me off, Master Lin?"*
*"First, drink this. A toast to his memory."*

Qiuyuan rose slowly, the heavy chains dragging. He took the cup, but instead of drinking, **poured the wine onto the ground** — a libation for the dead.

*"Sigh… Only you were there when Dongyuan met his wretched end. This is such a dire case that even the top brass are watching. With no trace left by the true culprit, the Justice Commission had no choice but to scapegoat you."*
*"I could not save Grandmaster Liang. That failure is mine alone."*
*"Do you still recall the fiend who… peeled themselves from Dongyuan's body?"*
*"I'll never forget their frequency, even if they were reduced to ashes."*
*"The Ministry scoured every record, but nowhere was there mention of a Resonator who could take on another's form and body."*
*"…I cannot say if some fragment of Grandmaster Liang still lingered in that moment."*
*"Perhaps Dongyuan was no longer himself when he sent you on that case in Jinzhou. A man so righteous would have never ordered the pursuit of a minor official who only killed Tacet Discords to protect the people."*
*"That is why I spared him — that man, Geshu Lin. The Grandmaster Liang I knew would never have given such an order."*
*"Dongyuan was never wrong about you. If you wish to know the truth behind his death, you can only begin from that case. Though I fear… you may never sort everything out."*
*"I have spilled more than my share of blood. But because of you, Grandmaster Liang, and Doctor Zhang, I was able to live differently, if only for a time… I could only repay such trust in another life."*

The Minister moved toward the gate. His face was solemn, but his eyes held firm reassurance.

*"Dongyuan once promised that you would not end your life in a cell or on a sickbed."*
*"According to Mingting's law, to execute a felon like you, the Justice Commission must first receive a decree approved by both the Emperor and the Sentinel, forwarded by the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs. The execution can only be carried out an hour after they receive the decree."*
*"Without those writs returned, no man dares execute you."*
*"Therefore, Qiuyuan, remember: from now on, the path ahead is yours alone. Even if it costs you your life, you must see this case to its end. If you are to die, let it be on the road to avenging Dongyuan."*

This story delivers multiple simultaneous revelations: the nature of Dongyuan's death (a Fractsidus Resonator capable of body-transference — an ability with no match in Mingting's records); the wrongful order to pursue Geshu Lin (which Qiuyuan recognized as out of character and therefore chose to disobey); the Justice Commission's scapegoating of Qiuyuan in the absence of any other identifiable culprit; and Minister Lin's bureaucratic jailbreak — the execution decree cannot be executed without writs returned to the Ministry, and the Minister controls the return of those writs. The path out is not a dramatic escape but a procedural window held open by a man who chose to hold it.

Qiuyuan's libation — pouring the wine on the cell floor rather than drinking it as a toast — is the most restrained and most powerful gesture in all five stories.

## Qiuyuan Character Story V: "Reforged"

Five years had passed since he escaped Mingting and became a **wanted fugitive across the six cities**.

Mingting remained unchanged — bustling and prosperous. His own clothes had grown tattered, worn by the dust and frost of the endless journey across Huanglong. Minister Lin from the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs had fallen ill and retired. The young man who had survived his blade had become a general guarding the border — but even he had fallen to the madness of the Threnodian.

His master had taught him when to draw the sword and when to sheathe it. Yet when he held back, everything plunged into the abyss.

The doctor once said that a sword could both kill and save. Yet he had failed to save the one he wished to.

Fate twisted relentlessly. Everything had changed, yet somehow, nothing really changed. He still had not found the one who killed Grandmaster Liang. But he had never stopped seeking the truth.

If Minister Lin had not sent word of a lead from the Grand Marshal, head of the Ministry of War, Qiuyuan might never have dared return to Mingting.

*"You've come."*

Through torrential rain, a straw-hatted figure emerged from the shadow of a bridge.

*"Scar, the Fractsidus Overseer, escaped from Jinzhou's prison not long ago. Intel places him near Septimont City in Rinascita. He's a wanted criminal of Jinzhou and of Huanglong. He must be captured."*
*"You could do it yourself."*
*"Rinascita's different. Individuals who resemble the Threnodian's Resonator have been sighted there."*
*"…So this reminds you of me?"*
*"You were the key to Dongyuan's case, and Geshu Lin is the key to your case. Since Jinzhou's Threnodian is what brought Geshu Lin down… Follow the thread, and you may learn why Dongyuan gave you that order, and... perhaps discover the true culprit."*
*"Everything on Fractsidus and Rinascita's Threnodian is in this dossier. Take this letter with my seal and start with Jinzhou. Your niece, the Magistrate's Bodyguard, is there. Find out how Scar escaped, then head east to Rinascita."*

Qiuyuan rarely saw this distant niece, let alone spoke to her. All he knew was that she had become the Jinzhou Magistrate's bodyguard.

*"She may not meet me."*
*"She will. You are family. She will believe in your words. Also… {PlayerName} is in Rinascita."*
*"…That {PlayerName}?"*
*"Yes. You may ask {them} about what happened in Jinzhou as well."*
*"…Understood. Give me the dossier. I'll leave at once."*

Qiuyuan turned, but the figure called after him.

*"Wait! Since you're back, visit Dongyuan. I know you offer incense at his grave every year."*
*"I'll find the clues first, then return to honor his spirit. As for that fugitive you mentioned…"*
*"Capture him if you can, execute him if you can't. I'll handle the Justice Commission."*
*"Understood."*

Qiuyuan nodded, vanishing into the rain without further words.

He knew he carried the weight of unseen court intrigues. Mingting would not even formally assign someone to capture Scar. He, a lone fugitive, was entrusted to do so. Yet he didn't care. His aim was simple: uncover the truth he had been denied.

And when that day came — when vengeance was taken, debts repaid — then what? Would that be the sheath he had been seeking?

*Perhaps, with age, he would finally know.*

This story is the bridge between Qiuyuan's backstory and his role in the main story arc. The chain of connections is spelled out explicitly: Scar (Fractsidus) escaped Jinzhou → Fractsidus individuals resemble the Threnodian's Resonator → Geshu Lin fell to the Threnodian's madness → Geshu Lin was the subject of the wrongful order Qiuyuan disobeyed → that order connects to whoever was wearing Dongyuan's body when it was given. The niece mentioned as Mingting's Magistrate Bodyguard is Sanhua. The *"{PlayerName}*" in Rinascita is Rover.

The story ends, uniquely, on a question rather than a statement. Five character stories of accumulated debt, loss, and forward momentum — and the final paragraph is Qiuyuan asking himself what comes after. He does not know. He is walking into the rain anyway.

## Qiuyuan: Cherished Items

**Bamboo Flask (Doctor Zhang's Remedy)**
*"A bamboo flask Qiuyuan often carries, crafted from black bamboo native to Chongzhou. Its natural fragrance has long faded, replaced by the lingering scent of the medicinal decoction it always contains. When Qiuyuan once teetered on the brink of death, a doctor from Chongzhou saved him with a potent regimen. While he recovered, his Forte did not, though it can be temporarily restored to full strength with the decoction in this flask. Yet even this power serves only as an embellishment to the already refined swordplay he takes pride in."*

The flask is the daily-carried evidence of a debt and a compromise. Doctor Zhang's regimen traded Qiuyuan's full Forte access for his life; the decoction recipe was given in partial restitution. He carries both the cost and the partial compensation. His final sentence is quietly telling: even restored Mindsight is only an embellishment to swordplay he takes pride in on its own terms. He is not dependent on the flask to be effective; he prefers not to need it.

**Iron Weight (ISA Token)**
*"A token of Mingting's Internal Security Agency that Qiuyuan always carries. It should have been confiscated along with his uniform after he left the service, yet someone within Mingting ensured he kept it. '…I hereby entrust this to you. Let all officials of every state know: whoever bears this token carries my presence.'"*

The final quoted line is Grandmaster Dongyuan's voice. The ISA iron weight is the cherished item most directly connected to a person he was unable to save and whose death he is still trying to account for. He carries it as an amputee carries the sensation of a missing limb — the authority it conveys is real, the person who gave it is gone, and the act of carrying it is both memorial and ongoing mission.

**Bamboo Flute**
*"He came to know the world through his ears, not his eyes. The weary sighs of his parents, the whispers of wind coming from Chongzhou's hollow bamboos, the clash of blades, the drop of his master's blood, the furious cries of his foes... And then, in Mingting, he heard light, but also the shadows lurking beneath it. He heard the greedy begging for mercy. He heard schemes snarled with lies. He heard resentment and hope spoken in another kind of voice. He cannot see, yet he hears all things clearly and inevitably. They are already there before the sounds ever reach him, as natural as iron becoming a sword, jade slumbering within stone, or music waiting in the heart of a bamboo flute."*

The flute item is not described as an instrument Qiuyuan plays or has made — it is described as a philosophical statement about his relationship to perception. He does not need to see the world; he hears it. The sounds listed constitute a compressed biography. The final line extends the logic: music is already waiting inside a bamboo flute before it is played. Truth is already present before it is revealed. He hears what is there before it reaches him.

## Qiuyuan: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite — A Humble Roadside Meal**
*"Years ago, I'd just wrapped up a case in Yuezhou and had to rush back to Mingting overnight. I was exhausted and famished when I stumbled into a diner by the roadside. A bowl of rice, braised pork, a bottle of rice wine. A humble meal, yet nothing has ever filled me like that night."*

His favorite meal is not a dish but a condition: exhaustion, hunger, an unexpected diner, and the specific relief of having completed something and being on the way back. The food is incidental. The memory is the meal.

**Disliked — An Unnamed Mingting Drink**
*"Mingting has a certain... drink. Greenish-gray, with a smell you don't forget. Trust me, you don't want to find out how it tastes..."*

He does not name it. The pause before *"drink"* and the refusal to specify further are precisely calibrated. Of all the things Qiuyuan has encountered — massacres, near-death, five years of fugitive travel — this unnamed Mingting beverage receives his most unambiguous rejection.

## Qiuyuan: Hobby and Trouble

**Hobby — Cooking for Patients**
*"Those years I stayed with Master Zhang, I cooked for his patients. Each meal was carefully crafted for their individual need. Beyond those, I know a few spicier dishes from Chongzhou. Whether they suit you, that, I cannot say."*

The hobby is an extension of the second character story's central scene: Qiuyuan at a stove, tending fires, cooking for Doctor Zhang's patients, finding in ordinary acts the texture of ordinary life. He learned to cook because the patients had individual dietary needs. He adapted. It is the same methodical attention he applies to swordsmanship, turned toward something that feeds rather than harms.

**Trouble — Two Names on a List**
*"Agents of the Internal Security Agency work from a list — names to arrest, names to eliminate. It's all there. Now, only two names remain on mine: the Fractsidus operative who murdered the man who saved my life, and Geshu Lin. Blood debts are paid in blood. That's the natural order. Until my blade ends them, my mission will never end."*

His Trouble is his purpose. The two names are not a burden he resents — they are the structure that makes forward motion possible. What the fifth character story suggests is more unsettling: he does not know what comes after. The list will eventually be empty. The Trouble voice line does not address what follows the completion of the list, because he does not know, and the character stories end at the threshold of finding out.

## Qiuyuan: Ideals

**Life Before Purpose**
*"Ideals, wishes... those things mean little to someone who lies down each night unsure if tomorrow comes. So I see no point in dwelling on them. All I know is I can't die yet. Not before I've had my revenge, not before I've fulfilled my duty. What happens after that... I'll leave that to fate to decide."*

The ideals voice line is the most concentrated statement of Qiuyuan's relationship to the future. He does not have ideals in the conventional sense because ideals require a stable enough relationship to the future to be worth maintaining. He has obligations and a survival condition (*"I can't die yet"*). What comes after the obligations are met is genuinely uncharted territory — *"I'll leave that to fate."* For a man who reads intentions through a bamboo forest in his mind, the admission that he cannot see his own future is the most honest thing he says.

## Qiuyuan: Narrative Chronicle

- **[Early Life — Chongzhou origin]** Qiuyuan is born blind in Huanglong; grows up in or near Chongzhou; parents are present in early memory (their weary sighs appear in the Bamboo Flute cherished item text); develops early awareness of the world through hearing before Resonance Awakening
- **[Resonance Awakening — Unknown Timing]** Awakening occurs at unclear time and circumstances; classified as Unclear Resonator; Mindsight ability allows perception of surroundings through bamboo forest mental construct, detection of intentions and vulnerabilities, and manifestation of tangible illusions; restrains use deliberately throughout all documented observations
- **[The Massacre — "Five years ago" relative to Story I]** A massacre — a *"hell of fire and blood"* — connected to the man who later becomes Qiuyuan's master (the Sword Specter of Chongzhou); Qiuyuan is present and survives; this is the source of the flame that burns throughout all five stories; precise nature and Qiuyuan's role in the events are not fully clarified in available sources
- **[Five Years Training — The Bamboo Forest]** The Sword Specter takes Qiuyuan into the bamboo forest and trains him for five years; Qiuyuan wins countless battles but cannot defeat his master; the master teaches him to see the bamboo forest in his mind — the Mindsight made martial
- **[The Final Move — Story I]** Qiuyuan names his master as the Sword Specter; the master acknowledges it; the final move is given in a duel; outcome undocumented in available sources
- **[The Killing Spree — Between Stories I and II]** After leaving the bamboo forest, Qiuyuan kills dozens with his bare hands; driven by vengeance and hatred; exhausted of purpose after the violence; begins allowing enemies to wound him without defending
- **[Doctor Zhang's Hut — Story II]** Near death; found and saved by Doctor Zhang (wandering doctor of Chongzhou); Zhang's potent regimen saves Qiuyuan's life but permanently cripples his Mindsight; Qiuyuan spends an indeterminate period at the hut, tending the stove, cooking for patients, experiencing ordinary life; his Mindsight is replaced by silent darkness free of hatred; receives the bamboo flask and medicinal recipe
- **[ISA Recruitment — Story III]** An ISA agent visits Doctor Zhang's hut repeatedly over weeks; identifies Qiuyuan, presents the ISA iron weight (Qiongqi beast-head token), and recruits him; the persuasive argument is Doctor Zhang's own philosophy extended to court-level impact; Zhang sends Qiuyuan away without allowing a farewell; Qiuyuan prostrates in the dirt path before leaving
- **[ISA Service — Unspecified Duration]** Serves as senior agent under Grandmaster Liang Dongyuan; described as one of Mingting's finest operative; Dongyuan keeps the ISA token for Qiuyuan when he eventually leaves the service; builds relationship with Minister Lin of the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs; Dongyuan confirms the token's authority directly to Qiuyuan
- **[Dongyuan's Death and Wrongful Imprisonment — Story IV]** A Fractsidus Resonator capable of body-transference operates through Dongyuan; Qiuyuan receives an order to pursue Geshu Lin (a minor official who protected civilians from Tacet Discord attacks); recognizes the order as inconsistent with Dongyuan's character; disobeys; is present when Dongyuan dies; the Fractsidus Resonator peels from Dongyuan's body; no matching Resonance record exists; Justice Commission scapegoats Qiuyuan; he is imprisoned and scheduled for execution
- **[The Jailbreak — Story IV]** Minister Lin enters the execution cell 90 minutes before the scheduled execution; holds conversation under Ministry questioning authority; weaponizes the bureaucratic requirement that execution writs must be returned to the Ministry before execution proceeds; does not return the writs; Qiuyuan is effectively free to leave; Lin's final instruction: *"from now on, the path ahead is yours alone"*
- **[Five Years as Fugitive — Between Stories IV and V]** Wanted across six cities; clothes worn to tatters; Minister Lin falls ill and retires; Geshu Lin, *"the young man who had survived his blade,"* becomes a border general and falls to the Threnodian's madness; Qiuyuan continues investigating without any institutional support; offers incense at Dongyuan's grave each year despite fugitive status
- **[Return to Mingting — Story V]** Minister Lin (recovered sufficiently to send word) contacts Qiuyuan with a lead from the Grand Marshal; meets a straw-hatted operative in the rain; receives the Scar mission dossier and instructions to find Sanhua in Jinzhou, then travel east to Rinascita and contact Rover; makes the decision to follow the thread regardless of personal risk
- **[Version 2.7 Main Story — Ongoing]** Enters Jinzhou in search of Sanhua; main story quest Chapter II Act XI: Dawn Breaks on Dark Tides; Segue: A Stranger in a Strange Land; follows the thread toward Rinascita, Scar, Geshu Lin, and the Fractsidus operative who was wearing Dongyuan's face
