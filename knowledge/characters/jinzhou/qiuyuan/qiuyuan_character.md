## Qiuyuan Profile: Basic Information

- **Full Name:** Qiuyuan (Chinese: 仇远, Qiú Yuǎn)
- **Identity:** Unclear Resonator; Aero-Aligned; former senior agent of Mingting's Internal Security Agency; current wanderer and deniable operative across Huanglong
- **Affiliation (Former):** Internal Security Agency (ISA), Mingting — separated following events surrounding the death of Grandmaster Liang Dongyuan; carries fugitive status across six cities per the Reforged story
- **Affiliation (Operative):** Informally directed by Minister Lin (Ministry of Sentinel Affairs) and the Grand Marshal; not formally reinstated
- **Birthplace / Region:** Huanglong — Mingting and Chongzhou specifically; trained in Chongzhou's bamboo valleys by an unnamed master
- **Relative:** Sanhua (distant relative; her branch of the lineage moved from Chongzhou to the borderlands)
- **Version Added:** Version 2.7 (second half, following Galbrena); banner title: Wanderer Knows No Far and Near
- **Rarity:** 5-Star
- **Element:** Aero
- **Weapon:** Sword
- **Forte Power:** Bambooscape
- **Resonance Classification:** Unclear (per the Forte Examination correspondence; distinct from Natural or Congenital — the test data presents ambiguity, likely compounded by his deliberate restraint of his Forte during testing)
- **Special Characteristic:** Blind; perceives the world through **Mindsight** — an ability to construct a mental "bamboo forest" model of his surroundings, read opponents' intentions and vulnerabilities, and even manifest the Mindsight into tangible illusions; the Mindsight was temporarily crippled when Doctor Zhang's life-saving regimen pulled him back from death

## Qiuyuan Profile: Ontological Overview

Qiuyuan is a Version 2.7 limited 5-star character who returns the game to Huanglong for a story arc involving Mingting's Internal Security Agency, a Fractsidus-linked murder, and a truth that has been kept from him for more than five years. He is a blind swordsman — the blindness is not a limitation he has heroically transcended but a definitional aspect of his combat identity. His Mindsight replaces conventional vision entirely in both combat and social reading: the Forte Examination report notes that he perceives opponents' intentions and vulnerabilities through a bamboo forest in his mind, and his Thoughts III voice line reveals that he reads everyone's inner life this way — hearts visible as shifting hues and silhouettes — except Rover's, which appears to him as darkness he cannot parse.

His narrative is one of the most complete biographical arcs in the game's character archive. Five stories move him from the aftermath of a massacre he survived at the hands of his own master, through near-death and recovery at a doctor's hut, through ISA service under a superior who made the work meaningful, through that superior's death and his own wrongful imprisonment, through a jailbreak, through five years as a fugitive, and finally to a mission that gives him a thread back toward the truth. None of it resolves in the stories themselves — they end at the beginning of the main story arc, with Qiuyuan walking into rain.

His tagline — *"Name the target. I'll take care of the rest."* — is not bravado. It is the compressed syntax of a man who has been doing exactly that, with and without institutional authorization, for his entire adult life.

## Qiuyuan — Character/Appearance Design

Qiuyuan's design synthesizes the visual language of classical Chinese ink painting, bamboo imagery, and blind warrior archetypes into a silhouette that is immediately legible as both ancient and precise. His idle animations — playing a bamboo flute, drinking from his bamboo flask, sprinting along bamboo stalks — are among the most distinctive in Version 2.x.

- **Eyes:** Pale and unfocused, with the settled emptiness of a person who does not track visual movement; he does not look *at* things; the gaze is forward and internally directed; the visual mark of his blindness is unmistakable and undecorated
- **Hair:** Dark, worn partially gathered; clean lines, no ornamentation; the design of a person who discarded what serves no purpose
- **Outfit:** Ink-washed grey and white tones in overlapping cloth layers; the palette directly mirrors the Bambooscape Forte and the combat skill nomenclature (Inkwash, Inksplash of Mind, Thus Spoke the Blade); the layered cloth structure reads like monochrome ink wash on paper — depth through tone rather than color; practically cut for movement and reach
- **Bamboo Flask (Black Bamboo):** Always present at his hip; crafted from black bamboo native to Chongzhou; its original fragrance has long faded, replaced by the medicinal decoction that temporarily restores his Mindsight (crippled by Doctor Zhang's life-saving intervention); he describes it in the Chat I voice line: *"It sharpens perception but brings the risk of madness. Most people aren't worth my blade, though. For them, this bamboo stick will do."*
- **Iron Weight (Qiongqi Beast-Head Token):** The ISA token he retained when he left Mingting's service — it should have been confiscated with his uniform; Grandmaster Dongyuan arranged for him to keep it; the beast head is forged from fine steel, radiates a chill, and embodies the Emperor's authority: *"whoever bears this token carries my presence; let all officials of every state know."* He carries the authority of an institution that has declared him a fugitive
- **Bamboo Flute:** Third cherished item; played in his idle animation; the item description is a compressed biography heard rather than seen: parents' weary sighs, hollow Chongzhou bamboo whistling in wind, blade clashes, his master's blood dropping, enemies' cries, and then the sounds of Mingting — light, but also shadows beneath it; greedy pleading for mercy; schemes tangled in lies; resentment and hope in another kind of voice; he hears all of it, and the flute plays among it
- **Bamboo Stalks (Movement/Combat):** Both his hold Resonance Skill and his idle behavior involve bamboo stalks as literal terrain; his master taught him using a bamboo forest as the training ground; he spent his recuperation holding only a bamboo stick outside Doctor Zhang's hut; his Forte combat states are named for ink painting done in bamboo settings; bamboo is simultaneously his origin, his training ground, his sanctuary, and his combat metaphor

## Qiuyuan: Psychological Profile

**Perception as Discipline**
Qiuyuan's Mindsight allows him to read anyone's intentions through shifting hues and silhouettes in his inner bamboo forest. He consciously restrains it — the Forte Examination specifically notes this is the impression of his evaluators. The bamboo decoction in his flask restores it temporarily when he chooses to deploy it fully. Even without it, his swordsmanship is independently refined to a level that makes him one of the finest blades in Mingting. The restraint is not modesty; it is a preference: he takes genuine pride in the swordsmanship that belongs entirely to him, not to his Resonance ability.

**Composure Over Undisclosed Depth**
Dongyuan's annotator in the Forte Examination asks: *"Isn't this lad a little too devoid of emotion?"* This is the outside view. The character stories carry what is underneath: a flame that could not be quenched by five years of rain, hatred described as something that couldn't be cleansed, regret tangled in vengeance. His composure is a practice applied over feelings that are fully present. His Ascension V voice line: *"In my younger days, all I cared about was keeping my blade sharp. It once felt weightless in my hand. But now, with the lives I've touched and choices I carry, my blade seems to grow heavier with each swing."* The skill does not diminish. The weight accumulates.

**Vengeance as Task, Not Identity**
His Trouble voice line: two names remain. Blood debts are paid in blood. The natural order. This is not rhetorical — it is his current operational status. What makes the fifth story more complex is the question he asks himself on the road away from Mingting: *"when vengeance was taken, debts repaid — then what? Would that be the sheath he had been seeking?"* He does not know. He is fulfilling the present obligation while the future remains structurally unknown.

**The Ordinary as Revelation**
When Doctor Zhang's regimen saved Qiuyuan's life, it crippled his Mindsight. For the duration of his recovery, his inner bamboo forest of blades was replaced by *"pure, silent darkness, free of hatred and rage. There were sighs and tears, words of gratitude and joy, the scent of medicine, and the crackle of stove fire."* He opened doors. He tended the stove. Patients came. This was the ordinary, and the second character story identifies it as the moment Qiuyuan realized he was *"no different from the ordinary."* Doctor Zhang saw that this would not hold — *"bloodshed clings to your face"* — but the moment of recognition is the most honest and exposed event in his archive.

**Trust as Debt**
Three people gave Qiuyuan a form of trust he names as un-repayable: his master (brought him out of the massacre the master also created); Doctor Zhang (saved his life when he was begging for death, then returned him to the world); Grandmaster Dongyuan (made the service meaningful, kept the ISA token for him, and died before Qiuyuan could repay any of it). In the fourth story, from a cell one and a half hours before his scheduled execution, he says: *"Because of you, Grandmaster Liang, and Doctor Zhang, I was able to live differently, if only for a time... I could only repay such trust in another life."* He does not believe he will survive what he is about to do.

## Qiuyuan: Relationships

**Unnamed Master ("Sword Specter of Chongzhou")**
Creator and destroyer in the same person. His master is the man who caused the massacre that destroyed Qiuyuan's world five years before the first character story begins, and also the man who trained him in the bamboo forest for those same five years afterward. The first story ends: *"It was he who wrought the hell of fire and blood five years ago. It was also he who brought Qiuyuan out of that hell of fire and blood."* When Qiuyuan names him directly as the Sword Specter, the master acknowledges it and gives him the final move. The flame burns in both of them. Qiuyuan's swordsmanship and Qiuyuan's grief are the same man's legacy.

**Doctor Zhang (Wandering Doctor — Chongzhou)**
Structural pivot of the second and third stories. Found Qiuyuan after his killing spree, chose to save him (*"whether you deserve life or death, I cannot say — the choice lies with you alone"*), crippled his Forte in doing so, gave him the bamboo flask and recipe, refused to let him die uselessly, and sent him back to the world when the ISA agent came. His parting framing of Dongyuan — *"that man, Liang Dongyuan... he's the one in court who can 'save' people"* — was the argument that moved Qiuyuan to leave. Doctor Zhang's reason for leaving Mingting himself — *"to heal all, rather than to heal one"* — is the philosophical framework Qiuyuan carries as his model for what service could mean.

**Liang Dongyuan (Grandmaster, ISA — Deceased)**
The superior who made Qiuyuan's service meaningful and whose death is the operative purpose of the entire arc. Described as a man so righteous he would never have ordered the pursuit of a minor official who only killed Tacet Discords to protect people — and yet he apparently gave that very order. The fourth story reveals that a Fractsidus Resonator capable of assuming another's form had peeled themselves from Dongyuan's body; the Grandmaster Qiuyuan knew may not have been himself when he issued that final order. Qiuyuan pours the libation wine on the cell floor rather than drink it. He says: *"I could only repay such trust in another life."*

**Minister Lin (Ministry of Sentinel Affairs)**
The official who engineers Qiuyuan's escape from a scheduled execution by weaponizing bureaucratic procedure — the execution decree requires writs returned to the Ministry, and without those writs, no man dares carry out the sentence. Lin enters the cell personally with wine and a solemn face. He tells Qiuyuan: *"from now on, the path ahead is yours alone."* Five years later, he sends the lead that brings Qiuyuan back to Mingting and into the main story arc.

**Sanhua (Distant Relative)**
Both are swordspeople from Chongzhou-origin lineage; her branch moved to the borderlands, his stayed and passed through Mingting. *"Like me, her only interest used to be swordplay. But now, it seems she's found a purpose for her blade in Jinzhou's Magistrate. I'm glad for her."* He is observing in her something he has not yet found for himself: a settled place to point the blade that is not defined by debt or vengeance.

**Rover (Protagonist)**
The Thoughts III voice line is the most unusual observation Qiuyuan makes about anyone: *"I'm blind, but I can still 'see' what's in people's hearts. Arrogance, hatred, malice, pleasure... Through my mind's eye, they take form in shifting hues and silhouettes. But I can't see yours. A rare exception."* His Mindsight reads everyone. Rover is darkness he cannot parse. His response to this, per Thoughts V, is not withdrawal but commitment: *"I can't see you, but I can feel your calling. Should you ever need, call my name. This hidden blade shall unsheathe again to defend your faith."*
