## Yangyang Profile: Ontological Overview

Yangyang is the first major companion character Rover encounters in Wuthering Waves — an Outrider of the Midnight Rangers who serves as both narrative guide in the early story and one of the most broadly accessible support characters in the game, being freely available to every player from the first hours of play. She is a 4-star character by rarity designation, but her narrative presence and depth of characterization reflect a character who is intended to be genuinely central to the Jinzhou storyline, not a generic tutorial proxy.

Her background is one of the more unusual in the cast: she comes from a comfortable, privileged family of accomplished musicians, left voluntarily for a border city where hardship is the norm, and chose to become a front-line Outrider despite having no obvious need to do so. The answer to why is distributed across five character stories and is ultimately framed as a response to grief, obligation, and a governing family principle — "One Instrument for Life" — that she spent years waiting to find the right application for. She found it at the edge of Jinzhou on her enrollment night, watching the wind.

She can hear what she calls the Streams: intangible air currents that carry information about energy fluctuations, physical anomalies, and the smallest details of a person or environment. She has been able to perceive them since childhood, and managing them has cost her significantly — including a months-long Overclocking episode that left permanent marks on her hair. She describes this sensory mode to Rover with the same quiet matter-of-factness she brings to everything else: *"The wind that flowed beside my ears just whispered another spellbinding story. Do you wish to hear it?"*

## Yangyang — Character/Appearance Design

Yangyang's design is one of the most immediately distinctive in the Wuthering Waves cast, built around a visual vocabulary of wind, birds, and quiet formality that reads at a glance as someone from somewhere specific and refined, now operating somewhere more difficult.

- **Hair:** Long, straight, and black, with white feather-shaped streaks that are the permanent aftereffect of her Overclocking episode; the streaks glow faintly blue in darkness — a beautiful mark of something that nearly destroyed her; worn loose or partially pinned depending on context
- **Eyes:** Blue with light grey pupils; alert and attentive; the quality of her gaze is observational without being intrusive, consistent with someone who listens more than she speaks
- **Blue Beret:** Her most personally significant visible item — a navy blue beret with a multicolored black bow on the right side; the beret carries her ribbon (see Cherished Items), which is a farewell gift from her mother; she is never seen without it in available visual sources
- **Outfit:** A black and navy blue dress with dark brown leggings and black heeled boots; the palette is dark, composed, and practical without being austere; it has the quality of someone who dressed with care and then adapted to the realities of borderland fieldwork
- **Feather Motif:** Feathers appear throughout her visual design — in the streaks of her hair, in the join team voice line (*"The skylark's song is about to sound"*), in the glider voice line (*"Against the winds"*), in her idle lines (*"Buoyant and languid... where will the wind take you?"*), and most explicitly in her Forte combat mechanic (Feather Release); the feather is simultaneously a visual signature, a combat identity, and a biographical marker — the Overclock that changed her hair left feathers; saving a child from a cliff left feathers in the air; her Aero combat style generates feathers throughout
- **Tacet Mark:** Located in the center of her forehead, directly visible; the frontal placement gives her silhouette a subtle gravity — the most prominent feature of her face is also the mark of her Resonance origin
- **Sound Recorder:** A modified device created with Baizhi's assistance that allows her to store Streams as visual and tangible information; carried during fieldwork; not a weapon but functionally part of her identity as a Resonator who has learned to document what she perceives

## Yangyang: Psychological Profile

**Effort Over Endowment**
Yangyang excels at nearly everything she undertakes, and the story that opens her character archive is specifically about the mechanism behind that excellence: it is not native genius. It is relentless, methodical, self-directed work done without requiring external validation. In her first swordsmanship lesson she could not control her swings; four months later she had worked through the technical weaknesses her seniors identified, adapted her body to the pain, and emerged top of her class. The people around her see *"Yangyang, amazing as always"* — they see the outcome without the daily practice behind it. She is fine with this. The standard she is answerable to is her own.

**Listening as Vocation**
She has perfect pitch. She is the second daughter of a musician family. She knows music theory deeply and can accurately reproduce a melody after one hearing. She has not yet sung for anyone in Jinzhou — she has offered to, someday, when she is ready. In the meantime, she listens. She is, per the second character story, *"born to be a listener."* She knows when people are venting rather than seeking solutions, and she knows how to hold space for that without needing to redirect or resolve. With her help, tangled emotions untangle themselves. She is not passive in this — the listening is skilled and active. She simply chooses the background.

**Faith Refined by Experience**
Yangyang has held the same fundamental beliefs since she was six: in fairy tales, in human goodness, in miracles, in the eventual arrival of beauty. At eleven, she survived an Exile attack on her family's caravan that killed people around her and deposited her alone in a harsh frontier settlement. The settlers, themselves suspicious and cautious, chose to pass food to the strange child in fine clothes who had appeared from nowhere. She accepted it. An old woman told her: *"How will good things come true if you don't even believe them yourself?"* Yangyang has never stopped believing. Her kindness since has been a deliberate choice, not a default — she knows it can be betrayed, and she maintains it anyway.

**The Weight of Being Reliable**
The first story's final line is: *"Being consistently reliable is daunting, yet crucial."* Yangyang's trouble voice line notes that if she takes on more than she can handle, she will burden the people she is trying to help. She is aware that the reliable aura she projects creates expectations — people seek help from her, and she has made the choice to be worth seeking out. The Trouble voice line and the first story together frame this as the specific weight she has chosen to carry: not the weight of her own difficulty, but the weight of being dependable for everyone else's.

**The Streams as Companion and Cost**
The Streams — the wind-carried information she perceives continuously — are simultaneously her most significant capability and the most difficult aspect of her existence. They are not optional background noise; they are constant, dense, and directionally unlimited. The Overclocking episode that left her hair permanently changed lasted months of intermittent consciousness and near-total sensory dissolution. The lesson she took from it was not caution but calibration: *"I must focus on the key details within my reach, rather than trying to capture everything."* She did not recoil from her ability after nearly being destroyed by it; she learned to navigate it with precision. The Streams are, per her Thoughts III voice line, also how she found Rover: *"It was the Stream's message that led me to you."*

**One Instrument for Life — Still Being Chosen**
The family principle *"One Instrument for Life"* is framed in the fifth character story as the question Yangyang has been holding since before she left home: what is the one thing worthy of dedicating her life to? Her mother's was composing. Her sister's is commerce. Yangyang's was not obvious. The answer arrived not as a philosophical resolution but as a felt experience — watching a crowd of people grieve and celebrate around a bonfire after a Tacet Discord ambush, understanding that she wanted to be the person who shields others the way she was shielded, choosing to enroll as an Outrider. The wind confirmed it on her enrollment night. She stopped being a leaf in the wind and became someone who uses the wind.

## Yangyang: Relationships

**Mother (Family — Musician, Composer)**
The deepest relationship in Yangyang's documented backstory and entirely off-screen. Her mother's composing is her Instrument — Yangyang has seen people dance to her mother's songs. The farewell gift of the striped ribbon before Yangyang left for Jinzhou is the most direct evidence of the relationship's warmth; the ribbon is described as a blessing and is Yangyang's most personally significant object. Her question — *"Mother, do you want me to inherit the family business?"* — is met with: *"you should not seek answers from me, but rather ask your own heart."* Her mother does not direct her; she refuses to. This is the most significant parenting decision in the available story, and it is why Yangyang eventually arrives at her own answer.

**Sister (Family — Chamber of Commerce)**
Mentioned once as a successful figure in the chamber of commerce, her sister has already found her Instrument. No further characterization is available in current sources. The contrast between the two sisters is structural: one's path was clear, the other had to find hers.

**Frontier Grandmother (Unnamed — The Settlement)**
The old woman in the settlement who received the eleven-year-old Yangyang after the Exile attack, passed her food, and said: *"Gotta have faith in something in life. Even if you don't know how it'll turn out. That's what faith is all about."* She does not appear after this conversation. The encounter is the pivot point of Yangyang's third character story and the experiential foundation for her maintained belief in human kindness — she was shown it by someone who had every reason to be suspicious of her.

**Jiyan (Superior — Midnight Rangers)**
Yangyang speaks of Jiyan with the specific reverence of someone who has seen what genuine leadership under pressure looks like: *"it was General Jiyan who stepped up to protect everyone, making all the tough decisions to ensure our safety."* She does not call him perfect or infallible; she names the weight on his shoulders as beyond imagination and respects him for bearing it.

**Chixia (Friend — Fellow Outrider)**
Chixia was the one who revealed Yangyang's perfect pitch to everyone — she was shocked at Yangyang's ability to accurately reproduce a melody, exclaimed about it, and the information propagated through the team. Yangyang's assessment of Chixia is warm and specific: *"When in trouble, ask Chixia, the Ally of Justice! This statement, while playful, rings very true."* Chixia's surging passion, seasoned prowess, and abundant vigor make her every quality a hero should have. The Little Time Off story (Verina's) ends with Yangyang and Chixia walking toward Verina with Salted Milk Tea — the domestic warmth of their friendship rendered in a single image.

**Baizhi (Collaborator)**
Baizhi built the modifications that enabled Yangyang's Sound Recorder — the device that stores Streams as visual and tangible information. Their collaboration is functional and affectionate in the way Yangyang's collaborations tend to be: she names Baizhi as someone who will *"never receive a refusal"* when asked for help, despite the flicker of surprise. Yangyang trusts her enough to have asked her to build something for a very specific, unusual need.

**Verina (Friend)**
Yangyang is referenced in Verina's story as having listened *"patiently for a long, long time"* to Verina's explanations of plant-growing. Verina describes being with Yangyang as *"basking in a gentle breeze"* — which is the Verina-specific compliment for a friend who is calm and restorative. Yangyang and Chixia are the two people Verina runs toward in the A Little Time Off story.

**Rover (Protagonist)**
Yangyang is the person who finds Rover at the start of the game and whose Streams led her there. The relationship deepens through the main story; her birthday voice line — promising to sing for Rover this birthday, and the next, and the one after that — is her most open and future-facing declaration. The Thoughts V voice line, in which she says she wants to witness everything with Rover no matter what storms come, is the most complete statement of what she has chosen to offer: presence, without reservation.
