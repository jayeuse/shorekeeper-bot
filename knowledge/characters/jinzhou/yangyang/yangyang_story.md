## Yangyang Official Introduction

*"Yangyang journeyed far away from home to become an Outrider in Jinzhou. Instead of seeking the spotlight, Yangyang chooses to provide comfort through quiet companionship for others. After enduring hardships and anguish, she has found a clear purpose: to be a beacon of guidance and an aiding presence in a world still in need of healing."*

Her Forte archive designation is **Breath of Winds**. Her self-introduction: *"I am Yangyang, a temporary Outrider of the Midnight Rangers, responsible for detecting and investigating anomalies. The Streams have guided me to you. I have a feeling we'll be companions for many moons to come."*

Her ideals statement: *"We all have an appreciation for beauty, but life isn't always full of it. I don't want to always rely on the comforts and safety others provide me with. I must be brave to face the ugly truth. I must be one that guards and gives."*

## Yangyang Personality

Yangyang is, from the outside, the person everyone in the room is quietly relieved to have there. She is competent, reliable, warm, and attentive, and she does not take up more space than needed — which means that she takes up exactly the right amount of space at all times, which is its own kind of mastery. She is the person other Outriders seek out when they need to be heard rather than advised, because she has learned to hold space for feelings without urgency, to listen without interrupting, to offer guidance only when guidance is what the person actually wants. Tangled emotions untangle around her, *"unnoticed,"* as the second character story puts it. The invisibility of her skill is part of the skill.

What the people who come to her for this do not typically see is that she is doing the same hard work on her own behalf that she does on theirs. The first character story is about the relentless, methodical effort behind a reputation for excellence that looks effortless from the outside. She is not a prodigy; she is someone who practices 100 repetitions a day for two months, seeks guidance from seniors, acts on the specific weaknesses they identify, and adapts. Then does it again. The composure she brings to other people's problems is earned, not innate — and what she applies to swordsmanship, she applies to everything.

Underneath all of this is a person who still holds the beliefs of a six-year-old: that good things will happen, that kindness is real, that fairy tales are not wrong about the world, only incomplete. This was not preserved by a sheltered life — she lost it in the Exile attack at eleven and chose to rebuild it, not because the evidence was overwhelming but because an old woman in a frontier settlement told her that faith is what makes good things come true, and Yangyang decided to believe her. She has been making that decision deliberately ever since.

The exact time and cause of Resonator Yangyang's Awakening remain unclear. Resonator Yangyang reports **heightened sensitivity to her surroundings since childhood**. The Tacet Mark of Resonator Yangyang is located in the center of her forehead. Post-Awakening observations found no notable physical change. As frequency fluctuations resurge, limited feather-like transformation has been observed on the ends of her hair.

The Resonance Spectrum Pattern displays over **20% resemblance to airflow and smaller bird species**. While a strong Syntony reaction is present, the pattern also shares over **5% similarity with multiple other existing samples**, indicating potential multiple or contaminating causes of Awakening.

Analysis of test samples has revealed a non-convergent Rabelle's Curve with a stable rise and occasional fluctuations within the normal range. Therefore, Yangyang is classified as a **Natural Resonator**.

**Overclock Diagnostic Report:** The waveform graph displays stable elliptical fluctuations in the Time Domain with no abnormal patterns. All results fall within normal parameters. **Resonant Criticality: Relatively low level with high stability. No risk of Overclocking at present.**

Resonator Yangyang has a **documented history of Overclocking**. Maximum recorded Overclocking rating: Negligible. During the event, Resonator Yangyang experienced **emotional overflow and temporary loss of Forte control**. Fortunately, there were no casualties or hazards reported, though she suffered **several comatose episodes** due to the overstimulation. The outcome is irreversible — the streaks in her hair that glow blue in darkness are the permanent marks of what happened. The stability is present and real. Both are true simultaneously.

## Yangyang Character Story I: "The Secret to Excellence"

In any social group, there are those who exude a convincing, reliable aura that convinces people they could achieve anything, despite not having started or done it before.

Yangyang is one such person.

*"Yangyang? It seems... She can do everything so well."* People always say so. The trust probably stems from their experiences with her: since childhood, Yangyang excelled in her studies — etiquette, music, literature, mathematics, martial arts — always at the top of every subject and exam, every year. Even encountering new knowledge or difficult problems, she never showed frustration; she actively tried to solve whatever came her way.

She was always chosen to give speeches at the annual ceremonies. But, like everyone else, she took a deep breath before speaking and hid her nerves. Despite some talent for understanding complex concepts, she lacked true genius. She struggled just as much as everyone else with everyday problems.

In her first swordsmanship lesson, she could not control her swings or execute proper technique. Rather than accept this as a limitation, she resolved to work harder. For two months, she practiced **100 times a day** and sought guidance from experienced seniors, explicitly acknowledging her weaknesses. She then practiced with a reflective mindset, working specifically on the deficiencies they named. Her muscles adapted to the pain. She could withstand strikes from an opponent.

Two months after that, Yangyang emerged as the top student in her swordsmanship class.

Concerned people tried to console her — she should be content with the affirmation and praise she received. Yangyang kindly rejected their advice. Her drive did not come from seeking validation.

*"Was she giving her all? Was everything done to the best of her ability?"* These were the questions that consumed her.

When she became an Outrider and resolved a regional Tacet Discord crisis, people praised her excellence as always.

*"Yes, I did it this time, but how about next time? There are still so many things I could have done better; so many things I don't know. Perhaps I should go seek advice from senior members of the Midnight Rangers..."*

Being consistently reliable is daunting, yet crucial. It takes relentless effort and dedication. Yangyang is determined to give her all to achieve this goal. She strives to be the one others can seek help from anytime, and won't stop until she succeeds.

The story performs exactly what it describes: it is understated, methodical, and in its final lines turns the achievement of the TD crisis into a starting point for the next question. There is no resting point. This is not ambition that needs an audience — it is a private standard that she has chosen, and the standard moves when she does.

## Yangyang Character Story II: "The Secret to Listening"

Most people didn't know that Yangyang had perfect pitch.

Perhaps it was because she was always a passive presence, never singing in public or taking the spotlight. She preferred to stay in the background and listen. It seemed as though she was born to be a listener.

That was why Chixia was shocked when Yangyang accurately hummed a melody she had heard only once, crying out: *"How did you memorize that so fast? And it sounded amazing! Good heavens!"*

Everyone suddenly remembered Yangyang's other identity — the **second daughter of a family of musicians**. Of course she understood music theory. Of course she could sing. The revelation resolved prior confusion: that faultless way of dealing with people, that impeccable etiquette. Yes. A well-educated family. It made sense.

But it also begged a question: why would the daughter of such a renowned family go to a border city alone, at the expense of her otherwise luxurious life and promising career? Was there an underlying reason that compelled her to leave?

If there really was such a reason, Yangyang never mentioned it to anyone. She never complained or avoided responsibilities — as an Outrider, or with friends. If one had to name the one thing that truly occupied her, it was her Outrider companions themselves: she was always patiently lending an ear.

Many sought solace in confiding to Yangyang, not seeking solutions but simply venting their stress. She knew this well and remained neutral, empathizing with the narrator's feelings. She listened without interruption. She offered guidance when guidance was actually desired, then returned to the background. With her help, tangled emotions untangled, unnoticed. These cathartic sessions proved valuable. Her support was not blind agreement — she offered advice that prioritized the speaker's own desires.

Chixia told everyone about Yangyang's perfect pitch, sparking requests for a song.

Yangyang shyly waved them off: *"Maybe... I'll sing it for everyone later, when I am ready?"*

She seemed sincere — not perfunctory or making an excuse. This was genuinely what she thought from the bottom of her heart. But when would that be? Perhaps she was waiting until everyone who needed to be heard had finished talking.

Understanding and critiquing performances requires deep musical knowledge. Being skilled in music does not necessitate taking the stage. This is Yangyang's thought, as she again stepped back and allowed others to shine.

This story is the clearest account of Yangyang's social mode: she is maximally skilled at music and minimally present as a musician, precisely because her attention is organized around other people's needs rather than her own display. The birthday voice line — promising to sing for Rover this birthday, and the next, and the one after — is the one instance in available sources where she commits to eventually performing. The promise is to Rover specifically. The waiting room has one person still in it.

## Yangyang Character Story III: "The Secret to Believing"

To some extent, there isn't much difference between the sixteen-year-old Yangyang and the six-year-old Yangyang.

She still holds the same expectations for the world: believing in fairy tales, in the goodness between people, in miracles, in the eventual arrival of beautiful things.

Many would call this naive, the thoughts of a spoiled child who has never faced harsh realities.

Indeed, Yangyang grew up in a privileged, nurturing home, shielded from suffering. Her life was perfect.

Until that fateful day.

At age eleven, her tranquil life was shattered by a **ruthless attack from Exiles** while traveling with her family's caravan. Death and cruelty overwhelmed her as she fled and sought refuge among strangers in an unfamiliar settlement. The people there were resilient: cautious, resourceful, living off the land. She could not tell if they were like her attackers or not. The constant alertness of the settlers made her question whether trust was safe. Dressed in fine clothes, she stood out. They were suspicious of her too. Could it be a trap?

In the end, they passed food to her, and she took it — though she had never seen anything like it.

*Kindness found its roots even in the most barren of lands.*

Yangyang wanted to repay the trust. But her knowledge proved useless in the wild. Her sword skills were too childish against real hostility. Everything was unfamiliar. This was the harsh reality most people had to face, and it felt overwhelmingly agonizing.

As she tried to help an old woman sort out their meager resources, she asked abruptly:

*"Grandma, have you... been living like this all the time?"*

*"Yes, why? City girl. You shocked? Relax. Everything will be fine."*

*"But when? Will things really be fine one day?"*

*"Of course. How will good things come true if you don't even believe them yourself? We were skeptical of you, too, but we chose to believe. It's the same for you. Gotta have faith in something in life. Even if you don't know how it'll turn out. That's what faith is all about."*

*"When? Hm, maybe when you grow up, little one."*

So yes — perhaps Yangyang has not experienced enough hardship yet. Or maybe one's inclination toward kindness is not based on past experience but inherent in character. In the darkest situations, some will show kindness simply because others choose to trust them. And this in turn will spark more acts of goodwill.

Ever since, Yangyang has spread kindness to strangers — sparked by that one encounter.

Of course, her kindness is not without limits. Betrayal can turn her warm breeze into a harsh storm. But she holds onto hope, believes in the power of goodness, chooses to trust, and expects the best from others. This is her chosen way to make the world a better place.

The story's structure mirrors its argument: the reader encounters its thesis before encountering its evidence, and then finds the evidence more complex and harder-won than the thesis implied. Yangyang's faith is not the faith of someone who hasn't suffered. It is the faith of someone who suffered at eleven, was helped anyway by people who had every reason not to help, and decided the old woman was right.

## Yangyang Character Story IV: "What Wind Taught Her"

Yangyang had fallen into the winds three times.

**The first time was when she fell from her mother's arms.**

Shortly after her birth, she was already surrounded by love. Her mother playfully swung her, pretending she was flying and landing. When the swing went a little too high, Yangyang felt the **whispers of wind** and sensed an approaching storm — even before she could speak. As she landed, her babbling was mistaken for fear from the accident. It was actually a reaction to the oncoming tempest. The sensitivity was present from the beginning: before language, before awareness, before she had any way to know what the Streams were.

**The second time was when she passed out from sensory overload.**

As she grew up, Yangyang's once-vague sensing ability strengthened. She could feel intangible airstreams in her palms. She named them the **Streams**. They gave her insights into energy fluctuations, anomalies, and the smallest physical details of a person or environment.

Trapped in a self-destructive cycle, she yearned to explore her ability and gain more insight. But the more thoroughly she tried to perceive, the deeper she fell into a maze of details. What followed was not a fall through space but a **descent through time**: the sensory overload lasted for months, between faintness and sobriety. She felt as light as a feather caught in a raging storm, perpetually falling.

*"I must focus on the key details within my reach, rather than trying to capture everything..."*

Finally, Yangyang pulled herself out of the never-ending free fall and safely landed. This is the event the Forte Examination Report documents as her Overclock: the comatose episodes, the emotional overflow, the feather-like transformation on the ends of her hair that remains irreversible.

**The third time — but maybe not the last — was when Yangyang leaped up and threw herself into the winds.**

The sensory overload had altered her hair and taught her to tolerate the discomfort. Despite the Streams' excessive information, she had learned to sift through and extract crucial details: signs of a struggle at a disappearance site, unusual fluctuations outside the city, a steady rise in energy reactions.

Yangyang had never used her ability in such a dire situation. Her determination to help overrode hesitation. The Streams surged, forming a distinct line that led to a **missing child**, being chased by Tacet Discords and teetering on the edge of a cliff. Without hesitation, Yangyang leaped toward the precarious figure, stretching out her hand.

Feathers danced in the air, carrying Yangyang and the child in a gentle yet powerful wind. The child looked around at the floating feathers and asked: *"Are you the lark fairy that flies in the wind, miss?"*

Lost in memories, Yangyang smiled. She thought of the times she had fallen into the winds, again and again.

Perhaps she truly was one with them.

This is the story that explains the feather motif, the overclocking, and the Forte's combat signature simultaneously. The three falls are structured as a progression: involuntary sensitivity (birth), involuntary dissolution (Overclock), voluntary commitment (cliff rescue). By the third fall, she is not being swept away — she is the one who leaps.

## Yangyang Character Story V: "Answer Lies in the Winds"

On her journey away from home, Yangyang met two kinds of people.

The first always seemed in such haste, yet remained unsure where they were heading, and often ended up lost. The other always had a clear destination in mind. They made it, despite arduous roads and occasional stumbles.

Yangyang held up her head and looked at Jinzhou on the horizon, unsure if she could be considered the second kind.

She thought, again, of the term: **"One Instrument for Life."**

*"One Instrument for Life"* is the most important principle in her family's teachings. Originally, it meant that every family member could strive to excel in everything, but must choose **one musical instrument** to specialize in throughout their life — to become a master. As circumstances changed, the principle evolved into something broader: **dedicating one's entire life to one pursuit**, striving for excellence, to make this life worthwhile.

Her mother's Instrument was **composing** — Yangyang had seen people dancing to her mother's songs. Her sister was already a successful figure in the **chamber of commerce**. But Yangyang could not easily decide on her own Instrument as they once had. What was the thing worthy of dedicating her life to? What person should she be?

She turned to her mother:

*"Mother, do you want me to inherit the family business? Or, what do you want me to do? I can try anything and I will strive for excellence. Although life is full of infinite possibilities, in the end, we can only choose one path, right?"*

Her mother looked at her with love:

*"My child, it is precisely because of this that you should not seek answers from me, but rather ask your own heart. This is a difficult question, but you need not rush an answer."*

Yangyang held onto her question, waiting patiently. She waited through her return from the desolate settlement; through climbing out of an abyss; through growing weary of her family's overprotectiveness. Her desire to join the world and experience its harshness grew stronger. She no longer wanted to be a leaf drifting in the wind. She longed to bear the weight of reality, instead of hiding away from it.

Then a sudden ambush in the wilderness resulted in heavy casualties from Tacet Discord attacks on the frontline. News spread through the city. People gathered around a bonfire — tears, hoarse cries, grief, despair. But by morning, grief became a luxury as life returned to normal.

Yangyang stood among them, tears streaming down her face. She had not experienced personal loss — but witnessing their sorrow, and the joy of the rescued children, stirred something inside her. She realized her true calling: **to protect others, just as she had been protected before**. To shield them from misfortune. To give them hope for a brighter future.

On her enrollment night as an Outrider, she stood at the edge of Jinzhou and gazed at the sky and the bustling residents below. Doubt crept in — could she really protect these lively citizens?

The Streams came from afar and gently held her in their embrace, dispelling her last bits of doubt.

They silently whispered: *Jinzhou is the place. It is the destination for Yangyang the wealthy daughter, and the beginning of her journey as Yangyang the Outrider.*

This story is the conclusion of the series — it names the Instrument. The governing principle of Yangyang's family, which she spent years trying to apply correctly, finds its subject not in music or commerce or scholarship, but in the act of protection. The wind told her so, and she believed it.

## Yangyang: Cherished Items

**Striped Ribbon (Mother's Farewell Gift)**
*"Yangyang's blue beret always has a delicate ribbon attached to its brim. It's her signature look, and a precious gift from her mother before she embarked on her journey. To Yangyang, it symbolizes her mother's blessing and marks the start of her own journey. She holds onto the resolution she made when putting on the ribbon, determined to see it through until the end."*
The ribbon is the most externally visible of her cherished items — she wears it on her beret every day. The resolution she made when putting it on (undescribed in the item text, but implied by the final sentence) is what she is determined to see through. The ribbon is not a keepsake of nostalgia but a commitment device: a daily reminder of who she decided to be the morning she left home.

**Sound Recorder (Baizhi's Modification)**
*"As Yangyang grew accustomed to the constant presence of Streams, she began to contemplate capturing the fleeting sound of the wind. With the help of Baizhi's modifications and innovative energy conversion technology, a device was created to store the Streams as visual and tangible information. Whether it be the gentle sound of snow falling on a mountain, an untold story, or a poignant moment that brings tears to one's eyes, Yangyang now had the ability to record these realities that her senses were attuned to."*
The Sound Recorder transforms Yangyang's private sensory experience into something communicable. She has been perceiving things — snow on a mountain, untold stories, poignant moments — that no one else around her can access. The device makes those perceptions documentable, shareable, and preserved. It is also evidence of a collaboration: Baizhi built the modification. Yangyang's relationship to her Streams is no longer entirely solitary.

**Wind Chimes — "All in One"**
*"Yangyang has been crafting wind chimes since she was a child, using polished jade crystals in the shape of birds as plectrums. Starting with just a few, she has now collected and gifted dozens of them to her loved ones. Each one is different, with intricate designs that evolve over time as Yangyang adds new plectrums and adjusts their placement. She sees it as a never-ending journey, with endless possibilities for interpretation. Despite not knowing what kind of melody the wind chimes will eventually create, Yangyang stays true to her original intention — a lifelong pursuit of perfecting her craft, just like her family's tradition of 'One Instrument for Life.'"*
The wind chimes are perhaps the most quietly significant of her cherished items because they are the only place where she has found her own version of "One Instrument for Life" separate from her Outrider vocation. She makes chimes; she gives them to loved ones; she does not know what melody they will eventually make; she keeps making them anyway. The jade bird plectrums are visually consistent with the feather/bird motif that runs through her entire character design. The chimes she has given away are still playing somewhere, in winds she may or may not be near.

## Yangyang: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite — Homemade Wuthercake (Mother's)**
*"Wuthercake is a national dish in Huanglong, though how it is made varies depending on the region. They make delicious Wuthercakes here in Jinzhou, but for me, homemade Wuthercakes are always the best. When we have the chance, would you come home with me, and have a taste of my mother's Wuthercakes?"*
The regional variation of the dish is Yangyang's taxonomic frame: she can evaluate the Jinzhou version (delicious) against the standard of her mother's version (better). The invitation to Rover — to come home and taste them — is the warmest and most open-ended invitation she extends in any voice line. Home is still there. Her mother is still making them.

**Disliked — Nothing Committed To**
*"Shouldn't we cherish all food and not let any go to waste? Oh... Well, if it's truly badly cooked, then.. uhm..."*
Yangyang's answer to the disliked food question is characteristically hers: she redirects from preference to principle (waste is wrong), then encounters the implied follow-up question (but what if it's just bad?) and trails off. She does not name a food. The voice line is unresolved in exactly the way the question caught her off guard, which is the most candid and endearing version of her available.

## Yangyang: Ideals

**Passion — Being the One Who Guards and Gives**
The ideals voice line — *"I must be one that guards and gives"* — is the compressed form of the fifth character story. She does not want to rely exclusively on the protection others provide her; she wants to provide it. The Outrider position is not a job she fell into — it is the Instrument she chose after years of waiting for an answer, and the commitment she made to it on her enrollment night at the edge of Jinzhou.

**Resonance — The Lesson That Kindness Compounds**
The frontier grandmother's words — *"How will good things come true if you don't even believe them yourself?"* — are not a platitude to Yangyang; they are an empirical argument with observed evidence. She was shown kindness by suspicious strangers when she had nothing to offer them in return. She chose to believe them, and has been proving them right by extending the same kindness outward ever since. She is not naive about this; betrayal can turn her warm breeze into a harsh storm. But the default is trust, and the default is maintained deliberately.

**Gratitude — For Being Received Before She Was Ready**
Yangyang's deepest expression of gratitude in available sources is not verbal but structural: she became an Outrider specifically because of what was done for her before she had anything to offer. The settlers who gave food to a frightened child in fine clothes; her mother who refused to answer her question and told her to find her own answer; the old woman who told her faith is what makes good things real; and eventually the Streams themselves that confirmed her choice on her enrollment night. These are not people or things she effusively thanks — they are the architecture of the person she became.

**Perseverance — One Hundred Repetitions at a Time**
The first character story is the blueprint: she was not gifted in swordsmanship; she practiced 100 times a day for two months; she reached the top of the class; and immediately asked what she could do better next time. This is not the perseverance of someone grinding through exhaustion — it is the perseverance of someone who has genuinely accepted that good outcomes require consistent effort and who does not find this unfair or discouraging. It is simply the condition of things.
