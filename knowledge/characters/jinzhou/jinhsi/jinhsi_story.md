## Jinhsi Official Introduction

*"Through repeated ages, the Lament has preyed upon mankind's vulnerable hope. Yet, grasping the nettle, she strives with all her might to forge a path toward tomorrow."*

Jinhsi, Magistrate of Jinzhou, is described by official sources as one who *"gently brightens the hopes of her people, like rays of winter sunlight."* As the revered Sentinel's Appointed Resonator, she displays humility and wholeheartedly commits herself to guiding her people toward a brilliant future.

Her formal introduction reads: *"Being the Magistrate of Jinzhou, Jinhsi bears her noble and weighty share of duties."* She met Rover in the main story of Version 1.0 and was confirmed as a playable character in Version 1.1 under the banner title "Thawborn Renewal."

## Jinhsi Personality

Jinhsi's most defining characteristic is the alignment of her identity with her duty — she does not experience her role as Magistrate and Sentinel's Resonator as constraints but as the truest expression of who she is. This is unusual; most people who inherit great responsibility exhibit at least some private resistance. Jinhsi seems to have internalized her purpose completely.

She is perceptive beyond what her youth might suggest. Rather than reading her people's skepticism about her appointment as a personal rejection, she understood it as symptom of accumulated exhaustion — the spiritual fatigue of a populace that had endured the Lament's cycles too many times. Her response was not a show of power but patient, visible presence: attending community events, listening to demands firsthand, making herself findable.

There is also something quietly practical about Jinhsi. She recognized that digital tools could help her work faster without sacrificing personal connection, and arranged for Huaxu Academy to build her an on-the-go document management system. She is, in short, an effective administrator who understands that hope is a resource, not an abstraction.

## Jinhsi Character Story I: "The Nightmare and the Scale"

*"Jinhsi had frequent nightmares in her childhood. Once, she was drowning in her dream, caught in the surf and about to sink to the bottom of the ocean. A piece of driftwood floated by, and she clung to it, returning to shore against the tide. When she woke up, what she was holding in her hand was a piece of scale."*

This story establishes the earliest evidence of Jinhsi's bond with Sentinel Jué predating any formal awakening. The scale she clutched on waking bore resemblance to those of the Sentinel — implying a connection that operated through her unconscious mind before it became controllable or conscious ability.

The dream itself is worth examining: drowning, driftwood, surviving by clinging to an unexpected lifeline. These are not the dreams of someone who feels protected; they are the dreams of someone learning, even in sleep, that survival requires effort and a willingness to grasp at what is offered. Jinhsi's future role as a hope-bearer for Jinzhou is foreshadowed in this image — she would herself become the driftwood others clung to.

## Jinhsi Character Story II: "The Weight of the People's Weariness"

*"Taking on this great responsibility, Jinhsi felt determined to guide Jinzhou towards a better future, despite her initial challenges. She also understood that trust had to be earned through hard work and dedication."*

When Jinhsi assumed the Magistrate's office as the Sentinel's Appointed Resonator, she encountered skepticism — not malice, but the tired hesitation of people who had been disappointed before. Rather than being wounded by it, she looked past the skepticism to what lay beneath: *"the weariness etched on the faces of the people — a weariness born of life's hardships and a lack of hope."*

Her insight was that material solutions alone could not restore Jinzhou. Beyond food, safety, and infrastructure, the city needed a **reliable source of hope** — someone whose presence itself communicated that tomorrow was worth caring about. Jinhsi identified this as her core challenge and accepted it without self-pity.

This story illustrates her governing philosophy: leadership as the cultivation of collective will, not merely the administration of collective needs.

## Jinhsi Character Story III: "The Sentinel Granting Wishes"

During a reconstruction event in Jinzhou, the young Magistrate performed a public act known as the *"Sentinel Granting Wishes"* — a "miracle" staged with deliberate intention. The exact nature of this event is not fully specified in available records, but it was designed to serve as a visible, memorable demonstration of hope and divine favor, channeling Jinhsi's connection to Sentinel Jué into something her people could witness.

The act was not deception — Jinhsi's Resonance Ability genuinely allows her to project holographic likenesses of Jué with Sentinel-scale power. But the framing as a "miracle" and a "wish-granting" ritual speaks to her understanding of the symbolic dimension of leadership: people needed not just proof that the Sentinel existed, but proof that the Sentinel cared, and that Jinhsi herself was a trustworthy vessel of that care.

The event represents the convergence of Jinhsi's practical governance and her role as Sentinel's Resonator — using divine connection not for personal power, but as a tool of civic restoration.

## Jinhsi: Cherished Items

**Loong Buns (Homemade)**
- A plate of Loong Buns made by Jinhsi herself, each adopting a pink hue with a sprinkling of osmanthus powdered sugar
- The act of making them personally — amid an administrative schedule described as perpetually overwhelming — is significant. It suggests that cooking is a form of private restoration for Jinhsi, a way of grounding herself in the tactile and small-scale after endless large-scale concerns
- The Loong shape directly references Sentinel Jué, embedding her bond with the Sentinel even in domestic, personal gestures

**Fragment of Sentinel Scale**
- Implied through her childhood story: the scale she held on waking from her drowning nightmare
- A physical artifact of her unconscious bond with Jué; never formally catalogued as a "cherished item" in available sources, but narratively functions as her most intimate connection to her destiny

## Jinhsi: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite:** Loong Buns (self-made, as noted above). The osmanthus powdered sugar and pink hue suggest a preference for flavors that are delicate and slightly sweet — consistent with a character who finds warmth in small comforts.

**Disliked:** No officially confirmed disliked food is recorded in available sources. Her perpetual administrative overload suggests she may eat more out of necessity than preference during working hours, reserving genuine appreciation of food for moments of deliberate rest.

## Jinhsi: Ideals

**Passion — Duty as Devotion**
Jinhsi does not experience the Magistrate's office as an external obligation. Her dedication reads as intrinsic — she would pursue Jinzhou's welfare even if stripped of her title. The passion driving her is not ambition but attachment: she genuinely loves the city and its people.

**Resonance — The Bond with Jué**
Her relationship with the Sentinel is more than a power source. It is a formative identity — she was bonded to Jué before she could understand what that meant, and her sense of self has been shaped by that bond ever since. She treats the Sentinel's charge not as a burden but as a trust.

**Gratitude — Toward Her People**
Despite being in a position of authority, Jinhsi expresses something like gratitude toward the citizens of Jinzhou — for allowing her to serve them, for the faith they extend even when worn thin. Her practice of attending community events in person rather than delegating reflects a genuine sense that proximity to ordinary people is not a duty but a gift.

**Perseverance — Against the Lament's Cycles**
The Lament is not a singular crisis — it is a repeating pattern that has preyed upon humanity's hope across ages. Jinhsi's official introduction frames her as someone who *"grasps the nettle"* rather than yielding. Her perseverance is not heroic posturing but quiet, sustained refusal to accept that hope is too fragile to maintain.
