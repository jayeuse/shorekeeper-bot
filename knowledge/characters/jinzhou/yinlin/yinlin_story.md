## Yinlin Official Introduction

*"Previously known as an outstanding Jinzhou Patroller, Yinlin is steady and reliable, yet harbors hidden depths of secrets. She excels at exploiting resources at her disposal to uncover lurking crimes. Beneath her aloof and flamboyant exterior, Yinlin possesses a heart of gold, reserved only for those she deems trustworthy."*

Her Forte archive designation is **Enforcer Puppet**. Her self-introduction, given when asked directly: *"Name's Yinlin. As for what I do... Shhh, we don't talk about that in public."*

Her ideals statement, offered as a candid declaration of her worldview: *"A utopia free of all vices and crimes may sound tempting, but once you think about it, it is nothing more than a naïve dream. That's why we exist — to uncover the hidden and unapproachable darkness that the majority refuse to acknowledge, and do the dirty work in their place."*

## Yinlin Personality

Yinlin is a person who has been defined, from infancy, by what she is not allowed to be. She cannot have her real parents' names — she did not have her own name until a man in an ugly mask gave her one while carrying her out of a burning building. She cannot disclose her identity on duty. She cannot, as a suspended agent operating outside sanctioned channels, even claim the institutional backing she spent years earning. Every layer of what might constitute a stable identity — family name, career title, operational affiliation — has been removed or suspended at some point. What remains is Yinlin, and Yinlin is a great deal.

The composure she projects in the field is not the composure of someone who has not encountered disorder. It is the composure of someone who has encountered so much of it that her ability to remain calm within it is a skill, not a condition. When she wraps lightning threads around a criminal's arm in a dim warehouse, she is not performing menace — she is applying a precisely calibrated tool to a specific problem, and the measured calm is part of the precision. The moment he feels the paralysis is the moment she has already resolved the situation.

Her voice lines reveal someone who is genuinely interested in the people she encounters, genuinely observant of human psychology — *"many crimes are motivated by love and responsibility. Ironic, isn't it? The two fundamental values of human society can also lead to its downfall"* — and genuinely capable of warmth when she trusts the recipient. The warmth is reserved. When she decides someone earns it, she gives it without performance.

The Rover represents something unusual in Yinlin's operating framework: someone she can simply be herself around, without a persona layered over the top. This is not something she offers lightly — the line *"perhaps I can just be myself, Yinlin... and no one else. It doesn't feel awful... to have someone who knows and remembers me for who I truly am"* is among the most unguarded things she says in any source, and the careful hedging around it ("perhaps," "doesn't feel awful") marks it as a concession wrested from someone who does not make concessions easily.

## Yinlin Forte Examination Report

*Evaluation Basis: [Resonance Assessment 2917-G]*
*Forte Power Designation: Enforcer Puppet*

Resonator Yinlin's Tacet Mark is located on the exterior side of her left thigh. No significant physical changes have been observed in Yinlin's body after her Awakening. A mild electrical current has been detected on the surface of her skin, but it has not resulted in any detectable adverse effects on her body.

Yinlin possesses the power to converge electric currents into thin threads. Her manipulation of these threads is extraordinarily proficient. Her Resonance Spectrum Pattern is found to be similar to the Spectrum Pattern of known conductive materials. Tests have found strong Syntony.

Analysis of test samples has revealed a non-convergent Rabelle's Curve with a noticeable periodic waveform, classifying Yinlin as a **Congenital Resonator**.

**SIDENOTES:** Yinlin has a **preexisting arrhythmia** that may or may not be related to her Forte. Blocking of her Forte could potentially increase the risk of atrial fibrillation.

The waveform graph shows zigzag fluctuations. The Time Domain pattern is irregular. Both upper and lower peak values are high and approach Criticality level. The waveform displays segments of partial fuzziness and an abnormal waveform is present. **Resonant Criticality: Relatively low, with low stability. Risk of Overclocking remains.** Resonator Yinlin has no recorded history of Overclocking. Her body shows physical strength but also signs of **prolonged Overclock-inducing item exposure** — flagged for further monitoring.

Resonator Yinlin is advised to avoid strenuous activity and erratic schedules. The combination of arrhythmia, abnormal waveform, and evidence of Overclock-inducing item exposure in a character with no Overclocking history makes Yinlin's Forte profile one of the more medically complex in the evaluation records. Story IV reveals exactly how her arrhythmia and Forte interact under extreme combat conditions.

## Yinlin Character Story I: "Scarlet in the Shadows"

The Exile Leader stabbed a dagger into a broken table in a dim warehouse. His organization had a plan: a convoy would depart Jinzhou that night with valuable cargo, and they had spread rumors to prevent the shipper from hiring Lollo Logistics. The shipper had instead engaged a group of Resonators as bodyguards. The Leader wasn't worried. He had a new recruit — a woman with striking scarlet hair sitting in the corner with a subtle grin on her face.

One of his men, Ming, reached for her arm to test her. His fingers froze before they made contact. Lightning threads enveloped him, paralyzing without touching.

The Exile Leader waved it off. She brushed her hair and the threads vanished. *"Leave him be, for my sake. All he has left is his sharp tongue. Save your games for later."*

Later that night, Ming lay on the ground, stunned and immobilized. The convoy passed safely in the distance. Through the fog of the paralysis, he understood — the feeling from the warehouse, repeated. *She was the mole.* He tried to warn his team. A horrifying puppet loomed over him. He screamed. A flash of lightning drowned out the sound.

This story is told without naming Yinlin explicitly. It does not need to. The scarlet-haired woman who paralyzes a criminal through the air before he can touch her arm, redirects his aggression into a tactical position, and disables the entire operation while maintaining her cover long enough to ensure the convoy's safe passage is Yinlin operating in ideal conditions — unhurried, patient, already holding the outcome before anyone else in the room realized the game had started.

## Yinlin Character Story II: "The Puppet Show"

A man in a hooded cloak moved through Jinzhou's streets with a frightened boy in tow — his organization had taken the boy hostage to leverage his crime-boss grandfather. The grandfather had not reported the kidnapping to the Patrollers, likely because he was an outlaw himself.

At the city gate approach, the cloaked man's path was blocked by a crowd gathered around a street performance: a woman with long scarlet hair running a puppet show, her flying puppets so lifelike that children and parents alike could not look away. The man waited for the performance to end.

*"Alright everyone, who wants to join me for the next show? Raise your hand!"*

Nearly every child's hand went up.

*"Yes, you in the back with your father. Come on down!"*

Every eye turned to the cloaked man and the frightened boy. The man hesitated but could not refuse without drawing attention he could not afford. He approached. The boy slipped free of his grasp in the confusion. The man reached to grab him back — and grabbed a puppet instead. An electric current knocked him unconscious.

Later, at the crime boss's mansion, the boy was returned by the scarlet-haired woman — Yinlin — with the kidnapper bound by her puppet. The old man was simultaneously relieved about his grandson and quietly appalled at the implication:

*"I'm only after them. They happen to be your enemy. I'm just using you. But your grandson is innocent."*

She scanned the assembled gangsters and smiled.

*"Or do you really believe you can thwart me, the infamous mole, with your little crew?"*

He signaled his men to stand down. She walked out with graceful poise. He watched her go and laughed bitterly.

*"She's always in and out as if she knew I won't stop her, talking about we aren't the target 'yet'... Sounds like I'm never off the hook."*

This story is the fullest portrait of Yinlin's operational style available in character sources: the puppet show as genuine performance and intelligence cover simultaneously; the rescue of an innocent as a side objective that does not compromise the primary mission; the confrontation with an outlaw boss navigated through a reading of his psychology accurate enough to predict his decision before he made it. She knew he would stand down. She walked out because she had already calculated that he would.

## Yinlin Character Story III: "Names Born From Ashes"

*"I regret to inform you that 'Sparrow' and 'Canary' have been confirmed deceased. Their identities were compromised and the criminal organization took swift action."*

The young man — a subordinate of his mentor's operation within the Public Security Bureau — received this news while holding a baby in the crook of his arm. He was already suspended. The colleague informing him mentioned that the baby was also a Resonator, complicating matters further. She would be in his care. Investigators would come to the house the next day.

He told them he would pack tonight and find a new place to move. *"Of course. Feel free to use this opportunity for a fresh start."* He noted the suspicious glint in the Patroller's eyes and understood what the investigation would actually be.

That night, in the roaring flames, the young man moved through the burning building with the baby calm and blank-faced in his arms. He had created puppet doubles of them both using his Forte — decoys that appeared to be sleeping, buying time for the escape through a hidden passage. As his pursuers checked the puppets, he lowered his hood at the appearance of a stranger passing in the dark, waited for the stranger's report to be made, and then walked away through the smoke.

He took out a mask from his pocket. It was ugly. He put it on. He shielded the baby's eyes with his hand and they slipped away from the flames without looking back.

*"From this moment on... I will go by 'Dollmaker', and you shall answer to 'Yinlin' — a name your parents had long chosen for you."*

This is the foundational story of Yinlin's existence. Her parents named her before they died, which means the name she carries is the one they intended — the continuity of identity runs through the name even when everything around the name was destroyed and replaced. Dollmaker gave her the name, but the name was already hers. This detail, small as it is, is the thread connecting her to parents she never knew.

## Yinlin Character Story IV: "A Corner in Darkness"

Yinlin struggled to stand using a nearby table, grimacing through pain in her side. A figure emerged from the shadows, pulling blades from his own body and tossing them aside. He had the advantage and he knew it.

*"I don't know why you risked everything to infiltrate our organization just for some records on two dead Patrollers... But it appears that I have emerged victorious."*

She summoned a lightning whip and hurled it at him, but the pain caused her aim to falter. It landed meters away.

*"My Forte can induce arrhythmia. As long as you're a human, there's no way you can avoid it."*

The Tacet Mark on his arm lit up again. Yinlin collapsed. Her heart stopped.

He spoke as she lay motionless — recognizing her as a Secret Agent, taunting the futility of the mission, dismissing what she had found. He crouched with his dagger.

*"Time to finish you off. What a pity. You should have known not to mess with the Fractsidus..."*

Before the dagger struck, a surge of lightning ran through it, paralyzing him in place.

*"How... How did you..."*

As he lost consciousness, he saw it: **flickering lightning within Yinlin's chest**. She had given herself an electric shock to restart her own heart.

Too drained to move her body, she directed Zapstring with hand gestures alone — advancing the puppet toward him, retrieving documents from his pockets. She read what she had found.

*"The Fractsidus... It's time to uproot this hidden darkness."*

This story answers the Forte Examination Report's most unsettling notation — the preexisting arrhythmia, the risk of blocking her Forte increasing atrial fibrillation risk — with what those clinical facts actually mean in practice. Her arrhythmia and her Forte are so entangled that one can kill her (an enemy using a cardiac-disrupting Forte) and the other can resurrect her (self-administered electric shock). She has turned a medical vulnerability into a survival tool. The story also formally names the Fractsidus as her primary target and implies that the "records on two dead Patrollers" she risked everything to recover are the documentation of her parents' deaths — the evidence of who ordered the compromise that killed "Sparrow" and "Canary."

## Yinlin Character Story V: "Farewell to the Past"

*"Master Dollmaker, I want to join the Public Security Bureau."*

Yinlin was an adult now. Dollmaker received the news without surprise. The elderly woman beside him — Lady Guan, who had been her surrogate grandmother in the Séance Society — wore a worried expression.

*"Miss Yinlin, you are like my own granddaughter. I don't want to see you put yourself in danger. The Public Security Bureau is not a place for us."*

*"Don't worry, no one knows who I really am. To them, I'm just an ordinary citizen. Master Dollmaker will create a fake identity for me."*

*"Maybe, but I don't want to lose my family again... Please stay here at the Séance Society. Don't go anywhere else."*

Dollmaker closed his notebook and gestured for Lady Guan to stop. His reasoning was clinical:

*"Go for it, Yinlin. My research has reached a critical point and conflicts with the Jinzhou Public Security Bureau are inevitable. To have insiders among their high-ranking officials should prove beneficial to us all."*

Yinlin corrected his framing:

*"No, I want to become a Secret Agent... just like my parents. I want to carry on their ideals in this way."*

She could not see behind Dollmaker's mask — he had never removed it in her presence, as far back as she could remember. But she caught a glimpse of complex emotions in his eyes. Something rare.

After a long pause, he nodded. *"Before I bring your parents back with the puppets, you have the freedom to do whatever you please... Once my plan succeeds, we will seek revenge on the Public Security Bureau and eliminate all those who betrayed them in one swift strike!"*

Yinlin started to respond. He had already turned back to the puppet he was working on. She nodded silently and left, knowing their paths had diverged.

This story establishes the full complexity of Yinlin's motivation and the tension within her closest relationships. She is not joining the Bureau for Dollmaker's reasons (institutional access for his research). She is not staying for Lady Guan's reasons (the safety of the family they have constructed). She is joining for her own reasons — to carry on her parents' ideals in the form they chose. The departure is quiet and mutual, and the distance it creates between her and Dollmaker is left open. His plan to "bring her parents back with the puppets" and "eliminate all those who betrayed them" is a plan she neither endorses nor confronts. She simply leaves it behind as she walks out the door.

## Yinlin: Cherished Items

**Zapstring (Puppet)**
*"Once a simple puppet, now a fierce weapon thanks to Yinlin's Resonance abilities. She may deny it, but her love for the doll is evident in every detail, despite its wear and tear from years of battle. It was once just a practice piece for a doll maker, but now it serves as Yinlin's trusted ally on the battlefield."*
Dollmaker was the one who taught Yinlin puppet manipulation — originally to help her control her Forte, as the electric threading that characterizes her Resonance translates directly into puppet control. Zapstring began as a novice doll maker's practice piece, acquired or gifted at some point in Yinlin's time with Dollmaker, and has been thoroughly repurposed into a combat and intelligence tool. Its wear and tear is the evidence of years of use in real operations. Yinlin does not discuss sentimentality attached to it, which is consistent with her operational persona and inconsistent with the care she has demonstrably taken with it.

**Book on Qipao (Secret Codebook)**
*"Yinlin's qipao may seem to be merely a fashion choice, but it actually serves as a secret codebook for communicating with her liaison. This book on qipao designs is constantly changing and filled with encrypted images and symbols, making it challenging even for experienced liaisons to use."*
The qipao itself contains hidden weaponry and enables unrestricted movement; the codebook embedded in its patterns is the operational layer. The fact that the codebook changes constantly means Yinlin is maintaining active communication infrastructure even while operating without official Bureau backing. She still has a liaison. She is still running operations — just without institutional cover or sanction.

**Investigative Magnifying Glass**
*"This Investigative Magnifying Glass is given to new Patrollers by the Public Security Bureau. It's small but powerful, helping them find hidden clues. Yinlin, as a secret agent, doesn't use it often. But it still holds significance for her: It is a tribute to her late parents and a reminder of her true identity."*
The magnifying glass is the most emotionally significant of her cherished items and the most quietly held. It is the standard-issue tool given to new Patrollers — the institutional artifact that marks entry into the organization her parents served and died for. She does not use it in field operations (her methods do not require it). She keeps it as a connection to them, and as an anchor for who she actually is when every other identity layer has been stripped away: the daughter of "Sparrow" and "Canary," carrying on their work.

## Yinlin: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite — Aureate Fried Rice**
*"I've tried many so-called delicacies through my job, but none has left a lasting impression like the plate of Aureate Fried Rice I once had. I believe it's not the fancy ingredients that make a dish truly special, but the skill and care put into its preparation."*
The Aureate Fried Rice preference is characteristically Yinlin: she has eaten at high-end establishments as a professional necessity, and the meal that stayed with her is neither the most expensive nor the most elaborate. It is the one made with skill and care. The evaluation criterion is the same one she applies to people.

**Disliked — None confirmed; operational accommodation**
*"Dining with a target requires always catering to their taste. When the food is subpar, I know how to choke it down with a grin. That's why there isn't any dish I truly dislike... It's not the food that ruins a meal, but the company. With you by my side, I suppose I'd be pleased to eat anything."*
This answer, offered to Rover, reframes the concept of food preference as a function of context rather than content. She has trained herself out of having a disliked food because having one is an operational liability. The final sentence is the rare unguarded Yinlin — the company changes the meal.

## Yinlin: Ideals

**Passion — The Dirty Work**
Yinlin's governing ideal is the one she states plainly: someone must uncover the darkness that the majority refuse to acknowledge and do the dirty work in their place. This is not a reluctant duty for her — it is the framework that gives the mission meaning. The darkness is real and persistent; her role is to be the person who goes into it. She does this without requiring recognition, without institutional sanction after her suspension, and without expecting the people she protects to understand what was done on their behalf.

**Resonance — Inheritance Made Action**
The resonance between Yinlin and her deceased parents runs through every decision she has made since she was old enough to make them. She does not know their faces, their voices, or the texture of their personalities. She knows their code names and their mission, and she has made both her own. Joining the Bureau as a Secret Agent was the only way to be with them — not as memory, but as continuation. The investigative magnifying glass she carries but does not use is the physical emblem of that resonance.

**Gratitude — For the Name**
Dollmaker gave Yinlin many things: survival, shelter, training, identity documents. The name he gave her was not his to create — her parents had chosen it before they died. His act of giving it to her was the one gift that was genuinely theirs. She carries gratitude for it without sentimentalizing it; she left his path when it diverged from hers, and she did not apologize for that.

**Perseverance — Operating Without a Net**
Suspended from the Bureau that is her institutional home, running operations without official backing or legal protection, investigating an organization powerful enough to have infiltrated the Patrollers, and having been clinically dead in the middle of one of those operations — Yinlin persists. The voice line *"no rush. The impatient ones will fall right into our lap"* is not patience as resignation. It is patience as operational discipline: she will still be there when the target makes their mistake, and she is in no hurry to be anywhere else.

## Yinlin: Narrative Chronicle

- **[Before Memory — Birth and Parental Loss]** Yinlin is born to two Secret Agents of the Jinzhou Public Security Bureau, code-named "Sparrow" (father) and "Canary" (mother); her parents chose her name before their deaths; their identities are compromised by an unknown criminal organization (linked to the Fractsidus or its allies); both are killed; the infant Yinlin is an Electro Congenital Resonator
- **[Infancy — The Burning Safehouse]** A young subordinate of the Patroller mentor who mentored Yinlin's parents receives news of their deaths while holding the infant Yinlin; investigators arrive the next day; he creates puppet decoys to simulate both of them sleeping, escapes through a hidden passage during the building fire, and adopts the name "Dollmaker"; he gives Yinlin the name her parents had chosen for her
- **[Childhood — Raised by the Séance Society]** Yinlin grows up under Dollmaker's care and the Séance Society's community; Lady Guan functions as a surrogate grandmother; Dollmaker teaches her puppet manipulation to help her control her Forte; Zapstring (originally a novice puppet maker's practice piece) enters her life during this period; Yinlin is classified as a Congenital Resonator with preexisting arrhythmia possibly related to her Forte
- **[Youth — Decision to Join the Bureau]** Yinlin informs Dollmaker and Lady Guan of her intention to join the Public Security Bureau as a Secret Agent; she frames it as carrying on her parents' ideals; Dollmaker approves for his own strategic reasons (institutional access); Lady Guan pleads for her to stay; Yinlin departs, knowing that their paths have now diverged and that she and Dollmaker will eventually be on opposite sides
- **[Active Service — Patroller and Secret Agent]** Yinlin enters the Bureau using a fake identity Dollmaker creates for her; she excels as a Patroller and is identified as outstanding in that role; she advances to Secret Agent — the same classification her parents held; she gains access to criminal intelligence networks throughout Jinzhou
- **[Active Service — The Exile Operation]** Yinlin infiltrates an Exile Leader's criminal organization; she builds trust, identifies the convoy operation plan, enables the convoy's safe passage by paralyzing the criminal team from within, and exposes herself only at the moment the operation is concluded (Story I)
- **[Active Service — The Kidnapping Case]** Yinlin runs a street puppet show as operational cover, identifies a child being smuggled out of Jinzhou by a hooded criminal, extracts the boy during the performance using a puppet decoy, returns him to his grandfather (a crime boss), and identifies the grandfather as a future target while using the current operation as a demonstration of her reach (Story II)
- **[Suspension — Public Security Bureau]** Yinlin is suspended from active Patroller duties; the circumstances of the suspension are not elaborated in available sources; she is barred from disclosing her identity or operating under Bureau sanction; she continues anyway
- **[Covert Operations — Fractsidus Investigation]** Operating without institutional backing, Yinlin infiltrates a Fractsidus-connected organization to retrieve records on two deceased Patrollers — her parents; she is identified as a mole by a Fractsidus operative whose Forte can induce arrhythmia; the operative stops her heart; Yinlin uses a self-administered electric shock to restart it; she extracts documents using Zapstring while physically incapacitated (Story IV); the documents confirm the Fractsidus's scope
- **[Version 1.1 — Main Story Involvement]** Rover encounters Yinlin in Jinzhou; she is investigating the Séance Society connection to the Version 1.0–1.1 main story arc, targeting the organization from the outside as it interacts with the Lament crisis; she becomes playable (Event Convene: Solitary Path)
- **[Ongoing]** Yinlin continues covert operations against the Fractsidus, pursuing the organization responsible for her parents' deaths without institutional cover, a partner, or any guarantee that the investigation will be sanctioned if successful
