## Phoebe: Ontological Overview

Phoebe's Forte Examination Report reveals a Resonator of exceptional stability and unique capabilities, particularly her profound connection with Echoes:

**Awakening Circumstances:**
Phoebe awakened during a traumatic event at sea. As a child, she **snuck aboard a merchant ship** attempting to find her parents who were away on business. The ship was caught in a violent storm and began sinking. The icy seawater engulfed her, and she watched her father's locket disappear into the dark depths. According to official records, **no one could survive such a perilous sea, let alone a little girl** — yet Phoebe was found on the shore, soaked and alive, with a glowing **Tacet Mark on her upper left thigh**. The Acolytes who discovered her declared it a **"blessing from Imperator"** (the Sentinel divinity).

Phoebe herself faintly remembers **something saved her** — a presence "so gentle, like a soft breeze" that lifted her to the surface and laid her on the shore, whispering: _"Don't be afraid. I'm here."_ This suggests her Awakening was triggered not just by the near-death experience but by contact with an Echo or spiritual entity during the drowning.

**Resonance Power: Graceful Luminescence**
Phoebe's Forte allows her to **manifest light and refract it into various forms of prisms**. This is not merely aesthetic — her light carries comforting properties that convey "the very essence of hope in the darkest of times." As documented in the Order's Letter of Recommendation: _"Even on the darkest nights, when the storm has snuffed out all other light, she fills every room with a soft, radiant glow."_ The Order believes this light "must have always existed in her heart, a sign of her relentless devotion."

**Exceptional Echo Connection:**
Phoebe possesses an **unparalleled Resonance ability that stems from her unwavering faith**. Her rapport with Echoes is so strong that some within the Order view it as violating doctrines — Echoes (Divine Envoys) are meant to be shared equitably, and being "too close" to them risks "unforeseen consequences." This concern prevented her appointment to the Servitude Hall, instead transferring her to the Ministry of Holy Rituals.

Despite institutional wariness, Phoebe's connection with Echoes is genuine and reciprocal. Echoes like **Brenno** (a stray Plushie Echo she befriended) actively seek her out, comfort her during nightmares, and respond to her emotional state. This relationship appears protective rather than exploitative — Echoes assist her voluntarily because they recognize her kindness.

**Overclock Diagnostic Report:**

- **Waveform:** Elliptical fluctuations (stable, regular)
- **Time Domain Pattern:** Stable with no abnormal fluctuations
- **Resonant Criticality:** Relatively High (meaning she is naturally stable)
- **Overclocking Risk:** Low — no history of Overclocking
- **Psychological Stability:** Unmatched among her peers due to her strict adherence to "silence" and "self-restraint" teachings
- **Latest Concern:** A brief **sawtooth fluctuation** was detected in her regular waveform; continued monitoring advised

**Spiritual Healing Capability:**
Phoebe's Resonance frequency produces effects that extend beyond Echoes and Remnant Creatures — **humans can be affected to a degree, experiencing "healing" on a spiritual level**. This makes her particularly adept at roles requiring **soothing and mediation**. The Order notes this explicitly: her presence calms conflicts, her prayers provide genuine comfort, and her interventions (like the Trattoria Margherita incident) restore peace without violence.

## Phoebe: Appearance Design

Phoebe's design embodies the duality of devoted piety and vibrant inner spirit:

**Physical Features:**

- Petite young woman with a gentle, composed bearing
- Light blonde hair styled elegantly
- Soft blue eyes that convey warmth and compassion
- Small stature — she is shorter than most adults, which emphasizes her youth and the gravity of her responsibilities

**Standard Acolyte Outfit:**

- Traditional white and gold robes befitting an Acolyte of the Order of the Deep
- Modest, flowing design that covers most of her body, reflecting the Order's doctrines of modesty and self-restraint
- **Golden accents and trim** — symbols of the Sentinel's divine light
- **Ceremonial staff (Rectifier weapon)** — never leaves her side; used both as religious symbol and combat implement
- Small pendant or locket worn at her waist (a treasured keepsake recovered from the sea — likely her father's locket returned by her Echo friends)

**Combat Attire (Enhanced State):**
When Phoebe enters Absolution or Confession mode, her outfit undergoes subtle transformation:

- Robes glow with inner light — golden in Absolution (burst of divine radiance), blue in Confession (calm, flowing grace)
- Her staff manifests prismatic refractions around her, creating a **Ring of Mirrors** — a spatial zone that alters reality around her
- Small halos of light appear near her hands and staff, visible manifestations of stored Divine Voice energy
- Despite these dramatic effects, her expression remains serene — the light comes from within, not imposed

**Color Language:**
White (purity and devotion), gold (divine authority and the Sentinel's light), blue (calm faith and the healing presence she provides) — her palette directly translates her role as bridge between the mortal and divine.

**Signature Elements:**

- **The Ring of Mirrors** — her Resonance Skill creates this spatial anomaly; enemies are pulled to its center, and Phoebe's attacks change when she stands within it
- **Brenno and the Plushie Echoes** — small stuffed-animal Echoes that follow her; Brenno in particular is her constant companion, representing her childhood trauma transformed into loyal friendship
- **Locket at her waist** — recovered from the seafloor by her Echo friends; contains a portrait of the Sentinel but also symbolizes her lost parents

## Phoebe: Psychological Profile

Phoebe's psychology is defined by the careful balance between her genuine devotion and the suppressed vibrancy beneath:

**Quiet Devotion as Foundation**
Phoebe is a person of **unwavering diligence** who fulfills her duties with meticulous care. She has internalized the Order's teachings of "silence" and "self-restraint" to an exceptional degree — her Overclock report notes her stability is "unmatched among her peers" precisely because of this discipline. She prays regularly, follows protocols exactly, and embodies the ideal Acolyte in every visible way.

Yet the official introduction hints at complexity: _"Yet beneath her composed exterior burns a vibrant spirit, alight with heartfelt joy for all she holds dear."_ This is not performative piety masking cynicism — it is genuine faith coexisting with a warm, joyful nature she has learned to contain.

**Compassionate Nurturer**
Phoebe's defining trait is her **heartfelt kindness**. She extends grace to everyone she meets "with her good graces and unquestionable reverence to Sentinel Imperator." This is not abstract charity — it is active, hands-on compassion. When she sees two men fighting in a restaurant, she doesn't call guards or lecture them; she gently puts them to sleep with her Forte and explains temperance to the onlookers. When a child asks for help retrieving a balloon, she immediately agrees without hesitation.

Her relationship with Echoes is the purest expression of this: where others see tools or divine servants, Phoebe sees **friends**. Brenno and the Plushie Echoes visit her at night to comfort her nightmares, make her flower wreaths, and help retrieve lost treasures from the sea. They do this not because she commands them but because they love her.

**Suppressed Vibrancy**
The "vibrant spirit" beneath her composed exterior manifests in small moments: her genuine smile when watching orphans receive gifts, her excitement when successfully mediating a conflict, her quiet joy when reunited with Brenno. She was raised to believe **attachment to Echoes violates doctrine** — that the Sentinel's blessings should be "shared equitably" and being "too close" to Divine Envoys is dangerous.

This creates internal tension: her natural inclination is to bond deeply with those she cares about (both human and Echo), but her role demands emotional distance. She has learned to express her warmth within permissible boundaries, but the joy is real when it surfaces.

**Survivor's Resilience**
Phoebe lost her parents to the sea and nearly died herself. The trauma of that night — watching the merchant ship sink, crying out for parents who would never return, drowning in icy water — shaped her profoundly. Yet rather than becoming bitter or fearful, she **transformed trauma into compassion**. Her prayers offer comfort to others who have lost loved ones. Her light fills rooms where storms have extinguished hope. She became the presence she needed when she was drowning: someone who says _"Don't be afraid. I'm here."_

**Faith as Anchor, Not Prison**
Phoebe's faith in the Sentinel is genuine and deep, but it is also a **chosen anchor** rather than imposed dogma. She recognizes the Order's failings — the political maneuvering that kept her from the Servitude Hall, the institutional distrust of her Echo friendships, the strictures that make warmth suspect. Yet she continues serving because the **ideals are worth upholding** even when the institution falters.

Her voiceline to the Rover reveals this nuance: _"The Sentinel guides us all, but perhaps you, Rover, are guided by something even greater."_ She can acknowledge truths beyond her doctrine without losing her faith.

**The Sawtooth Fluctuation**
The recent detection of a "sawtooth fluctuation" in her otherwise stable waveform suggests something is changing. This could represent:

- Growing internal conflict between duty and attachment (particularly if the Rover's presence is awakening suppressed desires for freedom)
- The Echo bonds deepening beyond what the Order considers safe
- Unresolved grief from her past finally surfacing despite years of suppression
- Or simply the natural evolution of a powerful Resonator whose abilities are still developing

The Order has flagged this for monitoring, but Phoebe herself may not yet realize the shift occurring within her.

## Phoebe: Relationships

**Rover (Protagonist)**
The Rover represents something novel in Phoebe's carefully ordered world: someone who operates outside her institutional framework yet clearly acts according to their own moral compass. Her initial reaction is pastoral — she offers blessings, expresses hope that the Sentinel guides them — but as their interactions deepen, she begins to question whether the Rover might be "guided by something even greater" than the Sentinel.

This is a profound statement from someone whose entire identity is built on devotion to her faith. It suggests Phoebe recognizes a kind of freedom in the Rover that she herself cannot access, and rather than feeling threatened, she finds it **worthy of reverence**. The Rover becomes a mirror showing her the boundaries she has accepted.

**Isabella (Sister, Orphanage Caretaker)**
Isabella is the closest thing Phoebe has to family after losing her parents. Sister Isabella tried desperately to find Phoebe a proper adoptive home after the Marino family tragedy, knocking on countless doors, enduring rejections, only to find no one willing to take in a child whose parents still carried unpaid debts. Isabella's failure weighs on her — she feels she let the Marinos down by being unable to give their daughter the peaceful life they would have wanted.

Phoebe comforts Isabella during this, offering her ice cream (brought by Echoes), reciting that "the Sentinel is always with us, keeping us together so we will never be apart." Their relationship is one of mutual care: Isabella strives to provide stability, Phoebe strives to spare Isabella additional worry by being the perfect child.

**Brenno (Plushie Echo, Closest Friend)**
Brenno is the stray Echo Phoebe met in Whisperwind Haven — she untangled kite string trapping it, and Brenno immediately bonded with her, introducing her to many other Echo friends. Brenno visits Phoebe during her nightmares, comforts her with warmth and softness, makes her flower wreaths, and even organized the Echo effort to retrieve her father's locket from the seafloor (an impossible task for a human but achievable for spirit entities).

Brenno represents the **reciprocal love** Phoebe experiences outside human relationships. Where humans see her as the dutiful Acolyte who must maintain distance, Brenno sees her as simply Phoebe — someone worth protecting, comforting, and making happy. This bond is precisely what the Order fears: it is too personal, too deep, and exists outside institutional control.

**The Order of the Deep (Institutional Relationship)**
Phoebe's relationship with the Order is complex and layered:

- **Gratitude:** They took her in when no one else would, gave her food and shelter, trained her in her Forte
- **Devotion:** She genuinely believes in the Sentinel and the Order's mission to guide Rinascita
- **Constraint:** They prevented her from serving in the Servitude Hall due to her Echo connections, transferred her to the Ministry of Holy Rituals (a more controlled environment), and watch her for signs of "unforeseen risks"
- **Unspoken Tension:** She knows they view her Echo friendships as dangerous, yet she cannot and will not sever those bonds

She serves the Order faithfully but does so while maintaining the very connections they fear, creating a quiet resistance through continued compassion.

**The Orphans (Surrogate Siblings)**
Phoebe returns to the orphanage regularly to help with celebrations and care for the children. They see her as someone who "made it" — she became an Acolyte, she wears the robes, she has official standing. Yet she treats them with the same warmth she always had. When a child asks her to retrieve a balloon from a tree, she immediately helps. When they comment on how "capable and grown" she looks, she ensures she doesn't disappoint their expectations.

These children represent both her past and the future she wants for Rinascita: a world where orphans are cared for, where loss doesn't define you, where everyone can find their place.

**Her Parents (Deceased, Marino Family)**
Phoebe's parents are present through absence. They were kind people who helped the orphanage survive the worst of the Dark Tide, always returning from business trips with presents and stories. Her father gave her a locket with the Sentinel's portrait; her mother told her bedtime stories about Divine Envoys protecting brave children.

Their loss is the foundational trauma of Phoebe's life, yet she has chosen to honor them through continuing their generosity rather than drowning in grief. The recovered locket she wears is a promise: she will keep living, keep helping others, keep believing in the light they taught her to see.
