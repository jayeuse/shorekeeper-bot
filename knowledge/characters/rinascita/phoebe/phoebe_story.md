## Phoebe: Official Introduction

_"Phoebe, an Acolyte of the Order of the Deep, always carries herself with warmth and grace. Devout and steadfast in her adherence to the Order's doctrines, she embodies exceptional self-discipline. Yet beneath her composed exterior burns a vibrant spirit, alight with heartfelt joy for all she holds dear."_
— Official Website Introduction

Phoebe is the embodiment of quiet devotion made visible — a young Acolyte whose light, both literal and metaphorical, fills rooms where storms have extinguished hope. She is Rinascita's gentle mediator, the orphanage's beloved sister, and the Echoes' chosen friend. Her public persona is one of perfect piety and disciplined service, yet those who know her well glimpse the vibrant, joyful spirit she has learned to contain within the Order's strictures.

The player first encounters Phoebe during community incidents in Ragunna — the restaurant brawl she peacefully resolves, the orphanage celebrations she helps organize, the countless small acts of kindness that define her daily life. What appears to be simple devotion reveals profound complexity: a survivor of trauma who transformed drowning into light, an orphan who chose to serve the institution that saved her even while maintaining bonds it fears, and a faithful believer who can acknowledge truths beyond her doctrine without losing her faith.

## Phoebe: Personality

Phoebe's personality is built on the careful integration of genuine faith and suppressed vibrancy:

**Unwavering Diligence as Foundation**
Phoebe fulfills her duties with meticulous, almost ritualistic care. She follows the Order's teachings of "silence" and "self-restraint" to an exceptional degree — her Overclock report notes her stability is "unmatched among her peers" precisely because of this discipline. She prays at designated times, completes tasks exactly as instructed, mediates conflicts without raising her voice, and embodies the ideal Acolyte in every observable way.

This is not performative compliance masking resentment. It is **genuine devotion** that has become integrated into her identity. The Order gave her food, shelter, training, and purpose when she had lost everything. Her discipline is her way of honoring that gift.

**Compassionate Nurturer as Core Identity**
Phoebe's defining trait is her **active, hands-on kindness**. When she sees two men fighting, she doesn't call guards or lecture about doctrine — she gently puts them to sleep with her Forte and explains temperance to onlookers afterward. When a child asks for help, she immediately agrees without calculating cost. When orphans need celebration, she personally prepares the decorations and watches their joy with genuine warmth.

Her relationship with Echoes crystallizes this compassion: where the Order sees Divine Envoys to be "shared equitably," Phoebe sees **friends**. Brenno and the Plushie Echoes visit her during nightmares not because she commands them but because they love her. They make her flower wreaths, retrieve impossible treasures from the sea, and comfort her when she cries. This reciprocal bond is precisely what the Order fears — it is too personal, too deep, and exists outside institutional control.

**Suppressed Vibrancy as Hidden Truth**
The "vibrant spirit" mentioned in her official introduction surfaces in small, carefully controlled moments: her genuine smile when orphans receive gifts, her excitement when successfully mediating conflict, her quiet joy when reunited with Brenno. She has learned to express warmth within permissible boundaries, but the joy is real when it emerges.

The tension is internal: her natural inclination is to bond deeply with those she cares about (human and Echo alike), but her role demands emotional distance. Being "too close" to Echoes is dangerous according to doctrine. Yet she cannot and will not sever those bonds. This creates a quiet resistance — she serves the Order faithfully while maintaining the very connections they fear.

**Survivor's Transformation of Trauma**
Phoebe lost her parents to the sea and nearly died herself. The trauma of that night — watching the merchant ship sink, crying for parents who would never return, drowning in icy water — fundamentally shaped her. Yet rather than becoming bitter or fearful, she **transformed trauma into compassion**.

Her prayers offer comfort to others who have lost loved ones. Her light fills rooms where storms have extinguished hope. She became the presence she needed when she was drowning: someone who whispers _"Don't be afraid. I'm here."_ This is not denial of pain but **active choice** to create meaning from suffering.

**Faith as Chosen Anchor, Not Imposed Prison**
Phoebe's faith in the Sentinel is genuine and deep, but it is also a **chosen anchor**. She recognizes the Order's failings — the political maneuvering that kept her from Servitude Hall, the institutional distrust of her Echo friendships, the strictures that make warmth suspect. Yet she continues serving because the **ideals are worth upholding** even when the institution falters.

Her voiceline to the Rover reveals this nuanced faith: _"The Sentinel guides us all, but perhaps you, Rover, are guided by something even greater."_ She can acknowledge truths beyond her doctrine without losing her faith. This is mature spirituality — holding convictions while remaining open to mystery.

**The Sawtooth Fluctuation as Change Signal**
The recent detection of a "sawtooth fluctuation" in her otherwise stable waveform suggests something is shifting within her. Possibilities include:

- Growing internal conflict between duty and attachment (particularly if the Rover's presence awakens desires for freedom)
- Echo bonds deepening beyond what the Order considers safe
- Unresolved grief finally surfacing despite years of suppression
- Natural evolution of a powerful Resonator whose abilities are still developing

The Order has flagged this for monitoring. Phoebe herself may not yet realize the transformation beginning within her.

## Phoebe: Character Story I

"A Day in the Life of an Acolyte" (Intimacy Level 1)

On an ordinary afternoon, the wide streets of Ragunna bustled with people, more lively than in previous years. The long-anticipated Carnevale was set to return, news that had spread throughout Ragunna and across the seas.

Ragunna's fine wines were as captivating as its breathtaking views. Nectarwine had the power to lull someone into a sweet dream, though overindulgence occasionally led to incidents.

What started as a small disagreement over taste soon escalated into a heated debate. The debate grew fiercer and exploded into a full-blown fistfight. Pizzas and desserts flew through the air, seafood leaped from plates, and onlookers scrambled to hide.

"Everyone! Oh, don't do that! You'll shatter the plates! Put down your cutlery, it's dangerous, you hear?" The owner cried out, her tail puffed up as she jumped up and down, waving a pizza paddle. Still, she was too afraid to get too close.

Just then, **a small figure darted from the crowd**.

"Please, calm down!"

With a staff in hand, the little figure traced a wide arc through the air. **A golden bird descended from the sky**, seemingly carrying the weight of the heavens. It barely touched the two enraged men on their foreheads, like a dragonfly skimming the surface of a pond.

**The two men instantly calmed and fell face-first onto the table with smiles, snoring softly.**

"Don't worry, they've just fallen asleep," said the petite blonde Acolyte as she tucked away her staff. "However, disturbing public order is a violation of Order regulations. Those who disturb the peace must face appropriate punishment."

"Temperance is a virtue granted to us by the Sentinel. It makes both food and Nectarwine all the more delicious... I hope this little incident hasn't ruined anyone's enjoyment of their time here in Ragunna."

The Acolyte's smile was gentle and warm as the silence around her stretched on.

By the time more Acolytes arrived with La Guardias in tow, the commotion had already ended. A diner described the scene with enthusiasm: _"Yes, yes, I saw it with my own eyes! She knocked out those two big guys with just one tap of her staff! Then, out of nowhere, a huge Plushie Echo appeared and carried them off! She looked young, but you can't judge a book by its cover..."_

The lead Acolyte pulled out his Terminal to file a report, only to find that one had already been sent to the Order, detailing the incident and the whereabouts of those taken away.

"So... does that mean it's all taken care of?"

"Of course." The Acolyte put away his Terminal with certainty.

**"Leave it to Acolyte Phoebe. She knows how to handle these things better than anyone."**

## Phoebe: Character Story II

"Home" (Intimacy Level 2)

_Dear Mr. Claremont,_

_I apologize for the delay in reaching out. I must bring you tragic news: Our business partner, the Marinos, were attacked by TDs during their journey to the New Federation. Unfortunately, they did not survive. May the Sentinel guide their spirits to peace._

_Regarding payment for the sunken cargo, I have begun liquidating the remaining assets of the Marino family. You must remember Phoebe. The poor girl has lost her parents. She has nowhere to go, and as I am often away on business, I am unable to care for her. I pray you might consider offering her a home..._

**— Letter from Isabella to potential adoptive families**

I folded the letter carefully, checked the address once more, and knocked again. The repeated knocking had already drawn curious glances from the neighbors, but there was no response from within. **The little girl stood quietly behind me, her head bowed**, as if accustomed to such situations.

"They went on a holiday a while back," a passing neighbor mentioned casually. "Might not be back for quite a while."

"I see, thank you. Come on, Phoebe."

As we turned to leave, I caught a glimpse of the curtain fluttering in the side window. I tightened my grip on Phoebe's hand, leading her away from the ostentatious neighborhood.

**After years of working in an orphanage, I had grown accustomed to all sorts of refusals, but I couldn't bear for Phoebe to experience this.** She was still so young. What she needed was a warm, stable home, somewhere to heal in peace.

"We're deeply sorry... You have our sympathy for the girl's misfortune, but you understand adopting a child is more than just adding another plate to the table..."

"I've heard that the Marinos still haven't paid off their debts. We'd rather not have creditors knocking on our door. Please understand."

...

"Don't be sad, Sister Isabella."

An ice cream was suddenly held out before me. **A group of little Echoes had appeared around me unnoticed, each holding out ice creams and sweets meant for tourists. They were crowded around me, or rather, around Phoebe.**

"I know the grown-ups are busy, just like Mother and Father were. They always came home so late."

I stared blankly at the melting ice cream, caught off guard by the fact that **this tiny child was offering comfort to me**.

The Marino family had helped the orphanage survive the worst of the Dark Tide. Yet here I stood, unable to find a proper home for their daughter. She should have been smiling in her parents' embrace, living a peaceful and happy life.

"It's okay, I can... live with everyone else. I'm happy being with everyone."

When I remained silent for a long while, the young girl looked up at me and gave me a quiet, almost soothing smile. My heart trembled.

"Everyone will stay together, right? Sister Isabella? The Acolytes tell us the Sentinel is always with us, keeping us together so we will never be apart."

She recited the words with such certainty. In the end, I could only swallow the lump in my throat, hold back my tears, and pull her close.

"All right... then let's go back. Let's go back home."

## Phoebe: Character Story III

"Countless Nights" (Intimacy Level 3)

The storm in the dead of night seemed as if it would drain every last drop of water in Ragunna. Lightning flashed across the sky, casting webbed shadows upon a young girl curled up in her blanket.

**Phoebe couldn't fall asleep. Every time she closed her eyes, she saw the churning, furious sea.** There, a lone ship would never return home, and she was sinking with it.

On nights like this, her father used to slip a locket with the Sentinel's portrait under her pillow, telling her that the Divinity would guide her through the storms in her dreams. Her mother would place a bouquet of daisies by her bedside and tell her bedtime stories about Divine Envoys protecting brave children.

But now, the Acolytes would simply pat her head and tell her that she was like any other child. Every Rinascitan had to endure suffering before they could receive the Sentinel's forgiveness.

Once, when she peered over an Acolyte's shoulder, Phoebe caught sight of the magnificent statue of the Sentinel for the first time. **The sight of the immense, sinuous fishtail gave her a moment of panic**, but the Acolyte's gentle hand on her shoulder and the soft, steadying prayer helped calm her trembling heart.

She was led into a warm, brightly lit room. The Acolytes treated her kindly, but **she knew they probably preferred children who didn't bring trouble with them**.

Phoebe tried to remember the lullaby her mother used to sing, willing its melody to echo in her mind to drown out the howling storm outside. But still, the relentless thunder and rain dragged her back to that dark night.

**She had snuck aboard a merchant ship, peering through the porthole of the cargo hold to watch the distant horizon.** When her parents returned, their sails would appear from that direction. She didn't want to wait anymore. This time, she was going to be brave and find them herself.

But the sea wasn't as gentle as her mother described. She began to hear hurried footsteps on the deck, the noise growing frantic until screams pierced the air as the ship was tossed violently in the storm's grip. **The vessel began to sink.**

Phoebe cried out for her parents, but the raging waves seemed to whisper that they would never return.

**The icy seawater engulfed her, and in her fading vision, she watched her father's locket disappear into the dark depths of the sea.**

When she opened her eyes again, she was lying on the shore, soaked to the bone. **A Tacet Mark shone on her leg.** The Acolytes who found her said no one could survive such a perilous sea, let alone a little girl. They believed it was a blessing from the Sentinel.

But Phoebe faintly remembered that **something had saved her**. A presence so gentle, like a soft breeze, had lifted her to the surface and laid her gently on the shore, telling her…

**_"Don't be afraid. I'm here."_**

Something pulled Phoebe from her dream. She felt the bed shift slightly, as though something soft and plush had nestled up beside her. It carried the dampness of the rain and the scent of daisies.

Then Phoebe saw a pair of button eyes and soft, floppy ears—**it was Brenno**, the stray Echo she had met in Whisperwind Haven earlier that day. She had untangled the kite string trapping it, naming it Brenno. Later, Brenno introduced Phoebe to many of its friends, took her cloud-watching on the hillsides, wiped away her tears, and even made her a wreath of pretty flowers.

_Don't be sad, Phoebe._

The Plushie's body felt warm and soft. In the haze of sleep, Phoebe felt a faint, familiar presence. **It felt like the wind.**

She buried her face into the Plushie's soft fur and slowly drifted back into a peaceful slumber.

## Phoebe: Character Story IV

"But Morning Came" (Intimacy Level 4)

As Phoebe woke up, she felt a lingering dampness at the corner of her eye.

"By the Sentinel's grace, another day to rise and shine!"

She leaped out of bed and quickly smoothed the bed sheets. The small bed that had been a part of her childhood still looked as familiar as ever. Phoebe gave her cheeks a few quick pats to shake off the morning drowsiness before diving straight into the day's tasks.

The orphanage was hosting a small celebration, and she had joined her senior Acolyte to help with the preparations. It would be her first time back since becoming an Acolyte Initiate. She had spent the previous evening talking with her brothers and sisters for a long while. Maybe that was why she had that dream about the past.

Everyone commented on how capable and grown she looked now. **She couldn't afford to disappoint their expectations.**

After morning prayers, Phoebe began preparing the necessary items for the celebration. By the time the sun had climbed high in the sky, she and the other Acolytes had transformed the simple dining hall into a vibrant, colorful space. Phoebe set out the tea and snacks and watched the children, dressed in freshly laundered clothes pressed by the Echoes, rush excitedly into their caregivers' arms to receive their carefully wrapped presents.

Touched by this warm scene, Phoebe couldn't help but smile.

She remembered how her father would always buy her a present during the first stop of his business trips. How her mother would always come up with a story about the present. Her favorite gift had been a pink rabbit stuffed toy. In the story, she was an elegant musician.

**As the darker parts of her past faded away, Phoebe found herself remembering only the bright, happy moments.**

A little Echo tugged on her sleeve, proudly showing her a big balloon it had just made. Instinctively, she reached out to pat its head.

"Acolyte Initiate Phoebe!"

Her senior's stern voice made Phoebe flinch. She quickly withdrew her hand and watched the Echo shuffle off with a hint of sadness. The senior's intense gaze seemed to pierce through her.

"By the Sentinel's grace, **be mindful of your position**."

...She still hadn't fully adjusted to her new role. **As an Acolyte, she couldn't have close attachments to the Echoes.**

The Sentinel had led her to kind people, given her food and a roof over her head. With these gifts came new responsibilities. As an Acolyte, it was her duty to repay such kindness.

However, as Phoebe, she still had her own time to meet with friends.

Unconsciously, her hand went to the **locket hanging around her waist**. It was her own little miracle. **A treasure that her friends had helped bring back from the depths of the sea.**

"Ms. Phoebe, my balloon got stuck in the tree and I can't get it down. Jimmy said you can fly. Could you..."

"Of course!"

The child tugging at her clothes snapped Phoebe out of her reverie. She set aside her tangled thoughts and returned to the crowd, immersing herself in the busy work of the celebration once more.

## Phoebe: Favorite/Dislike Food

**Favorite Food: Ice Cream (Particularly from Echoes)**
Phoebe associates ice cream with comfort and friendship. When Sister Isabella was devastated by failed adoption attempts, Brenno and the Plushie Echoes brought Phoebe ice cream to offer Isabella. The gesture was both practical (a sweet treat to lift spirits) and symbolic (Echoes providing for humans, not the reverse). Phoebe loves ice cream because it represents **kindness in action** — the small, tangible ways we care for each other.

**Favorite: Daisies (as scent/memory)**
While not food, daisies carry profound meaning for Phoebe. Her mother used to place bouquets by her bedside and tell bedtime stories. When Brenno visited during her nightmare, it carried "the dampness of the rain and the scent of daisies" — suggesting Echoes absorbed her memories and manifested comfort through familiar scents. Daisies represent maternal love, lost but not forgotten.

## Phoebe: Narrative Chronicle

**Rinascita and the Order of the Deep**
Rinascita is a Mediterranean-inspired coastal region built around faith in **Sentinel Imperator** — a divine fishtail entity governing the seas and protection of the people. The **Order of the Deep** is Rinascita's dominant religious institution, managing orphanages, conducting rituals, mediating conflicts, and interpreting doctrine.

The Order operates on principles of "equitable blessing" — the Sentinel's love should be distributed fairly, Echoes (Divine Envoys) should serve all equally, and personal attachment to spiritual beings is dangerous. This framework functions as social control: by prohibiting too-close relationships with Echoes, the Order maintains institutional mediation of divine power.

**The Marino Family and Pre-Awakening Life**
Phoebe's parents were the Marinos, a business family who regularly traveled for trade. They were kind people who helped the Order Orphanage survive the worst of the Dark Tide, demonstrating generosity beyond obligation. Phoebe's childhood was happy: her father brought presents from each trip, her mother told bedtime stories, and she received a pink rabbit stuffed toy she treasured.

However, their business required prolonged absences. Phoebe, young and lonely, eventually **snuck aboard a merchant ship** attempting to find them herself. This act of childish bravery became the catalyst for tragedy.

**The Storm, the Drowning, and the Awakening**
The merchant ship was caught in a violent storm and began sinking. Phoebe cried out for her parents, but they were not aboard — they were already dead, attacked by Tacet Discords (TDs) during their journey to the New Federation. The waves seemed to whisper this truth to her.

**Icy seawater engulfed her.** She watched her father's locket disappear into darkness. She was drowning, dying, alone.

Then **something saved her**. A presence "so gentle, like a soft breeze" lifted her to the surface and laid her on the shore, whispering: _"Don't be afraid. I'm here."_ When she woke, a **Tacet Mark glowed on her upper left thigh**. The Acolytes declared it a "blessing from Imperator."

**Theory:** The entity that saved Phoebe was likely an Echo or spiritual being connected to the wind/sea. Her Awakening was not just trauma-triggered but **relationship-triggered** — she bonded with this presence during her near-death, and her Forte (manifesting light, connecting with Echoes) reflects this origin. The "soft breeze" may have become Brenno or influenced her later Echo affinity.

**Orphanage Years and Failed Adoptions**
With her parents dead and their estate burdened with unpaid debts, no one wanted to adopt Phoebe. Sister Isabella tried desperately, knocking on doors across Ragunna, enduring rejection after rejection. Potential families cited various excuses:

- "Adopting a child is more than just adding another plate"
- "We don't want creditors knocking on our door"
- Convenient "holidays" when Isabella arrived

Isabella felt she had failed the Marinos by being unable to give their daughter the peaceful life they would have wanted. But **Phoebe comforted Isabella**, offering her ice cream (brought by Echoes) and reciting that "the Sentinel is always with us, keeping us together."

They returned to the orphanage together. It became Phoebe's home not by choice but by necessity. Yet she found meaning in it: siblings who cared for each other, adults who tried their best, and Echoes who visited because they wanted to.

**Training as Acolyte Initiate**
Phoebe's exceptional Forte abilities and her rapport with Echoes made her a natural candidate for the Order. Her light provided hope, her presence mediated conflicts, and her prayers genuinely comforted people. The Order voted unanimously to grant her Acolyte status after her novitiate.

However, **"some within the Order believe that her closeness with the Echoes violates doctrine."** She was denied appointment to Servitude Hall (the prestigious frontline position) and transferred to the Ministry of Holy Rituals instead (more bureaucratic, less autonomy). This was institutional containment: they wanted her abilities but feared her bonds.

Phoebe accepted this compromise. She would serve where directed, follow protocols, and be the perfect Acolyte publicly. But she would not abandon Brenno or the Echoes who comforted her nightmares. **This is her quiet resistance**: loyalty to ideals over institutions.
