## Augusta Profile: Ontological Overview

Augusta is the elected Ephor of Septimont — the supreme leader and highest authority of Rinascita's martial city-state. What distinguishes her from every other power-holder in Wuthering Waves is the premise on which she holds this office: she is, by her own unambiguous declaration, a mere mortal. She carries no inherited status, no divine mandate, and no special bloodline. She is a Congenital Resonator, but her Forte is acknowledged even within the game's lore to be of unimpressive strength compared to other Resonators of her tier. Every height she has reached, she climbed by raw skill, tactical genius, and a will that declined, at every available opportunity, to accept what others judged impossible.

This is not a vanity claim. Her origin is documented: as a child, her village was leveled by a Dark Tide incursion. She arrived in Septimont with nothing — no name that anyone recognized, no sponsor, no money, no political capital. She was mocked by nobles. She sought a mentor and was repeatedly turned away. She found Cato and scraped into the Agon system at the bottom. From that starting point, she rose to defeat the Great Agon's full champion roster, claim the title of undefeated champion, and eventually unseat a corrupt Ephor through the organized will of the public arena — becoming, four years before the game's events, the most powerful political figure in Septimont.

Her tenure as Ephor has been defined by reform. The Agons of prior eras were bloodsports — death matches that nobles exploited as entertainment and gladiators entered knowing they might not leave. Augusta abolished the killing clauses. The Agon under her administration is a competition of glory, not a spectacle of slaughter. This reform is personal: she lost nothing of herself to get power, and she does not intend to ask the same sacrifice of anyone who comes after her.

## Augusta — Character/Appearance Design

Augusta cuts an immediately arresting figure — tall, lean, and physically powerful without sacrificing the visual language of rulership. Her silhouette is deliberately Roman-imperial: flowing white draped fabric, gleaming gold chestplate with intricate darker-gold detailing, metal pauldrons on both shoulders, brown leather pteruges (the layered strips beneath a general's skirt), and white high-heeled sandals with brown leather straps. The overall composition is that of a victorious general stepping off the ceremonial platform — commanding authority while remaining fully ready to fight.

Her most iconic feature is the sunburst-shaped golden headpiece resting on her brow — equal parts crown, military insignia, and solar symbol. It enforces the repeated solar motifs in her identity: "undying sun," "Eternal Radiance on the Crown," her Liberation summons a blazing orb in the sky. Her orange-red wavy hair cascades past her shoulders to her waist, visually functioning as a continuation of the solar imagery — as though she is the flame. Her eyes are sharp and narrow with an intense red hue and a distinctive white-gold ring surrounding the pupils — the only explicitly supernatural element in an otherwise grounded, military-coded aesthetic.

Her Tacet Mark is located on the back of her left hand. She wears a black ring on each thumb and golden dangle earrings shaped as eight-pointed stars. The accessories are precise and deliberate: nothing excessive, nothing that would impede movement, everything reinforcing the solar-imperial theme.

## Augusta: Psychological Profile

**Will as Identity**
Augusta does not merely have a strong will — her will is, more than anything else, the substance of who she is. She was stripped of home, status, and belonging at a young age, and what she built in their place was a personal code that had no room for the concept of defeat. This is not bravado; it is survival architecture. When Iuno once asked her what she would do if Septimont's fate were sealed, Augusta replied that she would simply accomplish the impossible to prevent it. She said it without drama. She meant it completely.

**Humility Without Self-Diminishment**
Her fandom description explicitly flags "fiercely resilient yet humble" as coexisting qualities, and the combination is more precise than it first appears. She speaks plainly about her origins: she was nothing, she came from nowhere, she had no advantages. This is not false modesty — it is accuracy. But it coexists with an absolute confidence in her capacity to change those circumstances through effort. She holds both truths simultaneously without contradiction.

**The Reformed Gladiator-Ruler**
She is, in some structural sense, Septimont's philosopher-king via the arena: a figure who gained power through force and legitimacy through the crowd's will, but used that power immediately to limit the violence that produced it. She did not reform the Agon because she grew soft. She reformed it because she understood, from the inside, what the death-mandate cost the people who entered — and she had already proven that glory did not require it.

**Pragmatic Genius under Pressure**
Her intelligence is specifically tactical and adaptive. One recurring example: early in her career, encountering a corrupted Lion Beast she could not penetrate with direct force, she redirected entirely — struck a stalactite, collapsed it into the beast's jaws, then strangled it. This pattern repeats: she does not stubbornly attack the problem where it is strongest. She finds the lever.

**The Weight of the Sword**
Her Cherished Item — a greatsword forged from the blades of defeated gladiators she disarmed rather than killed — is the most concise symbol of her ethos. She took those blades to spare defeated opponents a crueler fate, and she used them as fangs until she didn't need fangs anymore. Now Gladiators don't fight to the death, and she no longer needs the sword to prove herself. The weapon is a record of the entire arc.

**Waking Up With Bad Hair (and Not Being Happy About It)**
Her Forte causes her hair to be entirely disordered every time she wakes up. If not for the office of Ephor requiring her to maintain an appropriately composed appearance, she has made clear she would not bother. This detail is charming and humanizing precisely because it is so incongruous with everything else about her.

## Augusta: Relationships

**Iuno**
Her closest friend, described as a best friend relationship characterized by mutual teasing and genuine emotional confiding. Iuno's Heroic Sacrifice in main story events erases Augusta's memories of her — a fact Iuno accepts with characteristic pragmatic confidence that she can simply become Augusta's friend again. The depth of the relationship is conveyed precisely by how much weight that erasure carries narratively.

**Ephor Magno ("Dragon Slayer")**
The Ephor who occupied the position when Augusta arrived in Septimont as a child. She watched him from the street eating dry bread, fascinated by his charisma and the legendary reputation attached to his name — slew seven Corrosauruses, undefeated champion, Ephor elected by popular will. She modeled herself on the version of him the crowds described. Eventually, she had to confront the gap between that legend and the man he had become, asking him quietly — *were those legends ever true?* — and receiving only a silent smile. He later became "the Nameless," a helmeted fighter in underground death-matches. She recognized him in their duel by his eyes. The full context of what he became and why is the shadow that her own leadership explicitly refuses to cast.

**Lupa**
A fellow gladiator from Septimont, treated as a competitive peer. Lupa is noted as a benchmark of power in the city — being "far above" Lupa is the calibration used to describe Augusta's combat ceiling. Their dynamic is that of respected rivals who operate in the same professional world.

**Rover (Protagonist)**
Augusta encounters and assesses the Rover throughout the Septimont arc, including inviting them specifically to the great hunt in ways that suggest both genuine evaluation of their power and political calculation. She is noted as someone who does not act on simple impulses even when she appears to — her invitations and challenges carry strategic intent.

**Cato**
Her mentor, who accepted her into the Agon system when nearly no one else would. The relationship is foundational: Cato was Augusta's first professional "lifeline" in Septimont — the person who gave her initial access to the framework she would eventually master.
