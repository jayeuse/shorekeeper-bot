# Phrolova Character Documentation

## Phrolova Profile: Basic Information

- **Name:** Phrolova (Chinese: 弗洛洛)
- **Title:** Overseer of the Fractsidus / The Conductor of Forsaken Depths
- **Role:** As an Overseer, Phrolova commands significant authority within the Fractsidus hierarchy, directing Tacet Discord operations and manipulating historical narratives to serve the organization's goals (e.g., in Rinascita).

- **Identity:** Former Overseer of Fractsidus; Mutant Resonator; former violinist of Ostina
- **Affiliation:** Fractsidus (former); Independent (post–Chapter II: Act XI)
- **Birthplace:** Ostina — an ancient village on Solaris-3, destroyed by a Lament meteor strike

## Phrolova Profile: Ontological Overview

Phrolova is a playable Havoc Mutant Resonator in Wuthering Waves and a former Overseer of the Fractsidus. Her Resonance Forte grants her the ability to manipulate and transfigure the fundamental frequencies of humans, Echoes, and Tacet Discords alike — making her one of the most singularly powerful Resonators ever documented in the game's lore. She is formally classified as a "Mutant Resonator" in official Resonance Assessment files, a designation indicating her awakening path is unique, unreplicable, and arose from conditions that cannot be replicated by conventional means.

Her Resonance awakening occurred at the precise moment of her death — when a shard of the Lament struck and obliterated her home village of Ostina — simultaneously taking everything she loved and granting her abilities that transcend ordinary mortality. As a consequence of her partial Tacet Discord mutation, she has ceased aging, possesses extraordinary regenerative capacity (including full organ regrowth within minutes), and exists in a liminal state between human life and Tacet Discord. Official Resonance Assessment report RA-F2005-G notes that her frequency waveform "displays sawtooth and needle-like fluctuations" with "intense and chaotic Time Domain activity," and that her peak value "remains indeterminable." Her Resonant Criticality is rated extremely low due to extremely unstable frequency readings, though she consistently demonstrates control of Overclocking states — hinting she may unlock methods of stabilizing this state in ordinary Resonators.

Following her physical death at the end of Chapter II: Act VII (Version 2.5), her consciousness merged with the Lost Beyond — a Sonoro Sphere she constructed from the residual power of the Threnodian Leviathan, recreating her destroyed village as a space where the frequencies of her deceased loved ones persist. By Chapter II: Act XI (Version 2.7), she physically restored herself and returned to reality. After fulfilling a final task for the Grand Architect, she departed Fractsidus to find her own path.

Phrolova is the first playable antagonist character in Wuthering Waves, and the first playable character to have physically died onscreen, albeit temporarily.

## Phrolova – Character/Appearance Design

Phrolova presents as an attractive, pale-skinned young woman of average build and height.

- **Hair:** Long greyish-white hair worn in elegant braids, fastened with deep red holders — evoking both a musician's composed poise and mourning aesthetics.
- **Eyes:** Heterochromatic as a result of her Tacet Discord mutation — her left iris carries a faint hue alteration distinguishing it from her right. In alternate combat forms, removing her eyepatch reveals this more dramatically, often accompanied by blood-tear visual effects.
- **Baton:** Her primary instrument and weapon is a conductor's baton carved from a Lycoris (red spider lily) flower, which channels her Forte ability. Notably, the petals of this baton are potent enough to manipulate Echoes and Tacet Discords on their own, and can be given to others to use.
- **Tacet Mark Location:** Upper left arm.
- **Aesthetic Themes:** Her design is saturated with death-and-rebirth symbolism. Red spider lilies (Lycoris radiata) — flowers symbolizing finality and the afterlife in Japanese and Chinese culture — appear throughout her visual identity, combat animations, and lore. Her attire carries somber, classical concert-hall undertones, appropriate for a performer in perpetual mourning.
- **Dual-Form Design:** Phrolova possesses a visual dual-identity — her composed, melancholic default state and a more explosive, unhinged combat state released in her Liberation. This contrast is thematically deliberate: reflecting the core duality of creation and destruction, grief and violence, life and death.
  **Musical Notation Reference (Prydwen):** Her conductor baton plays three quarter notes (crotchets) per bar during her Forte sequence — a time signature associated with waltz dancing. The tempo of her Liberation is notably slow: approximately 1.2–1.5 seconds per Hecate attack.

## Phrolova – Capabilities and Skills

**Dream Weaving and Sound Manipulation:**
Phrolova possesses the unique ability to weave illusions and manipulate perception through sound frequencies. This power, often referred to as "Dream Weaving," allows her to create immersive, reality-bending scenarios — effectively trapping victims in their own memories or fabricated nightmares (as seen in the "Lost Beyond"). This ability is key to her control over the "Secret Gardens" in Act VII.

## Phrolova – Psychological Profile

Phrolova is deeply melancholic and enigmatic, her presence emanating a pervasive sadness that seems to envelop those around her. This sorrow is not performative — it is the authentic distillation of a life defined entirely by tragedy, loss, and betrayal.

**Core Traits:**

- Melancholic and somber by nature; her grief extends atmospherically to others in her presence
- Carries a rich and complex internal life beneath a quiet, serious exterior — she is philosophical, artistic, and deeply nostalgic
- Performative in measured doses: she sometimes invites others to "witness" her, reflecting an awareness of her own symbolic weight in the world
- Questions her own sanity intermittently — she speaks of feeling thoughts "blown away by the wind," indicating a mind perpetually at war with suffocating internal and external realities
- Deeply prizes control, order, and structure; despises disorder and unpredictability
- Skeptical of blind idealism; she views "will without pragmatic basis" as ephemeral and ultimately empty
- Considers abstract ideals (e.g., paths that long to converge but never do, like Ferris wheel houses reflected in water) — allegorical commentary on connection and disconnection

**Philosophical Outlook:**
Phrolova does not view death as an absolute end, but as a portal to "a different kind of rebirth" — a state of existence transcending ordinary mortality, experienced through her own liminal nature. She covets what she terms "universal truth," and her jealousy of Cartethyia — a being with "whole will" as a child of Divinity — reveals a deep hunger for ontological completeness: a self that is entirely and unconditionally whole rather than fractured between life and the Beyond.

**Grief and Obsession:**
Her defining psychological wound is the obliteration of her family and village, compounded by what she perceived as the Rover's broken promise. These twin traumas — annihilation and abandonment — calcified her grief into a near-religious obsession with resurrection. She willingly crossed moral thresholds (turning loved ones' reverberations into Tacet Discords; assisting in catastrophic acts; sacrificing innocent lives) not out of cruelty but from a love so absolute it corroded her ethical foundations entirely. She is the personification of personal tragedy converted into singular, terrible ability.

**History Manipulation (Rinascita):**
Phrolova's role in Rinascita involved the subtle manipulation of collective memory and history. By influencing the Order of the Deep and exploiting Fenrico's fears, she effectively rewrote the narrative of the region's past, positioning the Lament as a cyclical, inevitable event that could only be transcended through the Fractsidus' methods. She used her "Dream Weaving" abilities (see _Skills_) to implant false memories or altered perceptions into key figures, ensuring the populace remained compliant and fearful of the "Dark Tide" while obscuring its true nature as a manifestation of their own collective trauma.

**Emotional Range:**
Despite her cold exterior, Phrolova is capable of warmth, curiosity, and wry humor. In the Lost Beyond she displays a guarded softness — wanting visitors to experience "simple joy" in the recreated village. Her food preferences are revealing: she accepts the "sour and bitter" taste of redcurrants, having "grown accustomed to it" — a metaphor for her tolerance, even quiet fondness, for hardship and complexity.

## Phrolova Relationships

**The Rover (Protagonist):**
Phrolova and the Rover share the game's most haunting narrative parallel. In the past, during one of her post-Lament performances, the Rover promised to return and hear her completed melody — a promise broken by their periodic memory resets. This perceived abandonment transformed growing trust into years of festering resentment, becoming a quiet but powerful catalyst for her radicalization. The present Rover, stripped of those memories, cannot grasp the weight of what was once broken. Despite being her killer (however indirect — the Rover accidentally stabbed her while throwing their sword at the Gem), Phrolova meets them in the Lost Beyond not with rage but with somber acceptance: "The present cannot be changed."

**The Grand Architect (Fractsidus Leader):**
Phrolova views the Grand Architect with pragmatic respect rooted in mutual utility rather than devotion or love. She recognizes them as an essential reference point and collaborator. The Grand Architect exploited her grief by adopting the guise of Triss — a young girl from Phrolova's destroyed village — to deepen her allegiance and manipulate her actions. Despite this manipulation, Phrolova honored her final promised task before formally departing Fractsidus, reflecting a bounded but genuine sense of personal honor.

**Hecate (Echo Companion):**
Hecate is Phrolova's closest and most loyal companion — a unique Echo described as a "three-faced Witch of the Underworld" originating from the Lost Beyond, the Sonoro Sphere where all decaying frequencies converge. Phrolova can freely summon and puppeteer Hecate in and out of combat. The mythological Hecate from Greek tradition (goddess of magic, witchcraft, and crossroads) mirrors Phrolova's own thematic domain — death, liminality, and the crossroads between worlds — with near-perfect precision.

**Fenrico:**
Fenrico's betrayal during Chapter II: Act VII caused severe damage to Phrolova — physically, politically, and strategically. He plotted against her, annulled the Fractsidus–Order of the Deep alliance, and forced her to seal the Threnodian's energy inside the Lost Beyond while leaving her vulnerable and nigh-powerless. Yet Fenrico's deeper motive — preserving the collective consciousness of Ragunna's inhabitants against the Dark Tide invasion — gave moral weight and complexity to his betrayal.

**Cristoforo:**
A Fractsidus-affiliated figure whose dialogue with Phrolova helps establish that the Lost Beyond was formed from Leviathan's power. Phrolova maintained friction with Cristoforo and the Order of the Deep, particularly around disagreements on how to proceed with the Divinity project.

**Cartethyia:**
Phrolova is noted to carry a degree of jealousy toward Cartethyia — a child of Divinity possessing "whole will." This envy illuminates Phrolova's existential incompleteness: her desire for absolute, unbroken selfhood rather than her fragmented existence perpetually straddling life and the Beyond.

**Her Family and the Villagers of Ostina:**
The emotional center of all Phrolova has ever done. She preserved their frequencies inside Tacet Discords and built the Lost Beyond partly as a memorial space — a realm where their echoes could persist for her to visit. Her tragedy is that she ignores the suffering of frequencies kept "alive" beyond their natural decay, because she cannot bring herself to release them. Her obsession mirrors what was inflicted upon her village — extending the Lament's aftermath indefinitely, rather than letting them rest.
