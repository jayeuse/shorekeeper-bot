## Cartethyia Profile: Ontological Overview

Cartethyia is a Congenital Resonator born from the collision of two opposing cosmological forces in Wuthering Waves' world of Solaris-3: the destructive Threnodian Leviathan, source of the Dark Tide that has long threatened Rinascita, and the protective Sentinel Imperator, divine guardian of the region. Rather than destroying her, this contradiction becomes her singular power — her "Second Awakening" allows her to transition freely between two forms, a precise manifestation of her mastery over opposing principles.

Throughout Rinascita's history she was known only as **Fleurdelys**, the Blessed Maiden — a prophesied divine vessel revered by all. In truth, Fleurdelys was only one part of her whole: the self-sacrificing aspect that sealed the Threnodian within herself and waited in the Avinoleum for a chosen one to end the cycle. In a final act of defiance and hope, she split her essence, casting off the portion that retained her childhood selfhood as **Cartethyia**, sealing two sacred swords alongside the imprisoned divine form. The events of Chapter 2 Act 4 complete the revelation: she is simultaneously the Blessed Maiden (prophesied restorer), the Defier (challenger of the Threnodian's will), and the Death Crier Fleurdelys (seal and sacrifice).

Now freed and whole, she walks Rinascita as a self-described wandering knight — anonymous, unencumbered by title, and finally living the childhood dream she never thought possible. Her story is one of the most layered identity arcs in Wuthering Waves' main narrative.

## Cartethyia — Character/Appearance Design

Cartethyia presents as a petite young woman — her small stature a deliberate visual contrast to the immense, near-divine power she contains. Her Cartethyia form is characterized by a light, windswept aesthetic: flowing white-and-silver garments accented with pale teal and gold that evoke both nobility and motion, suggesting a figure perpetually caught mid-step in open air. Her silver-white hair is worn loosely, often partially pinned but never fully constrained, reinforcing her ethos of freedom over formality. Her eyes carry a clear, candid quality — bright with the sincerity of someone who has chosen transparency over pretense.

Her transformation into **Fleurdelys** shifts her palette toward deep midnight blues, gold filigree, and richly layered skirts that suggest coronation robes and ceremonial regalia. The Fleurdelys form is taller, statelier, and visually heavier — the weight of a title and a history made physical. Three spectral Sword Shadows orbit or materialize around her during combat: the **Sword of Divinity**, **Sword of Discord**, and **Sword of Virtue**, each corresponding to a mythological identity she carries. The contrast between her two forms is the visual thesis of her character: a girl who contains a god, and a god who yearns to be just a girl.

Design motifs recurring across both forms include windmill imagery, floral laurels, and blade silhouettes — references to her childhood in Egla Town and the knight-and-windmill stories that shaped her ideals.

## Cartethyia: Psychological Profile

**Sincerity as Core Value**
Cartethyia's life motto — "The truth is a beacon in the maelstrom of my life" — is not abstract philosophy; it is a survival principle forged through years of fractured identity and enforced sacred performance. Her insistence on honesty and directness in all interactions is the psychological anchor she developed after a prolonged period of amnesia and isolation in the Inverted Tower.

**The Free Spirit beneath the Crown**
At her core she remains the "rambunctious girl" from Egla Town who patrolled her village with a wooden sword, dealing with every minor injustice she could find. This spontaneous, mischief-prone nature did not disappear under the weight of her titles — it went dormant, and has since re-emerged as her defining characteristic now that she is free. She approaches even grave situations with a lightness that can seem naive but is deliberately chosen: she has seen the alternative.

**Grief and Guilt as Quiet Undercurrents**
Her freedom comes at a cost she does not forget. She feels a genuine sense of indebtedness toward **Cantarella** for a future she feels she "took away," and carries the awareness of the sacrifices made around her story with a gravity that rarely surfaces in easy conversation but shapes her sense of duty deeply.

**The Dream of the Nameless Guardian**
She actively and earnestly dreams of being a wandering knight who fights injustice and then rides away anonymously — no recognition, no monument, no title. This is not false humility; after centuries of being defined by a sacred function, anonymity is the most radical freedom imaginable to her.

**Empathy through Listening**
She reads emotional frequencies with unusual precision, interpreting bonds and connections through what she describes as resonance. Her relationship with the Rover is described partly in these terms — she perceives their mutual frequencies as constituting a profound bond, not just camaraderie.

## Cartethyia: Relationships

**Rover (Protagonist)**
Cartethyia regards the Rover as her "Swordbearer" and "Godkiller" — the chosen one who gave her the chance at a different ending. She names them a "faithful companion" and promises to be their sword in return. The bond reads their mutual resonance as something close and defining; she is perhaps the character in Wuthering Waves who most directly acknowledges the narrative weight of the Rover's existence.

**Cantarella**
A relationship of quiet guilt and complex gratitude. Cartethyia feels she took something away from Cantarella — a future, a path, a possibility — and carries that indebtedness sincerely. The precise nature of what was "taken" is left contextually layered, but it informs a respectful, slightly sorrowful warmth in how she regards her.

**Phoebe**
She appreciates Phoebe's genuine kindness deeply, but this appreciation comes with its own difficulty: Phoebe's goodwill makes it hard for Cartethyia to speak certain uncomfortable truths. Her empathy clashes with her commitment to honesty when the honest thing would wound someone so earnest.

**Carlotta**
She is genuinely admiring of Carlotta's fresh and forward-looking way of thinking — a perspective Cartethyia, who has spent much of her existence looking backward at sacred duty, finds both refreshing and instructive.

**Lupa**
Cartethyia maintains an unquestioning acceptance of Lupa that is described as elder-sibling in quality — protectively fond despite Cartethyia's physical stature being smaller. Lupa represents a kind of grounding companion relationship.
