## Galbrena Profile: Basic Information

- **Full Name:** Galbrena (Chinese: 嘉贝莉娜, Jiā bèi lì nà); the name is taken from a legendary Tacet Discord known as the **"Flame Demon" Galbrena**, which she hunted and consumed; her birth name was **Angel**
- **Known Epithets:** "Discord Slayer," "Fiend of Ever-Burning Flame," "Ice Cream Demon" (informal; given by children)
- **Identity:** Congenital Resonator; Fusion-Aligned; Black Shores Consultant; wandering Discord Slayer operating across Solaris-3 alongside the Tacet Discord companion **Chimera**
- **Affiliation:** The Black Shores (current); formerly Septimont, Rinascita
- **Birthplace:** Rinascita (Septimont city-state; born of Ragunna exile parents who settled there)
- **Version Added:** Version 2.7 (introduced in Version 2.6; banner "Fiend of Ever-burning Flame," October 9 – October 29, 2025 [first half])
- **Rarity:** 5-Star
- **Element:** Fusion
- **Weapon:** Pistols
- **Forte Power:** Infernal Descent
- **Tacet Mark Location:** Not specified in available sources; verify with the Wuthering Waves Fandom Wiki
- **Post-Awakening Physical Changes:** The Forte Examination Report documents full physical transformation during Overclocking events — the report classifies the maximum Overclocking rating as Negligible specifically in the context of what this represents for her: a Resonance Ability that was *almost entirely depleted* by the Dark Tide recovering enough to Overcock at all; the Black Shores researchers describe this as "a signal of rebirth" rather than deterioration; her current body integrates frequencies from multiple consumed Tacet Discords and continues to evolve; she has wings capable of flight; she regenerates physical damage at an "unnerving speed"; the researchers note that her case compels a reconsideration of Overclocking's definition
- **Companion:** Chimera — a Tacet Discord fused from a goat and serpent form; one of the first Tacet Discords created by the Leviathan; Chimera initially attempted to trap Galbrena but was undone by her bloodlust and became her ally; both are voiced characters with separate voice actors

## Galbrena Profile: Ontological Overview

Galbrena is a Discord Slayer who does not prove her kills with extracted Tacet Cores or Terminal readings. She proves them by raising her right hand and calling its name — summoning the living frequency of the Tacet Discord she has consumed into visible, audible form, so that the crowd has no choice but to believe. This is what makes her dangerous and what makes her repellent: she does not merely defeat Tacet Discords, she absorbs them. Her body is an inner empire of consumed frequencies. The Black Shores researchers who examined her wrote in the margins of their own report: *"A walking museum of Tacet Discords… Can such power truly belong to a human?"*

She was born Angel, in Septimont, Rinascita, to exiled parents from Ragunna. She was a gifted child in a city that respected only strength, raised on her mother's Codex of kindness and compassion, and developed her own creed in response to Septimont's specific brutality: treat others with goodwill, never start a fight, but if someone strikes you, strike back harder. She became a hunter. The High Tide corrupted her Resonance Ability. She survived by consuming a Tacet Discord to restore it, paid the price of near-total dissolution, and then — in what the researchers call "miraculous" — Overcloced and came back. She consumed the legendary Flame Demon whose name she now carries and took it as her own. She kept going.

She is now old enough that the gold in her hair has darkened, that her fallen feathers carry *"a bitterness weathered with time,"* that the ice cream she orders at open bars while ignoring hostile stares is a deliberate small indulgence rather than a naive one. She is cold and decisive, and when she faces her prey she is merciless — but she could have escaped the Shrieking Legion alone, and she stayed. Because she stayed, the squad lived. She offered the survivors popsicles from nowhere and asked, with a casual grin: *"Want one?"*

## Galbrena — Character/Appearance Design

Galbrena's visual design is built around the contradiction between divinity and consumption — between the pale-gold hair and radiant light she was born with, and the dark, flame-marked, feathered huntress she became.

- **Hair:** Long; dark; worn loose; the pale gold of her birth has darkened with age and transformation; the precise current color varies across official art but reads as a deep auburn-to-black register in most renders
- **Wings:** Large, dark feathered wings capable of flight; her feathers are razor-sharp and can cut Tacet Discords in two; the Fandom backstory notes that those who collect her fallen feathers discover *"a strange gentleness, and... a bitterness weathered with time"* within them; the wings are a primary visual signature and also the source of her Flamewing combat attacks
- **Eyes:** Described in early characterization as blazing with radiant light in her youth; current description is cold and decisive without specific color specification in available sources; verify with Fandom Wiki Gallery
- **Signature Pistols:** Lux & Umbra — her signature weapon, which exists both as an in-game item and as a visual component of her design; the dual pistols are present in her splash art and combat animations; their light/dark naming mirrors her own dual identity as both Angel and Galbrena
- **Chimera:** The Tacet Discord companion appears alongside her in splash art — described as a dragon-like, wolfy creature with a large golden helmet; in the lore, Chimera is fused from goat and serpent form; the companion is audibly present in combat voice lines and reactions; their design relationship is that of the consumed-and-retained, the demon that tried to devour her and instead became bound to her
- **Ice Cream:** A recurring visual and characterization motif — she orders blueberry ice cream shakes at bars while being stared at; she conjures popsicles for survivors; children call her the Ice Cream Demon; the ice cream is specifically described as cooling *"the fire still smoldering in her chest"* — it is not an affectation but a functional counterpoint to the constant internal heat of multiple consumed Tacet Discord frequencies
- **Design Inspirations:** Multiple sources note that Galbrena's kit and aesthetic are explicitly inspired by the Devil May Cry franchise — specifically Dante and Vergil; her Fated End stacking mechanic (the enemy Combo counter running from C through S rank below the target HP bar) is a direct reference to DMC's Style Rank system; her acrobatic combat animations, wing attacks, and dual-pistol fighting style reinforce this; the reference is confirmed rather than implied by developers

## Galbrena: Psychological Profile

**Coldness as Calibration**
Galbrena's archive description leads with *"cold and decisive."* The operative word is decisive — the coldness is not affect, it is a working state. She does not explain herself to crowds that suspect she is a demon. She nods along to the theories (human-TD hybrid experiment; pact with a demon in exchange for emotions) and uses their misapprehension as a shield against questions she does not want to answer. The hunters who have fought beside her know the truth: beneath the cold mask burns an *"intense lust for the hunt."* The two are not in conflict. The coldness is how she functions; the fire is why.

**Strike Back Harder**
The governing creed Galbrena developed as a child — *treat others with goodwill, never start a fight, but if someone strikes you, strike back harder* — runs through every inflection point of her story. The High Tide struck her by corrupting her Resonance Ability. She struck back by consuming a Tacet Discord. Chimera attempted to trap her. She struck back by defeating him again and again until he was bound to her instead. The shapeless evil that destroyed her life struck her by taking the form of every Tidespawn she hunted. She struck back by becoming capable of destroying them all. The creed is not about aggression — it begins with goodwill and non-initiation. It is about the proportionality of response once the first blow has already been landed.

**The Question of What She Has Become**
Story III ends with a sentence that trails off in available sources, mid-thought, on the question of whether her current body can even be considered human and whether she can wield darkness for good. The Forte Examination Report's marginal notation — *"an inner empire. I saw it… an empire rising within her"* — is the most vivid external description of what she carries. She has consumed Chimera, the legendary Flame Demon Galbrena, Harpyia, Dullahan, Balor, the Nameless Dreadshade, and others still pending verification. Each is in there. None of them are hostile under her own frequency's suppression. She has made them all subjects of the empire. The question of whether this makes her more or less human is one she intermittently asks herself and does not answer.

**Kindness and Power**
The tension between Angel's upbringing (the mother's Codex: kindness, compassion, tolerance, forgiveness) and the world Galbrena operates in runs through all three available character stories. Chimera's words in Story III — *"kindness without power is the cruelest tragedy"* — are framed as "not without truth." Her mother's words — *"If a tree's roots cannot pierce into hell, then its branches will never reach heaven"* — are also present, and Galbrena's response to them is characteristically her own: she has never yearned for heaven. If hell is where she must go, she will drag demons down with her. The synthesis of kindness and power for Galbrena is not balance or moderation — it is the descent, with everyone else's safety as the condition of her willingness to go that deep.

**The Hunt as Vow**
The Initiate's Proving Grounds vow — to become a hunter, to protect more people with the power she was given, to ride a Griffrex into battle — is the vow Galbrena carries even after everything that followed destroyed its original context. Story III reframes it: the hunting ceremony she never attended, the Sacred Flames the Priestess should have lit — these are now ignited instead by the infernal blaze of the name she took. The hunt is no longer a career path. It is the continuation of a vow made before she knew what the vow would cost.

## Galbrena: Relationships

**Angel (Former Self)**
Galbrena rarely speaks of her past. When pressed, her answers are brief and evasive. The character archive's first story begins with the deliberate framing device of naming what she was before she was called Galbrena: *"Back when her hair still shimmered pale gold and her eyes still blazed with radiant light. Back before the world ever called her... 'Galbrena.'"* The distance between Angel and Galbrena is the full length of the three character stories — the Proving Grounds, the High Tide, the pact with Chimera, the first devouring, the hunt that never ended. Angel is not gone; the gentleness in the fallen feathers is hers. But she is not the surface.

**Chimera (Companion — Tacet Discord)**
The most unusual relationship in Galbrena's documented life. Chimera is one of the oldest Tacet Discords in existence — created by the Leviathan, fused from goat and serpent, carrying a devouring power he believed would trap any being who received a second life into dependence and eventual submission. He did not account for someone who would keep accepting near-certain death, keep winning, and in winning keep binding him further to a living prison of flesh and blood. He became her companion not through trust or affection but through defeat — repeated, conclusive, and eventually transformative. The Fandom story summary describes him as now sharing her goal: he wants the Threnodian dead as well, for his own reasons rooted in his origin.

**Augusta (Childhood Friend — Septimont)**
The red-haired girl Angel met at Uncle Filo's forge who gave her the laughter her childhood had lacked. They sat by the forge and talked deep into the night about dreams and troubles — Augusta cared most for the stories behind each weapon; Angel was captivated by the ritual of quenching. Augusta's current role in the available lore is as a character mention in Story II's Cherished Item category (verified by Category tag: Augusta Story Mentions), confirming she appears in the game's story in a capacity beyond this memory. The friendship at the forge is the warmest interpersonal moment in Galbrena's available backstory.

**Uncle Filo (Mentor — Septimont Blacksmith)**
The blacksmith whose forge became Angel's sanctuary. Few children went there, but something instinctual drew her to the blazing fire and the clash of hammer on iron. He taught her — and Augusta — about quenching: *"Even the finest blade is just raw metal, unless it is quenched and tempered."* The metaphor is the most explicitly thematic statement in her backstory, and it is placed in his mouth rather than hers.

**Arkyria (Rival — Proving Grounds)**
The favored contender at the Initiate's Proving Grounds, famed for having once repelled the Dark Tide's creations alone. His will, tempered in life-and-death trials, forced Angel to retreat again and again until she unleashed her Resonance Ability for the first time. His question upon defeat — *"Why you? Why were you gifted with such power?"* — was the same question Angel had carried since childhood. Neither of them had an answer. The encounter is the last document of her life before everything changed.

**Angel's Parents (Ragunna Exiles — Septimont)**
Her father stepped forward to fight when monsters attacked; her mother gave food to those with less even when she had little to spare. Their acts of kindness as Ragunna exiles in a city suspicious of their origin earned them a place in Septimont. Her mother raised her by the Codex. Both were active hunters in the period before the High Tide. The backstory's account of watching them return exhausted and bloodied is the direct antecedent of Angel's vow. Their current status is not specified in available sources.
