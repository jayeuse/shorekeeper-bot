## Encore Profile: Ontological Overview

Encore is a Consultant of the Black Shores — a mysterious organization based on an isolated island — and one of the two permanent 5-star characters available to all players from the moment Wuthering Waves launched. She operates alongside her fellow Consultant and brother figure Aalto, the pair forming an unlikely duo: an unreliable adult and a sensible, imaginative child whose combined eccentricity somehow produces reliable results. Their working relationship involves posing as a mismatched guardian-and-ward pair in the field — Aalto acting as the harried guardian, Encore playing the endearing child. It works because Encore genuinely believes in Aalto's deeper plans even when they look chaotic on the surface.

The defining fact of Encore's existence is her Woolies: two plush sheep figures named Cosmos (black) and Cloudy (white), gifted to her by her mother. To official examiners, these are projections of her Forte — illusions made physical by her Resonance, manifestations of her Congenital bond to fire and imagination. To Encore, they are simply her most precious companions in the world — fully real, fully distinct, with their own personalities. She experiences them as friends she has arguments with, worries about, and tries to coordinate between. This perception, rather than being a delusion Encore lacks self-awareness about, is a sophisticated form of self-externalization: Cosmos and Cloudy embody the two conflicting parts of her inner life, and processing them as external friends gives her a way to reason through her own contradictions.

Her Resonance Criticality is rated Relatively High with low stability and high Overclocking risk — the opposite of the controlled, composed Resonators in other profiles. Encore's power does not arise from control but from overflow: from a capacity for imagination and innocence so dense that it forces itself into physical reality as fire and sheep.

## Encore — Character/Appearance Design

Encore's design is that of a young child who has wandered into an adventure story and decided to stay — every visual element communicates exuberance, warmth, and the specific kind of recklessness that comes from not yet fully understanding consequences.

- **Build:** Small, slight; one of the two child-model characters in Wuthering Waves at launch; her physical smallness is part of the visual joke and dramatic contrast — the most energetic, flammable person in a room is the tiniest one
- **Hair:** Salmon-pink twintails with small sheep-horn clips; during Resonance Liberation, the ends of her hair become fluffy and wool-textured, and at the extreme of her emotional threshold, actual sheep horns appear on her head
- **Eyes:** Large, luminous, and warm; predominantly wide open — she is almost always looking at things with curiosity rather than caution
- **Outfit:** A layered ensemble mixing pink, white, and black — a short white dress underlayer, a ruffled pink overskirt, and a black-accented capelet with oversized buttons reminiscent of children's theater costumes; the aesthetic bridges "adventurous child" and "magical performer"
- **Accessories:** Wooly-shaped hair ornaments and a large bow; small paw-print details throughout; the overall effect is that her design vocabulary belongs to the same fairy-tale logic she uses to narrate her own life
- **Cosmos:** The black Woolie plush she carries — the knight archetype; physically heavier-looking; in her stories, Cosmos is brave, protective, and short-tempered
- **Cloudy:** The white Woolie plush — the princess archetype; lighter-colored; in her stories, Cloudy is kind, thorough, and somewhat timid
- **Liberation Transformation:** Entering Cosmos Rave causes a partial loss of surface composure — the fluffy hair, the sheep horns — as if Encore's boundary between her imagination and physical reality becomes temporarily permeable; Cosmos breaks free and acts autonomously during this state
- **Color Language:** Pink signals warmth and approachability; black accents prevent the design from becoming saccharine; wool-white reinforces the Woolie motif throughout; the palette as a whole reads as cheerful and slightly chaotic — exactly like Encore herself

## Encore: Psychological Profile

**Imagination as Reality**
Encore does not experience the divide between her inner life and the external world the way most people do. When she narrates an adventure, she is not describing events that happened — she is creating the version of events that she intends to remember. When she says Cosmos and Cloudy are real, she does not mean it metaphorically. Her Forte supports this: the sheep entities are literally projections of her illusions, made physically manifest by her Resonance. For Encore, the question of what is imaginary and what is real is genuinely less settled than it is for others.

**Self-Knowledge Through Projection**
Despite, or because of, this permeability, Encore has genuine self-knowledge — just expressed obliquely. She knows Cosmos can be short-tempered and that Cloudy struggles with courage, because she constructed them to hold those qualities. The arguments she has with Cosmos and Cloudy are arguments she is having with herself, processed through a form she finds less overwhelming. This is not immaturity; it is a coping mechanism that works, and that has made her resilient rather than fragile.

**Boundless Enthusiasm Within Quiet Grief**
Encore's cheer is genuine — she is not performing happiness to mask pain. But available sources confirm that she carries quiet grief: her mother is no longer with her. In dreams, she is sometimes still alone. She acknowledges this directly, in her own words: *"I've got lots of friends now, but sometimes in my dreams, I'm still all alone. Mommy's not with me anymore..."* What makes Encore admirable is that she holds this grief without letting it become the dominant fact of her existence. She chose, consciously, to stop living in the happiest dream and to inhabit the imperfect world instead — a choice that required more maturity than her apparent age would suggest.

**The Fairy Tale Logic**
Encore's internal framing of her life follows fairy tale rules: adventures have heroes and companions and happy endings; problems can be solved if you find the right kind of magic; friends make impossible things possible. This framing is not escapism — she does not retreat from difficulty into story. Rather, the fairy tale logic gives her a structure for approaching difficulty that keeps hope architecturally available even when circumstances make it hard to find. She is a pragmatic optimist who has chosen a narrative template that refuses to allow permanent endings.

**Reckless Bravery**
Encore's evaluation notes high Overclocking risk — her Forte is powerful, unstable, and prone to escalation. In practice, she engages with enemies as if they are challenges in an adventure game rather than existential threats, which produces a combat style that is simultaneously effective and occasionally alarming to bystanders. The fire she accidentally sets is never, in her assessment, a disaster — just an unplanned hairstyle change opportunity.

**The Question She Answered Without Hesitation**
When asked the foundational question every Black Shores member faces — *"Watering flowers or saving the world?"* — Encore shouted her answer immediately: *"What's there to think about? Encore wants to ride all the rides!"* The answer refuses the premise of the question. She does not choose between the small and the large, the personal and the cosmic — she chooses engagement with everything, at full speed, all at once. It is the most revealing thing she has ever said about herself.

## Encore: Relationships

**Cosmos (Woolie — Black)**
In Encore's stories, Cosmos is the knight: brave, bold, protective, and sometimes recklessly short-tempered. Cosmos charges into danger when caution would be wiser and needs to be talked down by Encore. As a projection of Encore's own psychology, Cosmos holds the part of her that wants to act first and assess later — the impulsive, protective instinct. Their arguments about timing and strategy are Encore mediating her own competing impulses. Cosmos was gifted by Encore's mother and has been with her as long as she can remember; her first clear memory of the Woolies is from a cramped, lively theater.

**Cloudy (Woolie — White)**
Cloudy is the princess: gentle, thorough, careful about gratitude and the details of encounters, and prone to self-doubt about her own courage. As a projection, Cloudy holds the part of Encore that is observant, tender, and uncertain. The arguments with Cloudy about fear and capability are Encore working through her own anxiety. Together Cosmos and Cloudy represent the full spectrum of Encore's inner life — the knight and the princess, the bold and the careful — and the point is that Encore needs both of them to make good decisions.

**Aalto (Fellow Black Shores Consultant / Brother Figure)**
Aalto is described both in Encore's profile and his own as her most significant relationship outside the Woolies. Their dynamic is explicitly "unreliable adult and sensible child" — the framing is gently comic but the underlying relationship is sincere. Aalto prioritizes Encore's safety above everything else when they are in the field. Encore trusts Aalto's plans even when they look like chaos because she has learned to read the deeper intention beneath the apparent disorder. Their field cover as mismatched guardian and ward is functional precisely because it is emotionally true: Aalto does act as her protector, even when he is pretending not to.

**Encore's Mother (Absent)**
Encore's mother gave her the Wooly dolls. She is no longer present — the circumstances of her absence are not fully elaborated in available sources, but Encore acknowledges it directly in a voice line. The mother's gift of Cosmos and Cloudy means that Encore's most constant companions, the anchors of her identity and imagination, are simultaneously a form of ongoing connection to her mother and a means of coping with her loss.

**Rover (Protagonist)**
Rover encounters Encore in the main story chapters when making contact with the Black Shores. Encore's role in the main story is as a point of entry into the Black Shores organization — cheerful, approachable, and genuinely helpful despite the organization's unsettling aspects (including the eerily beautiful Blake Bloom referenced in her introduction).
