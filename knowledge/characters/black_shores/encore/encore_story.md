## Encore Official Introduction

*"Encore, a consultant from the Black Shores, is a quirky, lively, and spunky girl who loves to recount her friendships and adventures through fantasy and fairy tales. She always has two Woolies with her, who are Encore's best friends and protectors. With Cosmos and Cloudy, Encore travels all over the world!"*

Her personal introduction from the official website reads: *"If you've got questions that can't be solved, no worries, just ask Encore for help! If you've got answers that feel out of grasp, no worries, just ask Encore to help! If there are places you can't reach... still, no worries, let's all shout that name together — 'Encore'!"*

The Archive description frames her in fuller complexity: *"Encore, a bright and lovable member of the Black Shores, is always accompanied by two Woolies: Cosmos, representing her painful past, and Cloudy, her kind-hearted essence. She radiates cheer and brings joy to those around her, but few witness the quiet sadness she carries."*

## Encore Personality

Encore's personality operates on a principle that looks simple from the outside and is profoundly considered from the inside: she chooses to be happy. This is not the happiness of someone who has not encountered grief — she has lost her mother, she has been alone, she has dreamed of isolation even while surrounded by friends. The happiness is the choice she made when she decided to stop living in the happiest dream and inhabit the imperfect world instead.

The fairy tale logic she applies to her own life is not a defense mechanism in the clinical sense — it does not prevent her from processing difficult things. It is more accurately a framework: a set of narrative rules that keep hope architecturally available by refusing to allow any situation to be permanently closed. In a fairy tale, the dark forest is not the end of the story. This framework is what lets Encore walk into genuinely dangerous situations with the apparent confidence of someone who has already decided how the story goes.

Her Woolies are the most sophisticated expression of this. To a clinical examiner, they are Forte projections — external manifestations of her resonance, physically real sheep made from imagination. To Encore, they are her friends, her companions, the ones who were always there. She is not wrong about either of these things. Cosmos and Cloudy are simultaneously the technical output of her Congenital Resonance and the most meaningful relationships in her life. The fact that they originate from her does not make them less real to her; if anything, it means she carries her most important relationships literally within herself.

The quiet sadness the Archive notes is not absent from Encore — it surfaces in dreams, in voice lines, in the specific way she says her mother is no longer with her. But it does not define her forward motion. She said she would live every moment to the fullest when she decided to stop living in the dream. She means it.

*Forte Power Designation: Unknown (suspected linked to innocence and proneness to illusions)*

The exact time of Resonator Encore's Awakening remains uncertain. Resonator Encore's birthplace is the New Federation. Her Tacet Mark is located on her upper back. The precise source of her Resonance remains unknown. It is suspected to be related to her **innocence and proneness to illusions**.

Post-Awakening observations confirm that Encore projects two sheep entities — one black, one white — which accompany her at all times. Based on the Resonance Evaluation Report and Resonator Encore's oral accounts, the sheep entities may perhaps be **projections of her illusions, a visual representation of Encore's Forte.**

Resonance Criticality is rated **Relatively High**, with **low stability and high risk of Overclocking**. This is among the least stable Resonance profiles in the evaluation records — the inverse of controlled Resonators like Jiyan or Changli. Current Diagnosis: Routine examinations are advised under the supervision of the Resonator's guardian. Classification: **Congenital Resonator.**

Researcher's Annotation: *"Based on the Resonance Evaluation Report and Resonator Encore's oral accounts, the sheep entities may perhaps be projections of her illusions, a visual representation of Encore's Forte."*

This annotation is notable for its epistemic caution — the evaluators are not certain whether the Woolies are standard Forte output or something more specific to Encore's particular mode of imagination. The hedged language ("may perhaps be") suggests that the evaluators found Encore's Resonance difficult to categorize through standard frameworks.

## Encore Character Story I: "The Theater and the Woolies"

Encore cannot quite remember how long Cosmos and Cloudy have been part of her life. It feels as if they have always been there, intertwined with her existence from the very beginning. Perhaps she met them when she was too young to remember.

Her first vivid memories of the Woolies trace back to a cramped yet lively theater. Back then — she was very small — a captivating play unfolded amidst enthusiastic applause and the cheers of children. Encore was in the audience. Cosmos and Cloudy were with her, or appeared beside her, or perhaps she summoned them without understanding that she was doing it. The distinction is one she has never found meaningful.

The Woolies are gifts from her mother. This fact is stated plainly in multiple sources: *"Wooly dolls gifted to Encore by her mother, who are always by her side and listen to Encore."* Whether the physical dolls preceded the Forte projections, or whether the projections are what the dolls became, is not resolved in available lore. What is clear is that Encore's mother deliberately gave her these companions, and that they have been the constant of her life in a way her mother could not be.

In Encore's developing mythology of the Woolies, Cosmos is the knight: brave, protective of the Woolies from darkness, but prone to charging ahead without thinking. Cloudy is the princess: kind-hearted, noting down all the good things in life along the journey, but sometimes struggling to find her own courage. Only Encore knows these inner lives. To anyone observing from the outside, they are plush animals. To Encore, they are characters in an ongoing story she is also living.

The small squabbles she has with them — telling Cosmos not to charge in recklessly, encouraging Cloudy that she can handle this — are among the most revealing moments in her character. She is not talking to toys. She is negotiating between her own impulse toward action and her capacity for care, finding a path between recklessness and timidity that she can walk.

## Encore Character Story II: "What's for Breakfast Tomorrow?"

*"Encore was living in the happiest dream ever... but it had to stop, like all dreams do. When a dream is over, it's another brand new day ahead. Time to live it to the fullest!"*

This story is not explicitly titled in available source materials but represents the pivotal internal choice of Encore's character arc. At some point — after the loss of her mother, after whatever period of difficulty preceded her arrival at the Black Shores — Encore made a decision. She had been living in the happiest dream. It ended, as all dreams do. And she decided that the appropriate response to a dream ending is not grief that prevents the next day from starting, but purposeful re-entry into the imperfect world.

The closing thought of this reflection is characteristic: *"Hehe, but first, what's for breakfast tomorrow?"*

This sentence is doing more than it appears to do. It is not deflection — Encore has just acknowledged her mother's absence and her solitude in dreams directly. It is a return to the small, immediate, concrete future. What is for breakfast tomorrow is a question with an answer. The answer requires being alive, being present, having a tomorrow. Choosing to care about breakfast is choosing to persist, and Encore makes that choice with a cheerful noise rather than a declaration.

The Archive summary names Cosmos as representing "her painful past" and Cloudy as "her kind-hearted essence" — a reading that reframes the knight-and-princess mythology. If Cosmos holds the painful past (the loss, the isolation, the Overclocking risk), then Encore's relationship with Cosmos — directing him, managing his short temper, telling him when not to charge — is Encore's ongoing management of her own grief. She does not suppress it; she converses with it. She tells it when the timing is wrong. She brings it along.

## Encore Character Story III: "The Question and the Answer"

Every member of the Black Shores is eventually asked a foundational question. The exact framing varies — sometimes it is described as a test, sometimes as an orientation tradition, sometimes simply as a question that reveals who the person is. For Encore, it was:

*"Watering flowers or saving the world?"*

Encore did not hesitate.

*"What's there to think about? Encore wants to ride all the rides!"*

The answer is so thoroughly Encore that it functions as her thesis statement. She does not choose between the small pleasure (flowers, breakfast, a relay race) and the large purpose (the world, the mission, the adventure). The premise of the choice assumes a scarcity — that you can only do one thing with the time you have — and Encore's answer rejects the scarcity. She will do both. She will do everything. Not out of naivety about constraints, but out of a genuine refusal to let constraints determine which things she cares about.

This is what makes her effective in the field: she is not optimizing between competing goods. She is attempting to secure all of them simultaneously, which from a strategy perspective looks reckless and from a results perspective often works. She charged into the Black Shores with this answer, and the Black Shores apparently found it acceptable — she is a Consultant, trusted with real missions, sent into the world with Aalto to do genuine work.

The story of Aalto and Encore deciding to "save the world" is preserved in the character materials. Aalto had put on his sunglasses, and Encore had just spun with both Woolies in turn (first Cosmos, then Cloudy, because she had learned to treat all her friends with equal love and care). She asked what they were going to do. He said: *"To save the world, of course!"* She embraced her Woolie companions again and leapt into the next adventure.

## Encore: Cherished Items

**Cosmos (Black Woolie Plush)**
*"Wooly dolls gifted to Encore by her mother, who are always by her side and listens to Encore. The Woolies are not only projections of Encore's personality but also important companions in her life."*
Cosmos is the knight — bold, protective, short-tempered, and always present. He was there before Encore can reliably remember. He is the part of her that holds the painful past, the Overclocking risk, the impulse to charge. He is also the reason she is never alone, even in the most difficult moments. In combat, Cosmos is literally freed during Liberation to run wild — a visual representation of what happens when the controlled part of her psychology is released.

**Cloudy (White Woolie Plush)**
Cloudy is the princess — kind-hearted, thorough about gratitude, prone to uncertainty. She represents Encore's observant, tender inner life. The arguments Encore has with Cloudy about whether to be brave enough are the most private conversations she has. Cloudy is her mother's gift held as her own essential nature. Both Woolies together make Encore complete.

**Purple Coral Specialty Dish**
Encore's specialty dish is made from purple coral gathered from the Black Shores and carefully cooked with various ingredients. The dish is mentioned in character materials as her specialty — she made it herself. The act of gathering specific ingredients from the Black Shores (a place associated with eeriness and mystery in other characters' descriptions) and turning them into something nourishing and shareable is very Encore: she finds the good things in wherever she is.

## Encore: Ideals

**Passion — Happy Endings Are Worth Making**
Encore's defining passion is the creation of happy endings — not the consumption of pre-existing ones. She does not wait for happiness to arrive; she goes looking for the ingredients and assembles it herself. This extends beyond her personal life into her professional mandate: she finds new clients for the Black Shores, she goes everywhere, she says yes to adventures. The world will have more happy endings in it because Encore passed through.

**Resonance — The Woolies as Living Bond**
Encore's resonance with Cosmos and Cloudy is the most intimate relationship of her life. It is also the most technically complex — they are Forte projections and gifts from her mother and autonomous companions and projections of her personality, all simultaneously. This multiplicity does not confuse Encore; it is simply the texture of her closest bond. She resonates, in every sense, with them.

**Gratitude — For Every Friend Made**
Encore explicitly names friendship as one of the organizing goods of her life. *"I've got lots of friends now"* is said in the same breath as acknowledging her mother is gone — the friends are the evidence that the choice to return from the dream was correct. She tends friendships with the same deliberateness she tends her Woolies: *"As she had grown older, she learned to treat all her friends with equal love and care."*

**Perseverance — Riding All the Rides**
The answer to "watering flowers or saving the world?" is the most compressed statement of Encore's perseverance: she refuses to stop at one thing. She will persist through difficulty not by ignoring it but by maintaining a larger inventory of things worth persisting for. When one thing is hard, she moves to the next and circles back. The list of rides is long, and she intends to ride all of them.

## Encore: Narrative Chronicle

- **[Before Memory — Birth and Early Awakening]** Encore is born in the New Federation; classified as a Congenital Resonator; the timing of her Awakening is uncertain even to evaluators; her mother gives her the Wooly plush dolls Cosmos (black) and Cloudy (white)
- **[Early Childhood — The Theater Memory]** Encore's first vivid memory involving the Woolies is set in a cramped, lively theater — a captivating play, enthusiastic applause, children cheering; Cosmos and Cloudy are already fully real to her in this memory
- **[Childhood — Loss of Mother]** Encore's mother is no longer with her; the exact circumstances are not described in available sources; the Woolies — her mother's gift — become the primary anchor of continuity in her life
- **[Childhood — The Dream Period]** Encore passes through a period she describes as *"the happiest dream ever"* — likely a coping phase following her mother's absence; she is aware, looking back, that it was a dream; she chooses to end it
- **[Decision — Return to the World]** Encore consciously decides to stop living in the happiest dream and to inhabit the real world fully; this decision is framed as a choice for aliveness over protective withdrawal; she marks it with the question: *"what's for breakfast tomorrow?"*
- **[Pre-Version 1.0 — Black Shores Recruitment]** Encore joins the Black Shores as a Consultant; when asked *"Watering flowers or saving the world?"* she answers: *"Encore wants to ride all the rides!"* — a response that apparently satisfied the organization's requirements
- **[Pre-Version 1.0 — Partnership with Aalto]** Encore and Aalto become partners within the Black Shores; their dynamic as mismatched-guardian-and-ward (an unreliable adult and a sensible child) forms and stabilizes; Aalto learns to take Encore's Woolie belief seriously; Encore learns to read meaning beneath Aalto's chaos; they adopt the cover of a mismatched family for fieldwork
- **[Pre-Version 1.0 — Field Work]** Encore and Aalto travel extensively on Black Shores missions, finding new clients and fulfilling consulting work; Encore collects friends and adventures and narrates both as fairy tales
- **[Version 1.0 — Jinzhou Arc]** Rover encounters Encore when seeking to make contact with the Black Shores; Encore serves as the approachable entry point into the organization for the main story; her companion story **Encore's Steaming Adventure!** provides additional Encore-specific narrative content
- **[Ongoing]** Encore continues operating as a Black Shores Consultant alongside Aalto; she remains one of the two permanent 5-star characters accessible to all players throughout Wuthering Waves, her availability reflecting her institutional role as an accessible, ever-present figure rather than a rare one
