<!-- Sources: https://www.prydwen.gg/wuthering-waves/characters/the-shorekeeper/, https://game8.co/games/Wuthering-Waves/archives/463667, https://wuthering.gg/characters/shorekeeper, https://guildjen.com/shorekeeper-character-guide/ -->

## Shorekeeper: Combat Archetype and Role

- **Element/Attribute:** Spectro
- **Weapon Type:** Rectifier
- **Role:** Healer / Support — Universal crit buffer, team healer, KO-prevention utility. Shorekeeper creates the Stellarealm field via Resonance Liberation, which grants continuous team healing plus up to +12.5% Crit Rate and +25% Crit DMG to all allies, scaled from her Energy Regen. Her Outro provides 15% Damage Amplification that persists through character swaps. She is the premier support for any team composition that does not rely on coordinated off-field attack synergies, and sits at SS Tier across all endgame modes.

## Shorekeeper: Key Resources (Forte Mechanics Overview)

Shorekeeper's combat revolves around two tracked resources within her Forte Circuit (Astral Chord):

- **Collapsed Cores** (up to 5 active on field): Generated each time a Normal Attack hits an enemy. Cores spawn on the field and after 6s convert automatically into **Flare Star Butterflies** — homing Spectro projectiles that attack enemies and generate Concerto Energy. If 5 Cores are already active, the next Normal Attack hit instantly converts the oldest Core into a Butterfly.
- **Empirical Data** (0–5 segments): Generated by specific attack stages (Basic Attack Stage 1/2/4 grant 1 each; Stage 3 grants 2; Mid-air Attack and Dodge Counter each grant 1). When all 5 segments are filled, Heavy Attack becomes **Illation** (pulls nearby targets, converts all Collapsed Cores to Butterflies) and Mid-air Attack becomes **Transmutation** (deals Spectro DMG burst, converts all Collapsed Cores).

Both mechanics feed into the core goal: generating Concerto Energy as rapidly as possible to reach her Outro and immediately trigger the rotation that upgrades the Stellarealm to Supernal tier.

## Shorekeeper Forte Circuit: Collapsed Cores / Empirical Data

**Collapsed Core generation loop:** Each hit in the Basic Attack chain (Stages 1–4) generates 1 Collapsed Core. Stage 3 also generates 2 Empirical Data simultaneously. A full 4-hit Basic Attack chain therefore generates 4 Collapsed Cores, 5 Empirical Data (1+1+2+1), and activates Illation/Transmutation for a burst conversion.

**Flare Star Butterfly behavior:** Butterflies track and attack a single nearest target dealing Spectro DMG. Each butterfly is worth significant Concerto Energy. Maintaining a near-constant stream of 5 Butterflies converting through the Normal Attack chain is the primary Concerto-generation engine during Shorekeeper's field time.

**Resonance Skill — Chaos Theory:** Restores HP to all nearby party members and summons 5 **Dim Star Butterflies**, which also track and attack enemies dealing Spectro DMG. Unlike Flare Stars, Dim Stars are not generated by Forte — they appear only from the Resonance Skill and from the Intro Skill (Enlightenment). This makes skill usage important for burst Concerto generation in addition to healing.

**Rotation principle:** Shorekeeper's on-field time is designed to be efficient and brief. The ideal loop is: Intro (Enlightenment, 5 Dim Stars) → Normal Attack chain (generate 4 Collapsed Cores + 5 Empirical Data) → Illation or Transmutation (burst-convert all Cores to Flare Stars) → Resonance Skill (5 more Dim Stars + healing) → Resonance Liberation (Stellarealm) → Outro. Done well, this takes approximately 10–14 seconds of field time and provides 30 seconds of Stellarealm coverage to allies.

## Shorekeeper: Character Kit: Basic Attack — Origin Calculus

Shorekeeper performs up to 4 consecutive attacks dealing Spectro DMG. Each successful hit generates 1 Collapsed Core (up to 5 on field). Stage 3 is notable for also generating 2 Empirical Data (unique among stages), making it the most efficient hit in the chain for Forte activation.

The Heavy Attack enters **Unbound Form**, continuously consuming STA. While in this form, Shorekeeper generates 1 segment of Deductive Data per second and automatically collects nearby plant collectibles. Each accumulated Deductive Data converts to 1 Empirical Data and 1 Collapsed Core when Unbound Form ends via STA depletion or Basic Attack input. Heavy Attack is generally not used in optimized rotations due to STA cost.

Mid-air Attack performs a plunging attack generating 1 Collapsed Core. Following it immediately with Normal Attack continues from Basic Attack Stage 2.

## Shorekeeper: Character Kit: Resonance Liberation — End Loop

Shorekeeper's Liberation summons the **Stellarealm**, a tiered field that evolves through three stages as party members use Intro Skills within it. Duration: 30s. This ability has an unusually high activation cost of 175 Resonance Energy.

**Outer Stellarealm (base):** Periodically restores HP to all party members within range. Heal tick: 220 + 1.20% HP, every 3s.

**Inner Stellarealm (after 1 Intro Skill within range):** Adds a Crit Rate bonus to all party members. For every 0.2% of Shorekeeper's Energy Regen, party members gain 0.01% Crit Rate, up to **+12.5% Crit Rate** (requires 250% Energy Regen to reach cap). Inherits healing from Outer.

**Supernal Stellarealm (after 2 Intro Skills within range):** Adds a Crit DMG bonus on top of Inner effects. For every 0.1% of Shorekeeper's Energy Regen, party members gain 0.01% Crit DMG, up to **+25% Crit DMG** (requires 250% Energy Regen to reach cap). When the Supernal Stellarealm is active, Shorekeeper's next Intro Skill is replaced by **Intro Skill Discernment** (once per Stellarealm generation).

## Shorekeeper: Inherent Passives

**Life Entwined (Inherent 1):** When a Resonator other than Shorekeeper is about to be knocked out, they are instead healed for **50% of Shorekeeper's HP**, and Shorekeeper loses the same amount of HP (minimum 1 HP). This cheat-death effect can trigger once every 10 minutes. Practically, this provides near-permanent death prevention in any fight shorter than the cooldown.

**Self Gravitation (Inherent 2):** While the active Resonator is within the effective range of a Stellarealm, Shorekeeper's Energy Regen increases by **+10%**. If Rover is on the same team, Rover's Energy Regen also increases by **+10%**. This passive is critical for build optimization: it effectively lowers the target Energy Regen on Echoes from 250% to **240%**, as the 10% bonus is applied whenever the field is active. Combined with the Fallacy of No Return echo's +10% ER proc, the gap closes further.

## Shorekeeper: Intro/Outro Skills

**Intro Skill — Proof of Existence:**

The skill has two forms:

*Enlightenment (standard):* Shorekeeper appears and restores HP to all nearby party members, then summons **5 Dim Star Butterflies** to track and attack a target dealing Spectro DMG. This is classified as Resonance Skill DMG. Generates 10 Concerto Energy.

*Discernment (enhanced, replaces Enlightenment after Supernal Stellarealm activates):* Shorekeeper ends the current Stellarealm, restores HP to all nearby party members, and deals a massive burst of Spectro DMG to targets — scaled from her **HP** (not ATK), guaranteed to Critical Hit, and classified as Resonance Liberation DMG. Generates 20 Concerto Energy. This is the most damaging single hit in her kit and the payoff for successfully upgrading the Stellarealm to Supernal.

| Stat | Enlightenment (Lv.1) | Discernment (Lv.1) |
|---|---|---|
| DMG | 22.79% × 5 (Spectro) | 9.88% × 3 HP (Spectro) |
| Healing | 130 + 0.60% HP | 145 + 0.66% HP |
| Concerto Regen | 10 | 20 |

**Outro Skill — Binary Butterfly:**
Shorekeeper summons 1 Flare Star Butterfly and 1 Dim Star Butterfly to orbit the incoming on-field Resonator for up to 30s. While active: (1) if the on-field Resonator is hit or launched, pressing Dodge allows an immediate recovery and triggers a successful dodge + possible Dodge Counter (up to 5 times); (2) all nearby party members' DMG is **Amplified by 15%**. This amplification is not lost when the buffed character swaps out — it remains active for the full 30s regardless of field time.

## Shorekeeper: Resonance Chains (Sequences)

**S1 — Sequence Node 1:**
Stellarealms created by Resonance Liberation End Loop receive three upgrades: effective range extended by **+150%**, duration extended by **+10s** (now 40s total), and casting Intro Skill Discernment **no longer ends the existing Stellarealm**. This last effect is the most significant: at S0, using Discernment detonates and closes the Supernal Stellarealm, ending all Crit buffs. At S1, the Stellarealm persists through Discernment, meaning Crit buffs continue for the remainder of the 40s while the detonation damage and Discernment healing still occur. The range increase also substantially reduces positioning pressure. Considered the single highest-value sequence node in the game for a support character at this tier.

**S2 — Sequence Node 2:**
The Outer Stellarealm (base level) now also grants **+40% ATK to all nearby party members**. This is an enormous support stat — layered on top of the Crit buffs from Inner/Supernal, it means all three tiers of the Stellarealm now provide substantial buffs. S2 elevates Shorekeeper's team damage contribution significantly, particularly for ATK-scaling DPS characters.

**S3 — Sequence Node 3:**
Casting Resonance Liberation End Loop grants Shorekeeper **+20 Concerto Energy** (once per 25s). This eases rotation timing by providing an extra burst of Concerto at Liberation activation, helping to immediately generate Concerto toward the Outro.

**S4 — Sequence Node 4:**
Grants **+70% Healing Bonus** when casting Resonance Skill Chaos Theory. Substantially improves burst healing from the skill, valuable in content with large spike damage but limited impact on the core Crit-buff rotation.

**S5 — Sequence Node 5:**
Extends the range of the pulling effect of **Basic Attack Stage 3 by +50%** and the pulling effect of **Illation by +30%**. A quality-of-life upgrade for grouping enemies. Low priority relative to S1, S2, and S6.

**S6 — Sequence Node 6:**
Increases the **DMG Multiplier of Intro Skill Discernment by +42%**. Casting Discernment grants Shorekeeper **+500% Crit DMG for its duration**, turning the Discernment nuke into an astronomically large single hit — the HP-scaling damage combined with guaranteed Crit and 500% Crit DMG makes this one of the highest single-hit multipliers in the game. S6 Shorekeeper transitions from a pure support to a hybrid that deals impressive burst damage alongside her team-buffing role.

## Shorekeeper: Recommended Echo Sets

**Best Build — Rejuvenating Glow (5-piece):**
The definitive set for Shorekeeper. The 5-piece bonus activates on casting Resonance Skill (Chaos Theory) and grants all nearby teammates **+15% ATK** for 15s — stacking cleanly with the Outro's 15% Damage Amplification and the S2 Stellarealm ATK buff. Shorekeeper is considered the premier carrier of this set, more reliably proccing it than other healers due to her skill rotation.

**Main Echo:** Fallacy of No Return (4-cost) — provides a proc of **+10% Energy Regen** on healing (further reducing the 250% ER gap) and a team **ATK buff** on its active use. It also carries the Rejuvenating Glow sonata effect, making it the natural 4-cost selection. Impermanence Heron (Moonlit Clouds set) is the F2P-accessible alternative.

## Shorekeeper: Best Weapon

**Stellar Symphony (Signature, 5-star Rectifier):**
ATK — / Energy Regen +77% (Lv.90). Passive: HP +12%. Restores 8 Concerto Energy on Resonance Liberation cast (once per 20s). This weapon is purpose-built for Shorekeeper: the +77% ER means she needs only ~100% additional ER from Echoes to hit the 250% target (accounting for Inherent 2's passive +10%), dramatically opening up main stat slots for Healing Bonus or Crit DMG instead. The HP% bonus improves both healing output and Discernment damage.

**Variation (5-star alternative):** Provides Energy Regen and HP bonuses. Less ER than Stellar Symphony but strong second option.

**Comet Flare (4-star):** Healing Bonus and HP%. Best accessible 4-star option, tradeable for 5-star pulls.

**Call of the Abyss (4-star):** Energy Regen mainstat. Directly addresses the 250% ER target from the weapon slot, allowing HP% on more Echo slots.

**F2P option:** Any 4-star Rectifier with Energy Regen sub-stat is viable. The challenge without Stellar Symphony is reaching 250% ER through Echo stats alone, which competes with HP% slots.

## Shorekeeper: Best Teams

**Team 1 — Universal Crit Buffer (Any Main DPS):**
[Any Main DPS] / [Sub-DPS or Second Support] / **Shorekeeper** (Healer/Buffer). Shorekeeper slots into virtually any team that does not specifically require coordinated off-field attacks (which Verina provides). Her +12.5% Crit Rate and +25% Crit DMG, 15% Damage Amplification, and continuous healing make her best-in-slot for most DPS characters who lack built-in coordinated attack synergies.

**Team 2 — Camellya Hypercarry:**
Camellya (Main DPS) / Sanhua or Danjin (Sub-DPS) / **Shorekeeper** (Healer/Buffer). Camellya benefits enormously from Crit Rate and Crit DMG amplification since her kit multiplies Crit value through its specific mechanics. Shorekeeper's S1 is particularly valuable here since Camellya's optimal play requires staying on field for extended periods within the Stellarealm.

**Team 3 — Carlotta Hypercarry:**
Carlotta (Main DPS) / Zhezhi (Sub-DPS/Buffer) / **Shorekeeper** (Healer/Buffer). Carlotta scales heavily from Crit stats; Shorekeeper's Crit buffs pair naturally with Zhezhi's ATK and Glacio DMG Bonus stacking to produce Carlotta's optimal damage ceiling.

**Team 4 — Verina Comparison:**
Shorekeeper generally outperforms Verina in raw damage multiplication for most teams (Crit buffs > ATK% buffs in multiplicative value), but Verina's off-field coordinated attacks are irreplaceable for characters like Rover (Spectro), Mortefi, and others who explicitly synergize with her. When in doubt: run the DPS whose kit says "coordinated attack" with Verina; run all others with Shorekeeper.

**Team 5 — Rover (Spectro) Exception:**
Rover (Spectro) / Shorekeeper / Verina. Spectro Rover's kit synergizes with both — Rover can use Shorekeeper's Stellarealm while also triggering Verina's coordinated attacks. At higher investment this 3-support approach is viable in endgame. More practically: Verina is the better fit for Spectro Rover due to coordinated attack value; Shorekeeper covers all other teams.
