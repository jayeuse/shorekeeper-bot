# Camellya Story Documentation

## Camellya Official Introduction

*Bloom Bearer from the Black Shores, Camellya is a laid-back character free from shackles, with her personal aroma of "danger." Intrigued by the belief in fate, she wanders the lands of her own accord, following such a chain of scenarios and its vague guidance.*

Camellya is an enigma wrapped in a camellia. A Bloom Bearer who handles combat-oriented germination tests for Black Shores candidates, she refuses all sense of institutional responsibility, existing within the organization for her own purposes while treating its resources as a personal archive. Her philosophy, stated simply: she joined the Black Shores to pass time. Justice, truth, and the salvation of Solaris are not her concerns. The only thing that reliably holds her attention is Rover — a singular fixation that has persisted across multiple eras, multiple cycles of memory loss, and more suffering than she allows herself to name directly.

## Camellya Personality

Camellya's personality is constructed around a single paradox: she is genuinely indifferent to almost everything, and genuinely fixated on one thing. The indifference is not performance — it is the natural posture of a being who has experienced enough memory loss to treat emotional investment as epistemologically unreliable. Caring about outcomes when you might not remember them tomorrow is a form of unnecessary suffering she has simply stopped engaging in. This makes her company pleasant in an odd way: she is not anxious, not status-seeking, not agenda-laden. She is present, sharp, and waiting to be surprised.

What surprises her is rarity. Rover is the only person who consistently provides it. Her voicelines describe a decade of boredom punctuated only by Rover's existence — not because Rover is uniquely heroic or uniquely powerful, but because Rover is consistently unexpected. This is the highest compliment in Camellya's vocabulary: "you keep me on my toes." The corollary is that she has been paying very close attention to this person for a very long time, from a careful distance, collecting information without revealing her own interest.

Her relationship to pain is notable for its specific quality: not stoicism, but gallows humor. She laughs in the medical pod when the doctors confirm her condition is untreatable. She finds the idea of giving Rover a flower that will die when she dies both "stunning" and faintly sad — and chooses, in the same breath, to do it anyway. There is no tragedy in her self-presentation, only a cheerful acknowledgment of the facts. She will follow her instincts until death finds her. She will try not to die too soon. These are the same decision, framed differently.

The character stories reveal a further layer: the woman Camellya is now was preceded by a woman (Lady Flora) who was warmer, more openly attached, given to small complaints and small joys. Lady Flora huddled against the cold rather than ignoring it. Lady Flora worried that Rover went west without waking her, that she would be a burden if she lost control, that her dreams were bleeding into reality in ways she couldn't manage. Camellya has shed the overt attachment — partly by choice, partly by loss — but the core drive remains unchanged. The seed persists even when the flower is different.

## Camellya Character Story I: "To the Days Ahead"

*She awoke from her dream.*

*The sun broke over the horizon, casting a warm glow across the Black Shores. It was the dawn of another ordinary day. Camellya opened her eyes, stretching leisurely atop the treetops. Her movements were graceful, and a fall would be unthinkable. Even if she did slip, the vines would cradle her fall. She loved sleeping like this, away from any disturbance or people.*

*"What a beautiful morning," she mused. She felt stable, in mind and body, and the thought of needing the medical pod seemed distant. This was a good thing.*

*She gave Rover a gift — a bracelet designed to monitor frequencies, just like the one Rover had given her before. It was the first gift she had ever prepared for someone.*

*"How peculiar!" she mused. To wonder about someone's safety, or whether they even existed — it was truly a novel feeling! Surely, it meant that she cared about their life. Such thoughts were foreign — she had never cared about her own life, or that of others.*

*Still, she had no intention of following Rover to Rinascita. Their views on certain aspects of Solaris' history differed, and just as she did not intend to meddle in their choices, she didn't feel the need to explain her own. In the Stellar Matrix, she hadn't completely lost all memories of her past. Yes, her past… Camellya squinted, a soft hum escaping her lips as she contemplated her next move. She wanted to investigate further on her own, biding her time until the moment felt right.*

*She had followed Rover's travels from Jinzhou till now. A singular force, always moving forward, gathering a diverse array of companions along the way. She understood that this was still not her path, yet she no longer resisted observing it.*

*Feeling suddenly inspired, she activated her Terminal and drafted a brief message to Rover, set to send just before their departure.*

*"Suddenly, a thought struck her. 'I will give them a flower.'"*

*As this idea took root in her mind, it consumed her completely. She had to find Rover — now! The flower, born from her very being, would neither wither nor decay unless she perished. She was stunned by her own eagerness and felt a flicker of sadness. As she reflected on it, a dull ache interrupted her thoughts.*

*With a sigh, she chose not to dwell on it. Yes! A smile graced her lips as she resolved to keep that flower blooming for Rover. She would do her utmost not to die too soon.*

*Camellya leaped from the treetops, joyfully headed toward Rover, her cherished seed of fate.*

**Analysis:** The first story establishes the present-day Camellya: stable, self-aware, warmly eccentric. Two details carry the most weight. First, the bracelet exchange — she gives Rover the same kind of monitoring bracelet Rover once gave her, reversing the caretaker dynamic in a way she finds genuinely surprising about herself. Second, the impulse to give a flower that will last until she dies: she is "stunned by her own eagerness," notices a "flicker of sadness," and chooses, with full knowledge of what the flower represents, to do it anyway. The resolution to "not die too soon" as her response to her own mortality is quintessential Camellya — acknowledging the limit and converting it into a gentle obligation to herself.

## Camellya Character Story II: "Labyrinth"

*She awoke from her dream.*

*The walls of the medical pod sank into darkness. Enveloped in heavy silence due to sound dampening, Camellya blinked and turned her dry gaze to the right. The indicators on the Terminal screen had returned to normal. The shackles around her wrists and ankles had loosened, yet she still felt weak, forcing her to remain lying in the narrow confines of the pod.*

*She tried to recall what had happened. She forced a precarious composure trying to remember, but the lost memories felt like a bottomless black hole — gazing into it revealed only darkness. Her memories had fractured once again.*

*Before her arrival at the Black Shores, she had wandered across Solaris for a long time. The cave where she first awoke showed no signs of other people, only twisted plants climbing the walls. Barefoot, Camellya waded through the water until she emerged into the open air, discovering a desolate village. Her foot struck something — a stone slab bearing the faded inscription, "Petalfall Village."*

*It took her a long time to escape that abandoned place. When hunger struck, she hunted wild beasts. When thirst overtook her, she drank from mountain springs. It took several months to traverse the dense forests and finally arrive at a place bustling with people. Only later did she realize that the village had likely been forgotten for hundreds of years.*

*Even after returning to civilization, Camellya's memories remained blank. She felt like an untamed animal, living on pure instinct. The rules and regulations of the human world were foreign to her, but she wasn't frustrated — using her Forte was enough. Power was an instinctive weapon, and she wielded it freely.*

*Initially, everything in Solaris fascinated her. However, as she gradually learned more, boredom set in quickly. Arriving at the Black Shores had been an accident, yet Camellya felt a strange familiarity with it. A year later, she found her name in the Tethys System and learned that she had once lived and worked there, known then as Lady Flora.*

*Driven by curiosity, she began her investigation of the Black Shores, eager to uncover her past and the stories of Lady Flora. What had truly happened to her?*

*As those thoughts surfaced, a sharp pain pierced her head again, and the flower in her chest suddenly swelled, darkening to a deep purple. [...] Fragments of memories flickered in her mind — Ah, Lady Flora had forbidden her from recalling the past.*

*Yet, she knew there was little they could do. They had already examined her carefully, concluding that her Overclocking symptoms were unique and untreatable, manageable only through restraint. Death could offer relief, but in her current condition, she couldn't even simply die.*

*"I'll follow my instincts until death finds me."*

*"It hurts!" she thought. Despite her agony, Camellya laughed loudly.*

**Analysis:** The second story is Camellya's origin narrative — not her cosmological creation, but her practical entry into the present world. Waking in a cave with no memories, in a village abandoned for centuries, subsisting on instinct alone: this is the baseline condition she emerged from. The detail that she still remembered the name "Camellya" when everything else was erased is central to her identity — it is the one thread that survived the cutting. The discovery of "Lady Flora" in the Tethys archives provides her investigation's starting point and immediately triggers an Overclocking episode, which tells us the memories are not simply forgotten but actively suppressed by whatever programming Lady Flora herself installed. The laugh at the end, through genuine pain, is not bravado but her fundamental way of being: this is the process she has, and she will follow it to its end.

## Camellya Character Story III: "A Fleeting Moment"

*She awoke from her dream.*

*Lady Flora opened her eyes, feeling the cold air brushing against her fingertips, making her hands and feet slightly numb.*

*The vehicle had come to a halt in a cave, a refuge from the snowstorm outside, where an endless silver expanse of snow stretched before her. Frost flowers drifted gently from the sky, landing delicately on the tip of her nose. Shivering, she huddled deeper into her thick clothing. She was not the kind of plant that could withstand the cold.*

*"Where did the leader go?" she asked [...] "The leader? They went west. They said something interesting had turned up and wanted to take a look." "Why didn't they take me? I am their guard, after all!" she complained under her breath.*

*Her fellow Bloom Bearer chuckled: "You were asleep! You haven't been sleeping well on the journey, always shivering in the cold. They didn't want to wake you while you finally caught some rest."*

*Grumbling quietly, Lady Flora swung the door open. "Anyway, I'm off to find them now!"*

*Leaving the warmth of the vehicle, Lady Flora dragged her feet across the snowy expanse, memories of their first encounter in Petalfall Village flooding back. She had known they would come to this snowy plain for the mission, and despite her dislike of the cold, she knew the time spent with the leader of the Black Shores would be worth it.*

*Lady Flora relished this existence, brimming with the thrill of the unknown, and Rover had an uncanny talent for transforming the ordinary into something extraordinary. She didn't mind danger. In fact, she rather enjoyed it. To her, danger signified change, and change led to excitement. She did find, however, that with her leader by her side, she stifled her impulse to seek out trouble for fun.*

*Camellya raised her hand, the delicate chain on her wrist catching the light. The bracelet monitoring her Rabelle indicators read as normal. She squeezed her wrist and exhaled softly. "Good, everything is as it should be. If I start spiraling out of control, they would have to look after me. It would derail the entire mission." She hated losing control.*

*In recent years, Lady Flora had been haunted by bizarre and fragmented dreams. They felt like memories of a life she had never lived. In these dreams, she was always searching for Rover, but the two never found each other.*

*She needed to find them as quickly as possible. Shoving those tangled thoughts aside, she focused on pinpointing Rover's frequencies. After crossing two hills and a frozen lake, she finally spotted a blurry figure in the distance.*

*"Camellya, how did you sleep?"*

*Their golden eyes sparkled with warmth, and she couldn't help but smile back.*

*"Hehe… With my leader by my side, of course I had the sweetest dreams."*

**Analysis:** Lady Flora is Camellya with the volume turned up: visibly cold, audibly grumbling, openly affectionate in a way the present-day Camellya carefully doesn't perform. The snowstorm scene is intimate precisely in its domesticity — a woman who has complained she is not the kind of plant that can withstand cold, dragging herself across a frozen plain to find one specific person before anything else. The detail of her monitoring bracelet, checking her own Rabelle curve to ensure she will not become a burden to the mission, shows that even in this warmer incarnation, her self-control is an act of care for Rover rather than for herself. The dreams she describes — always searching for Rover, never finding them — are the early manifestation of the loop that will define her across multiple eras.

## Camellya Character Story IV: "Eve"

*She awoke from her dream.*

*The interval between unconsciousness and consciousness became shorter with each passing day. Lady Flora was starting to find it hard to tell where her dreams ended and reality began.*

*Memories surged like turbulent waves, crashing into her mind and wreaking havoc on her senses. Pain ripped through her body, relentlessly and unforgivingly. She opened her mouth to scream, but Overclocking had turned her throat into a gnarled, coarse root, silencing her cries.*

*After struggling to take just a few faltering steps, she collapsed onto a muddy riverbank, trembling, her fingertips spasming uncontrollably.*

*A void of chaos swirled within her, yet there was one thing that stood out in sharp relief: Rover.*

*She remembered everything now. Who she was, who Rover is, the reason for her being, Solaris, and the Lament…*

*Before the madness of Overclocking engulfed her, those memories were always buried deep, locked in the depths of her being. Each time she awoke, she lost all recollection, left only with an aching void. As time passed and her body weakened under the strain of Overclocking, glimpses of her mission and roots would resurface, only to vanish again in an endless cycle.*

*How many times had it happened? Lady Flora had no answer.*

*Civilizations crumbled into dust, only to rise anew; life faded, only to blossom once more. She was witness to the ebb and flow of Solaris, yet remained forever a wanderer in her own mind.*

*"Rover…" The name slipped from her lips like a prayer.*

*They had never truly met or exchanged words, and now, with her memories fading, she couldn't even tell if they were friends or foes. Yet somehow, the next time she awoke, she knew her instincts would lead her to seek them out. It was not a tender yearning. It felt more like the weight of a heavy chain, one Lady Flora could not resist. Yet she felt no hatred for this chain.*

*"Next time, if only I could truly get to know Rover and share a conversation with them."*

*Lady Flora closed her eyes and surrendered to the darkness as the pain peaked.*

*The lake's cool water swelled across her cheek, the camellia withering as its vines retreated into her body. The autumn night breeze blew through the leaves, caressing her as she lay by the lake. To the north, the villagers of Petalfall Village would soon awaken from their dreams, calling to one another as they prepared to set out on a new day of fishing.*

**Analysis:** The fourth story is structurally the most important: it reveals the loop. Lady Flora has been cycling through Overclocking events, memory loss, re-awakening, and gradual recovery for an uncountable number of repetitions spanning civilizational timescales. The detail that she has watched "civilizations crumble into dust, only to rise anew" is not metaphorical — she has genuinely outlived multiple cycles of Solaris history, always returning to Petalfall Village, always finding her way back, always losing herself again. The survival of Rover's name as the last coherent signal before consciousness fails is the story's central image: not "a tender yearning" but "the weight of a heavy chain" — and she feels no hatred for it. This story also reveals Petalfall Village as the location where Lady Flora and Rover first actually met (she was fighting Tacet Discords attacking the village), providing the origin point for their entire relationship.

## Camellya Character Story V: "Once Upon a Time"

*She awoke from her dream.*

*The wind howled fiercely at the rooftop's edge as she stretched, squinting down at the lively streets below. Vehicles crisscrossed the roads, their taillights forming a vibrant, fiery chain. Resting her chin on her hand, she began to hum. It was a song she had learned from Rover. Of course, she hadn't heard it directly from them — she had picked it up secretly.*

*They might not be enemies, but their relationship was far from friendly — at best, it was a one-sided acquaintance. Rover's presence had caught the eye of various factions, including her own organization.*

*She was merely a "chosen seed," a "flower" that bloomed for the sole purpose of hunting Rover down. Most days, the injections left her in a fog, drifting through a semi-conscious haze, with only her instinctual yearning for a certain person carved deep into her heart.*

*The substance she was administered had a sharp, acrid scent, dragging her reason into a mire, robbing her of clarity and self. Her thoughts whirled chaotically, like a bomb on the brink of detonation, forced to reset just before the countdown expired.*

*But she didn't care. Outside of Rover, little else piqued her interest.*

*It was said this distance was necessary to evade the Tethys System's tracking. Contact was forbidden.*

*But how she longed to meet Rover! Perched dangerously on the edge of the building, she laughed at the fantasy. Vines entwined around her arms, climbing higher, their delicate buds slowly unfurling at their tips.*

*"I'll give them a flower."*

*The moment the thought emerged, it consumed her entirely. She envisioned the moment she would find Rover and present this gift — a blossom sprouted from her very being. It would neither wither nor decay unless she perished. But just as quickly as it came, the thought vanished into the chaos of her mind, leaving no trace behind.*

*Little did she know, centuries later, the scene before her would vanish in the Lament. She would wander endlessly through a labyrinth of memories until one autumn morning, by a lake in Petalfall Village, where she would finally meet Rover, there to rescue the village from Tacet Discord attacks. From there, she would join the Black Shores and share a few hundred years together before Rover would eventually leave her. She, too, would leave the Black Shores, only to be reunited decades later in Jinzhou. All those futures had yet to unfold, just as possibilities slumbered within seeds, waiting for fate to blossom.*

*She leaped from the building, her vines weaving through the city's structures, searching for them.*

**Analysis:** The final story is the earliest point on the timeline and the most cosmologically significant. In a pre-Lament era, Camellya existed in an organization (not the Black Shores) that used her as a "chosen seed" — something designed or assigned to pursue Rover, kept at chemical distance from full cognition so the Tethys System's tracking couldn't detect a direct connection. The injections, the fog, the "bomb on the brink of detonation": this era's Camellya is barely herself, held in place by pharmacological restraint, her only constant the directional pull toward one person she has never met face-to-face. The impulse to give a flower appears here too — thought, then immediately lost in the chemical noise — which means the gesture Camellya plans in Story I is one she has attempted to carry out across eras without ever quite reaching the moment. The narrator's closing lines, telling us everything that will happen across centuries before ending with her jumping from the building still searching, convert the entire story cycle into a single tragic-joyful forward motion: not tragedy because she suffers, but because she has always been moving toward the same thing and now, in the present, she is finally close enough to give the flower.

## Camellya: Cherished Items

**Wooden Doll**
An intricately carved wooden doll, shaped in Rover's likeness and crafted from the roots produced by Camellya's Forte. It reflects her unique aesthetic. Before their reunion in Jinzhou, Camellya had made countless attempts to sculpt the doll's face — each attempt leading to frustration as she could never quite perfect the final stroke, because she had never clearly seen Rover's face. Now, after all the vague attempts and disappointments, her vision has finally come to life. As she gazes upon her creation, she can't help but chuckle. "So, this is what it was meant to be like — just as adorable as I imagined."

**Spectrum Bracelet**
A frequency spectrum-monitoring bracelet designed by the Black Shores, created at Camellya's strong insistence. Its design deliberately resembles a cherished gift from a bygone era — the bracelet Rover once gave Lady Flora — equipped with similar tracking features to keep the wearer's location and safety known from afar. Camellya understands that she is no longer the Lady Flora of old, nor does she wish to be. Gone are the days of shadowing Rover, but so what? Both of them will do what makes them happy. For her, this is the sole anchor in an endless sea of meaninglessness.

**A Camellia**
A flower dedicated to Rover. Whether as a leader, a Modulator, or now as Rover — the hero of Jinzhou, a guide — Camellya has never cared about their title or accompanying responsibilities. However, she knows she cannot, and indeed will not, let her own perspective on life color their judgment. So let this everlasting flower convey her feelings where mere words may fall short: "It only blooms for you, forever and always." The flower is grown from her own Forte — it will not wither or decay unless she herself dies. It is simultaneously a love declaration and a living mortality marker.

## Camellya: Favorite/Disliked Food

No specific food preferences are listed in accessible in-game archives. Her voicelines reference a general indifference to most things, but her Thoughts lines note finding unexpected comfort in mundane flavors — particularly the sweet taste of field rations and festival foods that trigger distant emotional memory rather than the specific memory itself. Star-shaped candies appear as a recurring comfort item in her voicelines (possibly a shared motif with the broader Black Shores world rather than her own specific preference).

She is explicitly stated to be "not the kind of plant that could withstand the cold," which her Lady Flora identity demonstrates through literal discomfort in winter environments; warmth-associated foods may be implied preferences.

## Camellya: Ideals

**Instinct as the Only Reliable Compass:** Across every cycle of memory loss, Camellya's instincts have been more reliable than her conscious intentions. She has made peace with this: reason is fragile and memory is temporary, but the drive toward certain things — combat, freedom, Rover — survives everything else. Her ethics, to the extent she has any, are grounded in this recognition: she will follow what she is most essentially, and not pretend to values she cannot maintain across the loop.

**The Pursuit of What Is Genuinely Interesting:** Camellya's worldview holds that boredom is the primary condition of existence and rarity is the only cure. This is not nihilism — she finds life beautiful and combat joyful and Rover endlessly fascinating — but it is a philosophy built around authentic experience rather than inherited duty. She will not do things she does not mean, and she will not claim motivations she does not have. This makes her, paradoxically, one of the more honest people at the Black Shores.

**Caring Without Ownership:** She will not follow Rover to Rinascita. She will not explain her past. She will not place her own choices as a weight on Rover's shoulders. The flower she gives blooms only for them, but she grows it herself and gives it freely. This is her model of connection: deeply personal, completely non-possessive, expressed through the most permanent thing she has (something that dies when she does) rather than through words or presence.

**The Refusal of Self-Pity:** Camellya's Overclocking is severe, her condition is untreatable, her memory is unreliable, and she has been through cycles of suffering that most beings do not survive once. She laughs. Not because she is in denial but because the alternative — treating her own suffering as the central fact of her existence — would mean losing the last thing she can control: her orientation toward her own life. She will not be defined by what is happening to her body. She will be defined by what she does between episodes.

## Camellya: Narrative Chronicle

- **Ancient era (pre-Lament):** Camellya exists as a "chosen seed" in an unnamed organization, chemically suppressed to prevent Tethys tracking, tasked with locating Rover. She observes Rover from rooftops, learns a song from them without direct contact, thinks about giving a flower, and loses the thought before she can act. The city of this era is later destroyed by the Lament.
- **Petalfall Village encounter:** After wandering through labyrinthine memory cycles, Camellya (as Lady Flora) first meets Rover directly by a lake in Petalfall Village during an autumn morning, while Rover is defending the village from Tacet Discord attacks. This is the first actual meeting between them.
- **Black Shores era (as Lady Flora):** Lady Flora joins the Black Shores and serves as one of Rover's (the Astral Modulator's) most dedicated Bloom Bearers and bodyguards. They travel together across Solaris — farmland, snowfields, coastal missions. Lady Flora monitors her own Rabelle curve out of concern for being a burden. Over "a few hundred years," they are companions and comrades.
- **Departure:** Rover (the Astral Modulator) eventually leaves the Black Shores (memory erasure, departure to find a path through the Lament). Lady Flora, too, leaves the Black Shores at some point after Rover's departure.
- **Cycles of Overclocking and amnesia:** Lady Flora undergoes repeated Overclocking events, each resetting her conscious memory while preserving instinctual directional pull toward Rover. She cycles through this loop an unspecified number of times, watching civilizations rise and fall, always waking near Petalfall Village.
- **Post-Lament awakening:** Camellya wakes in a cave near Petalfall Village — which has been abandoned for hundreds of years — and makes her way out of the forest over several months. She retains no memories but remembers her own name.
- **Black Shores (current era, Version 1.0 intro — 1 year prior to Version 1.4 release):** Camellya arrives at the Black Shores by accident, feels a strange familiarity, passes the Bloom Bearer test, gains access to Tethys archives, and discovers the name "Lady Flora" associated with her past identity.
- **Reunited in Jinzhou (Version 1.0 main story):** Camellya and Rover meet in Jinzhou. Camellya joins the Rover's circle and accompanies them through the main story from Jinzhou onward.
- **Stellar Matrix (Version 1.3):** During the Stellar Matrix events, Camellya does not completely lose all memories of her past, gaining a clearer picture of Lady Flora's history.
- **Present day (Version 1.4+):** Camellya is a Bloom Bearer at the Black Shores. She is stable enough to sleep in treetops without the medical pod. She has received and given monitoring bracelets with Rover. She is carving a wooden doll in Rover's likeness. She is preparing a camellia to give as a gift. She is conducting her own investigation into her past and Lady Flora's mission, trusting no one at the Black Shores with what she knows, biding her time for the right moment.
