## Verina Official Introduction

*"A botanist with a deep love for all flora, Verina finds beauty and meaning in every plant she studies. Her compassion extends not only to her botanical subjects, but also to everyone she meets."*

Her Forte archive designation is **Nature Calling**. Her self-introduction: *"I'm Verina. If you come across any plants that pique your interest, do let me know! Huh? You want me to go on field researches with you? Of-of course! It's my honor. But it can get quite dangerous out in the wild. Please be careful and stay close to me."*

Her ideals statement, delivered with characteristic botanical specificity: *"Maintaining a balanced ecosystem is no easy feat, especially when it involves regulating the population of various species. But what if we could grow flowers that have a calming effect on the Tacet Discords? This could greatly reduce the strain on our frontline soldiers and potentially save lives."*

## Verina Personality

Verina is a person whose way of understanding things grew directly out of a single lesson her mother gave her as a child: two leaves from the same tree are never truly identical. If you look closely enough, their veins spread in different directions. This realization — that apparent sameness conceals real difference, and that the difference is visible to anyone willing to look carefully — became the foundational operating mode for everything Verina has done since. She studies plants with this attention. She studies people the same way. She keeps notes on her friends, recording their preferences and sorrows, because it must feel nice, she says, to have someone remember.

She is also someone who has lost a great deal and has found a way to keep going that does not require her to pretend the loss is resolved. Her parents are both dead. She left the New Federation after losing them, arrived with little, joined an exploration team, and then built a life in Jinzhou that is genuinely her own — a greenhouse, botanical research, military bouquet orders, solo walks through the streets on rest days. The Moments of Woe story is the account of a difficult afternoon when seedlings died overnight and she forgot to eat lunch and thought, *"if Mom and Dad were still around, I wouldn't have to worry about all this."* And then she stood up, put on her gardening gloves, and continued.

The plants are not a consolation prize. They are the answer she keeps finding to the question of whether life has meaning when the answer is not immediately available. They grow despite adversity. They tell her, in whatever frequency she hears them on, that it does.

## Verina Character Story I: "Mushrooms 101"

Riggs, leader of one of the Pioneer Association exploration teams, always begins the same way when asked about Verina: *"Boy, you're in for a story."*

When she first joined, she was always by herself and didn't talk to people. The team worried — she was young, she had lost her parents, she was kind and hardworking and never complained about mountain climbing or getting water or scavenging for food, but she kept everything bottled up and they worried about what might happen if something went wrong.

Then something went wrong, and it was funny.

One of the team's members liked mushrooms and went out to pick some for his soup. Verina took hold of the pot and would not let go. By the time the team got to them, they were in a shouting match. The man was bragging about his wilderness experience, not quite considering that Verina was a trained botanist. She spread all the mushrooms out and named every part of every one of them — rings, caps, butts — and the team gradually understood she was identifying them as poisonous, and also delivering an impromptu field lecture on how to distinguish each species.

When she finished, she tapped a piece of wood she had found like an instructor and asked if they understood.

They looked at each other and burst into laughter.

*"W-why are you all laughing... Did I get something wrong? Eugh... Sob..."*

She panicked and started pulling the mushrooms out of the pot to go over them again one by one. The team was already clapping. Riggs yelled: *"Bravo! That's our little botanist, folks!"*

She still thinks about this day and feels embarrassed — Riggs says she'd put her head inside a mushroom pot if she could. But ever since that afternoon, she has given her opinions carefully and patiently whenever anything plant-related comes up.

This is the story that explains how Verina rejoined the living after her parents died: not through a dramatic turning point, but through a shouting match about poisonous mushrooms that ended in applause. The team's laughter was not at her — it was the laughter of people delighted to finally see who she was. The panic she felt was the panic of someone who had not yet learned to trust that being seen would be safe. The clapping taught her.

## Verina Character Story II: "Cultivating Flowers"

The people of Jinzhou love flowers. During tense battles, the military often requested extra bouquets with their supplies. These orders ended up in Verina's hands.

It felt strange at first. The bouquets bloomed for only a short time — picked today, wilted tomorrow. A flower that had bloomed in a greenhouse might have withered petals by the time it reached its recipient. Why did people want them?

Then she read the wishes attached to one of the orders.

*"In honor of my comrade in arms. He gave his life for the peace of Jinzhou, yet his name is not known to many. Yet I still remember his heartfelt smile when he received an Iris from a friend long ago. If one's name cannot be remembered, let the meanings of these flowers honor him forever."*

That night, Verina locked herself in the greenhouse.

She understood now. Flowers are adored for their intricate leaf patterns, but it is the fleeting feelings they evoke that truly captivate. Flowers are not the feeling — they carry it, briefly, imperfectly, from one person to another. She carefully selected and cultivated the finest seeds, trying to preserve in her garden the emotions associated with each bouquet. She hoped the recipients would experience the true essence of the flowers — a connection to the feelings they held within.

She wished she could work a little harder and a little longer.

This story operates as Verina's answer to the question her Chat II voice line poses: what do you do when the beauty of flowers doesn't make people protect them, when they pluck expensive flowers and flaunt them? The Iris story gives her a reason that doesn't require ambient appreciation as its premise — some people don't need to understand botany; they need a vessel for grief, and the flower is that vessel. Verina can provide that. It is enough.

## Verina Character Story III: "Noting the Differences"

People always ask Verina why she loves plants so much.

She always gives the same answer: when she was little, her mother would place two pieces of leaves in her hand and ask what the differences were between them.

*"Aren't they exactly the same? They came from the same tree."*

Her mother shook her head. *"Look closely, Verina."*

She looked closely. Both leaves appeared the same on the outside. But their veins spread out in varying directions. They drew completely different patterns.

This changed everything. The differences she had never noticed or cared about were suddenly visible. Some flowers look similar but grow in completely different environments. Some plants grow in nearly identical environments but take on various shapes. She became captivated by the similarities and differences — how to help plants thrive in different environments, how to cultivate stunning flowers.

And then she started doing the same thing with people.

She began to notice the unique qualities of those around her — their preferences and sorrows, the way some people like lemongrass and some don't. She jotted it all down. *"It must feel nice to have someone remember their preferences."*

This is the story that explains everything about Verina in the fewest words. The botanical lesson became a perceptual method; the perceptual method became a way of relating to people; the way of relating to people became the practice of keeping notes on her friends. The leaf observation her mother asked her to make when she was a child is the reason she remembers, specifically, that Chixia's food is extraordinarily spicy and that Mortefi's lab lights are on when she checks the nocturnal plants at night.

## Verina Character Story IV: "Moments of Woe"

Verina has her moments of woe, too.

Plant cultivation is unpredictable. She would wake up to find dried seedpods in the petri dish after a night's rest. She would work tirelessly all day and realize she had skipped lunch again. In these moments, she missed her parents.

*"If Mom and Dad were still around, I wouldn't have to worry about all this..."*

A hot, hearty lunch would be right there when she walked in the door. Help would come whenever she needed to work through a difficult problem. But all of that had long since become a memory, left in the notes she had lost when she departed the New Federation.

Now she had to pull herself together and live on her own.

Sometimes she wondered: *Is there any meaning to all the work I've done? Is there any meaning in the life I'm living?*

But then she would always find the answer in her thriving plants. Despite the struggle, the dry seedpods continued to produce new life. Their growth whispered tales of perseverance and joy. Nestled in her green oasis, she could hear them sharing their stories of pain and triumph, reminding her of the beauty of growth and blooming.

Perhaps this was the meaning of her endeavors.

She stood up and put on her gardening gloves. Even if her parents aren't by her side, she is determined to keep working hard and show them how much she's grown.

After a brief moment of sorrow, she picked up the spray can and carried on with her day's work.

This story is the most direct portrait of Verina's emotional interior. The botanical framing of the answer — the dry seedpods producing new life as the argument against meaninglessness — is not a metaphor for her. She does not think *"this is like what I'm going through."* She hears the plants tell her their stories of pain and triumph and is reminded by them, directly, that growth and blooming are worth the struggle. The boundary between the language she uses for plants and the language she uses for herself is not clearly maintained because there is not a clear boundary. The plants are genuinely part of how she processes her own experience.

## Verina Character Story V: "A Little Time Off"

Verina would give herself time off from time to time.

She set the flower room temperature and watering schedule, exhaled in relief, and grabbed her adventure notes to explore the streets of Jinzhou. Days of work, and she knew — both she and her plants needed a break.

Jinzhou was a far cry from her hometown in the New Federation. No inflated flower prices. No elaborate competitions. As she strolled through the bustling alleyways, steam rising from vendors' stalls, shoppers moving around her, she felt something she tried to name: *"hmm, is 'invigorated' the right word for it?"*

She walked and remembered: studying plants with her parents; the painful loneliness after their absence. But now, standing on her own two feet, she could finally treat herself to a peaceful vacation. After what felt like an eternity of walking alone, she had grown into her own person.

Then she saw them.

Chixia and Yangyang were walking toward her with freshly purchased Salted Milk Tea. The Midnight Rangers must also be on vacation. Verina rummaged through her satchel and pulled out a few bouquets of tiny, star-like flowers.

This was the most precious gift she would ever give her friends. The selection had taken far, far too long, and while the Starflowers had waited for the best of times and sunshine, she had also waited for sunshine that would make her feel warm.

Now that she saw her sunshine, Verina ran toward her friends.

There was a long road ahead, and it would take some time for the flowers to bloom.

For now, she was content to enjoy the vacation.

This is the story that establishes what the Starflowers mean — and why her birthday voice line to Rover, in which she gives Rover a Starflower and says it represents hope and miracles, carries the weight it does. The Starflowers are the most precious gift she gives. She waited *"far, far too long"* to find the right ones for Chixia and Yangyang. The selection process is botanical and emotional simultaneously, as all of Verina's processes are. The image of her running toward her friends is the simplest and most complete portrait of her happiness that the story collection contains.

## Verina: Cherished Items

**Expedition Goggles (Pioneer Association)**
*"Verina cherishes her Pioneer Association expedition goggles, remnants of a journey and warm memories like Starflower. Now useless for danger detection, they hold sentimental value to her."*
The goggles were issued or gifted during her time with Riggs's exploration team — the period in her life when, after her parents' deaths and her departure from the New Federation, she found her way back to other people. The comparison to Starflowers in the item description — *"warm memories like Starflower"* — links the goggles directly to the A Little Time Off story and the flowers she gives to friends she loves. They are not kept because they are functional (they are not — they are no longer reliable for danger detection). They are kept as evidence that a particular chapter of her life existed, and that it was good.

**Eternity in Bloom (First Fieldwork Flower Sample)**
*"The flower sample from Verina's first fieldwork. She waited for the flower to bloom and carefully preserved it. Verina sees the blooming of a flower as an everlasting moment of life's beauty, encapsulating her love for both flowers and life itself."*
This is Verina's oldest botanical object and her most philosophically significant one. A single flower from her first field research, preserved — the preservation of a moment of blooming as a way of holding time still. She sees the blooming as *"an everlasting moment of life's beauty,"* which is not a naive sentiment but a specific one: not that the flower lasts, but that the moment of its blooming is real and does not stop having been real. For someone who studies things that die, this is the operating belief that makes the work meaningful.

**Notes on Wilderness Exploration (Handmade Map)**
*"Whenever she goes out to participate in expeditions or field trips, Verina always finds shortcuts to explore or avoids lurking dangers with the help of her plant friends. Verina draws them all. Behind a small map with childish strokes are valuable lessons, proof that Verina is following in her mother's footsteps."*
The map is drawn in *"childish strokes"* — it is not a professional document but a personal record, started when she was still very young, probably during her first forays into field research with her mother. The plants she hears guide her to shortcuts and away from danger; she draws both onto the map. Each entry is a lesson. The collection is *"proof that Verina is following in her mother's footsteps"* — the most direct statement in any of her cherished item descriptions of what her botanical vocation means to her in terms of her parents' legacy. She lost her mother's actual notes when she left the New Federation; this map is the continuation she built herself.

## Verina: Favorite/Disliked Food

**Favorite — Milk (and milk-based foods)**
*"My mom always said milk would help me grow taller, like how plants need fertilizer. And every time I drink it, I feel warm and cozy. Maybe that's what happiness feels like."*
The favorite food is a favorite memory. Her mother's voice saying milk would help her grow taller is preserved in the warmth and coziness she still feels when drinking it. The comparison to fertilizer is not whimsical — it is Verina applying the only framework she has (botanical) to a question about nutrition, exactly as she always does. The final line — *"maybe that's what happiness feels like"* — is not sad despite its hedging; it is a genuine attempt to name a feeling she associates with safety.

**Disliked — Large, Greasy Dishes (Steaks)**
*"I'm not a big fan of big, greasy dishes—like steaks... It's not that they aren't tasty, I just hate the idea of letting food go to waste if I can't finish it. It doesn't sit right with me."*
The dislike is not about the flavor of the steak but about the waste. She cannot finish large greasy dishes and the prospect of leaving food unfinished *"doesn't sit right with her."* This is a conservation instinct applied to food — consistent with someone whose relationship to living things includes attending carefully to what is used and what is wasted.

## Verina: Ideals

**Passion — Growing Closer to the Truth of Life**
Verina's driving intellectual passion is understanding how life grows and persists — how plants thrive in different environments, how they respond to frequency, how they might even respond to the Tacet Discords. Her ideals voice line is not a generalized statement of loving nature but a specific research aspiration: flowers that could calm Tacet Discords, reducing frontline casualties. This is the form her love of plants takes as a vocational goal — practical, directed, and oriented toward reducing harm to people she will likely never meet.

**Resonance — Continuation as Inheritance**
Verina's mother taught her to look at leaves. Her mother's notes were lost. The handmade exploration map is the continuation of those notes, drawn in childish strokes, grown more detailed with every expedition. The first fieldwork flower is preserved as the originating moment. The expedition goggles hold the warmth of the people who welcomed her after her parents died. Every cherished item is a form of continuation — of her parents' work, of the Pioneer team's warmth, of the first botanical observation that made her who she is. She does not frame this as inheritance or tribute; she simply keeps doing what she was taught, and keeps careful notes.

**Gratitude — For Being Received**
The Mushrooms 101 story ends with Riggs shouting *"Bravo! That's our little botanist, folks!"* The A Little Time Off story ends with Verina running toward Chixia and Yangyang. The birthday voice line to Rover ends with *"I just want you to always be happy, Rover."* The throughline is gratitude for being received — for being seen clearly and welcomed rather than merely tolerated. She was alone after her parents died. She is not alone now. She holds this with the same care she gives everything else she does not want to let go.

**Perseverance — One Dry Seedpod at a Time**
The meaning of Verina's work is not guaranteed in advance. She has to find it — in dry seedpods that produce new life, in plant frequencies she can hear, in the gradual accumulation of evidence that growth is possible even after adversity. This is not a grand declaration but a daily practice, renewed each time she puts on her gardening gloves after a difficult afternoon. The Moments of Woe story is the most honest account of this practice: she does not resolve the grief, she does not arrive at certainty, she stands up and continues, and the plants continue with her.
