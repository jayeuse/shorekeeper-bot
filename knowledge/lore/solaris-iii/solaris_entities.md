# Solaris-III Lore: Entities

<!-- Sources: https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/Tacet_Discord, https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/Threnodian, https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/Sentinel -->

## Tacet Discord: Definition and Origin

- **Tacet Discords** (abbreviated **TDs**) are sentient beings formed by residual chaotic frequency energy under the Waveworn Phenomenon. They possess **ever-changing, amorphous forms** and are described as one of humanity's most persistent and dangerous threats.
- TDs possess a unique internal structure called a **Tacet Core** (Chinese: 声核, "Sound Nucleus"), also known academically as a **Reverberation Body**. This core is the distilled concentration of their frequency identity.
- Every Tacet Discord is driven by an **instinctive imperative to consume and merge with other frequencies** — this is not choice but biological necessity. Without continual feeding, their frequencies decay and they cease to exist.
- By feeding on and merging with other frequencies, Tacet Discords **mimic the abilities, shapes, and behaviors** of what they consume. A TD that has consumed large amounts of fire-element frequency will exhibit fire-based abilities and have high resistance to fire.
- Despite being universally labeled as "monsters," research reveals that many TDs are not purely malevolent. Some appear to act out of instinct rather than malice, and some quests reveal TDs capable of more complex behaviors — mourning, loyalty, and even passive coexistence.
- The allegorical story of **Qichi Village**, told by Scar to Rover, suggests that TDs may represent humans who were transformed by the Lament — "white lambs" turned into monsters by a mysterious shepherd.

## Tacet Discord: Rank Classification

- Tacet Discords are classified into **four hierarchical ranks** based on their power level, size, and threat assessment:
- **Common Class** — the lowest tier; the standard Tacet Discords encountered throughout exploration. Individually manageable by most Resonators.
- **Elite Class** — the third-highest classification; significantly more powerful than Common TDs, often possessing specialized abilities.
- **Overlord Class** — the second-highest classification; powerful regional threats that typically require coordinated Resonator teams to defeat.
- **Calamity Class** — the highest classification; planet-scale threats of enormous power. Defeating a Calamity-class TD requires exceptional firepower and preparation. The **Bell-Borne Geochelone** is one such entity, described as having a body as huge as a mountain, capable of crushing armored vehicles and freezing rivers.
- Above all four ranks sit the **Threnodians**, which exist in a category beyond normal TD classification and are not officially ranked within this system.

## Tacet Discord: Frequency Classification

- In addition to power ranking, Tacet Discords are also classified by the **type of frequency they primarily consume**, which determines their abilities, elemental affinity, and physical form:
- **Whisperin** — TDs that primarily consume frequencies related to **human-shaped bodies** and biology. They often take humanoid forms and exhibit behaviors mimicking human psychology. The wraith-like Echoes near Lorelei's **Nimbus Sanctum** are examples of this class.
- **Howler** — TDs that primarily consume frequencies associated with **flora and fauna**. They take on bestial or plant-like forms and frequently exhibit animal behaviors.
- **Tranquilite** — TDs that primarily consume frequencies from **earth, stone, crystals, and other inorganic matter**. They tend to be physically robust and may incorporate rock or mineral structures into their bodies (e.g., the Elite-class Tranquelite that hurls boulders).
- **Staticoid** — TDs that attach themselves to and feed on **man-made objects**. Sometimes described as parasites of the structures or machines they inhabit.
- **Clamorling** — a fifth classification for which no full description has yet been provided in current game content. Their frequency diet and exact nature remain academically unclassified.

## Tacet Discord: Origins Beyond Biology

- While most TDs are understood to arise from the chaotic energy of Tacet Fields, some appear to have deeper origins rooted in **legend, history, and human collective memory**.
- The lore example of the **Bell-Borne Geochelone** illustrates this: an ancient army bell ringer saved a turtle, which became inseparable from the soldiers. When the bell ringer died in battle, legend says the turtle carried on ringing the bell. Eventually, that story and its emotional frequencies crystallized into a Calamity-class Tacet Discord.
- This suggests that TDs do not only emerge from raw chaotic energy — they can also be born from sufficiently resonant **human stories, emotions, and archetypes** that have been absorbed into the Remnant and subsequently manifested physically.
- Similarly, the **Dreamless** is a Tacet Discord directly birthed by **Ovathrax** through the cannibalization of the **Crownless**'s frequencies — demonstrating that Threnodians can deliberately create TDs.
- The **Crownless** was itself the vanguard of Ovathrax and a harbinger of the Scourge of War, suggesting a hierarchy of deliberately crafted TDs beneath each Threnodian.

## Threnodians: Lords of Ruin

- **Threnodians** are the most powerful class of beings produced by or associated with the Lament. They stand above all known Tacet Discords in terms of power and lore significance, and exist in a category entirely separate from the standard TD rank system.
- The word "Threnodian" derives from **threnody** — a song, poem, or speech expressing sorrow and lamentation, especially for the dead — mirroring the Lament's own sound-centric naming philosophy.
- Each Threnodian is an **embodiment of a specific fear or struggle** deeply rooted in the civilization it faces. They are not random entities — they manifest in specific regions because those regions have been saturated by the particular human fear they represent for centuries.
- Threnodians are **capable of vastly strengthening the effects of Waveworn Phenomenon** in their surrounding environment, amplifying the destructive potential of any active disaster.
- Critically, **Threnodians cannot be truly killed** as long as human emotion and will endure. Their existence is sustained by the negative emotions of the civilizations they are tied to. Even if physically defeated, as long as the fear or despair that birthed them persists in humanity, they will eventually reassemble and return.
- Just as a **Sentinel** has an appointed Resonator — a human chosen to serve as their partner and champion — so too can a Threnodian have a Resonator. However, being a Threnodian's Resonator comes at severe psychological and existential cost.
- A single Threnodian's revival is never an isolated incident. They are connected through a form of **telepathic link**, capable of sensing each other's state and triggering cascading catastrophic events across multiple regions simultaneously.

## Threnodians: The Law of Sentinels and Resonators

- Both Sentinels and Threnodians operate under a strict cosmic law: **each entity may have only one Resonator at a time**. Violation of this rule causes catastrophic consequences — both the entity and its current Resonator have their frequencies "rent asunder," effectively destroying them.
- This rule forms the basis of **Imperator's self-sacrifice plan** in Chapter II: by merging with Leviathan and then attempting to take a second Resonator (Cantarella), Imperator intended to trigger this law and destroy both itself and the merged Leviathan simultaneously.
- The plan was never fully executed as intended — Cantarella chose to return her resonance to Imperator rather than complete the bonding, but in doing so she gave Imperator enough control to briefly suppress Leviathan, allowing Rover and an inner Fleurdelys to seal the Threnodian's influence.
- **Mya**, a character introduced in Septimont, represents a failed Resonator for Leviathan — her eventual death by suicide illustrates the stark and horrifying difference between serving a Sentinel (purposeful, empowering) and serving a Threnodian (consuming, damaging, ultimately fatal).

## Threnodian Ovathrax: The Scourge of War

- **Ovathrax** is the Threnodian of **War**, bound to the region of **Jinzhou** in Huanglong. It is the first Threnodian encountered in the game's main story (Chapter I).
- Because Jinzhou has been a battleground for centuries — its geography, politics, and culture all shaped by relentless conflict — it is the natural seat of a Threnodian representing war. Ovathrax feeds on the frequencies of "weapons" and has long been converting Jinzhou's soldiers into Tacet Discords.
- Approximately **three years before the Rover's arrival**, Ovathrax's statue loomed over the Suspended Ruins, and the Battle Beneath the Crescent was fought in the Norfall Barrens. The **Midnight Rangers**, led by General Geshu Lin, engaged its forces at enormous cost. Geshu Lin vanished after striking what investigators believe was the **Crownless** — Ovathrax's vanguard — rather than the Threnodian itself.
- The **Crownless** is the vanguard/harbinger of Ovathrax, and the **Dreamless** is a secondary TD birthed by Ovathrax by cannibalizing the Crownless's frequencies. Both serve as Ovathrax's military arm.
- During _Chapter I: Act VI — Grand Warstorm_, Ovathrax's revival came early. Rover and Jiyan, with the Midnight Rangers, defeated the Crownless and Dreamless — after which Ovathrax inexplicably fled. Its current location remains unknown.
- Ovathrax's revival triggered measurable frequency fluctuations in other nations around Solaris-III, with Rinascita being the only region where no related TD activity was detected at the time.

## Threnodian Leviathan: The Unity of Corruption

- **Leviathan** is the Threnodian of **Unity and Assimilation**, bound to the region of **Rinascita**. It is the main antagonist of _Chapter II: Even When Divinity Remains Silent_.
- Leviathan was born from the **darker aspect of humanity's longing for unity** — specifically, shaped by the fear of the sea and the desire for total, undifferentiated belonging. It manifested as the shadow of civilization's yearning and has corrupted human belief systems from the very dawn of Rinascitan civilization.
- In Rinascita's earliest mythology, Leviathan was the divinity that ruled the sea, counterpart to Imperator's rule of the sky. But this theological framing masked its true nature: Leviathan **acted in Imperator's name**, producing false miracles and forging hollow grace, feeding on stagnant and obedient minds to sustain itself.
- The **Sentinel Imperator** and Leviathan have clashed since Rinascita's founding. Their first great conflict triggered the **first Dark Tide**, which devastated the archipelago.
- Over time, Leviathan merged with the weakened Imperator — corrupting the Sentinel into a mutated sea-horse hybrid form. As a result, Imperator's chosen Resonator, **Fleurdelys (Cartethyia)**, became simultaneously the Resonator of both Imperator and Leviathan, a situation with no precedent.
- The **Fractsidus**, led by Overseer **Cristoforo**, orchestrated Chapter II's events to fully resurrect Leviathan. Cristoforo also revealed that because a Threnodian's power and essence are one and the same, if Leviathan were to possess **Abby** through the Dark Tide, it could merge with the dormant powers of Ovathrax within Abby to achieve a complete rebirth.
- Leviathan was ultimately sealed (not destroyed) at the end of Chapter II. The residual energy of Leviathan still lingers in Rinascita's waters.
- Upon the Threnodian's sealing, it recognized **Abby** by the name **"Abraxas,"** suggesting a mysterious connection between Rover's companion and the Threnodian hierarchy.

## Threnodian Aleph-1: The Void Beyond the Stars

- **Aleph-1** is the Threnodian of **Voidance and Erasure**, bound to the region of **Lahai-Roi** and the **Roya Frostlands**. It is the main threat of _Chapter III_.
- Unlike Ovathrax and Leviathan, Aleph-1 is revealed to be an entity that does **not originate from Solaris-III**. Research by **Aemeath** and **Luuk Herssen** confirms that Aleph-1 is a Threnodian from **outer space**, having arrived through the **Stridergate** alongside the Exostrider during the second global Lament.
- This revelation has enormous cosmological implications: if Threnodians exist beyond Solaris-III, then the Lament may not be a local phenomenon confined to one planet — it may be a universal cycle of destruction that spans the cosmos.
- Aleph-1's domain is the **Void Storm** — the region-specific Waveworn Phenomenon that periodically cuts off all communication with Lahai-Roi and enables its control over the region.
- The **Exostrider** was specifically designed and deployed to protect Lahai-Roi from Aleph-1, acting as a "Sentinel Analog" despite not being an officially designated Sentinel. Its native wildlife — the **Exoswarm** — was created by the Exostrider's self-sacrifice, replacing the extinct pre-Lament species.

## Sentinels: Guardians of Civilization

- **Sentinels**, also called **Oracle Engines**, are immortal, immensely powerful beings created by the ancient **Homeland Civilization** and deployed to various planets — including Solaris-III — via massive mechanical carriers called **Exostriders**.
- Their foundational purpose is to serve as **guardians and guides** for the civilizations they are assigned to, standing at the brink of history to protect humanity from Threnodians and help it endure the Lament.
- Sentinels share wisdom, offer prophecies, and actively shape the development of the civilizations under their protection — sometimes in ways that the people themselves do not fully understand for centuries.
- Each Sentinel may designate **one Resonator** at a time — a human champion through whom they can act in the world and share their power. The law is absolute: a Sentinel with more than one Resonator will have both itself and its Resonator destroyed by a metaphysical sundering.
- Sentinels are described as the **direct opposites of Threnodians**: where Threnodians seek to destroy civilizations by feeding on despair and collapse, Sentinels seek to guide and protect civilizations by fostering growth and resilience.
- The Sentinels of **Huanglong** are unique in that they were **originally one being** before being split into seven, each assigned to one of Huanglong's seven cities. They are named after the seven astrological mansions of the Azure Dragon.
- **Rinascita** is said to have only one Sentinel: Imperator. **Lahai-Roi** has no formally designated Sentinel — the Exostrider acts as a "Sentinel Analog." The **New Federation** possesses a Sentinel, though its identity is the subject of scientific inquiry, with the capital accused of exploiting it.

## Sentinel Jué: The Dragon of Time

- **Jué** is the Sentinel of **Jinzhou** in Huanglong, taking the form of a massive **Loong** (a Chinese-style dragon). It is the first Sentinel encountered in the game's main story.
- In ancient times, Jué descended on **Mt. Firmament** alongside a colossal vehicle now known as the **Mianloong Chamber**. Early settlers of Hongzhen believed the Sentinel arrived from a faraway star, its vehicle serving as a hibernation pod.
- Jué's power is **temporal manipulation**: in its prime, it could decelerate, halt, rewind, and correct the flow of time. However, it sustained severe injuries — injuries that surpassed even its natural immortality — and lost one of its **Temporal Programs**, limiting it to only stopping and reversing time.
- To protect Hongzhen from Tacet Discords, Jué created a **Temporal Barrier** that greatly affects the flow of time within the area, which led the Hongzhen people to revere it devoutly and carve its likeness into the mountain, creating **Loong's Crest**.
- Jué's chosen Resonator is **Jinhsi**, Jinzhou's Magistrate. As an infant, Jinhsi was on the verge of death when Jué detected her unique Resonance and used its temporal power to reverse her death. Unbeknownst to many, the infant also inherited Jué's lost Temporal Program within her Resonance Ability.
- After the events of _Main Quest: Thaw of Eons_, Jinhsi inherits the full **Temporal Mandate** through her Secondary Resonance Awakening, allowing Jué to step back from active guardianship while the Magistrate fully assumes her role.

## Sentinel Imperator: The Wings of Separation

- **Imperator** is the Sentinel of **Rinascita**, originally taking the form of a **Pegasus**. Its power is the manipulation of space — specifically, the ability to **split and separate**.
- Imperator guided displaced peoples to the Rinascitan archipelago during the Lament era, leaving behind the **Genesis Nexus** as a repository of knowledge and technology to help the emerging civilization walk the path of freedom.
- Through the course of Chapter II, Imperator was revealed to have **merged with Leviathan** during the first Dark Tide. The merger was intended as a strategy to fully understand the Threnodian — but the corruption was profound, transforming Imperator's form from a Pegasus into a sea-horse hybrid, and gradually eroding its sanity.
- The people of Ragunna have worshipped images of Imperator for generations that **do not match its true form** — they have been unconsciously venerating the corrupted, sea-horse version. The Order of the Deep's statues and depictions are inaccurate, a detail noticed by both **Phoebe** and undercover Black Shores agent **Ariosto**.
- Imperator's chosen Resonator is **Cartethyia / Fleurdelys**. Due to the merger with Leviathan, Cartethyia became the Resonator of both simultaneously — an unprecedented violation of cosmic law that was also the foundation of Imperator's ultimate self-sacrifice plan.
- Imperator's plan involved taking **Cantarella** as a second Resonator, which would trigger the cosmic law of sundering and destroy both itself and Leviathan. Cantarella chose instead to return her power to Imperator, giving it enough control to allow Rover and Fleurdelys's inner self to seal Leviathan.
- Imperator died at the end of Chapter II, leaving **Rinascita without a Sentinel**. However, hope remains through Cartethyia, who now carries the legacy of its power.

## Sentinel Exostrider: The Machine That Protects

- The **Exostrider** is a massive mechanoid — not technically a Sentinel by the definitions of the Homeland Civilization, but recognized as a **"Sentinel Analog"** by the Spacetrek Collective.
- It arrived in the Lahai-Roi region during the second global Lament through the **Stridergate**, accompanied by **Threnodian Aleph-1**. Its purpose was specifically to protect Lahai-Roi from Aleph-1 and similar threats.
- When the Void Storm was unleashed by Aleph-1, the Exostrider shed its own components and transformed them into the **Exoswarm** — the biomechanical flora and fauna that replaced all of Lahai-Roi's extinct native species. This act of self-sacrifice is what makes the Exoswarm uniquely tied to the Exostrider's existence.
- The **Exostrider's Reactor Drive** currently serves as an **artificial sun** for the underground civilization of Lahai-Roi, providing light and energy within the subterranean dome. The Spacetrek Collective's **"New Solar Ceremony"** project is working to replace the Reactor Drive with a man-made sun called **"Helios,"** so the Drive can eventually be returned to the Exostrider.
- Special biological structures called **Soliskin** — fragments of the Exostrider's neural network — are shepherded and protected by the Roya Tribe, who set aside the **Bjartr Woods** and the **Solisylum** as sacred grounds. A ceremonial **Rite of Return** is periodically conducted to replenish the Exostrider's neural state with Soliskins.
- The Exostrider is deeply damaged and its ability to fulfill its protective function is at risk — which is a central tension of the Lahai-Roi storyline.
