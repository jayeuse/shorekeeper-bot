# Solaris-III Lore: Nations and Factions

<!-- Sources: https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/Huanglong, https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/New_Federation, https://wutheringwaves.fandom.com/wiki/Fractsidus -->

## Faction: The Black Shores

- **Role:** Covert peacekeeping and scientific organization dedicated to mitigating the Lament.
- **Location:** An isolated archipelago built on massive Tacetite deposits.
- **Leader:** The **Shorekeeper** (Guardian of Secrets).
- **Founder:** The **Rover** (in a past incarnation).
- **Technology:** The **Tethys System**, a civilization-calculating supercomputer fueled by Lament data that predicts disasters with 24-48 hour accuracy.
- **Core Philosophy:** To remember constantly ("The Fallacy of No Return").

## Faction: The Fractsidus

- **Role:** Primary antagonistic faction. A global terrorist syndicate.
- **Ideology:** Views the Lament as **forced evolution**. They seek to trigger the **"True Lament"** to push humanity to abandon biological purity and merge with Tacet Discords.
- **Technology:** **Masks of Constraint** — artifacts that suppress human rejection of TD frequencies, granting power at the cost of sanity and humanity.

## Faction: The Fractsidus — Hierarchy

- **Command:** **Overseers**. Elite leaders who have "crossed the veil of death" (survived traumatic revival).
- **Operatives:** **Artificers** (Executioners, Inspectors, Gunmasters).
- **Known Overseers:**
  - **Schwarzloch (Grand Architect):** The supreme leader. Master of identity manipulation.
  - **Phrolova (The Conductor / Hecate):** Highly influential, recently resigned.
  - **Cristoforo (The Playwright / Arachnos):** Manipulator of Rinascita's politics.
  - **Scar:** Advocate of the "White Lamb" philosophy; seeks to recruit Rover.
  - **Hydra:** Chief scientific mind on biological modification.
  - **Zashiki-warashi:** Commander of Ashinohara operations.
  - **Eris:** Commander of New Federation operations.

## Nations of Solaris-III: Huanglong

- **Structure:** Centralized militaristic nation. Bisected by **Mountains of Spirit** into Inner and Outer Huanglong.
- **Capital:** Mingting (Inner).
- **Key City:** **Jinzhou** (Outer). The military bulwark against Ovathrax.
- **Governance:** Jinzhou City Hall (Magistrate Jinhsi).
- **Military:** **Midnight Rangers** (General Jiyan).
- **Huanglong** (Chinese: 黄龙, "Yellow Dragon") is the first nation visited by the Rover and one of the most militarily sophisticated civilizations on Solaris-III.
- It is composed of **seven cities**, each protected by its own Sentinel — seven entities that were once a single being before being split. The **Mountains of Spirit** divide Huanglong into Inner Huanglong (capital and five cities) and Outer Huanglong (Jinzhou).
- Huanglong's primary military force is the **Midnight Rangers**, tasked with protecting the nation from external threats including Tacet Discords and Threnodian activity.
- The capital city of Huanglong is in Inner Huanglong; **Jinzhou** is the outlying province where the game's Prologue and Chapter I take place. It is the seat of the Sentinel **Jué** and the Magistrate **Jinhsi**.
- Notable locations in Huanglong include: **Mt. Firmament** (Jué's domain, with the Mianloong Chamber), the **Gorges of Spirits**, the **Central Plains**, **Desorock Highland**, the **Dim Forest**, **Norfall Barrens** (site of the Battle Beneath the Crescent), and the ruins of the **Port City of Guixu**.
- Huanglong's academic and research community is represented by **Huaxu Academy**, which studies Waveworn Phenomena, Resonators, and the broader mysteries of the Lament.

## Nations of Solaris-III: Rinascita

- **Structure:** Loose confederation of independent island city-states.
- **Culture:** Maritime, defined by the "Order of the Deep" faith and the conflict between Imperator and Leviathan.
- **Key City-States:**
  - **Ragunna:** Trade/Religious center. Ruled by the **Order of the Deep** (Primus Fenrico/Cantarella) and the **Montelli Family**.
  - **Septimont:** Gladiatorial kratocracy. Ruled by Ephor Augusta and Priestess Iuno. Site of the **Colosseum Olymdos**.
  - **Lilyland:** Isolated city-state involved in a recent diplomatic incident with Aalto/Brant.
- **Rinascita** (meaning "rebirth" in Italian) is a confederation of independent city-states scattered across an archipelago, and is the second nation visited by the Rover. It has no central government or national capital due to its nature as a coalition.
- The two most prominent city-states are **Ragunna** — the cultural and spiritual heart of Rinascita, seat of the Order of the Deep and the Carnevale festival — and **Septimont** — a more militaristic city-state whose people hold the gladiatorial tradition and do not share Ragunna's religious devotion to Imperator.
- Rinascita's primary Waveworn threat is the **Dark Tide**, controlled by Threnodian Leviathan. The Carnevale is held as a tribute to Sentinel Imperator and the deeds of past Blessed Maidens who contained the tide.
- Within Rinascita lies **Avinoleum**, a Sonoro Sphere of particular importance — its inverted gravity environment contains the ruins of the Order of the Deep seminary and serves as both a site of historical study and an unusual battlefield.
- The dominant religious and governmental organization is **The Order of the Deep** (also called the **Church of the Deep**), which administers spiritual life and maintains the tradition of the **Blessed Maiden** — Imperator's chosen Resonator who restrains Leviathan.
- Rinascita's culture is vibrant and water-centric: the **Carnevale** and **Night of Remembrance** are major festivals, with music, performance, and maritime tradition at the core of Ragunnese identity.

## Nations of Solaris-III: Lahai-Roi and the Roya Frostlands

- **Structure:** Subterranean civilization beneath the Roya Frostlands.
- **Culture:** Academic "Utopia for Researchers." Worships the artificial sun (Exostrider's Reactor Drive).
- **Organizations:** **Spacetrek Collective** and **Startorch Academy** (Rabelle College).
- **Goal:** The **New Solar Ceremony** — creating a man-made sun ("Helios") to return the Reactor Drive to the Exostrider.
- **Lahai-Roi** is the third nation visited by the Rover, located at one of Solaris's poles beneath the frozen wastes of the **Roya Frostlands**. It is celebrated as the "Research Haven of Solaris" and a "Utopia for Researchers."
- After the first global Lament shifted Solaris's axis and transformed the surface into endless ice, the native **Roya Tribe** gradually migrated underground, eventually building a warm and humid subterranean civilization beneath thick layers of ice and snow.
- The underground civilization is powered by the **Exostrider's Reactor Drive**, which serves as an artificial sun within the dome. Its wildlife is exclusively composed of **Exoswarm** — biomechanical creatures formed from remnant pieces of the Exostrider.
- **Startorch Academy** and associated research facilities, built by the multinational non-profit **Spacetrek Collective**, lie beneath the ice fields. The Academy is where many of the game's Chapter III characters study or work.
- Lahai-Roi is regularly cut off from the world by the **Void Storm**, and has no officially designated Sentinel — only the Exostrider as a Sentinel Analog.
- The sacred **Bjartr Woods** and the **Solisylum** within it are set aside by the Roya as a protected realm for the **Soliskin** — fragments of the Exostrider's neural network — which outsiders are not permitted to enter.
- The current era of Lahai-Roi's history, defined by its collaboration with the outside world, is called the **Twilight Epoch** in Royan chronology.

## Nations of Solaris-III: The New Federation

- The **New Federation** is a nation on Solaris-III known for its intense technological culture — described by observers as essentially a "technology cult" running deep in every citizen's blood.
- It possesses cutting-edge technology including planes, aerospace companies (e.g., **Triumph Avionics**), advanced photography equipment, and hosts an annual global scientific exposition.
- The New Federation has significant class divisions, from the aristocracy (which characters like **Mortefi** belonged to) down to the **Exiles** — the lowest social class, barred from legitimate employment and forced into the outskirts.
- The **"Lawless Zone"** was once a gathering place for Exile-built camps and criminal gangs. After the central government waged war against the gangs, the **Ghost Hounds** mercenary group annexed the last of them, and the Zone now serves as a source of mercenaries for the upper class's schemes.
- The New Federation's Sentinel is the subject of active scientific inquiry, though the government is accused of **exploiting the Sentinel** for its own benefit rather than honoring its role.
- Its corporations have acquired a number of Rinascitan companies, and it maintains strong academic ties — **Lucilla**, president of Startorch Academy, was formerly the chief editor of the New Federation's top academic journal. The author **Karl** — writer of the widely popular book _Second Coming of Solaris_ — is from the New Federation.
