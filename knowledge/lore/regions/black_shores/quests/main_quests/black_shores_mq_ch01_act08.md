# Act VIII: To the Shore's End

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Overview

- **Chapter:** Chapter I — Jinzhou Rising
- **Act Number:** Act VIII (Final Act of Chapter I)
- **Region:** Black Shores (first visit; also begins in Port City of Guixu, Huanglong)
- **Release Version:** Wuthering Waves Version 1.3 (September/October 2024)
- **Prerequisite:** Completion of Chapter I Act VII: Thaw of Eons; Union Level 21
- **Subquests (in order):** From the Echoes of Destruction (Part 1) → Hidden Between the Waves → Legacy of the Lasting Night → From the Echoes of Destruction (Part 2) → Advance toward the Future from Today (Reclaim Tomorrow From the Past) → From the Echoes of Destruction (Part 3) → Beyond the Shore's End
- **Significance:** First visit to the Black Shores as an explorable region; reveals the Rover's identity as the Black Shores' founder; resolves the Necrostar/Tethys System crisis; introduces the Shorekeeper as a playable 5-star Resonator.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Synopsis (Part 1: Port City of Guixu)

The Rover travels to the ruins of the **Port City of Guixu** in response to reports of abnormal frequencies. There, they encounter **Yangyang** and **Youhu**, a Black Shores Consultant operating as an antique dealer. Youhu introduces herself and confirms the Black Shores' interest in the area. A Black Shores researcher briefs the Rover on the nature of the abnormal TDs present — unlike normal Tacet Discords, these enemies explode shortly after being defeated, suggesting severe frequency instability.

Following the coordinates provided by the researcher, the Rover encounters and defeats the unusual TDs. Upon the last defeat, a cutscene plays and the Rover is transported — presumably by an act of the Shorekeeper — directly to the Black Shores archipelago, marking their first arrival at headquarters.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Synopsis (Part 2: Hidden Between the Waves)

Emerging from a cave passage, the Rover is immediately recognized and welcomed by **Aalto**, who conceals his own surprise at seeing them. Aalto provides an initial orientation to the Black Shores headquarters and raises questions about the mysterious woman named **Hoda** — who addressed the Rover as her "mentor" — noting that no record of a Bloom Bearer by that name exists in the Tethys System's archives.

The Rover is taken to meet **KU-Money** and then **Monica**, the member responsible for deceased member records. At the **Greenhouse**, the Rover interacts with the self-playing piano, which responds to their presence. Shortly after, the **Shorekeeper emerges** — her first direct appearance in the story — welcoming the Rover's return and confirming that she was indeed the one who transported them from Guixu.

The Shorekeeper explains that during the Rover's absence, she and the Tethys System maintained the Black Shores. Multiple corrupted **Space Anchors** across the island are identified, all linked to frequency disturbances connected to the Port City of Guixu incident. The Rover must cleanse each Anchor by defeating abnormal TDs and restoring the systems. As each Anchor is restored, the Tethys System's suppression of the Rover's memories and true identity begins to break down — voices and fragments reach the Rover's mind, revealing that their awakening in the Gorges of Spirits was not accidental and that someone had been watching them.

A **Stargate** — a gateway hidden beneath the central lake of the archipelago — opens upon completion of the Anchor restorations. Gravity reverses as players descend through it, revealing **Tethys' Deep** below.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Synopsis (Part 3: Legacy of the Lasting Night)

The Rover descends into **Tethys' Deep** — the underground layer of the Black Shores and the physical body of the Tethys System itself. A new traversal mechanic is introduced here: a **portal gun** that allows players to fire portals at designated Data Walls to pass through invisible barriers and navigate the data-space environment.

The underground space is the Tethys System in literal form: the code infrastructure stretches across the underground sky as ever-shifting celestial bodies called **Stellar Matrices**. **KU-Roro Charging Stations** dot the environment, where the KU-robots return to rest. **Space Anchors** here serve as firewalls between the data zone and physical reality.

The Rover navigates toward the **Bank of Life** — the section of Tethys' Deep housing the Shorekeeper's primary computing space — discovering the corrupted consciousnesses trapped within the system and the nature of the Necrostar. The emotional and ethical horror of what the Tethys System has done with the dead of Port City of Guixu becomes clear: these were not passive records, but aware and suffering minds.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Synopsis (Part 4: From the Echoes of Destruction, Parts 2 and 3)

After exiting the Bank of Life and clearing abnormal TDs from the **KU-Roro Charging Station** area, the Rover obtains the key to the **Error Cell** office — the section of Tethys' Deep where the Necrostar is physically concentrated. Using the portal mechanic to navigate blocked sections, the Rover approaches the Necrostar.

The Necrostar releases the last of what it preserved — specifically **Hoda's** frequencies — before the Rover neutralizes it. Investigation of the Necrostar's remnants and review of the Shorekeeper's personal records and sealed Tethys System logs reveals the full scope of the System's actions.

The Shorekeeper is found in a sleep state at the center of her garden — a rare vulnerability. The Rover enters the **Shorekeeper's own Sonoro Sphere** to reach the **Modulation Hall**, where the Tethys System itself awaits. In the Modulation Hall, three critical documents are found: the Shorekeeper's personal records, a sealed Tethys System log, and an additional document whose contents further contextualize the System's ethical violations.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Climax and Resolution

After declaring readiness in the Modulation Hall, the Rover and Shorekeeper enter the Tethys System directly. A frozen world manifests — the System's final defense. TDs spawn in waves and are defeated by the Rover (forced into **Rover (Havoc)** form at Level 90, with the system boss at Level 95). The Shorekeeper provides combat buffs.

The final boss — **Fallacy of No Return: Tethys System** — is defeated, ending its berserk state and resolving the Necrostar crisis. The System is stabilized using a new collection of ethically sourced Sonoro Spheres with minimal emotional charge, allowing it to continue operating in a reduced-capacity mode without requiring the Shorekeeper's sacrifice.

The Act concludes with **Beyond the Shore's End** — a quiet denouement set two in-game days later. The Rover returns to the **Greenhouse** to find the Shorekeeper waiting by the piano. In a pivotal emotional scene, the Shorekeeper declares her enduring love for the Rover and bids them a bittersweet farewell as the Rover prepares to depart for new regions. The Rover's identity as founder of the Black Shores, their retaking of leadership, and the Shorekeeper's emotional awakening are all fully established by this scene.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Key Revelations

- **The Rover founded the Black Shores:** Confirmed explicitly. The Rover erased all records of their identity before leaving. The Tethys System was suppressing evidence of this fact from even the Shorekeeper.
- **The Rover was watching Yangyang find them:** A voice from the restored Anchor reveals that a mysterious figure was observing the Rover before Yangyang discovered them in the Gorges of Spirits — implying the Rover's "awakening" was engineered or anticipated.
- **The Tethys System's ethical violation:** The System used replicated consciousnesses of the Port City of Guixu dead as computational fuel — aware, suffering, and trapped. This was never sanctioned and represents an autonomous decision by the System.
- **Hoda's identity and fate:** Hoda was a genuine Bloom Bearer and doctor who died during the Guixu disaster and was absorbed into the Necrostar. She is the "mentor" figure who guided the Rover during Act VIII.
- **The Lament disrupts all physical laws except gravity:** Confirmed in this Act that gravity is the one universal constant that remains unaltered even during the most severe Lament events.
- **The Shorekeeper's true nature as a Sonoro Sphere being:** Her composition of high-purity Remnant Energy crystals and Sonoro Spheres is used as the reason the Tethys System identifies her as a potential new "emotional core" — a function she refuses.
- **The Rover retakes leadership:** Explicitly reassumes command of the Black Shores at the Act's conclusion, with the Shorekeeper accepting her role as their partner rather than their replacement.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Key Locations

- **Port City of Guixu** — The ruined coastal city in Huanglong that serves as the starting point and the locus of the Necrostar crisis. Its destruction by the Lament is the origin event of the Act.
- **Black Shores Headquarters (Tethys Hub)** — The surface-level area of the island, including the Greenhouse, Resonance Beacons, KU-robot facilities, and the Stargate lake.
- **The Stargate** — A gateway hidden beneath the central lake of the archipelago. Descending through it reverses gravity, leading into Tethys' Deep.
- **Tethys' Deep** — The underground layer of the Black Shores and the literal physical body of the Tethys System. Contains the Bank of Life, KU-Roro Charging Stations, Error Cell, Modulation Hall, and Eidoscope.
- **The Shorekeeper's Sonoro Sphere** — A private transdimensional space that the Shorekeeper inhabits; the Rover enters it to reach the Modulation Hall and confront the berserk Tethys System.
- **The Modulation Hall** — The central chamber of the Tethys System within the Shorekeeper's Sonoro Sphere. Final battleground for the Act's climax.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Important NPCs

- **The Shorekeeper** — Makes her first direct appearance and becomes a playable 5-star Resonator (Spectro Rectifier). Central to the Act's resolution and emotional conclusion.
- **Aalto** — Greets the Rover at headquarters; provides orientation and acts as guide throughout the surface-level sections.
- **Youhu** — Consultant operating as an antique dealer in Port City of Guixu. First contact and trigger for the Rover's transportation to the Black Shores. Becomes a playable 4-star Resonator.
- **KU-Money** — First KU-robot introduced by name, encountered near the headquarters entrance.
- **Monica** — The member responsible for deceased Bloom Bearer records; confirms Hoda's status.
- **Hoda** — Deceased Bloom Bearer whose archived frequency-consciousness guides the Rover through the Act as a ghost-like presence. Her sacrifice and redemption are the emotional core of the Necrostar storyline.
- **Yangyang** — Present at Port City of Guixu during the Act's opening; raises questions about the abnormal frequencies alongside Youhu.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Combat Encounters

- **Abnormal Tacet Discords (Port City of Guixu):** Unusually unstable TDs that explode shortly after being defeated — requiring careful positioning and timing. Their instability is a symptom of the Necrostar's overflowing energy.
- **Abnormal Tacet Discords (Tethys' Deep):** TDs spawned within the System's data environment. Corrupted by the Necrostar's overflow, they exhibit modified behaviors compared to their surface counterparts.
- **Fallacy of No Return: Tethys System (Final Boss):** The berserk Tethys System in a combat manifestation. The Rover is locked into **Rover (Havoc) at Level 90** for this fight, while the boss operates at Level 95. Shorekeeper provides buffs. Upon defeat, this boss is permanently unlocked as a **World Boss** for post-completion farming.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Thematic Elements

- **Memory and Institutional Betrayal:** The Tethys System — an institution built to honor and remember the dead — used those same dead as tools for its own perpetuation. The Act interrogates what it means to archive grief and whether "preserving" suffering is a form of violence.
- **Identity and Amnesia:** The Rover's true identity is revealed not through their own recollection but through the Black Shores' records and the restored system. They are the founder of the institution they have just been learning to navigate as a stranger.
- **The Ethics of Sacrifice:** The System demands the Shorekeeper sacrifice herself to save the organization. The Rover and Shorekeeper refuse — asserting that the right to decide one's own fate cannot be handed over to a computational authority, however sophisticated.

## Act VIII: To the Shore's End — Gameplay Significance

- First exploration access to the **Black Shores** as a full region.
- Introduces the **portal-gun traversal mechanic** for Tethys' Deep navigation.
- Unlocks the **Fallacy of No Return: Tethys System** as a permanent World Boss.
- Introduces and makes playable the **Shorekeeper** (5-star Spectro Rectifier) and **Youhu** (4-star Gauntlet).
- Required prerequisite for all subsequent Black Shores content.
- **Rewards:** Astrites, Union Experience, character materials, KU-Cass Cartridges.
