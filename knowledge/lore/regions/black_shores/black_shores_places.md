# Black Shores - Places and Locations

## Overview

The **Black Shores** occupies a hidden archipelago in the Stormy Sea, built atop one of the largest known formations of **Tacetite** in the world. The region is divided into three primary layers or sub-regions, each with a distinct spatial, environmental, and functional identity: the **Black Shores Archipelago** (the surface island), **Tethys' Deep** (the underground layer and body of the Tethys System), and the **Chronorift Metropolis** (the adjacent Honami City Sonoro Sphere, accessed from the shores). The island is concealed from outside navigation by a digital fog generated by the Tacetite's frequency emissions, rendering it undetectable and unreachable without invitation or the Shorekeeper's guidance.

---

## Black Shores Archipelago: Overview and Access

- The **Black Shores Archipelago** is the surface layer of the region — a collection of small islands resting atop the Tacetite foundation. Visually it presents as a remote, dark, and somewhat otherworldly island chain, with vegetation turned black by the Tacetite's pervasive frequency field.
- Access is **by invitation only** for outsiders. Those bearing the **Blake Bloom** flower (the organization's membership token) are permitted to set foot on the soil. All others are barred entry, and the digital fog prevents casual navigation to the island.
- The Tacetite foundation generates a vast energy field that forms a natural barrier concealing the archipelago within the seas. Communication with the outside world is only possible through the Black Shores' own **Resonance Beacon relay system**, as the Tacetite's frequency emissions disrupt all standard communication signals.
- Exploration of the Black Shores Archipelago unlocks in stages: reaching 50% exploration unlocks the text of the **Basic Introduction to the Black Shores I** (from the first page of the New Branch Employee Handbook distributed by KU-Tata); reaching 100% unlocks the **Basic Introduction to the Black Shores II**.
- All plants that take root on the island over time lose their natural colors due to the Tacetite's influence, gradually turning black. The **Blake Bloom** is the most refined expression of this transformation — a plant that has adapted entirely to the island's unique Remnant Energy environment.

## Black Shores Archipelago: The Stargate

- The **Stargate** is a gateway hidden beneath the central waters of the Black Shores Archipelago, located under a lake at the heart of the main island.
- It serves as the primary access point between the surface archipelago and the underground layer known as **Tethys' Deep**. Descending through the Stargate causes gravity to reverse, and players fall "upward" into the underground sky of the data zone below.
- The Stargate's opening during **Chapter I Act VIII** is triggered by the restoration of all corrupted Space Anchors across the surface island — it is not normally accessible until this condition is met narratively.
- Data surges endlessly downward through the Stargate like a torrent, visually emphasizing the transition from physical reality into the data-space environment of Tethys' Deep.
- From within Tethys' Deep, the Stargate appears as a point of light overhead — the "opening" through which the surface world is visible above a sky full of star-like Stellar Matrices.

## Black Shores Archipelago: Tethys Hub

- The **Tethys Hub** is the name for the main operational area of the surface archipelago — the headquarters building complex where most day-to-day Black Shores business takes place.
- It contains personnel management facilities, warning and alert systems, international envoy hosting areas, living quarters and preparation areas for Bloom Bearers and Consultants, and administrative offices.
- The Tethys Hub is organized across multiple vertical levels, connected by staircases and pathways. The uppermost level contains the **Greenhouse**, while lower levels house operational areas and robot facilities.
- KU-robots (Tethys Terminals) roam freely throughout the Hub, performing administrative and maintenance tasks. Each has its own personality and voice frequency encoded in human language, functioning effectively as a small robot society.
- Within the Tethys Hub, a **Resonance Beacon** serves as the primary fast-travel point for the surface region, with additional Beacons placed at key locations across the archipelago.

## Black Shores Archipelago: The Greenhouse

- The **Greenhouse** sits at the highest point of the Tethys Hub — the culminating structure at the top of a long series of staircases that ascend through the headquarters complex.
- It is a glass-and-plant enclosed space serving as both a botanical garden and the organization's most intimate communal area. The Greenhouse is where members are invited to plant seeds from their hometowns when they join the Black Shores, creating a living record of origins.
- The plants that grow here are a mix of flowers from across Solaris-III and the island's own black-pigmented vegetation — a deliberate visual contrast between the colorful memories members carry and the dark environment of the island itself.
- Notable plantings include a **money tree** planted by **Aalto** (he opted for a tree rather than a flower), countless flowers and seeds from **Camellya** (who created a private garden of her own within the space), and absent plantings from members like **Encore** (who did not bring seeds from her hometown).
- A **self-playing piano** sits at the far end of the Greenhouse. It activates on its own and responds to the Rover's touch, suggesting a deep, pre-amnesia connection between the Rover and this space. This piano is one of the first hints within the Black Shores that the Rover has been here before.
- The Greenhouse serves as the emotional and social heart of the organization — the space most associated with warmth, memory, and belonging. The **Shorekeeper** appears here at the conclusion of **Chapter I Act VIII**, waiting by the piano in the final scene of the chapter.

## Black Shores Archipelago: Haven of Sprouts

- The **Haven of Sprouts** is a sub-area of the surface archipelago distinct from the main Tethys Hub complex.
- It is associated with the Black Shores' more natural and organic facilities — agricultural, botanical, or early-stage research spaces distinct from the technologically intensive areas of Tethys' Deep.
- During **Act VIII: Hidden Between the Waves**, the Rover visits the Haven of Sprouts to interact with the KU-Key Robot for clearance to enter certain restricted zones of the island, using the robot's abilities to cleanse corrupted data cores in the area.
- The Haven of Sprouts is also where **KU-Shosho** can be found in the post-**Act XII** Segue quest, providing information about Chisa's whereabouts during her recovery period.

## Black Shores Archipelago: TD Observatory

- The **TD Observatory** is a monitoring installation on the surface of the archipelago used by Black Shores researchers to observe and track Tacet Discord activity across Solaris-III via the Resonance Beacon network and Tethys System integrations.
- During **Act VIII**, one of the corrupted Space Anchors is located at the TD Observatory, and a researcher named **Zhenjian** serves as the observatory keeper. The Anchor's corruption causes localized data-space intrusion effects visible in the area.
- Its position on the island reflects the Black Shores' core mission: constant, global surveillance of Lament and TD activity to enable predictive response before crises escalate.
- The TD Observatory is one of the facilities that makes the Black Shores uniquely capable relative to other nations: its integration with the Tethys System gives it access to a global scope of monitoring that no individual nation could replicate independently.

## Tethys' Deep: Overview and Nature

- **Tethys' Deep** is the underground layer of the Black Shores Archipelago and the physical body of the **Tethys System** itself. In a literal sense, the underground space *is* the Tethys System — the distinction between the computer and the environment it occupies does not exist here.
- It is unlocked during **Chapter I Act VIII: Legacy of the Lasting Night**, accessed via the Stargate beneath the Archipelago's central lake.
- Visually, Tethys' Deep presents as a vast underground space where data takes physical form: **Stellar Matrices** — high-dimensional code structures — stretch across the underground sky like ever-shifting constellations, earning the space the nickname **"City of Eternal Night."**
- The physical world and the data zone coexist uncomfortably in Tethys' Deep. **Space Anchors** — stabilizing devices positioned throughout both the surface and underground levels — act as firewalls between the two, maintaining separation. If Anchors malfunction, the data zone can invade physical reality, causing: regional overlap between digital and physical space, spatial perception disorders in personnel, and simulated objects from the Sand Table escaping into reality.
- The Tethys System's underground infrastructure has **no discernible energy source, physical infrastructure, or processing core** beyond the Tacetite formation. It is effectively powered by the island itself — a natural phenomenon of computational complexity.
- Exploration of Tethys' Deep unlocks the text of the **Advanced Introduction to the Black Shores II** at 100% completion.

## Tethys' Deep: Space Anchors

- **Space Anchors** are stabilizing devices that exist in both the surface archipelago and the underground Tethys' Deep environment. Their primary function is to **calibrate and stabilize the frequencies** of the Tacetite formation and isolate the Tethys System's virtual layer from physical reality.
- Each Anchor also facilitates communication and carries responsibility for observing the Etheric Sea, eliminating the need for a standard Nexus Tower on the Black Shores.
- During **Act VIII**, multiple Anchors are found to be corrupted by the Necrostar's overflow — the physical manifestation of the Tethys System's internal crisis bleeding into the real world. The Rover must cleanse each corrupted Anchor by defeating abnormal TDs and restoring the systems.
- When all surface Anchors are restored, the Stargate opens. When underground Anchors are similarly maintained, the balance between data and reality within Tethys' Deep is preserved.
- If Anchors fail without correction, the resulting instability can cause simulated objects from the Sand Table to materialize physically and spatial perception disorders to develop in Black Shores personnel — documented incidents that have occurred in the organization's history.

## Tethys' Deep: The Stellar Matrix and Data Torrent

- The **Tethys System** consists of two key components operating in Tethys' Deep: the **Data Torrent** and the **Stellar Matrix**.
- The **Stellar Matrix** is a supercomputational Sand Table that imitates the celestial bodies of Solaris-III. Each Stellar Matrix is a data package format that functions similarly to a highly compressed file — capable of storing immense amounts of information and even **simulating a Lament** within its data architecture.
- The first member to formally define a Stellar Matrix noted that standards for observing and evaluating one are similar to the astronomical standards of Solaris-III — the Matrices literally model the cosmos in data form.
- **Certain individuals** can access specific Stellar Matrices under specific circumstances — most notably the Rover during the events of **Act VIII** and **Camellya's Companion Quest**, where they navigate a specific Matrix to resolve the Necrostar crisis and a separate data-death encounter respectively.
- The **Data Torrent** is the second component — the constant flow of data through the Stargate and into the underground. It is through this torrent that information from the outside world enters the system, and through which Resonance Beacon data across Solaris-III is processed.
- The computational capacity of the system, when fully utilized, was once capable of **simulating the entire future of civilization** — but this attempt drained the system's resources to near-total shutdown. This capability remains theoretically possible but has not been safely repeated.

## Tethys' Deep: The Necrostar

- The **Necrostar** is a specific type of Stellar Matrix — a **data black hole** within the Tethys System that forms when faulty, forsaken, or corrupted data accumulates beyond a critical threshold.
- Inside the Necrostar, discarded data Matrices are swallowed and effectively destroyed — data that enters a Necrostar is considered permanently beyond recovery, beyond even Tacetite science's ability to retrieve.
- In **Act VIII**, the Necrostar encountered in the **Error Cell** formed from the accumulated anguish and frequency instability of the replicated consciousnesses the Tethys System had trapped — specifically the survivors and responders killed during the **Port City of Guixu Lament disaster**, including Bloom Bearer **Hoda**.
- As the Necrostar grew, it began radiating abnormal frequencies outward through the physical world, destabilizing the surface archipelago and triggering abnormal TD behavior in Port City of Guixu's ruins.
- The Rover's investigation leads them to the **Error Cell** where the Necrostar is located. The Necrostar releases its final preserved data — primarily Hoda's — before being neutralized by the Rover.
- Post-quest, the **"Proof of Her Existence"** trophy can be unlocked by speaking to **Mie** in the Error Cell basement, where she presents her findings about **Sumika's** presence in the Necrostar-adjacent Honami City loop.

## Tethys' Deep: The Error Cell

- The **Error Cell** is a section of Tethys' Deep dedicated to the containment and analysis of corrupted or anomalous data within the Tethys System.
- It serves as the location of the **Necrostar** during **Act VIII**, making it the central operational area for the quest's second and third sections.
- Post-Act VIII, the Error Cell becomes the primary workspace for the **Shorekeeper's computational analysis** — particularly during events requiring intense processing, such as the expanded frequency feature library scan conducted at the start of **Act XII**.
- An **Eidoscope** is located within or near the Error Cell, with a Resonance Beacon positioned nearby for fast-travel access to the deeper levels.
- The Error Cell also contains **sublevel areas** (Error Cell Sublevel 1 is referenced in Camellya's companion quest, where Beatrice operates). These sublevels host specialized research and testing functions beyond the main Error Cell floor.
- A **KU-Roro Charging Station** is accessible from the Error Cell area, where the KU-robots recharge and rest when their energy reserves run low.

## Tethys' Deep: The Modulation Hall

- The **Modulation Hall** is the innermost chamber of the Tethys System's physical architecture within Tethys' Deep, accessible through the **Shorekeeper's personal Sonoro Sphere**.
- It is the space where the highest-level computations and system-level decisions are executed. The Tethys System's "core" — the entity the Rover must directly confront in **Act VIII's** climax — is reached here.
- During the climax of **Act VIII**, the Rover reads three critical documents in the Modulation Hall before initiating the final confrontation: a specific document, the Shorekeeper's personal records, and the sealed Tethys System logs. These documents complete the picture of the System's ethical violations.
- The final boss **Fallacy of No Return: Tethys System** is fought in or adjacent to the Modulation Hall, manifesting the System's berserk state as a physical combat encounter.
- The Modulation Hall also serves as the setting for portions of **Camellya's Companion Quest** (*Forking Paths Among the Stars*), where Beatrice operates a research function and the Rover and Camellya navigate a Stellar Matrix's data-death scenario.

## Tethys' Deep: Bank of Life

- The **Bank of Life** is a section of Tethys' Deep associated with the archival of frequencies from the Lament's victims — the literal implementation of the Black Shores' mission of remembrance.
- It is the area where the Tethys System stores the emotional and frequency records of those lost to the Lament, which the Shorekeeper and the Black Shores maintain as a living archive.
- During **Act VIII**, the Rover exits the Bank of Life at the start of *From the Echoes of Destruction Part 2* and proceeds toward the Charging Station area, suggesting the Bank of Life is adjacent to or connected with the Error Cell zone.
- The Bank of Life's function — and the Tethys System's corruption of it by holding conscious replicas of the dead rather than passive records — is the ethical core of the Act VIII narrative. The Bank was meant to honor the dead; instead it became their prison.

## Tethys' Deep: Energy Hub

- The **Energy Hub** is a functional area within the Tethys' Deep infrastructure, referenced as one of the locations Bloom Bearers can access for certain operational purposes.
- It is one of the distinct named areas Camellya visits during her companion quest *Forking Paths Among the Stars*, where she interacts with a Black Shores Consultant in the Hub before the quest moves into Stellar Matrix navigation territory.
- The Energy Hub's role within the broader Tethys System architecture likely relates to power distribution and management for the underground data zone.

## Chronorift Metropolis (Honami City): Overview

- The **Chronorift Metropolis**, formally identified as **Honami City**, is a **Sonoro Sphere** — a transdimensional pocket of reality rich in Remnant Energy — located adjacent to the Black Shores Archipelago, accessible from its shores.
- First introduced in **Version 2.8: To the City Set in Amber**, Chronorift Metropolis was sealed within a time loop for twenty years (external time) before the Rover arrived and broke the cycle during **Chapter II Act XII**.
- Visually, the city has a distinctive **cyberpunk-meets-ruin aesthetic**: damaged urban architecture illuminated by neon lights and iridescent skies, combining the decay of a Lament-struck city with the eerie glow of Irideglow Crystal contamination. The overall effect has been described as *"ruins meets neon diner vibes"* — a hybrid of Lament destruction and suspended animation.
- The city is accessible after completing the Act XII main quest and the Segue; permanent access for all players was established via the **Stranger Things in Honami** event.
- The city's map function is disabled during the main quest traversal (except near Shashou Cafe within the Chronorift Metropolis zone) due to the spatial anomalies of the Sonoro Sphere. Full map access is unlocked post-quest.
- A **Stability** mechanic applies within Honami City: it functions as a secondary resource bar depleted by enemy attacks and Irideglow environmental contact. Managed using **Sparkling Water** consumables and **Namipon Stickers** collected throughout the city.

## Chronorift Metropolis: Shashou Cafe

- **Shashou Cafe** is the primary safe haven and social hub within the Chronorift Metropolis Sonoro Sphere.
- The cafe was created when its owner — **Shashou** — used her unique Resonance ability to **cut through space** and clip the cafe building into a protected pocket, shielded from Irideglow contamination and TD attacks.
- It serves as the operational base for Chisa and the Rover during their time inside the Honami City loop. Critical story conversations and planning sequences take place within its walls.
- Following the resolution of the Honami crisis, Shashou is recruited by the Rover to join the Black Shores organization. The cafe becomes a permanent fixture of the Chronorift Metropolis as an accessible landmark.
- At the end of the **Chapter II Segue: Flowing Starlight in the Iris**, the **party celebrating Chisa's recovery** is held at Shashou Cafe, with the Rover, Buling, Abby, Namipon, Shorekeeper, and Camellya all attending.

## Chronorift Metropolis: The Neon Tower

- The **Neon Tower** is one of the most visually iconic landmarks within Honami City — a tall structure that serves as a navigational anchor and point of orientation within the otherwise disorienting Sonoro Sphere cityscape.
- Its neon illumination contrasts with the Irideglow Crystal haze and the decay of the surrounding ruins, making it visible from a distance within the sphere.
- The Neon Tower is one of the key areas referenced in exploration guides for the city, positioned centrally enough to help players establish their bearings in the otherwise labyrinthine urban environment.

## Chronorift Metropolis: Meishin Passageway

- The **Meishin Passageway** is a route within the Chronorift Metropolis that connects key areas of the city's interior during the main quest.
- It serves as the navigational path leading toward the confrontation with the **Chronorift Yakujin** (Elegy of the Fallen), the Overlord-class entity controlling the Irideglow contamination and maintaining the time loop.
- Post-quest, the Meishin Passageway opens as part of the permanent exploration content of the Chronorift Metropolis.

## Chronorift Metropolis: Sazanami Avenue

- **Sazanami Avenue** is one of the named streets and open areas of the Chronorift Metropolis, serving as a primary thoroughfare in the city's exploration layout.
- It contains **Namipon Lightboxes** (reactivatable fast-travel shortcuts that deactivate when the player leaves the area), **Telephone Booths** (environmental interaction points with collectible lore), and hidden **Namipon** characters for completionist discovery.
- Notable: a **side quest / trophy segment** called "The Flower We Saw That Day" takes place on Sazanami Avenue, involving three children's hide-and-seek at bridge ends and near red signs — one of several story vignettes embedded in the city's post-quest exploration content.

## Chronorift Metropolis: Shiawase Fountain Square

- **Shiawase Fountain Square** is a public plaza within Honami City, named after a fountain at its center. It serves as a social gathering point within the city's layout.
- Contains **Namipon Lightboxes** and **Telephone Booths** for exploration and lore collection.
- A trophy-unlocking NPC interaction titled **"When Supernatural Confessions Became Commonplace"** takes place in Shiawase Fountain Square, involving characters named Kumi and Shingo.
- The Square's open design and fountain make it one of the more visually spacious areas in an otherwise dense urban environment, providing a sense of breathing room amid the city's layered ruins.

## Chronorift Metropolis: Exploration Points of Interest

- **7 Namipon Lightboxes** — Fast-travel shortcuts distributed throughout the city. These deactivate when the player exits the Sonoro Sphere and must be reactivated on re-entry, requiring players to walk to their locations before fast-traveling.
- **16 Telephone Booths** — Environmental interaction points containing lore fragments and collectible audio/text entries about Honami City's history and its residents prior to the Lament event.
- **12 Missing Namipon** characters — Hidden Namipon figures scattered throughout the city for collection as a completionist activity.
- **5 Irideglow Portals** (3 accessible) — Spatial portals connected to the Irideglow Crystal contamination. Navigating these portals is required for certain exploration routes and collectible access.
- **4 Overlord-class Tacet Discords** — Elite field bosses distributed throughout the city for combat challenges and Echo farming.
- **13 Lament Zones** (9 accessible) — Areas of intensified Waveworn activity within the Sonoro Sphere, offering challenge content and materials.
- **18 Capsule Machines** — Interactive machines distributed across the city, likely dispensing collectible items or cosmetics.
- **13 Supply Chests** — Standard exploration chests.
- **36 Summer Flowers** — Daily collectibles harvestable via a **Sensor** system. These reset daily and are a primary source of event-exclusive and exploration materials.
