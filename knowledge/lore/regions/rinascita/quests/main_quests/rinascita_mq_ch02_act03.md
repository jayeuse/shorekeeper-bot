## Act III: What Yesterday Wept, Today Doth Sing

## Act III: Synopsis

The Rover wakes up on the misty shores of Penitent's End after falling from a bridge during a dragon attack. They are found by Roccia and Pero of the Troupe of Fools, who reveal that Abby saved the Rover's life but has retreated into the Rover's body to recover. The Rover navigates the island's treacherous "Remnant Clouds" and Tacet Discords, eventually confronting the Dragon of Dirge in its territory. After the battle, the Rover helps the Troupe—specifically rescuing a member named Bardolino—and participates in a rehearsal for the upcoming Carnevale. The act concludes with a confrontation with Phrolova, who traps the Rover in a "created space" to witness a twisted finale, followed by a mysterious invitation from Carlotta to meet at the Cathedral of Mercury.

## Act III: Key Revelations

- **The Lie of the Pilgrimage**: The "Pilgrim's Sail" is exposed as a method of execution, not redemption. The Order of the Deep sends dissidents ("Fools") to Penitent's End to die by the hands of the Dragon of Dirge or the Remnant Clouds.
- **Origin of the Troupe**: The Troupe of Fools is comprised entirely of survivors of these death sentences. Brant, the leader, was exiled as a child for questioning why the Sentinel was worshipped despite never being seen.
- **Fool's Elysium**: The Troupe's base is built from the wreckage of the pilgrim ships, symbolizing their resilience ("dancing in chains").
- **Phrolova's Power**: Phrolova demonstrates the ability to manipulate space and summon Tacet Discords (like Hecate) using a "script," hinting at her role as a "Playwright" for the Fractsidus.

## Act III: Key Locations

- **Penitent's End**: A desolate, fog-shrouded island used as a dumping ground for the Order's enemies.
- **Fool's Elysium**: A hidden cavern sanctuary adorned with colorful, makeshift decorations made from ship debris.
- **Dragon of Dirge Territory**: The nesting ground of the region's Calamity-class Tacet Discord.

## Act III: Important Characters

- **Roccia**: The Troupe's "First Mate" and a fierce protector.
- **Brant**: The charismatic and optimistic Captain of the Troupe.
- **Phrolova**: A Fractsidus agent manipulating events from the shadows.
- **Carlotta**: Revealed to be aiding the Rover to expose the Order's secrets.

## Act III: Cultural Elements

- **"Dancing in Chains"**: The Troupe's philosophy of finding freedom and joy even in the face of certain death and oppression. They value laughter as their "treasure" and use their performances to speak truth to power.

## Act III: Political Developments

- **The Order's Crackdown**: The reinstatement of the Pilgrim's Sail (Fool's Sails) by Fenrico is confirmed as a tool for political purging rather than religious piety.

## Interlude: Old Man and the Whale

## Interlude: Synopsis

This quest serves as a narrative bridge involving Phoebe and the legend of a "ghost ship." The Rover aids Phoebe in investigating reports of a "Siren" and a "Ghost Ship" haunting the waters near the Riccioli Islands. They encounter Cetus the Tidebreaker, a massive whale-like Tacet Discord that is initially hostile. By deciphering ancient tablets and working with the eccentric Captain Ahab, they discover that the Tidebreaker is not a monster but a guardian tasked with guiding lost souls. The "Siren" is revealed to be a Lorelei's Kindred (a Tacet Discord) mimicking the dead. The quest culminates in the Rover and Phoebe repairing the ship Pequod and sailing into the storm to defeat the Siren, ultimately reconciling with the Tidebreaker.

## Interlude: Key Revelations

- **Cetus the Tidebreaker**: Far from being a mindless beast, the Tidebreaker is a benevolent entity that guides the souls of those lost at sea to the "realm beyond."
- **The Siren's Identity**: The "Siren" causing ships to wreck is a Tacet Discord variant of Lorelei, which uses the frequencies of the deceased to lure the living.
- **Phoebe's Growth**: Phoebe, a devout Acolyte, learns that the Order's teachings about "monsters" are not always absolute, leading her to question the rigid dogma she was raised with.

## Interlude: Key Locations

- **Riccioli Islands**: A tropical archipelago filled with marine resources and ancient ruins.
- **Reyes Island**: The specific location of the lighthouse and the final confrontation.
- **The Pequod**: A repaired ghost ship used to navigate the storm.

## Interlude: Important Characters

- **Phoebe**: An Acolyte seeking the truth behind the maritime legends.
- **Captain Ahab**: A spectral or eccentric figure obsessed with the "final island" and the whale.
- **Cetus the Tidebreaker**: The giant whale Tacet Discord.

## Interlude: Cultural Elements

- **Maritime Folklore**: The quest explores Rinascita's deep-seated superstitions regarding the sea, ghost ships, and the "call" of the deep.
