## Act X: The Bygone Shall Always Return

## Act X: Synopsis

The Rover returns to Ragunna to find the city in panic. Avidius, claiming to be the "Harbinger of the Dark Tide," attacks. It is revealed that Avidius is a construct created by Cristoforo (The Playwright), modeled after the memories of a "Mad King" (Caligula). The Rover must enter the Civilization Capsule (the Sentinel's Relic) to uncover the truth before defeating Avidius at Thessaleo Fells.

## Act X: Key Revelations

The Playwright's Power: Cristoforo can bring "fictional" or "remembered" entities to life using the Dark Tide. Avidius believed he was real, which gave him power.

The Sentinel's Message: The Civilization Capsule does not require the Threnodian's power to open because it was tainted by the Tide; it opens to the Rover's presence, delivering a final warning about the "Paradise" the Fractsidus intends to create.

Fenrico's Fate: Hints are dropped that Fenrico (the Primus) has been missing or acting strangely because he is preparing for a final sacrifice.

## Act X: Key Locations

Thessaleo Fells: The site of the confrontation with Avidius.

Civilization Capsule: An ancient data repository.

## Act X: Important Characters

Cristoforo: Revealed as a major antagonist manipulating events from behind the scenes.

Avidius (The Mad King): A tragic villain who is merely a puppet of memory.
