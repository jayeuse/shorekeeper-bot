## Act VI: Flames of Heart

## Act VI: Synopsis

Rover and Lupa venture into the Vale of Glory (the graveyard of heroes) to confront the source of the anomaly. They discover that Mya was a construct of the Threnodian Leviathan who committed suicide 20 years ago to prevent the Threnodian from manifesting. Her corpse became the Lady of the Sea, a monster now haunting the waterways. Lupa uses the Copper Mirror of Truth to witness the past and realizes she is meant to be the new vessel. However, Lupa rejects the "script" written by the Playwright (Cristoforo). By defeating the Lady of the Sea, they break the time loop. Cartethyia then reveals that the Sentinel hid critical memories in Septimont's Central Hub.

## Act VI: Key Revelations

- **Mya's True Identity**: Mya was not human but a Threnodian creation that gained a soul and chose death over enslavement.
- **The Playwright's Script**: The antagonist Cristoforo has been rewriting reality to force a tragic ending, using Mya's corpse as a conduit for the Threnodian.
- **Lupa's Defiance**: Lupa breaks the cycle not by fighting the Threnodian's influence with brute force, but by asserting her own identity and rewriting the ending of the play.
- **The Central Hub**: The Sentinel Imperator transferred their memories to a facility in Septimont before merging with the Leviathan, meaning the truth of the world is hidden there.

## Act VI: Key Locations

- **Vale of Glory**: A solemn valley where Septimont's greatest heroes are buried.
- **Old District**: A flooded, ruined section of the city where the Lady of the Sea resides.
- **Central Hub**: An ancient data repository hidden within the city's infrastructure.

## Act VI: Important Characters

- **Mya (Lady of the Sea)**: The tragic antagonist/victim.
- **Cristoforo (The Playwright)**: Revealed as a major manipulator working with the Fractsidus.
- **Arsinosa (Lioness of Glory)**: The guardian beast of Septimont who acknowledges Lupa.
