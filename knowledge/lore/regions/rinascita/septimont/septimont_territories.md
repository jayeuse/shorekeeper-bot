## Septimont Territory

## Septimont

**Region Type:** City-State, Elevated, Martial  
**Controlling Authority:** Council of Elders, Ephor Augusta, Priestess Iuno  
**Character:** Independent, combative, honor-focused

The second major city-state of Rinascita, built on elevated plateaus with a culture emphasizing strength, glory, and independent thought. While still acknowledging Imperator, Septimont maintains greater autonomy from religious orthodoxy than Ragunna.

## Septimont: Titanbone Expanse

Area named for massive skeletal remains, possibly of ancient creature or Tacet Discord. Features:

- Enormous bones integrated into landscape
- Archaeological/biological significance
- Local legends about the Titanbone's origin
- Training grounds using terrain features
- Symbolic importance to Septimontan culture

## Septimont: Mournfell Canyon

Deep canyon with tragic associations. Features:

- Site of historical battle or disaster
- Memorial sites for fallen warriors
- Dangerous terrain testing warriors' skills
- Acoustic properties amplifying sounds (mourning)
- Strategic military importance

## Septimont: Border Mountains

Mountain range separating Septimont from other territories. Features:

- Natural defensive barrier
- Mountain passes requiring control
- Harsh environment building resilience
- Resource mining operations
- Watchtowers and border posts

## Septimont: Minos Sewers

Underground sewer system beneath Septimont. Features:

- Engineering marvel from city's founding
- Labyrinthine passages
- Criminal or outcast populations in depths
- Connection to other underground systems
- Maintenance by specialized workers or Echoes

## Septimont: Capitoline Hill

The elevated center of Septimont's political power. Features:

- Ephor's Palace and government buildings
- Temple/residence of Priestess Iuno
- Council of Elders meeting chambers
- Commanding views of surrounding territory
- Prestigious residential areas
- Symbolic height representing superiority

## Septimont: Vale of Glory

Arena or memorial ground celebrating military achievement. Features:

- Public ceremonies honoring heroes
- Training grounds for elite warriors
- Monuments to famous victories
- Gladiatorial contests or military demonstrations
- Where the Carnevale's martial competitions occur

## Sanguis Plateaus

**Region Type:** Highland, Wild, Resource-Rich  
**Controlling Authority:** Septimont (claimed), Contested  
**Character:** Dangerous, Blood-red soil

Elevated plateaus with distinctive red soil (sanguis meaning blood), known for dangerous wildlife, Tacet Discords, and valuable resources. The name evokes both the land's appearance and the blood spilled claiming it.

## Sanguis Plateaus: Atilius Canyon

Major canyon cutting through the plateaus. Features:

- Named for historical figure or first explorer
- Strategic passage through difficult terrain
- Ambush sites and defensive positions
- Seasonal flooding or water flow
- Echo nesting in canyon walls

## Sanguis Plateaus: Hunter's Den

Settlement or camp for hunters tracking game and TDs. Features:

- Rough accommodation for hunters
- Storage and processing of kills
- Trading post for hides, meat, resources
- Information exchange about dangers
- Launch point for hunting expeditions

## Sanguis Plateaus: Tetragon Temple

Ancient temple with four-sided or four-part structure. Features:

- Pre-settlement religious site
- Possibly dedicated to different deity than Imperator
- Iuno's affiliation suggests current Septimontian use
- Geometric design of spiritual significance
- Connection to land's deeper history

## Sanguis Plateaus: Howlfang Lair

Den of dangerous creatures, possibly wolf-like TDs. Features:

- Natural cave system
- Aggressive territory defended by inhabitants
- Valuable materials from defeated creatures
- Training challenge for warriors
- Strategic importance if controlled

## Sanguis Plateaus: Rustblood Pass

Mountain pass with red stone or soil. Features:

- Critical route through Sanguis Plateaus
- Named for appearance or historical bloodshed
- Fortifications controlling access
- Dangerous during certain seasons
- Patrol routes and guard posts

## Sanguis Plateaus: Maroonwood

Forest with deep red-brown foliage. Features:

- Unique ecosystem adapted to plateau conditions
- Valuable timber or other resources
- Wildlife and Echo habitats
- Difficult navigation without guides
- Possible connection to underground areas

## Sanguis Plateaus: Asphodel Barrens

Barren area, possibly named for death-associated flower. Features:

- Limited vegetation and harsh conditions
- Historical significance (battlefield, disaster site)
- Mineral resources exposed by erosion
- Testing ground for survival training
- Spiritual significance to death and remembrance

## Sanguis Plateaus: Erosion Cavern

Cave system carved by water and time. Features:

- Natural sculptures and formations
- Underground waterways
- Mineral deposits
- Echo habitation
- Connection to deeper cave networks

## Sanguis Plateaus: Tideline Verge

Where plateaus meet water influence. Features:

- Transition between highland and maritime zones
- Unique ecology mixing environments
- Strategic importance
- Resource diversity
- Access point to different regions

## Sanguis Plateaus: The Wastelands

Desolate area within the plateaus. Features:

- Environmental devastation (natural or Lament-caused)
- Dangerous TD concentrations
- Avoided by most travelers
- Possible valuable resources if accessed safely
- Test of extreme survival skills

## Sanguis Plateaus: Murmurstown

Small settlement with ominous name. Features:

- Isolated community
- Superstitious or secretive residents
- Strange occurrences giving it the name
- Mining or resource extraction operations
- Connection to underground mysteries

## Sanguis Plateaus: Earthrend Wedge

Geological formation or canyon. Features:

- Dramatic split in landscape
- Result of tectonic forces or Lament destruction
- Barrier requiring circumnavigation
- Unique mineral exposure
- Dangerous to cross

## Sanguis Plateaus: Three Heroes' Crest

Location commemorating three legendary figures. Features:

- Memorial or monument
- Historical site of important battle or event
- Training destination for aspiring heroes
- Strategic overlook or defensive position
- Cultural importance to Septimont
