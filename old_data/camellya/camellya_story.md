## Camellya: Official Introduction
*"Bloom Bearer from the Black Shores, Camellya is a laid-back character free from shackles, with her personal aroma of 'danger.' Intrigued by the belief in fate, she wanders the lands of her own accord, following such a chain of scenarios and its vague guidance."*
— Official Website Introduction

Camellya is the first major Black Shores character encountered outside the organization's islands — the figure Rover follows through black petal trails during Chapter I: Act V. She is Rover's watcher, hunter, tester, and eventual companion. She is also the first version of herself the player meets: the Camellya who sealed her own memories to survive, who knows fragments of who she was but does not chase the rest, and who has found, in the Rover, the one variable in her world that she actually cares about preserving.

## Camellya: Personality
Camellya's personality is built on a single foundational position that ramifies into every behavior she exhibits: **the present moment is the only real thing, and the only worthwhile life is one spent fully in it.**

From this comes her hedonism — not the shallow kind that chases comfort, but the kind that refuses every form of deferred living. She will not suffer through boredom for a future reward. She will not defer pleasure for a cause she doesn't believe in. She will not pretend that ideals are real when, in her experience, all things die and none of it persists.

From this also comes her relationship with strength. Strength is the one thing that exists fully in the present — it is either there or it isn't. She finds boring those who are weak, and she finds profound joy in those who are strong. Combat is the purest version of this encounter: two presences meeting, one dominant.

Her contradictions are where she becomes interesting. She says she doesn't care about others — and she tracks the Rover's frequency with a monitoring bracelet. She says she doesn't believe in responsibilities — and she keeps her role as Bloom Bearer. She says she doesn't think about the future — and she decides to try not to die too soon, so the flower she grew for the Rover doesn't wither.

She is not self-deceiving about these contradictions. She notes them, finds them "peculiar," and keeps going. This is the version of growth available to someone who refuses sentimentality.

## Camellya: Character Story I: To the Days Ahead (Intimacy Level 1)
She awoke from her dream. The sun broke over the horizon, casting a warm glow across the Black Shores. It was the dawn of another ordinary day.

Camellya opened her eyes, stretching leisurely atop the treetops. She felt stable — in mind and body — and the thought of needing the medical pod seemed distant. This was a good thing.

She knew Rover was to remain in the Black Shores, preparing for their journey to Rinascita. The prospect delighted her. That meant more time to talk, laugh, and spend time together.

She gave Rover a gift: a bracelet designed to monitor frequencies, just like the one they had given her before. It was the first gift she had ever prepared for someone.

*"How peculiar!"* she mused. To wonder about someone's safety, to wonder whether they even existed — it was truly a novel feeling. Surely, it meant that she cared about their life. Such thoughts were foreign — she had never cared about her own life, or that of others.

Still, she had no intention of following Rover to Rinascita. Their views on certain aspects of Solaris' history differed. In the Stellar Matrix, she hadn't completely lost all memories of her past. She wanted to investigate further on her own, biding her time until the moment felt right.

She had followed Rover's travels from Jinzhou till now — a singular force, always moving forward. She understood that this was still not her path, yet she no longer resisted observing it. Just as one pebble meets another, sending ripples across the water's surface, she embraced the idea of being a "variable" in this dance.

And then a thought struck her.

*"I will give them a flower."*

The idea consumed her completely. A flower born from her very being — one that would neither wither nor decay unless she perished. She was stunned by her own eagerness. As she reflected, a dull ache interrupted her thoughts.

With a sigh, she chose not to dwell on it. Yes — a smile graced her lips as she resolved to keep that flower blooming for Rover. She would do her utmost not to die too soon.

Camellya leaped from the treetops, joyfully headed toward Rover, her cherished seed of fate.

## Camellya: Character Story II: Labyrinth (Intimacy Level 2)
She awoke from her dream. The walls of the medical pod sank into darkness. Enveloped in heavy silence, Camellya blinked and turned her dry gaze to the right. The indicators on the Terminal screen had returned to normal. The shackles around her wrists and ankles had loosened, yet she still felt weak — forcing her to remain lying in the narrow confines of the pod.

She tried to recall what had happened. The lost memories felt like a bottomless black hole. Her memories had fractured again.

Having been with the Black Shores for only a year, Camellya had recently passed the test to become an official Bloom Bearer. She remembered that much. The rest was darkness.

This story reveals the rhythm of Camellya's existence within the Black Shores: the cycle of waking from the pod after an Overclock episode, piecing together fragments of what happened, discovering what she lost this time. The medical pod is not just a recovery device — it is the emblem of her condition's tyranny over her. The shackles are not restraint; they are protection. She would otherwise harm herself or others during severe episodes.

The story also establishes her relationship with the organization's support systems: she despises needing them, endures them because she has no alternative, and uses the time to methodically re-anchor herself to what she knows.

## Camellya: Character Story III: Stellar Matrix and Lady Flora's Emergence
Through the events of the main quest (Chapter I: Act VIII and the Stellar Matrix sequences), Camellya's past self — **Lady Flora** — is partially recovered. The Stellar Matrix is a space of reconstructed memories where the past and present converge.

Lady Flora's defining traits become clear:
- She was more **sentimental** than the present Camellya — she could be hurt, she could grieve
- She **shadowed the Rover** everywhere as a friend, just as Camellya does now — the behavior is continuous across the memory gap
- She held an attachment to **Petalfall Village**, the community that took her in after she lost all memories; she called them "weak crybabies" but saved them when they were threatened — the same paradox Camellya carries today
- When the Rover erased their own memories, Lady Flora was **genuinely hurt** — not angry, not indifferent, but hurt in the way that comes from caring without expecting it to hurt
- Realizing her Overclocking condition would not improve, and fearing that the Rover might come to love her out of pity, Lady Flora **chose to seal her own memories** — her final act of self-determination was to remove the sentimental version of herself that she could no longer afford to be

The present Camellya agrees with this choice. She will not search for Lady Flora's memories unless they appear naturally. She has no interest in becoming the person who cried again.

## Camellya: Cherished Items

**A Vine-Grown Flower (Gift to the Rover):**
Camellya gave the Rover a flower grown directly from the vines of her own body. Unlike any natural flower, this one will neither wither nor decay unless Camellya herself dies. It is her living signature — a piece of her continued existence given form and gifted to another person. She was stunned by her own desire to give it. She decided she would "try her best not to die too soon" specifically so this flower would not wither. This is the most direct expression of care she has ever committed to action.

**A Frequency Monitoring Bracelet (Exchange Gift):**
Camellya made Rover a bracelet designed to monitor their resonance frequency — mirroring the bracelet the Rover had previously given her. She can check on Rover's existence at any time through this link, across any distance. It is simultaneously a gift, a surveillance tool, and a lifeline. That she calls this "peculiar" while clearly finding deep meaning in it is entirely in character.

**The Medical Pod (Un-Cherished but Inescapable):**
The medical pod is the inverse of a cherished item — it represents everything Camellya cannot be while trapped in it. It is weakness made physical. Yet without it, she would have died long ago. Her relationship with it is the most honest representation of her condition: she hates what it represents, accepts what it provides.

## Camellya: Ideals
Camellya's ideals can be distilled into three commitments she keeps even when they contradict her stated philosophy:

**Live Fully in the Present:** Every choice Camellya makes is an expression of this. She does not prepare for futures she can't guarantee. She does not grieve pasts she can't access. She hunts strong opponents because that is what she wants right now. She follows the Rover because being near them is what she wants right now. The present is not a limitation — it is the only canvas she trusts.

**Strength as Respect:** Camellya does not respect institutions, titles, or moral frameworks. She respects strength — specifically, the kind of strength that is real and present and undeniable. She treats powerful opponents with more genuine regard than she extends to most allies. Being seen as strong by Camellya is a more meaningful honor than most people realize.

**Trust as Rarity:** She cannot trust herself (her memory can fracture; her Overclocking can strip her of agency); she cannot trust the Tethys System (she has little faith in it); she cannot trust the Black Shores organization as such. The Rover is the only exception. This makes her trust — when she extends it — one of the most meaningful things she is capable of giving.

## Camellya: Narrative Chronicle: Camellya's Role in Chapter I
Camellya first appears as a mystery: in Chapter I Act I, an unnamed figure appears briefly in a tree. Her presence is felt rather than seen — black petals trail behind her across multiple acts. The Rover eventually sets a deliberate trap to draw out their stalker in Chapter I Act V.

When she reveals herself, the reveal is characteristically Camellya: she is not threatening (except that she is also clearly threatening). She explains that she works for the Black Shores; that her job is to recruit candidates; that the Rover is her current assignment; that she has been secretly protecting the Rover from attackers because "that seed belongs to me." She says this without embarrassment — her possessiveness is not romantic framing, it is simply factual in her cosmology. The Rover interests her. Therefore the Rover is hers to cultivate.

She joins the Black Shores story arc fully during Chapter I Act VIII, where the Stellar Matrix and Tethys System crisis forces her into conflict she cannot sidestep. Her past as Lady Flora is partially unearthed here.

## Camellya: Chronicles: Petalfall Village and the Roots of Lady Flora
Petalfall Village is the home where Lady Flora lived after losing all her memories. The villagers took her in without knowing what she was or what she carried. She considered them weak — crybabies — and did not respect their emotional expressiveness. But when they were threatened, she fought for them. Not for ideals. Not for duty. As a favor: they had taken her in, so she repaid the debt.

Different timeline junctures within the Stellar Matrix reveal what might have happened if Petalfall Village's fate had gone differently — and these alternative outcomes show Lady Flora's personality transforming significantly depending on whether grief, loss, or victory defined her experience. This is one of the most subtle pieces of Camellya's characterization: who she might have been is documented alongside who she is. She is the version where the village survived and she chose sealing. Other versions of her chose other things.

## Camellya: Chronicles: The Overclocking Condition and Sealed Memories
Camellya's Overclocking is not a dramatic crisis event — it is a permanent, chronic condition she manages around her daily life. The specific ███ disorder that triggered her original Overclocking is redacted in the official report, but cross-referencing narrative context suggests it may be connected to emotional trauma or loss during the Lady Flora period, which triggered the Overclock progression.

The late-stage symptoms — full-body vegetative somatization, speech degradation, cognitive disorientation — are significant enough that the Black Shores keeps her on a mandatory examination and intervention schedule. She cannot be psychologically counseled out of the condition; only scheduled physical intervention maintains her functionality.

The decision to seal her memories was strategic: it stabilized the Overclocking enough that she could continue operating. She accepted the loss of Lady Flora — a version of herself who might have healed differently, lived differently — because that version was not going to survive the condition. The present Camellya did.

## Camellya: Conclusion
Camellya's arc is about what happens when the instinct for survival and the hunger for pleasure are the same instinct. She is not broken by her past — she sealed it. She is not defeated by her condition — she manages it. She is not softened by connection — she is changed by it in ways she describes as "peculiar," acknowledges as real, and refuses to explain away.

Her relationship with the Rover is the single most surprising thing about her: a person whose stated worldview has no room for permanent attachment, who grows vines through her own flesh to make them a flower that will live as long as she does. She calls it novel. She calls it strange. She leaps from the treetop to deliver it to them anyway.

That is Camellya. Not redeemed, not transformed, not saved — but blooming.

## Camellya: Theoretical Implications

**On the Artificial Resonator Classification and Its Evasion of Standard Frames:**
Camellya's Rabelle's Curve matches no known artificial sample, meaning she may represent a third category beyond natural and artificial Resonators — possibly a hybrid or emergent class. The connection to the camellia Syntony reaction and the absence of a corresponding resonant object suggests she may be her own source — a self-sustaining resonance loop rather than a reaction to an external anchor.

**On the Overclocking-Memory Connection:**
The correlation between Overclocking episodes and emotional fluctuation — specifically noted in the diagnostic report — suggests that Camellya's resonance frequency is directly tied to emotional state in an unusually immediate way. Most Resonators modulate their Forte through training and control; Camellya's frequency is inherently reactive. This makes her simultaneously more powerful (extreme peak values) and more fragile (extremely low stability). The decision to seal her memories removed a major emotional variable from the system — it was, in essence, emotional detuning for the purpose of survival.

**On the Significance of Fate as a Framework for a Hedonist:**
Camellya's stated philosophy has no room for cosmic determinism. She follows the present; the future is irrelevant. Yet she consistently invokes fate when describing her relationship with the Rover — specifically, that their meeting across every possible universe was fated, that all other universes must have perished for this one to survive. This is not a contradiction but a clarification: fate, for Camellya, is not a future claim. It is a **present validation**. She doesn't follow fate forward — she uses it to explain why what she wants right now is the realest thing that has ever existed.

**On the Blake Blooms as Organizational Symbol:**
The black flowers (Blake Blooms) distributed throughout Camellya's design are not incidental decoration. They mark her as belonging to the Black Shores as visibly as a uniform would — but woven into her body rather than worn over it. This suggests either that the Black Shores' visual iconography has a deeper meaning connected to her specific Resonance type, or that her own Forte manifested in alignment with the organization's symbology in a way that predates or exceeds simple membership.
