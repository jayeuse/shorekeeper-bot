## Character Analysis: The Feathered Tempest
Cartethyia exists at the intersection of humanity and divinity, a "malicious creation" forged from the curse of the Threnodian Leviathan yet sustained by the grace of the Sentinel Imperator. She is a vessel designed to contain a cosmic war within a mortal frame.

## Character Profile: Ontological Overview
Her identity is bifurcated into two distinct personas, representing the duality of her nature:
- **Cartethyia (The Wandering Knight):** The "rambunctious girl" from Egla Town. This persona embodies freedom, mischief, and a grounded humanity.
- **Fleurdelys (The Blessed Maiden):** The divine construct. This persona is somber, tranquil, and terrifyingly powerful. It represents the "shackles of her titles" and the overwhelming weight of the Sentinel’s mandate.

## History: The Era of Innocence (The Knight of the Windmill)
Cartethyia’s origins lie in the humble countryside of Egla Town. Archival records describe her early years not as those of a divine vessel, but as a "rambunctious" and "mischievous" girl with no noble bloodline. Her psychological archetype was shaped by a local oral tradition: the story of a wandering knight who battled a giant as tall as a windmill. The young Cartethyia perceived a "heart of pure nobility" within this delusion. This inspiration manifested in her "patrolling" the village with a wooden sword, defending livestock from imagined beasts.

## History: The Era of Fleurdelys (The Blessed Maiden)
The transition from Cartethyia to Fleurdelys represents the imposition of external order upon internal chaos. She was identified by the Order of the Deep as a vessel of Divinity and bestowed with heavy titles like "Queen of Gale and Tide". In this role, she was stripped of her identity, performing solemn rites and crushing Tacet Discord outbreaks. This era was defined by a growing cognitive dissonance as she witnessed the corruption of the Rinascita nobility and the apathy of the Order.

## History: The Era of Reclamation (The Inverted Tower)
The turning point was her isolation within the Inverted Tower. Entering alone to confront the Divinity, she became trapped in a "long dream," battling metaphysical representations of sea creatures. To maintain sanity, she stitched hand puppets and staged plays. By determining the narrative of her puppets, she reconstructed her identity, emerging not as Fleurdelys, but as Cartethyia, the "free and unfettered wandering knight".

## Lore: The Paradox of Origins
Cartethyia’s resonance acts as a container for two opposing cosmic forces.
- **The Threnodian Leviathan:** She was formed through the "curse and malice" of the Leviathan, classifying her partially as a "malicious creation". This explains her instinctive aversion to fish and her "dangerous" aura.
- **The Sentinel Imperator:** Despite her cursed origin, she carries the protective blessing of the Sentinel.
This duality allows for her "Second Awakening," stabilizing these clashing frequencies to transition freely between forms.

## Character Design: Visual Semiotics
- **The Blue Vines:** A network of blue vines wraps around her chest/back, symbolizing the Threnodian's curse (parasitic constricting).
- **The Golden Helix:** Golden double-helix patterns on her sleeves represent the Sentinel's binding frequency (ordering the chaos).
- **Haultir:** Her sword is a symbol of human aspiration ("Don Quixote" archetype), connecting her to her choice to save others despite her origin.

## Psychological Profile: The Don Quixote Complex
Cartethyia uses the archetype of the "Wandering Knight" to armor herself against the reality of her nature. To fight a giant windmill is to fight an insurmountable force—like a Threnodian. If she is a knight, then her battles have meaning; she is not just a monster fighting another monster.

## Psychological Profile: Survivor's Guilt
She harbors complex guilt regarding Cantarella, believing she "stole" the future intended for the other girl. This drives her "older sister" attitude toward Lupa—she is desperately trying to give back to world she feels she has wronged.

## Relationships
- **Lupa (The Wolf):** A bond of mutual acceptance. Lupa does not recoil from Cartethyia's powers, validating her humanity.
- **The Rover (The Godkiller):** Cartethyia views Rover mythically as the "Swordbearer" who allowed her to choose her own ending.
