# Rinascita Main Quests - Comprehensive Guide

## Chapter II: All Silent Souls Can Sing (Structure Overview)

Chapter II takes place in Rinascita and consists of 12 acts plus segue chapters, following Rover's journey through Ragunna and Septimont as they investigate Echo anomalies, uncover the truth about Sentinel Imperator, and confront the Threnodian Leviathan.

---

## Prologue: Through the Sea Thou Break

## Prologue: Synopsis

After learning that Abby's frequency is weakening, Shorekeeper directs Rover to seek help from Sentinel Imperator in Rinascita. Rover journeys across the treacherous Stormy Sea alongside the Troupe of Fools (Brant, Roccia, and Lario), encountering the massive sea creature Cetus before arriving at Ragunna's port.

## Prologue: Key Revelations

- **Abby's Condition**: Abby's frequency is deteriorating and requires the Sentinel's intervention
- **Past Rover's Message**: Past Rover left encoded messages in Tethys System accessible only after resolving core issues
- **Ancient Pact**: Rover once made a pact with Sentinel Imperator
- **Troupe of Fools**: Traveling performers who celebrate freedom and counter-culture, serving as guides to Rinascita
- **Cetus Encounter**: First contact with Rinascita's unique marine megafauna
- **Ragunna Introduction**: Rover gains first impressions of Ragunna's Venice-like canal city structure and customs procedures

## Prologue: Key Locations

- **The Black Shores**: Starting point, Shorekeeper's domain
- **Stormy Sea**: Dangerous waters between Black Shores and Rinascita, known for violent storms
- **Cetus Territory**: Mid-sea region where the massive creature dwells
- **Ragunna Port**: Entry point to Rinascita, customs station
- **Genesis Nexus Registration**: First stop for registration and identification

## Prologue: Important NPCs

- **Shorekeeper**: Provides initial information about Abby's condition
- **Brant**: Captain of the Troupe of Fools, experienced sailor
- **Roccia**: Troupe member, provides cultural insights
- **Lario**: Troupe member
- **Customs Officials**: First contact with Ragunna's bureaucracy

## Act I: The Sacred Breeze So Often Breathes

## Act I: Synopsis

After registering at the Genesis Nexus, Rover is assigned Zani as a guide. During a Carnevale rehearsal, a Vitreum Dancer Echo loses control, claiming to be Imperator and asking for help. This triggers an investigation into Echo malfunctions that reveals political tensions between the Montelli and Fisalia families, with connections to a disaster 10 years ago when the Carnevale was suspended.

## Act I: Key Revelations

- **Imperator's Plea**: A malfunctioning Echo claims to be the Sentinel calling for help
- **Echo Control System**: Common Echoes in Ragunna have physical autonomous existence via Genesis Nexus
- **Carnevale Suspension**: The festival was halted 10 years ago following widespread Echo control failures
- **Family Rivalry**: Montelli family (progressive, Reserved Terminal developers) vs Fisalia family (traditional, Order-aligned)
- **Reserved Terminals**: Portable Echo technology independent of Order control, controversial innovation
- **Order's Authority**: Order of the Deep treats Echo malfunctions as divine punishment requiring "purification" (memory reset)
- **Political Investigation**: Rover gets caught in power struggles between noble families

## Act I: Key Locations

- **Ragunna City Square**: Central hub with Genesis Nexus
- **Bank Street**: Location of Averardo Bank (employer of Zani) and Mercurio Echoes shop
- **Cathedral of Mercury**: Religious center of the Order of the Deep
- **Montelli Quarter**: Reserved Terminal facilities and family holdings
- **Carnevale Rehearsal Stage**: Where the first Echo malfunction occurs
- **Trattoria Margherita**: Restaurant district, gathering place
- **Lighthouse of Dawn**: Coastal landmark
- **Pioneer Association**: Exploration guild headquarters

## Act I: Important NPCs

- **Zani**: Averardo Bank employee assigned as Rover's guide, pragmatic and weary
- **Phoebe**: Order Acolyte, earnest believer investigating Echo incidents
- **Carlotta**: Montelli family member, politically savvy
- **Cristoforo**: Mysterious playwright working on script about Blessed Maiden
- **Alberto**: Head of Averardo Bank, Zani's superior
- **Margherita**: Restaurant owner, information source
- **Fenrico (Primus)**: "Enlightened One," Imperator's only known Resonator, leader of Order

## Act II: Veils Off in Sun or Shadow

## Act II: Synopsis

Rover and Zani track Gilberto of the Fisalia family to Whisperwind Haven, where Echoes have been mysteriously vanishing. They discover Gilberto using strange Lycoris flower petals to transform Echoes into Tacet Discords while reciting prayers to a "Lord of Tides and Riddles." After defeating him, the team realizes the sea of clouds at Nimbus Sanctum is behaving abnormally—Lorelei, the powerful creature controlling the clouds, has been compromised. Rover journeys alone through the misty waters to confront Lorelei while encountering a mysterious masked woman who speaks cryptically about Ragunna's fate.

## Act II: Key Revelations

- **Lycoris Flowers**: Strange petals with powerful Resonator frequencies that can corrupt Echoes
- **Lord of Tides and Riddles**: Heretical deity worshipped by Gilberto, distinct from Sentinel Imperator
- **Lorelei**: Powerful Remnant creature residing in Atrium of Reflections, controls sea of clouds
- **Second Dark Tide Threat**: Lorelei's compromise could cause clouds to engulf Ragunna like previous disaster
- **Fisalia Conspiracy**: Family involved in deliberately causing Echo malfunctions to frame Montellis
- **Order Complicity**: Priest Alessio appears connected to Fisalia schemes
- **Mysterious Woman**: Knowledgeable figure who shares Gondola with Rover, discusses Ragunna's dual nature (tradition vs innovation)
- **Seminary Ruins**: Reverberations from ancient Order seminary consumed by clouds
- **Blessed Maiden Legend**: Fleurdelys, who sacrificed herself during Dark Tide, is central to local mythology

## Act II: Key Locations

- **Whisperwind Haven**: Windmill region, legendary home of Blessed Maiden Fleurdelys
  - Polyphemos Windmills

  - Egla Town (nectarwine production)
- **Nimbus Sanctum**: Sea of clouds region with perpetual mist

- **Gondola Network**: Echo-powered boats navigating canals and cloud-seas
- **Port Areas**: Harbormaster stations for cloud navigation
- **Train Station**: Transport hub in the clouds

## Act II: Important NPCs

- **Gilberto**: Fisalia family member conducting heretical rituals
- **Lorelei**: Guardian creature of the clouds, recently compromised
- **Mysterious Masked Woman**: Philosopher who discusses Ragunna's contradictions
- **Noah**: Gondola Echo encountered during travel
- **Priest Alessio**: Order priest connected to conspiracy
- **Cristoforo**: Playwright encountered again researching Blessed Maiden

## Act II: Combat Encounters

- **Controlled Echoes**: Corrupted by Lycoris frequencies

- **Lorelei Boss Fight**: Powerful Remnant creature battle in Atrium of Reflections

## Act II: Cultural Elements Revealed

- **Carnevale Tradition**: 7-day masquerade festival celebrating transcendence
- **Laureate System**: Champion who hears Sentinel's voice
- **Blessed Maiden Role**: Historical figure who saved Ragunna during Dark Tide

## Act IV: The Maiden, The Defier, The Death Crier

## Act IV: Synopsis

Rover searches for the Inverted Tower mentioned by Imperator. With help from Ciaccona, a bard with memory-recreation abilities, Rover re-experiences the meeting with Cartethyia and learns she's trapped in a Sonoro Sphere. Cantarella, head of Fisalia family, invites Rover to Porto-Veno Castle and reveals shocking truths: Sentinel Imperator has been corrupted and merged with Threnodian Leviathan. The sword Tyrvine contains Imperator's essence, meant to choose a "Blessed Maiden" Resonator. Rover and Cantarella journey to Avinoleum ruins to reach Cartethyia and uncover the truth behind the sealed tower.

## Act IV: Key Revelations

- **Imperator's Corruption**: Sentinel has merged with Threnodian Leviathan, creating hybrid hippocamp entity
- **Tyrvine's Purpose**: Sword of Divinity contains Imperator's essence fragment, seeks Blessed Maiden
- **Cartethyia's Location**: Trapped in inverted Sonoro Sphere in Avinoleum ruins
- **"The Maiden is Dead"**: Message discovered by Cantarella after failed resonance attempt
- **Fisalia Family Truth**: Not Threnodian worshippers but defenders trying to save Imperator
- **Second Resonance Awakening**: Process similar to Jinhsi's with Jué required to wield Tyrvine
- **Dark Tide Origin**: Avinoleum was origin point of Second Dark Tide
- **Three Swords System**: Tyrvine (Divinity/Imperator), Hognis (Discord/Leviathan), Haultir (Virtue/Cartethyia)
- **Inverted Tower Structure**: Central spire with three seals requiring three swords
- **Cantarella's Identity**: She is the "jellyfish-like" figure Cartethyia mentioned, has travelled Solaris-3 seeking aid

## Act IV: Key Locations

- **Porto-Veno Castle**: Fisalia family estate in Thessaleo Fells
  - Secret Room with true Imperator statue
  - Underground station access
- **Avinoleum Ruins**: Ancient Order seminary, largest ever built
  - Sonoro Sphere (inverted gravity field)
  - Tower of Salvation (first seal)
  - Tower of Unity (second seal)
  - Tower of Ascension (third seal)
  - Zargon Garden (dragon Reverberations)
  - Path of Bestowal (final approach)
  - Hall of Swordsmanship
  - Holy Spire of Confluence
- **Beohr Waters**: Submerged region containing Avinoleum
  - Beohr Trench (Cetus habitat)
  - Avinoleum Ground Remains
- **Egla Town**: Launch point in Whisperwind Haven

## Act IV: Important NPCs

- **Ciaccona**: Wandering bard with Resonance ability to recreate past experiences
- **Cartethyia/Fleurdelys**: Blessed Maiden figure, true identity complex
  - Created by Leviathan to be "perfect"
  - Split consciousness: Cartethyia (resistance) and Fleurdelys (host)
- **Cantarella**: Fisalia family head, former Blessed Maiden candidate
  - Sacrificed Second Resonance Awakening during Second Dark Tide
  - Travelled across Solaris-3 seeking help (mentions "friend" in Mingting)
- **Sebastian**: Cantarella's butler
- **Imperator (Sentinel)**: Corrupted entity, consciousness trapped in tower
- **Leviathan (Threnodian)**: Entity that assimilated Imperator through spiritual plague

## Act IV: Major Events

1. **Memory Recreation**: Ciaccona uses Forte to help Rover relive Cartethyia meeting
2. **Fisalia Invitation**: Unexpected summons from family matriarch
3. **Truth Revelation**: Cantarella explains Imperator's corruption and Threnodian assimilation
4. **Porto-Veno Secrets**: Discovery of true Imperator statue and family's real mission
5. **Journey to Avinoleum**: Travel through Beohr Waters to ruins
6. **Barrier Breakthrough**: Using Tyrvine to enter Sonoro Sphere
7. **Three Sword Quest**: Placing swords on pedestals to break seals
8. **Vision Sequences**: Each sword reveals memories and truths
9. **Cartethyia's Farewell**: Request to merge with Fleurdelys and end Threnodian
10. **Final Battle**: Rover's defiance, fighting Fleurdelys/Leviathan
11. **Imperator's Sacrifice**: Sentinel's consciousness fades after explaining first plan
12. **Cartethyia's Rebirth**: Awakened whole, personality shifts to more lively state

## Act IV: Imperator's First Plan (Revealed)

- **Goal**: Destroy Leviathan by exploiting universal law (one Resonator per Sentinel)
- **Method**:
  1. Allow partial assimilation while safeguarding core data
  2. Leviathan creates Fleurdelys as first Resonator from hybrid form
  3. Wait for new Blessed Maiden via Tyrvine + Second Awakening (Fisalia trials)
  4. Two Resonators creates law violation
  5. Conflicting frequencies collapse, destroying Threnodian
- **Outcome**: Plan failed when Cantarella sacrificed awakening to save Imperator temporarily
- **Result**: Imperator calls it "a plan born of hope" that was undone

## Act IV: Character Development

- **Rover**: Takes initiative, refuses predetermined fate, chooses own solution
- **Cartethyia**: Transforms from uncertain trapped figure to lively, whole person
- **Cantarella**: Reveals depth of sacrifice and dedication to saving Imperator
- **Abby**: Shows traces of Imperator's frequencies embedded in Rover's Tacet Mark

## Act IV: Thematic Elements

- Identity and selfhood (Cartethyia's split, Rover's role)
- Sacrifice and purpose (Cantarella's choice, Fleurdelys's duty)
- Corruption and resistance (Imperator's struggle, Cartethyia's defiance)
- Hope and defiance (Rover's rejection of predetermined ending)
- Memory and truth (recovering past, facing reality)

## Act IV: Artifacts Obtained

- **Tyrvine**: Sword of Divinity, contains Imperator frequencies
- **Hognis**: Sword of Discord, contains Leviathan frequencies
- **Haultir**: Sword of Virtue, Cartethyia's personal weapon
- **Bloodpact's Pledge**: Symbol of pact with Fisalia family

## Act V: Shadow of Glory

## Act V: Synopsis

Cartethyia vanishes after events in Inverted Tower. Following faint traces, Rover arrives in Septimont, a city-state known for its gladiatorial tradition and rejection of religious devotion to the Sentinel. The Great Agon (championship held every four years) is underway, drawing crowds from across Solaris-3. Rover teams up with gladiator Lupa to compete in the tournament while investigating strange Echo anomalies and Cartethyia's whereabouts. Dark forces manipulate events from shadows, and Rover receives cryptic warnings from Phrolova about an impending catastrophe tied to Lupa herself.

## Act V: Key Revelations

- **Septimont's Philosophy**: "All for glory, fear no death" - revere heroes, not Divinity
- **Arsinosa**: Septimont's Guardian Echo, half-human half-lion, born from settlers' battle-lust and pride
- **Lupa's Secret**: She carries Cartethyia's frequency and transforms into another woman (Mya) in brief flashes
- **Mya's Fate**: Famous gladiator who disappeared 20 years ago during Great Agon, possibly due to betting scandals and mental breakdown
- **Bloody Agon**: Former brutal tournament system before Ephor Augusta's reforms
- **Match-Fixing Conspiracy**: House Silva involved in illegal betting and Echo manipulation
- **Lycoris Influence**: Same corrupting flower petals from Ragunna appear affecting Echoes
- **Phrolova's Warning**: Claims Lupa will trigger Dark Tide surge in Septimont within three days
- **Nameless One**: Fractsidus member manipulating events, obsessed with heroic tales
- **Anomaly Overlap**: Real world and anomalous "Bloody Agon" overlap during finals
- **Cartethyia's Parallel Journey**: She fights in shadow alongside Rover, confronting Cristoforo

## Act V: Key Locations

- **Septimont Archipelago**: Warrior city-state opposite from Ragunna
  - Capitoline Hill (upper city, government center)
  - Lower City districts
  - Vale of Glory (burial ground for heroes)
- **Colosseum Olymdos**: Grand arena on hilltop for championship matches
- **Hero's Way**: Ceremonial path gladiators walk to reach Colosseum
- **Leonidas Hotel**: Premier accommodation, Lupa's lifetime suite gifted to Rover
- **Solis Restaurant**: Fulvia's establishment, gathering place for gladiators
- **Gryphon Fortress**: Noble district with ancient Gryphon statues
- **Old District Ruins**: Submerged former city area beneath waters, dangerous with Kerasaurs
- **Tercules Statue**: Ancient monument by water where Cartethyia passed through
- **Village Entry Point**: First settlement after arriving from Ragunna
- **Hero's Legacy Armor**: Ancient statue containing Echo awaiting worthy warrior
- **Temple**: Priestess Iuno's domain with eternal flames
- **Training Arenas**: Scattered smaller venues for qualifier matches

## Act V: Important NPCs

- **Lupa**: Star gladiator who left House Silva team over match-fixing
  - Exceptional combat skills, beloved by fans
  - Struggles with fear of losing favor and identity
  - Unknowingly connected to Mya and anomaly
- **Mya**: Legendary gladiator from 20 years ago
  - Disappeared during Great Agon under mysterious circumstances
  - Exploited by manager and instructor, mentally unstable
  - Used as cover for noble schemes involving bloodshed
- **Ephor Augusta**: Current leader of Septimont
  - Former champion four years ago, rose from humble origins
  - Implemented reforms ending "Bloody Agon" brutality
  - Swift in judgment, commands great respect
- **Priestess Iuno**: Prophetess with authority for final judgments
  - Born during eclipse, most gifted in her line

## Segue: Rust, Sword and the Sun

While waiting for an audience with Ephor Augusta at the Solis Tavern, the Rover and Abby encounter Nestor, a blind bard. Nestor offers to recount the "Epic of the Blazing Sun," the origin story of Augusta. Instead of merely listening, the Rover delves into the "script" of history, roleplaying as a young Augusta to experience her rise from a war-torn refugee to the ruler of Septimont.

## Segue (Rust): Key Revelations

Augusta's Origin: She was a commoner from a village destroyed by the Dark Tide, possessing no noble blood or powerful Resonance initially. Her primary motivation was survival and the desire to gain enough strength to protect her home.

The Nature of Glory: The quest deconstructs Septimont's obsession with "glory," revealing it often masks corruption, match-fixing, and the exploitation of the weak by the noble houses.

## Segue (Rust): Key Locations

Solis Tavern: A hub for information and storytelling in Septimont.

Septimont City (Flashback): A version of the city from 20 years ago, highlighting the stark class divide between the "Upper City" nobles and "Underground" gladiators.

## Segue (Rust): Important Characters

Nestor: A blind bard who chronicles history.

Cato: Augusta's mentor, a retired gladiator who taught her that survival requires more than just brute strength.

The Old-School Noble: A representative of the corrupt aristocracy who tried to sabotage Augusta.

## Internal Chapter I: New Arrival in The Big City

## Internal Chapter I: Synopsis

The player controls a young Augusta arriving in Septimont. She navigates the harsh "eat or be eaten" culture of the city. Rejected by official channels due to her lack of status, she seeks a mentor and finds Cato. The chapter focuses on her struggle to pass Cato's entry test: landing a single blow on him despite being vastly outmatched.
Key Gameplay Element: Management of Health, Martialism, Willpower, and Reputation.

## Internal Chapter II: Gladiator Training

## Internal Chapter II: Synopsis

Augusta undergoes grueling training under Cato. The narrative explores the psychological toll of the arena. Augusta is forced to enter the Underground Arena, where gladiators are sold as merchandise. She witnesses the "rot in high places"—nobles betting on lives—and realizes that the indulgence of the wealthy contributed to her village's destruction. She resolves to shatter these rules rather than just survive them.

## Internal Chapter II: Key Revelations

The Noble's Challenge: An arrogant noble mocks her "unremarkable Forte," leading to a wager that sets up the "Impossible Trial."

## Internal Chapter III: The Impossible Trial

## Internal Chapter III: Synopsis

To gain sponsorship, Augusta must complete three "impossible" tasks set by a corrupt noble:

Slay the Lion Beast: She defeats a beast immune to steel by using the environment (stalactites) to crush it.

Collect 100 Lives: She collects 100 swords from defeated opponents. This culminates in a duel with Aridel, the "Arena King." Despite the nobles rigging the floor with oil, Augusta uses Aridel's heavy armor against him, winning without killing him.

The Divine Blessing: The final task is revealed: she must obtain the blessing of Arsinosa, the Lioness of Glory—a feat no mortal has achieved.

## Internal Chapter IV: At the Summit of Septimont

## Internal Chapter IV: Synopsis

Augusta confronts Arsinosa at the peak of the Colosseum. Unlike previous challengers who sought to dominate the beast, Augusta fights with a "will of iron" that resonates with the creature. Instead of slaying the guardian, she earns its acknowledgement. This act defies the script of the nobles, who expected her to die, and cements her legend as the "Sun" of Septimont.

## Internal Chapter IV: Key Revelations

The Ephor's Legitimacy: Augusta's rule is founded on this moment—she rules by merit and the "consent" of the city's guardian, not by lineage.

## Act VIII: By Sun's Burning Hand

## Act VIII: Synopsis

The "High Tide" season arrives, bringing a surge of Tacet Discords to the Sanguis Plateaus. Ephor Augusta mobilizes the city's forces for a massive hunt. The Rover joins the expedition, ostensibly to participate in the hunt but secretly to investigate the Three Heroes' Crest. As the hunt progresses, political tensions flare as the noble houses vie for credit, but Augusta's leadership unites the factions against a massive Corrosaurus: Tyrannos.

## Act VIII: Key Revelations

The Three Heroes: The "Three Heroes' Crest" is a sacred site named after ancient heroes who previously held back the tide. It is revealed to be a focal point for the Dark Tide's energy.

Iuno's Role: While Augusta leads the charge, Priestess Iuno is revealed to be maintaining the metaphysical barriers (Anti-Corrosion Wards) that protect the soldiers from the "Blightcloud."

## Act VIII: Key Locations

Sanguis Plateaus: A red-earthed volcanic region.

Hunter's Den: The forward operating base for the Gladiators.

Three Heroes' Crest: The sacred summit where the final confrontation occurs.

## Act VIII: Important Characters

Augusta: Displays her military prowess and ability to command loyalty.

Iuno: Shows signs of strain from holding back the corruption.

Avidius: A mysterious figure who appears during the hunt, seemingly connected to the Dark Tide.

## Act VIII: Political Developments

Unification: The successful hunt consolidates Augusta's power, effectively ending the squabbling of the minor houses and preparing Septimont for total war.

## Act IX: By Moon's Fated Light

## Act IX: Synopsis

Following the victory in Act VIII, a chilling reality sets in: Priestess Iuno has been erased from the memories of everyone in Septimont except the Rover and Abby. Historical records now state she died three years ago. The Rover enters the Chaos Realm—a dimension of causality knots—to retrieve her.

## Act IX: Key Revelations

Iuno's Sacrifice: Born with prophetic sight, Iuno foresaw Septimont's total destruction. Three years ago, she sacrificed her "destiny" (her existence and sight) to become an "arrow against fate," altering a doomed timeline.

The Anchor: By saving her, the Rover anchors her back into reality. She loses her prophecy but gains the power to "anchor" reality against the Dark Tide, a crucial ability for the upcoming war.

Causal Limbo: The Dark Tide can weaponize causality, erasing threats from history—a tactic the Sentinel was trying to counter.

## Act IX: Key Locations

Tetragon Temple: Iuno's home, now devoid of her memory.

Chaos Realm: A surreal dimension of floating ruins and moonlit paths.

## Act IX: Important Characters

Iuno: The "forgotten" heroine.

Lillibet: An attendant at the temple who unknowingly helps Rover find the "knot" in causality.

## Act X: The Bygone Shall Always Return

## Act X: Synopsis

The Rover returns to Ragunna to find the city in panic. Avidius, claiming to be the "Harbinger of the Dark Tide," attacks. It is revealed that Avidius is a construct created by Cristoforo (The Playwright), modeled after the memories of a "Mad King" (Caligula). The Rover must enter the Civilization Capsule (the Sentinel's Relic) to uncover the truth before defeating Avidius at Thessaleo Fells.

## Act X: Key Revelations

The Playwright's Power: Cristoforo can bring "fictional" or "remembered" entities to life using the Dark Tide. Avidius believed he was real, which gave him power.

The Sentinel's Message: The Civilization Capsule does not require the Threnodian's power to open because it was tainted by the Tide; it opens to the Rover's presence, delivering a final warning about the "Paradise" the Fractsidus intends to create.

Fenrico's Fate: Hints are dropped that Fenrico (the Primus) has been missing or acting strangely because he is preparing for a final sacrifice.

## Act X: Key Locations

Thessaleo Fells: The site of the confrontation with Avidius.

Civilization Capsule: An ancient data repository.

## Act X: Important Characters

Cristoforo: Revealed as a major antagonist manipulating events from behind the scenes.

Avidius (The Mad King): A tragic villain who is merely a puppet of memory.

## Act XI: Dawn Breaks on Dark Tides

## Act XI: Synopsis

The climax of the Rinascita saga. The Dark Tide erupts at Three Heroes' Crest, threatening to swallow both city-states. Abby becomes corrupted by the Tide, infecting the Rover. The coalition of Ragunna and Septimont forces gathers for a final stand. The Rover enters the "Journeying Paradise" (the subconscious realm of the Tide) to confront the mastermind, Schwarzloch (The Grand Architect).

## Act XI: Key Revelations

Galbrena's True Identity: She is revealed to be a hero from a past era who sacrificed her humanity to become a "Monster Hunter," making a pact with the Tacet Discord Chimera.

The Threnodian's Revival: The enemy is a manufactured Threnodian created by the Fractsidus using a Gem.

The End of Divinity: The quest confirms the death/departure of the Sentinel Imperator and Primus Fenrico, marking the end of theocratic rule and the beginning of the "Age of Humanity."

Fractsidus Plan: Their goal was to merge all consciousness into a "Paradise" to avoid the pain of the Lament.

## Act XI: Key Locations

Three Heroes' Crest: The battlefield.

Journeying Paradise: A surreal, subconscious dimension reflecting the trauma of Rinascita.

## Act XI: Important Characters

Galbrena: Sacrifices herself in the Paradise to hold back Schwarzloch.

Schwarzloch: The leader of the Fractsidus, revealed to be Kharon, the ferryman.

Phrolova & Scar: Act as enforcers for the Fractsidus plan.

## Act XI: Political Developments

The Coalition: Ragunna and Septimont fight as one, laying the groundwork for a unified nation led by Augusta (Political) and Phoebe (Religious/Cultural).

## Segue: A Stranger in a Strange Land

## Segue (Stranger): Synopsis

In the aftermath of the war, the bard Ciaccona recounts the tale of Qiuyuan, a swordsman from Huanglong who aided in the defense of Ragunna. The player relives Qiuyuan's journey: arriving in the city, defending civilians, and confronting the corrupted form of Fenrico the First.

## Segue (Stranger): Key Revelations

Fenrico's Redemption: Fenrico had succumbed to the Threnodian's corruption but accepted his death as destiny, handing over the "First Relic" (Napoli's Lantern) to Qiuyuan to pass to the Rover.

Huanglong Connection: Qiuyuan was sent by the Grand Marshal of Mingting to assist the Rover, establishing a diplomatic link between the nations.

Geshu Lin: Qiuyuan reveals he was originally tasked to execute General Geshu Lin but discovered the General had fallen to the Threnodian, adding context to the Jinzhou storyline.

## Segue (Stranger): Important Characters

Qiuyuan: An agent of the Mingting Internal Security Agency.

Fenrico: The fallen Primus.

## Act XII: Stagnant Dawn on Wastelands

## Act XII: Synopsis

Following coordinates from the Shorekeeper, the Rover travels to Honami City, a metropolis erased by the Lament and preserved in a "stagnant" state within a Sonoro Sphere. They team up with Chisa (who has been trapped in time) and a detective persona named Polmes to investigate the city.

## Act XII: Key Revelations

Irideglow: The city is filled with crystallized Lament residue called "Irideglow," which drains body heat and sanity.

Controllable Sonoro Project: Research logs reveal the Fractsidus were experimenting here to expand Sonoro Spheres and control the Lament.

Time Dilation: Chisa believes only months have passed, but over 20 years have elapsed in the outside world.

## Act XII: Key Locations

Honami City: A frozen, modern metropolis with areas like the Neon Tower and Commercial District.

Chisa recovers at The Black Shores but suddenly returns to Honami City. Rover follows her. ## Overall Narrative Themes

## Themes: Core Conflicts

- **Identity and Truth**: Who is Cartethyia? What is Imperator's true nature?
- **Corruption vs Purity**: Threnodian assimilation vs resistance
- **Faith vs Reality**: Order's doctrine vs actual history
- **Progress vs Tradition**: Montelli innovation vs Fisalia conservatism
- **Glory vs Shadow**: Public heroism hiding darker truths
- **Choice vs Fate**: Predetermination vs free will

## Themes: Major Story Threads

1. **Abby's Deterioration**: Ongoing crisis requiring Sentinel's intervention
2. **Imperator's Fate**: Trapped, corrupted entity seeking liberation
3. **Cartethyia's Journey**: From trapped consciousness to whole being
4. **Political Intrigue**: Noble families maneuvering in Ragunna
5. **Echo Anomalies**: System-wide malfunctions indicating deeper issues
6. **Threnodian Threat**: Leviathan's assimilation attempt and defeat
7. **Septimont Mystery**: Parallel investigation in warrior city
8. **Fractsidus Manipulation**: Phrolova and Nameless One's schemes

## Themes: World-Building Elements

- **Rinascita Geography**: Two major city-states with contrasting philosophies
- **Echo Technology**: Dual systems (Genesis Nexus vs Reserved Terminals)
- **Cultural Division**: Ragunna's faith vs Septimont's strength worship
- **Historical Events**: Dark Tides shaping both cities
- **Power Structures**: Order dominance vs Ephor rule
- **Social Stratification**: Righteous/Fools in Ragunna, nobles/commoners in Septimont

## Themes: Character Growth Arcs

- **Rover**: From seeking help to becoming decisive hero defying fate
- **Cartethyia**: From uncertain fragment to confident whole self
- **Lupa**: Grappling with identity beyond glory
- **Cantarella**: From mysterious recluse to revealed sacrificial hero
- **Phoebe**: From naive believer to understanding corruption exists
