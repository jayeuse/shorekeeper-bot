## Act III: What Yesterday Wept, Today Doth Sing

**Synopsis**
The Rover wakes up on the misty shores of Penitent's End after falling from a bridge during a dragon attack. They are found by Roccia and Pero of the Troupe of Fools, who reveal that Abby saved the Rover's life but has retreated into the Rover's body to recover. The Rover navigates the island's treacherous "Remnant Clouds" and Tacet Discords, eventually confronting the Dragon of Dirge in its territory. After the battle, the Rover helps the Troupe—specifically rescuing a member named Bardolino—and participates in a rehearsal for the upcoming Carnevale. The act concludes with a confrontation with Phrolova, who traps the Rover in a "created space" to witness a twisted finale, followed by a mysterious invitation from Carlotta to meet at the Cathedral of Mercury.

**Key Revelations**

- **The Lie of the Pilgrimage:** The "Pilgrim's Sail" is exposed as a method of execution, not redemption. The Order of the Deep sends dissidents ("Fools") to Penitent's End to die by the hands of the Dragon of Dirge or the Remnant Clouds.
- **Origin of the Troupe:** The Troupe of Fools is comprised entirely of survivors of these death sentences. Brant, the leader, was exiled as a child for questioning why the Sentinel was worshipped despite never being seen.
- **Fool's Elysium:** The Troupe's base is built from the wreckage of the pilgrim ships, symbolizing their resilience ("dancing in chains").
- **Phrolova's Power:** Phrolova demonstrates the ability to manipulate space and summon Tacet Discords (like Hecate) using a "script," hinting at her role as a "Playwright" for the Fractsidus.

**Key Locations**

- **Penitent's End:** A desolate, fog-shrouded island used as a dumping ground for the Order's enemies.
- **Fool's Elysium:** A hidden cavern sanctuary adorned with colorful, makeshift decorations made from ship debris.
- **Dragon of Dirge Territory:** The nesting ground of the region's Calamity-class Tacet Discord.

**Important Characters**

- **Roccia:** The Troupe's "First Mate" and a fierce protector.
- **Brant:** The charismatic and optimistic Captain of the Troupe.
- **Phrolova:** A Fractsidus agent manipulating events from the shadows.
- **Carlotta:** Revealed to be aiding the Rover to expose the Order's secrets.

**Cultural Elements**

- **"Dancing in Chains":** The Troupe's philosophy of finding freedom and joy even in the face of certain death and oppression. They value laughter as their "treasure" and use their performances to speak truth to power.

**Political Developments**

- **The Order's Crackdown:** The reinstatement of the Pilgrim's Sail (Fool's Sails) by Fenrico is confirmed as a tool for political purging rather than religious piety.

## Interlude: Old Man and the Whale

**Synopsis**
This quest serves as a narrative bridge involving Phoebe and the legend of a "ghost ship." The Rover aids Phoebe in investigating reports of a "Siren" and a "Ghost Ship" haunting the waters near the Riccioli Islands. They encounter Cetus the Tidebreaker, a massive whale-like Tacet Discord that is initially hostile. By deciphering ancient tablets and working with the eccentric Captain Ahab, they discover that the Tidebreaker is not a monster but a guardian tasked with guiding lost souls. The "Siren" is revealed to be a Lorelei's Kindred (a Tacet Discord) mimicking the dead. The quest culminates in the Rover and Phoebe repairing the ship Pequod and sailing into the storm to defeat the Siren, ultimately reconciling with the Tidebreaker.

**Key Revelations**

- **Cetus the Tidebreaker:** Far from being a mindless beast, the Tidebreaker is a benevolent entity that guides the souls of those lost at sea to the "realm beyond."
- **The Siren's Identity:** The "Siren" causing ships to wreck is a Tacet Discord variant of Lorelei, which uses the frequencies of the deceased to lure the living.
- **Phoebe's Growth:** Phoebe, a devout Acolyte, learns that the Order's teachings about "monsters" are not always absolute, leading her to question the rigid dogma she was raised with.

**Key Locations**

- **Riccioli Islands:** A tropical archipelago filled with marine resources and ancient ruins.
- **Reyes Island:** The specific location of the lighthouse and the final confrontation.
- **The Pequod:** A repaired ghost ship used to navigate the storm.

**Important Characters**

- **Phoebe:** An Acolyte seeking the truth behind the maritime legends.
- **Captain Ahab:** A spectral or eccentric figure obsessed with the "final island" and the whale.
- **Cetus the Tidebreaker:** The giant whale Tacet Discord.

**Cultural Elements**

- **Maritime Folklore:** The quest explores Rinascita's deep-seated superstitions regarding the sea, ghost ships, and the "call" of the deep.

## Act IV: The Maiden, The Defier, The Death Crier

**Synopsis**
Searching for the "Inverted Tower," the Rover meets Ciaccona, a bard who helps them relive a memory of Cartethyia. This leads the Rover to Thessaleo Fells to meet Cantarella, the matriarch of the Fisalia family. Cantarella exposes the horrifying truth: the Sentinel Imperator has been corrupted and merged with the Threnodian Leviathan. The Rover learns of the "Blessed Maiden" trials involving the sword Tyrvine. Finally, at the Inverted Tower (Avinoleum), the Rover meets the consciousness of Cartethyia, who explains that she was created by the Leviathan but chose to defy her purpose. She tasks the Rover with inheriting her Resonance to separate the Sentinel from the Threnodian.

**Key Revelations**

- **The Sentinel-Threnodian Fusion:** The Sentinel Imperator did not simply vanish; they merged with the Threnodian Leviathan to contain it, creating the hybrid entity currently worshipped by the Order.
- **Cantarella's Refusal:** Cantarella was the true "Blessed Maiden" chosen by Tyrvine 20 years ago, but she refused the power to prevent the tragedy she foresaw, forcing Fleurdelys to take her place.
- **Cartethyia's Dual Nature:** Cartethyia split herself into Fleurdelys (the vessel/construct) and Cartethyia (the defying human consciousness).
- **The Rover's Role:** As an Arbiter, the Rover is the only one capable of wielding the power of the "false Divinity" to permanently seal the Threnodian's essence.

**Key Locations**

- **Porto-Veno Castle:** The cliffside estate of the Fisalia family, filled with deep-sea flora.
- **Avinoleum (The Inverted Tower):** An upside-down seminary suspended in the sky, serving as Cartethyia's prison.

**Important Characters**

- **Cantarella:** The cunning matriarch of the Fisalia family who deals in poisons and secrets.
- **Ciaccona:** A bard and survivor of the Golden Anthem poets who knows the truth about the Sentinel.
- **Cartethyia:** The tragic heroine who defied her created destiny.

**Political Developments**

- **Fisalia vs. Order:** The quest highlights the silent war between the Fisalia family (who know the truth) and the Order of the Deep (who enforce the lie).

## Act VI: Flames of Heart

**Synopsis**
Rover and Lupa venture into the Vale of Glory (the graveyard of heroes) to confront the source of the anomaly. They discover that Mya was a construct of the Threnodian Leviathan who committed suicide 20 years ago to prevent the Threnodian from manifesting. Her corpse became the Lady of the Sea, a monster now haunting the waterways. Lupa uses the Copper Mirror of Truth to witness the past and realizes she is meant to be the new vessel. However, Lupa rejects the "script" written by the Playwright (Cristoforo). By defeating the Lady of the Sea, they break the time loop. Cartethyia then reveals that the Sentinel hid critical memories in Septimont's Central Hub.

**Key Revelations**

- **Mya's True Identity:** Mya was not human but a Threnodian creation that gained a soul and chose death over enslavement.
- **The Playwright's Script:** The antagonist Cristoforo has been rewriting reality to force a tragic ending, using Mya's corpse as a conduit for the Threnodian.
- **Lupa's Defiance:** Lupa breaks the cycle not by fighting the Threnodian's influence with brute force, but by asserting her own identity and rewriting the ending of the play.
- **The Central Hub:** The Sentinel Imperator transferred their memories to a facility in Septimont before merging with the Leviathan, meaning the truth of the world is hidden there.

**Key Locations**

- **Vale of Glory:** A solemn valley where Septimont's greatest heroes are buried.
- **Old District:** A flooded, ruined section of the city where the Lady of the Sea resides.
- **Central Hub:** An ancient data repository hidden within the city's infrastructure.

**Important Characters**

- **Mya (Lady of the Sea):** The tragic antagonist/victim.
- **Cristoforo (The Playwright):** Revealed as a major manipulator working with the Fractsidus.
- **Arsinosa (Lioness of Glory):** The guardian beast of Septimont who acknowledges Lupa.

## Act VII: Dreamcatchers in the Secret Gardens

**Synopsis**
Rover travels to the Fabricatorium of the Deep, an underwater facility, to retrieve the residual energy of the Threnodian. They are thrown into the sea by Fenrico but rescued by a wounded Phrolova. Forming a temporary alliance, they navigate the "Lost Beyond," a surreal space created by Phrolova to store human consciousness. They discover that Primus Fenrico intended to save Ragunna by uploading everyone's minds into this space ("Unity"), but he succumbed to the Threnodian's corruption. Phrolova reveals her own motivation: an obsession with reviving the dead. She sacrifices herself to merge with the Lost Beyond, while Rover secures the truth about the "Dark Tide" being a manifestation of collective trauma.

**Key Revelations**

- **Fenrico's "Paradise":** Fenrico's plan was to create a digital afterlife (the Lost Beyond) to save his people from the physical destruction of the Dark Tide, but the plan was twisted by his corruption.
- **Phrolova's Obsession:** Phrolova is not driven by the Fractsidus ideology of destruction, but by a personal, impossible promise to resurrect a dead friend. Her experiments were attempts to fuse Tacet Discord bodies with human memories.
- **The Fractsidus Plan:** The Fractsidus intend to use the Carnevale to amplify belief and facilitate a "divinity separation," extracting the Sentinel's power.
- **The Dark Tide's Nature:** It is confirmed that the Dark Tide is the "subconscious ocean of the Rinascitans' shared trauma," not just a physical invasion.

**Key Locations**

- **Fabricatorium of the Deep:** A massive underwater factory where Echoes are manufactured.
- **The Lost Beyond:** A surreal, dream-like dimension created by Phrolova.
- **Lumen Tower:** The control center of the facility.

**Important Characters**

- **Phrolova:** Shows a more human, tragic side before her apparent sacrifice/merger.
- **Fenrico:** Exposed as a tragic figure who tried to be a savior but became a monster.
- **The Grand Architect (Schwarzloch):** Briefly appears (disguised) to give orders to Phrolova.

**Political Developments**

- **Collapse of the Order's Leadership:** The revelation of Fenrico's corruption and his plans effectively decapitates the Order of the Deep's leadership, setting the stage for the power vacuum in later acts.
