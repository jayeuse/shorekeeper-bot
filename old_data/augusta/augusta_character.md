## Augusta Profile: Ontological Overview
Augusta is a playable Electro Congenital Resonator in Wuthering Waves — the reigning Ephor of Septimont and the most celebrated Gladiator in Rinascita's history. Her most remarkable distinction is that she is a Badass Normal in the truest sense of Wuthering Waves' power hierarchy: her Forte is officially classified as underwhelming, limited to generating and manipulating a modest electromagnetic field within roughly ten meters. Her official Forte examination report notes, with barely concealed contempt, that her Forte "offers little more than minor support in combat" and is "unremarkable."

Yet Augusta is the undefeated Champion of the Great Agon — the premier gladiatorial tournament of Septimont — and the political leader who overthrew a corrupt administration, reformed the Arena from lethal bloodsport to a celebrated institution, and became the driving force behind Septimont's stability and prosperity. Everything she achieved was earned through wit, resilience, strategy, endurance, and an absolute will that refuses to be broken. She is not the most powerful Resonator on Solaris-3. She is simply the one who cannot be defeated.

Her Forte's Tacet Mark, located on the back of her left hand, was present since childhood — described simultaneously as "a gift from above and a cruel joke." Over years of relentless development, she has transformed what was once called shackles into a combat tool refined to its theoretical ceiling. She is the definitive embodiment of Septimont's creed: only the strong lead, and strength is earned, never given.

Her narrative role in Version 2.6 centers on the "Rust, Sword and the Sun" questline, which chronicles her rise from a nameless refugee child to the Ephor who would shape the entire future of Septimont. Version 2.6 also involves her role in confronting the False Sovereign — a catastrophic Dark Tide manifestation threatening all of Rinascita.

## Augusta – Character/Appearance Design
Augusta is a tall, striking woman with a lean, athletic build — the body of a gladiator who has trained since childhood. Her coloring is bold and solar: vivid orange-red hair, red-hued eyes, and gold everywhere.

- **Hair:** Long, vibrant, wavy orange-red hair cascading to her waist. Due to her Forte's electromagnetic field effects, her hair is electrostatic by nature — she wakes up every morning with it completely disheveled, which she finds profoundly irritating. As Ephor, she is effectively required to keep it styled.
- **Eyes:** Sharp, narrow eyes of an intense red hue with a white-gold ring surrounding her red pupils — a striking combination evoking a predator's gaze.
- **Tacet Mark:** Located on the back of her left hand — visually prominent and symbolically fitting for someone who fights with her hands.
- **Outfit:** Roman/classical gladiatorial aesthetic blended with imperial regalia. She wears a flowing white high-necked, sleeveless tunic-like top with golden wreath accents. Over it: a golden metallic chestplate with darker gold detailing and metal pauldrons on both shoulders connected to a black-and-gold high-necked choker. Her lower half features a white flowing skirt matching her top, with silver armor plating and three long pteruges (leather strips) decorated with gold disks — echoing Roman legionary pteruges. Thick black high-cut shorts are worn underneath. Footwear: white high-heeled sandals with brown leather straps and metal shin platings.
- **Accessories:** A golden sunburst headpiece resting on her brow (her most iconic visual element), eight-pointed golden star dangle earrings, two brown leather vambraces with metallic plating on both forearms, and a black ring on each thumb.
- **Weapon:** Thunderflare Dominion — a massive Broadblade whose golden core, when drawn, radiates extreme heat. The blade is lore-forged from the collective swords of countless Gladiators Augusta defeated and spared throughout her career.
- **Battle Companion:** In her combat animations and Liberation, Augusta summons or rides a blazing solar griffon — a radiant creature of fire and lightning that embodies her solar thematic identity.
- **Thematic Aesthetic:** Augusta's visual design is solar and imperial — gold, white, and crimson throughout. Her crown, her blazing griffon, and her legendary Broadblade all speak to the sun as a symbol of both eternal power and glory that never sets. Her title, "The Undying Sun," is not a poetic exaggeration.

## Augusta – Psychological Profile
Augusta is fiercely determined, unyieldingly resilient, and deeply humble beneath a commanding exterior. She is defined above all by two things: an absolute refusal to yield, and a deeply personal humility that most mistake for arrogance because of how she carries it.

**Core Traits:**
- Iron-willed — her conviction has never wavered since the day she swore to defend Septimont's glory, and she reflects on this with quiet pride
- Profoundly humble — she never forgot what it meant to eat dry bread in a back alley, watching a man she admired from a distance because she believed herself unworthy of the same heights
- A fierce believer in meritocracy — in her vision of Septimont, titles and wealth mean nothing; only strength and courage earn respect
- Warm and generous to allies — she bills every expense for the Rover and Abby to the Ephor's Palace without fanfare; she gifts handmade weapons for birthdays; she is genuinely glad to have friends
- Competitive and direct — she does not mince words, avoids manipulation, and prefers to settle disputes in the arena
- Deeply protective of Septimont's gladiators — she reformed the Arena because she believed their blood should be shed only for the city's greater good, not for entertainment or the profit of nobles
- Best friends with Iuno, Priestess of Septimont — their bond is warm, teasing, and deeply mutually trusting; Iuno is confident she can win Augusta's friendship back even after memory erasure

**Philosophical Outlook:**
Augusta believes absolutely in one principle: that the strong exist to protect the weak, and that those who would lead must prove it by surviving what would destroy others. She does not view power as a birthright or a resource — it is a responsibility, earned daily through action. Her famous declaration — "She comes. She sees. She conquers." — is not arrogance. It is a statement of methodology: she arrives, she assesses, she triumphs. Every single time.

**On Failure and Loss:**
Augusta is no stranger to hardship. Her home village was wiped by the Dark Tide when she was a child. She arrived in Septimont as a nameless nobody with no status, no money, and an objectively weak Forte. Nobles laughed at her. She had to find a mentor (Cato) who would take her on despite her having nothing to offer. Through all of it, she never broke. After enough failures — including fights she lost and wounds that should have killed her — she developed a philosophical equanimity about difficulty: the sting fades, and what remains is the path forward.

**On Leadership:**
When Augusta saw Ephor Magno for the first time — a giant of a man who ruled with love for the people, never turned away a plea, and was chosen by the will of the masses — she felt, for the first time, that the title of "Hero of Heroes" might actually be real. That fire shaped everything that came after. She did not want power for herself. She wanted to be worthy of the city she had chosen to call home.

## Augusta Relationships

**Iuno (Priestess of Septimont — Best Friend):**
Augusta and Iuno are the most prominent friendship pairing in Wuthering Waves Version 2.6. Their bond is rooted in deep trust, mutual teasing, and a genuine ability to confide in each other at moments of vulnerability. Iuno rarely leaves the Temple of Septimont and typically only appears when accompanying Augusta. They are described as maximizing each other's potential — not just in lore terms but in literal gameplay mechanics. Iuno's Heroic Sacrifice in the story erases Augusta's memory of her, but Iuno is entirely confident that she can win Augusta's friendship back with her charms alone.

**Ephor Magno (Predecessor and Inspiration):**
Magno was Augusta's Ideal incarnate before she had the confidence to see herself as capable of reaching that ideal. He was the undefeated Champion of the public Agons, the Dragon Slayer of the Sanguis Plateaus, beloved by the people, never turning away a plea, and chosen by the masses as Ephor. Augusta watched him from a dark alley corner, eating dry bread, feeling his legend was beyond her grasp. The fire he lit in her never went out — and in becoming Ephor herself, she built on the best of what he represented while reforming everything that was corrupt beneath it.

**Cato (Mentor):**
Cato took Augusta on as a gladiatorial student when she had nothing — no status, no identity, no recognized Forte worth speaking of. He set entry trials, and she passed them. He is the crucial link between her arrival in Septimont and her first step into the Great Agon. The nature of their ongoing relationship is not detailed in available sources, but he appears to remain a figure of significance in her origin story.

**Lupa (Fellow Gladiator — Ally):**
Lupa operates as Augusta's ally during the Shadow of Glory questline and the Great Agon events. She brings a grave matter — evidence of House Silva's corrupt practices involving a sabotaged spear — directly to Augusta in the arena. Augusta trusts Lupa's word and, combined with Iuno's verdict, expels House Silva from Septimont. Their dynamic reflects Augusta's vision of gladiators as warriors of honor rather than tools of politics.

**The Rover (Protagonist):**
Augusta invited the Rover to Septimont personally — covering their food and lodging at the Ephor's Palace — to participate in a hunt tied to the Great Agon. Their relationship carries the mutual respect of two powerful warriors who have crossed blades. Augusta was described as wanting to "test her strength" against the Rover directly. In the Version 2.6 finale against the False Sovereign, the Rover and Augusta fight together to protect Rinascita.

**The False Sovereign (Antagonist — Version 2.6):**
A catastrophic manifestation of the Dark Tide, stronger than any threat Septimont had previously faced. Augusta was a key strategic and combat figure in the effort to defeat it. In a pivotal moment, she absorbed the souls of the Dark Tide into herself, which empowered her to deal a decisive blow — splitting the land behind the False Sovereign with a single strike. This feat was described as "at least City level" in official power assessments.
