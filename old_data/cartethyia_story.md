## Narrative Chronicle: Overview
The story of Cartethyia is one of duality, spanning from the pastoral fields of Egla Town to the metaphysical depths of the Inverted Tower.

## Story: Chronicle I - The Girl Who Chased Windmills
The story begins in Egla Town, a pastoral settlement in the wider region of Rinascita. Here, Cartethyia grew up as a "rambunctious girl" with no noble lineage. Her defining moment occurred when a traveling dramatist visited the town. He told the tale of a knight who fought a giant that was actually a windmill—a story meant to be a comedy of errors.

Cartethyia, however, did not laugh. In the "delusion" of the knight, she saw the ultimate courage: the willingness to fight an enemy that the world deemed unconquerable or nonexistent. Inspired, she crafted a small wooden sword from a stage prop and began "patrolling" the wheat fields. She called herself a "nameless guardian," protecting the village from imaginary beasts and minor injustices. This childhood innocence established her core moral compass: heroism is not about destiny or power; it is about the will to act.

## Story: Chronicle II - The Blessed Maiden and the Betrayal
Her life changed when the Order of the Deep arrived. Despite her lack of "traditional discipline" or noble blood, the Order identified her frequency as compatible with the Sentinel Imperator. She was taken from Egla to the capital of Rinascita and christened Fleurdelys, the "Blessed Maiden".

For years, she performed the duties of a living saint: studying scriptures, listening to lamentations, and crushing Tacet Discords. However, proximity to the Order revealed its rot. She saw the nobility using her image to consolidate power while the poor suffered. She realized that the "Divinity" she served was being manipulated by human greed. When the Dark Tide threatened the region, the Order faltered. The nobility sought to flee or bargain. Cartethyia made a different choice.

## Story: Chronicle III - The Inverted Tower
To stop the Threnodian Leviathan from consuming Rinascita, a vessel was needed to contain the corruption. Since the Sentinel Imperator was bound, Cartethyia volunteered to enter the Inverted Tower, a metaphysical construct designed to seal the Dark Tide.
- **The Fusion:** Inside, she became the battlefield. The Threnodian’s malice poured into her, expanding her form and twisting her frequency. To prevent her total corruption, the Sentinel poured its own power into her.
- **The Split:** The strain fractured her. **Fleurdelys** became the giant, silent warden—a creature of immense power holding back the tide. **Cartethyia**, the human consciousness, retreated into herself, remaining "small," a ghost in the machine.
- **The Puppets:** For twenty years, she wandered the empty halls. She stitched dolls and staged plays of the "Knight" and the "Monster" to keep her sanity.

## Story: Shadow of Glory - Act 1 (The False Front)
The arrival of the Rover in Rinascita disrupted the twenty-year stalemate. The Rover found a Rinascita governed by a "Blessed Maiden" who was actually **Cantarella**, a puppet ruler installed by the Fisalia family. The nobility hid the truth of the Inverted Tower. The Rover, guided by Abby (who smelled the Threnodian), sensed the deception.

## Story: Shadow of Glory - Act 2 (The Arena and the Wolf)
The Rover encountered **Lupa**, a gladiator in the "Bloody Agon," and a strange, petite woman by her side—Cartethyia.
- **The Anomaly:** No one recognized Cartethyia despite her resemblance to the historical Maiden. She existed in a state of semi-invisibility.
- **The Battle:** During the tournament finals, Tacet Discords breached the arena. Cartethyia drew Haultir. The "delusional girl" and the "historical queen" merged. She fought as a master, protecting Lupa and revealing her identity.

## Story: Shadow of Glory - Act 3 (The Reunion)
The Rover, Lupa, and Cartethyia breached the Inverted Tower and found the physical body of Fleurdelys.
- **The Confrontation:** The Rover engaged Fleurdelys to subdue the rampaging energy.
- **The Sentinel's Offer:** The Sentinel Imperator offered to heal Cartethyia by erasing the traumatic memories of Fleurdelys.
- **The Choice:** Cartethyia refused. She recognized that Fleurdelys, with all her pain, was the one who saved the city. To erase her would be a betrayal. Cartethyia chose to absorb Fleurdelys, accepting the burden, size, and power.

## Story: Conclusion - The Wandering Knight
The fusion was successful. Cartethyia returned to her human form but retained the ability to manifest Fleurdelys at will (the Second Awakening). She declined to return to the Order, leaving the title of Blessed Maiden to history. Instead, she affirmed her pledge to the Rover. She would be a "wandering knight," traveling the world to fight the Threnodians as a free woman with a sword.

## Lore: Theoretical Implications (Rabelle's Curve)
The research notes mention that Cartethyia’s frequency follows a non-convergent Rabelle’s Curve.
- **Physics Interpretation:** A convergent curve settles into a stable state. A non-convergent curve is chaotic, constantly fluctuating without repeating.
- **Lore Implication:** The war inside her is never over. She exists in a state of dynamic equilibrium, constantly surfing the wave of the Threnodian’s malice. If she ever stops fighting (mentally), the curve might collapse, and the Threnodian could take over. This adds a layer of constant, invisible heroism to her existence.
